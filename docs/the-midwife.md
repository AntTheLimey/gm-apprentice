# the-midwife

## What It Does

Your adventure creation partner. the-midwife helps you develop a campaign concept, one-shot, or adventure arc through guided creative conversation — starting from a vague idea, a single image, or nothing at all.

It works in four phases: discovering what excites you, shaping the concept into something playable, structuring the key elements (NPCs, factions, locations, tensions), and producing a polished adventure brief ready for Session 0. If you have an existing campaign vault, it mines your canon for creative opportunities — unresolved threads, dormant factions, unfired Chekhov's guns — and weaves them into the new adventure.

Everything discussed gets filed to a `_midwife/` workspace on disk. Ideas you park or reject go into a seed bank for future inspiration.

## When to Use It

Reach for the-midwife when you need to:

- Start a brand new campaign from scratch
- Develop a one-shot or short arc concept
- Add a new chapter or adventure to an existing campaign
- Turn a vague idea into a playable adventure brief
- Brainstorm when you have no idea what to run next
- Mine your existing campaign for what naturally comes next
- Prepare the creative foundation before Session 0

## What You Need

**Obsidian:** Optional. the-midwife is fully functional without Obsidian. If you have an existing campaign vault, it reads your files to find creative opportunities and ensure new content fits your established canon.

**Game system:** Not required upfront. the-midwife lets the system emerge naturally from the concept. If you already know what system you're running, mention it and it'll tailor advice accordingly.

## Example Prompts

### Starting From Nothing

- "I want to run a game but I have no idea what"
- "Help me come up with a campaign concept"
- "What should I run next?"
- "Brainstorm some adventure ideas with me"
- "I want to run something for my group but I'm stuck"

### Starting From a Spark

- "I have this image of a lighthouse keeper who's been keeping something locked in the basement for thirty years"
- "I want to run something that feels like a heist but in a fantasy setting"
- "I keep thinking about a game set during a long sea voyage"
- "I want my players to feel dread — slow-building, atmospheric dread"
- "I saw a documentary about the 1920s dance marathon craze and now I can't stop thinking about it as an RPG scenario"

### Continuing an Existing Campaign

- "I need a new arc for my CoC campaign — what threads should I pick up?"
- "What's the natural next chapter based on what's happened so far?"
- "The players just resolved the main plot — what comes next?"
- "I want to do a time jump. Help me figure out what changed"
- "One of the PCs died last session and I want the next arc to deal with the fallout"

### Shaping and Refining

- "The concept feels too big for a one-shot — help me scope it down"
- "I've got the premise but I don't know who the antagonist is"
- "How do I make this work if the players refuse to take the obvious hook?"
- "This feels too linear — help me add branching paths"
- "What happens if the players do nothing?"

### Getting to Session 0

- "I think the adventure is ready — write up the brief"
- "Can you set up the vault for this campaign?"
- "Help me prepare the CATS pitch for Session 0"
- "Which of these NPCs should become real vault entities?"
- "I'm happy with the concept — what do I need to prep before we play?"

## What to Expect

When you start a conversation, the-midwife checks whether you're in an existing campaign vault. If you are, it reads your canon and surfaces creative opportunities before asking questions. If not, it asks what you're working with.

The conversation moves through four phases, though it adapts to what you bring. If you arrive with a fully formed concept, it validates and stress-tests rather than starting from zero. If you arrive with nothing, it generates seed ideas and asks which one pulls at you.

Throughout the process, the-midwife files confirmed decisions and parked ideas to disk automatically. You never manage files — it handles the workspace. Ideas you reject go into a seed bank that persists across adventures, so nothing is lost.

At the end, it produces an adventure brief with everything you need for Session 0: premise, tone, adventure shape, core tension, driving forces, key NPCs, locations, entry points, escalation plan, and a "what if they do nothing?" answer. It then offers to scaffold the vault (for new campaigns) or hand off to session-prep (for Session 0 planning).

The-midwife never decides for you. It sparks, shapes, and refines — but every creative decision is yours.

## Tips

- You don't need to arrive with an idea. Starting from nothing is a supported workflow — the-midwife will generate seeds and help you pick one.
- Be honest about what excites you. "I keep thinking about X" is more useful than a polished pitch.
- Don't worry about game system upfront. Let the concept lead and the system will follow.
- If you have an existing vault, start the conversation in that directory. The-midwife will mine your canon automatically.
- Three strong NPCs beat six sketches. The-midwife will push you toward depth over breadth.
- Parked ideas go into a seed bank. Next time you brainstorm, the-midwife will surface relevant seeds from past sessions.
- The adventure brief is a starting point, not a script. It's designed to give you enough structure to improvise confidently.
