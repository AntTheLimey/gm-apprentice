# session-play

## What It Does

Your at-the-table assistant during live play. session-play is optimised for speed — brief answers, essential facts only. It handles quick lookups, rules questions, on-the-fly content generation, and note capture.

## When to Use It

Reach for session-play when you need to:

- Look up an NPC's details or a location description quickly
- Get a fast answer to a rules question
- Generate an NPC, location, or shop on the spot
- Capture raw play notes for later processing

## What You Need

**Obsidian:** Optional. session-play works with any vault or plain folders. It reads existing files for lookups but doesn't create complex structures.

## Example Prompts

- "Quick — what's Inspector Barrow's current status?"
- "What did the investigators learn about the warehouse in session 4?"
- "I need a bartender for this scene"
- "Give me 3 options for where the cult could be meeting"
- "Note: players decided to go to the docks instead of the library"
- "Can they grapple while prone in GURPS?"
- "I need a gun shop — the players want to go shopping"

## What to Expect

Claude keeps responses to 1-5 sentences. No analysis, no unsolicited suggestions. When it generates content (NPCs, locations), it asks if you want to save it to the vault — you can defer that decision if players haven't chosen yet.

## Tips

- Keep prompts short. Claude matches your energy — brief in, brief out.
- Generated content is provisional until you confirm saving. Unsaved content gets flagged for wrap-up.
- Don't worry about detailed notes during play. Fragments and shorthand are fine — session-wrapup will process them properly.
