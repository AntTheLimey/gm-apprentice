# St Anselm's Church

## Overview
**Type:** Structure — deconsecrated church with hidden underground chambers
**Address:** Burial Hill Lane, Old Quarter, Kingsport
**Associated Faction:** Order of the Silver Twilight (secretly)
**Current State:** Ostensibly abandoned; secretly in active use by the cult

## Description

St Anselm's is a small stone church dating to the early 18th century, built on the foundations of an even older structure. It stands on Burial Hill Lane in Kingsport's old quarter — a narrow, winding street of crumbling colonial houses and overgrown lots. The church was deconsecrated in 1895 after a fire damaged the nave, and the diocese never funded repairs. It has been "abandoned" ever since.

### Exterior
The church is built of rough-cut gray granite in a plain New England meetinghouse style — a rectangular nave with a small bell tower (the bell was removed decades ago). The roof is intact but mossy. The windows are boarded from the inside. A wrought-iron fence encloses a small churchyard with tilted, weathered headstones dating to the 1700s. The gate is chained but the chain is newer than it should be — someone maintains access.

An overgrown path leads around the back to a cellar door, half-hidden by ivy. This is how the cult enters.

### Interior (Ground Level)
The nave is stripped bare — pews removed, altar gone, floorboards warped and rotting in places. Water stains on the walls. Bird droppings on the windowsills. The air smells of damp stone and mildew. It looks thoroughly abandoned.

The only notable feature: a door behind where the altar stood, leading down to the crypt.

### The Crypt
A stone staircase descends to a low-ceilinged crypt with arched alcoves. Most alcoves contain old coffins in various states of decay. The far wall of the crypt contains the entrance to the hidden chambers — a section of wall that pivots on a concealed mechanism. The mechanism is triggered by pressing a specific headstone in the wall.

### The Hidden Chambers (The Inner Sanctum)
Beyond the false wall, the architecture changes. The stonework is older and different in character — massive blocks fitted without mortar, carved with symbols that predate the church by centuries. The air is colder and carries that chemical smell.

**Antechamber:** A corridor lined with the recurring eye-within-crescent symbol. Torches in iron brackets (recently lit). The sound of chanting echoes from somewhere below.

**The Library:** A chamber approximately 20 feet square, filled with books, scrolls, and stone tablets. This is where the complete Revelations of Glaaki is kept, along with other forbidden texts. A desk and chair suggest someone has been working here recently — Professor Ashford's prison and workspace.

**The Ritual Chamber:** The deepest point. A circular room 30 feet in diameter with a domed ceiling. The floor is inscribed with geometric patterns in silver inlay. At the center: a circular pit, roughly 6 feet across, descending into absolute darkness. The temperature drops noticeably near the pit. Something moves in the darkness below, but it cannot be seen — only sensed. SAN check to approach: 1/1d6.

**The Cell:** A small room adjacent to the library with a heavy wooden door and iron bolt. Currently holds Professor Ashford.

## GM Notes
This location is the climax of the campaign. It should be the most atmospheric and frightening place the investigators visit. Build tension through the descent — each level deeper is colder, stranger, and more oppressive.

The church is the answer to "where is the inner sanctum?" from the coded letter. The investigators need to identify it as "the old church" mentioned by Ashford. The primary path to this identification is through decoding the letter and researching which churches in Kingsport might qualify. The coded letter's reference to "the old church" is the key connection.

The cult has been using the underground chambers for years. The connection between the church and the pre-colonial stonework beneath it is a mystery even the Order doesn't fully understand — they found the chambers and put them to use, but they didn't build them. Whatever shaped those stones was here long before Kingsport.
