# Ashford Study

## Overview
**Type:** Interior — private study within a residential home
**Address:** 14 Saltonstall Street, Kingsport
**Associated NPC:** [[Professor Ashford]]
**Current State:** Ransacked (as of October 12, 1923)

## Description

The study occupies the entire ground floor rear of Ashford's modest Colonial-era house. It is — or was — a scholar's sanctuary. Floor-to-ceiling bookshelves line three walls, packed with volumes on medieval history, Latin texts, and New England antiquities. A large mahogany desk dominates the center of the room, positioned to face the window that looks out onto a small, overgrown garden.

### As Found (October 12th)
When [[Mrs Whitmore]] discovered the room, it was in chaos. Books had been pulled from shelves and left open on the floor. Desk drawers were yanked out, their contents scattered. Papers covered every surface — manuscript pages, handwritten notes, sketches of symbols. A standing lamp had been knocked over. The curtains were drawn despite the morning hour.

The smell: old paper, pipe tobacco, and something faintly chemical — like formaldehyde or ether. The cot in the corner still had rumpled sheets soaked with sweat. A half-empty bottle of laudanum sat on the desk beside an unwashed teacup.

### Key Features
- **The desk:** Heavy mahogany, Victorian era. The new lock on the central drawer conceals a false bottom where the [[Coded Letter]] was hidden. Mal Bridges found it in Session 1.
- **The manuscript:** Ashford's fragmentary copy of the Revelations of Glaaki sits on a reading stand. Pages are covered with annotations in Ashford's cramped handwriting. Reading the text closely requires a SAN check (0/1d3).
- **The symbols:** Ashford drew the [[Strange Symbol]] repeatedly on loose sheets of paper — dozens of iterations, as if trying to understand or replicate it. Protective Elder Signs are scratched into the windowsills and door frame, suggesting he was trying to ward something away.
- **The bookshelf gap:** A section of the east bookshelf has been cleared. The missing books appear to be Ashford's personal journals from September and October 1923. These were taken — either by Ashford himself or by whoever ransacked the room.

## Clues Found Here

## GM Notes
The study has been thoroughly searched by the investigators in Session 1. There is little reason to return unless new information suggests looking for something specific. However, [[Mrs Whitmore]] has a key and the house remains accessible.

If the investigators return after Session 2, they may notice that someone else has been in the study — books moved slightly, the manuscript pages in a different order. [[Brother Ezra]] or another cult member has been checking whether the coded letter was found.
