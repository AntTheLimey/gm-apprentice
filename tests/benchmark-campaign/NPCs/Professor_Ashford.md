# Professor Henry Ashford

## Overview
**Role:** Central figure — the missing person whose disappearance drives the investigation.
**Age:** 58
**Occupation:** Professor of Antiquities, Miskatonic University
**Status:** Missing since October 12, 1923

## Physical Description
A lean, bookish man with wire-rimmed spectacles and thinning silver hair swept back from a high forehead. Clean-shaven. Typically dressed in a tweed suit with leather elbow patches. Has ink-stained fingers and the slightly stooped posture of a lifelong scholar. When last seen by [[Mrs Whitmore]], he looked gaunt and exhausted, with dark circles under his eyes.

## Personality
Ashford was known as a meticulous, cautious scholar — the kind of man who triple-checked his citations and never published a paper he wasn't certain of. His colleagues describe him as "steady" and "methodical." This makes his recent erratic behavior all the more alarming. Something in the manuscript he acquired fundamentally altered his worldview, and the man his friends knew has been replaced by someone desperate and obsessed.

## Background
Henry Ashford has been at Miskatonic for twenty-five years, specializing in medieval European manuscripts and their connections to New England colonial history. He is well-respected in his field, though his work has never been particularly famous. He is a widower — his wife Elizabeth died in 1918 during the influenza pandemic. They had no children. Since Elizabeth's death, his work has been his life.

## Motivations
- **Before disappearance:** Ashford became convinced that the fragmentary Revelations of Glaaki contained genuine metaphysical truths. His scholarly instinct drove him to seek the complete text, which led him to the [[Order of the Silver Twilight]].
- **Current:** Ashford is imprisoned beneath the old church, being forced to translate the complete Revelations. Part of him is horrified; part of him is fascinated. He is losing the internal battle.

## Secrets
- Ashford voluntarily attended Silver Twilight meetings before realizing what the Order truly was. He is partly responsible for his own predicament.
- He hid the [[Coded Letter]] in his desk as insurance — a breadcrumb trail in case something happened to him.
- He has seen what lies in the pit beneath the ritual chamber. He hasn't told anyone, and the knowledge is slowly breaking his mind.

## Connections
- [[Mrs Whitmore]] — His housekeeper of fifteen years. Loyal and worried.
- [[Brother Ezra]] — His captor. Ezra visited Ashford the night before his disappearance.
- [[Inspector Crane]] — Investigating his disappearance (poorly).
- Dr. Albert Wilmarth — His colleague at Miskatonic, who hired the investigators.

## GM Notes
Ashford is a sympathetic figure who made a terrible mistake. When the investigators find him, he should be pitiful rather than heroic — a broken man who chased knowledge too far. His rescue should feel earned but bittersweet. Even if freed, he will never fully recover from what he has seen and read.
