# Mrs. Margaret Whitmore

## Overview
**Role:** Witness and information source — Ashford's housekeeper.
**Age:** 62
**Occupation:** Housekeeper
**Status:** Active (deeply worried about [[Professor Ashford]])

## Physical Description
A small, wiry woman with steel-gray hair pinned in a tight bun. She has sharp blue eyes that miss very little and hands roughened by decades of domestic work. She wears plain dark dresses with a white apron and keeps a ring of keys at her belt. Despite her small stature, she has the bearing of someone who runs a tight household and does not suffer fools.

## Personality
Mrs. Whitmore is practical, observant, and fiercely loyal to Professor Ashford. She has kept his house for fifteen years and considers herself responsible for his wellbeing — his disappearance is a personal failure in her eyes. She is more perceptive than she lets on and has noticed more about Ashford's recent behavior than she initially revealed to the police.

She is suspicious of the investigators at first but warms to them quickly if they treat her with respect and demonstrate genuine concern for the professor. She has no patience for [[Inspector Crane]]'s dismissive attitude.

## Background
Margaret Whitmore was born in Kingsport and has lived here her entire life. She was widowed young — her husband, a fisherman, was lost at sea in 1901. She has one grown daughter who lives in Boston. She took the position with Ashford shortly after his wife's death, when it became clear that the professor could not maintain a household on his own.

## Motivations
- Find [[Professor Ashford]] and bring him home safely.
- Protect the professor's reputation — she is careful about what she reveals to outsiders.
- Maintain her own dignity in a situation that makes her feel helpless.

## Secrets
- She found a strange medallion under Ashford's pillow a week before his disappearance — a silver disc engraved with the [[Strange Symbol]]. She kept it and has not told the police. She will give it to investigators she trusts.
- She recognized [[Brother Ezra]]'s voice from the evening of October 11th. She heard him say "the time grows short" clearly through the study door.
- She has a spare key to every room in the Ashford house, including the study. She gave one to the investigators in Session 1.

## Connections
- [[Professor Ashford]] — Her employer of fifteen years.
- [[Inspector Crane]] — She reported Ashford's disappearance to him and is frustrated by his lack of urgency.

## GM Notes
Mrs. Whitmore is a grounding presence in the story — a normal, decent person caught up in extraordinary events. She should feel real and sympathetic. Her information is always reliable but sometimes incomplete (she tells what she saw, not what she inferred). She can serve as a recurring touchstone for the investigators: someone to report back to, someone whose worry reminds them what's at stake.

If threatened by the cult, she is brave but not reckless. She would not hesitate to use a kitchen knife if cornered.
