# Inspector Robert Crane

## Overview
**Role:** Police contact — unhelpful but not actively hostile.
**Age:** 47
**Occupation:** Detective Inspector, Kingsport Police Department
**Status:** Active (assigned to the Ashford case)

## Physical Description
A heavyset man with a ruddy complexion and a thick walrus mustache going gray at the tips. Wears a rumpled brown suit and a bowler hat that has seen better days. Smells faintly of pipe tobacco. Has the tired eyes of a man who has seen too many cases go cold and has stopped expecting breakthroughs.

## Personality
Crane is not a bad man, but he is a profoundly mediocre detective. He is methodical in the way that produces paperwork rather than results. He defaults to the most mundane explanation for everything — not because he's stupid, but because entertaining stranger possibilities would require effort and courage he no longer has. He is three years from retirement and counting the days.

He is polite to the investigators but patronizing, especially to Dr. Voss (he struggles with the idea of a woman physician doing detective work). He takes their reports seriously enough to file them but not seriously enough to act on them.

## Background
Robert Crane has been with the Kingsport police for twenty-two years. He was a good beat cop in his younger days — brave, diligent, well-liked. His promotion to detective was deserved. But the years have worn him down. He drinks more than he should. His wife left him in 1920. He solves the easy cases and lets the hard ones gather dust.

## Motivations
- Reach retirement without incident.
- Maintain his reputation as a respectable officer, even if it's built on inertia.
- Avoid anything that might require him to confront the unexplainable.

## Secrets
- Crane is not a member of the [[Order of the Silver Twilight]], but he has been paid to look the other way. A monthly envelope of cash appears in his desk drawer. He tells himself it's from a grateful citizen. He knows better.
- He has a file on "unusual incidents" at the docks going back three years. He has never followed up on any of them.
- He genuinely likes [[Professor Ashford]] — they played chess together at the Kingsport Club. His guilt about not trying harder is real but insufficient to motivate him.

## Connections
- [[Professor Ashford]] — Casual acquaintance, chess partner.
- [[Mrs Whitmore]] — She filed the missing persons report with him.
- The investigators — He is their primary police contact.
- The [[Order of the Silver Twilight]] — He takes their money to not investigate the docks.

## GM Notes
Crane works best as an obstacle born of institutional inertia rather than active villainy. He is not evil — he is weak. If the investigators confront him with overwhelming evidence, he can be turned. A hard Persuade roll (modified by presenting the bribe evidence) could make him an ally for the final session. Without that push, he will continue to obstruct through inaction.

If the investigators try to go over his head, the desk sergeant is sympathetic but can only authorize a patrol check, not a raid. The state police are an option but would take 24-48 hours to mobilize.
