# Tommy Flanagan

## Overview
**Role:** Informant — street-level contact with knowledge of Kingsport's underworld.
**Age:** 29
**Occupation:** Odd jobs, petty crime, information broker
**Status:** Active (introduced in Session 1 as a key contact for dock intelligence)

## Physical Description
A wiry, quick-moving young man with sharp brown eyes and a thin face perpetually in need of a shave. He dresses in a flat cap, a worn jacket, and boots that have been resoled more than once. He has a nervous habit of tapping his fingers on whatever surface is nearest and rarely sits still for long. His smile comes easily but doesn't always reach his eyes.

## Personality
Tommy is a survivor. He grew up on the rough side of Kingsport and learned early that information is more valuable than muscle. He is charming in a roguish way, quick-witted, and an excellent judge of character. He is also fundamentally self-interested — he likes the investigators well enough, but if the situation gets too dangerous, his instinct will be to disappear.

He has a genuine dislike of bullies and a grudging respect for people who stand up to power. Mal Bridges earned his trust on a previous case by not selling him out to the police when he easily could have.

## Background
Thomas Patrick Flanagan was born in Kingsport to an Irish immigrant family. His father worked the docks and died in a loading accident when Tommy was twelve. His mother took in laundry to make ends meet. Tommy dropped out of school at fourteen and has been hustling ever since — running numbers, carrying messages, doing the occasional breaking-and-entering job for people who pay well and don't ask his name.

He is not violent and avoids confrontation when possible. His value lies in what he knows and who he knows. He has contacts among the dock workers, the bootleggers, the petty criminals, and the working poor of Kingsport.

## Motivations
- Make enough money to leave Kingsport someday (he talks about Boston or New York).
- Stay alive and out of prison.
- Maintain his reputation as a reliable (if expensive) source of information.
- Pay off a gambling debt to a man named Rourke who is running out of patience.

## Secrets
- Tommy has been watching the strange activity at the docks for weeks, long before the investigators arrived. He knows more than he told them in Session 1 — specifically, he has seen [[Brother Ezra]] enter and leave the old quarter of town on multiple nights. He is holding this back as leverage for a bigger payday.
- He owes Rourke $150 — a significant sum. If the investigators don't come through with more money, he may try to sell information about them to the cult to clear his debt. Not out of malice, but out of desperation.
- He once saw something being carried out of a building in the old quarter — something wrapped in cloth that was the wrong shape and moved slightly. He has nightmares about it. He has never told anyone.

## Connections
- Mal Bridges — Previous working relationship. Mutual trust, within limits.
- [[Professor Ashford]] — No direct connection, but Tommy has heard dock workers mention "the professor" in connection with the strange deliveries.
- Rourke — His creditor. A dangerous man in his own right.

## GM Notes
Tommy is designed as a recurring contact who provides ground-level intelligence. He was introduced in Session 1 as a key informant who agreed to keep watching the docks. His information about Brother Ezra's movements in the old quarter could be a crucial lead pointing toward the church — but only if the investigators follow up with him.

He is also a moral test: a young man in over his head who could be an ally or a liability depending on how the investigators treat him. Pay him fairly and he's loyal. Ignore him or stiff him and he becomes unpredictable.

**Thread hook for Session 2/3:** Tommy was supposed to report back about dock activity. His information about Ezra's movements in the old quarter would help narrow down the location of the inner sanctum.
