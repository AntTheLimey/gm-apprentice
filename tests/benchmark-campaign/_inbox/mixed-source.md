## NPC PREP — Brother Ezra (scenario antagonist)

**Prof. Blackwood** — tall, gaunt, probably late 50s. Pale enough to look ill. Moves wrong.
Build stats for him:
- STR 60, CON 70, SIZ 65, DEX 85
- Dodge 55%, Stealth 80%, Grapple 50%
- Cthulhu Mythos 35% — he knows things

**Keeper note**: Dr. Blackwood should not be a straight-up combat threat in session 2. The point is that he's *wrong* — unsettling, too fast, too quiet. Save the full horror reveal for session 3.

## RESEARCH NOTES — Historical context, Kingsport 1923

Q: What would a "gentlemen's fraternal order" in Kingsport look like in 1923?
A: Elks Lodge, Masons, Knights of Columbus were all active in coastal Massachusetts. The OST would use this cover — probably presents itself as a literary society or antiquarian club. Respectable front. Members would be local businessmen, maybe a judge or two.

What if the cult had been operating in Kingsport for longer than the investigators realize? Could Ashford's grandfather have had dealings with them? His study did seem strangely stocked for a man who supposedly only discovered the occult recently.

Historical Q: Was laudanum still commonly available in 1923?
A: Yes, though tightening restrictions (Harrison Act 1914 aftermath). A professor might have a prescription. Makes sense as self-medication for what Ashford was experiencing.

I think I undersold [[Inspector Crane]] in session 1. He came across as just incompetent rather than potentially compromised. Should have played him more interestingly. Note to self: give him a moment of hesitation when the OST is mentioned — a beat too long before dismissing it.
