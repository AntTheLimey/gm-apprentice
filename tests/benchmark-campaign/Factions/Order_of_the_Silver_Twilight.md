# Order of the Silver Twilight

## Overview
**Type:** Occult secret society
**Base of Operations:** Silver Twilight Lodge, 42 Harmon Street, Kingsport (public front); hidden chambers beneath the old church in the Old Quarter (true base)
**Membership:** Approximately 30 members, most of whom are unaware of the Order's true purpose
**Leader:** The Grand Master (identity not yet revealed in play)

## Description

The Order of the Silver Twilight presents itself as an exclusive gentlemen's fraternal organization — a club for Kingsport's professional and mercantile elite. The lodge on Harmon Street is a handsome brownstone with a discreet brass plaque. Members enjoy a well-stocked library, a billiards room, fine whiskey, and the company of like-minded men of means. Membership is by invitation only.

This respectable exterior conceals a darker reality. The Order's inner circle — perhaps six to eight members — practices genuine occult rituals drawing on pre-human knowledge. They believe they are on the verge of opening a gateway to dimensions beyond ordinary space, using the ritual chamber beneath the old church. The rank-and-file members know nothing of this; they think the "rituals" are the harmless Masonic-style ceremonies common to such organizations.

## Structure

### Outer Circle (25+ members)
Kingsport businessmen, lawyers, doctors, and academics. They pay dues, attend dinners, and participate in ceremonial initiations that they believe are symbolic. They provide respectability, funding, and unwitting cover.

### Inner Circle (6-8 members)
The true practitioners. They include:
- **The Grand Master** — Identity unknown to the investigators. He directs the Order's occult operations.
- **[[Brother Ezra]]** — Enforcer and operative. Handles the Order's dirty work. Not originally from Kingsport.
- **Dr. Philip Hartwell** — A physician who provides medical support and procures chemicals needed for rituals. (Not yet encountered in play.)
- **Judge Marcus Abernethy** — Provides legal protection and political connections. (Not yet encountered in play.)
- Other unnamed members.

## Goals
1. **Immediate:** Complete the summoning ritual on the night of the new moon (October 20). This requires the translated passages from the Revelations of Glaaki, which [[Professor Ashford]] is being forced to provide.
2. **Long-term:** Open a permanent gateway to the dimension beyond the pit. The inner circle believes this will grant them power and immortality. They are wrong about the immortality part.

## Resources
- The Silver Twilight Lodge (social cover, meeting place)
- Atlantic Maritime Imports (front company for receiving cargo at [[Kingsport Docks]])
- The hidden chambers beneath the old church (ritual space, prison, library)
- Financial resources from wealthy members
- [[Inspector Crane]]'s deliberate inattention (purchased with bribes)
- [[Brother Ezra]]'s enhanced physical abilities

## History
The Order was founded in Kingsport in 1889 by a group of antiquarians who discovered the chambers beneath the old church. Initially, their interest was archaeological. But the texts they found in the underground library — including fragments of the Revelations of Glaaki — led them down a darker path. Over the decades, the Order evolved from a scholarly curiosity into a genuine occult conspiracy.

The binding ritual that created Brother Ezra in his current form was performed in 1912 and represents the Order's most ambitious (and most dangerous) achievement to date. The summoning ritual they now plan dwarfs it in scale.

## Connections
- [[Professor Ashford]] — Attended meetings as a guest, then discovered the true nature of the Order and tried to steal the Revelations. Now held prisoner.
- [[Brother Ezra]] — Primary operative and enforcer.
- [[Inspector Crane]] — Bribed to ignore suspicious activity at the docks.
- [[Mrs Whitmore]] — Unaware of the Order's existence, but vulnerable as leverage against Ashford.

## GM Notes
The Order works best as a conspiracy that is both powerful and brittle. They have money, connections, and an inhuman enforcer. But their ranks are thin, their secrecy depends on ignorance, and their plan has a hard deadline (the new moon). If the investigators can expose them — through evidence, witnesses, or by going to authorities above Crane — the outer circle members will scatter and the inner circle will be left isolated.

The Grand Master's identity can be revealed in Session 3 as a dramatic beat. Good candidates: the university chancellor, the mayor, or another figure the investigators have interacted with and trusted.
