# Session 3: The Inner Sanctum (Prep Notes)

## Session Goals
- Investigators locate the cult's hidden sanctum beneath the old church
- Confrontation with [[Brother Ezra]] and members of the [[Order of the Silver Twilight]]
- Resolution of [[Professor Ashford]]'s fate
- Climactic choice: stop the ritual or flee

## Planned Structure

### Act 1: Following the Trail to the Lodge
The investigators should follow up on the [[Order of the Silver Twilight]] connection by visiting the Silver Twilight Lodge on Harmon Street. This is a respectable-looking brownstone with a brass plaque. During the day, the lodge appears to be nothing more than a gentlemen's social club.

**Expected approach:** The investigators will likely try to bluff their way in or stake out the building. Rev. Marsh could use his clerical credentials to request a meeting. Mal could attempt a nighttime break-in.

**GM Note:** The lodge is a red herring for the location of the sanctum — the real underground chambers are beneath the old church, not the lodge. But the lodge visit should yield information that points them to the church. A ledger in the lodge office mentions "preparations at the St. Anselm's site" if the investigators manage to search the office.

### Act 2: The Descent
Once the investigators identify the church as the location, they can find an entrance to the underground chambers through the church's crypt. The entrance is hidden behind a false wall in the ossuary.

**The Inner Sanctum layout:**
- **Antechamber:** Stone corridor, unnaturally cold. Carved symbols on the walls (the [[Strange Symbol]] repeated). Faint chanting audible from below.
- **Library:** A chamber filled with forbidden texts, including the complete Revelations of Glaaki. This is where Ashford spent his final days of freedom, copying passages before he was discovered.
- **Ritual Chamber:** A large circular room with a pit at the center. The pit descends into darkness. Geometric patterns on the floor that hurt to look at directly. This is where the summoning ritual will take place.

### Act 3: The Confrontation
The cult will be present in force — [[Brother Ezra]] plus 4-6 cultists in robes. The ritual is in its preparatory phase (the new moon isn't until the 20th).

**[[Professor Ashford]]'s fate:** Ashford is alive but imprisoned in a cell adjacent to the library. He has been forced to translate passages from the Revelations of Glaaki for the cult. His mind is fragile — extended exposure to the text has cost him significant SAN. He can walk but is in poor physical condition.

**Combat/Resolution options:**
1. **Stealth rescue:** Extract Ashford without alerting the cult. Difficult but possible. Requires multiple Stealth checks.
2. **Disrupt the ritual:** Destroy the geometric patterns on the chamber floor. This requires getting past the cultists.
3. **Direct confrontation:** Fight. Brother Ezra is a formidable opponent (see NPC stats). The cultists are less dangerous individually but outnumber the investigators.
4. **Bring the authorities:** If the investigators can finally convince [[Inspector Crane]] (or go over his head to the state police), a raid could be organized. This takes time the investigators may not have.

### Possible Complications
- Brother Ezra may have set a trap, expecting the investigators after the dock encounter.
- One of the cultists could be someone the investigators know or trust.
- Ashford may not want to leave — his obsession with the texts may have consumed him.
- If the ritual is further along than expected, stopping it may require more than just physical disruption.

## NPCs Present
- [[Brother Ezra]] (primary antagonist)
- [[Professor Ashford]] (prisoner/rescue target)
- 4-6 unnamed cultists (stats: use standard Cultist template from Keeper's Guide)

## Key Items
- The complete Revelations of Glaaki (Mythos tome, SAN loss to read)
- Ashford's translation notes
- [[Coded Letter]] — if not already decoded, a copy exists in the library
- Ritual components (candles, chalk, a silver bowl containing blood)

## Contingencies
- If investigators don't find the church: have Dockmaster Hayes provide information about deliveries going to "the old church in the quarter" for a price.
- If investigators are captured: they wake up in cells near Ashford, creating a prison-break scenario.
- If investigators go to the police: Crane stalls, but a successful Persuade roll on the desk sergeant gets a patrol car sent. The officers arrive late but provide a useful distraction.

## GM Reminders
- This session should feel climactic — raise the stakes with atmosphere and pacing.
- The underground chambers should be deeply unsettling. Use sensory details: the smell of old stone and something chemical, the sound of chanting echoing from impossible directions, the way shadows move wrong in the lamplight.
- Ashford is a tragic figure. He sought knowledge and found horror. Play his broken state with sympathy, not just as a plot device.
