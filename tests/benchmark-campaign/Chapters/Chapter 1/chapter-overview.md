# Chapter 1: The Ashford Case

## Synopsis

The investigators are drawn into the disappearance of [[Professor Ashford]], a respected antiquarian at Miskatonic University. What begins as a missing persons case quickly takes on darker dimensions as the investigators uncover connections to the [[Order of the Silver Twilight]], a secretive fraternal order with occult ambitions.

The trail leads from Ashford's ransacked study to the fog-bound docks of Kingsport, where mysterious cargo is being received under cover of darkness. The final confrontation will take place in the hidden chambers beneath a deconsecrated church, where the cult prepares a ritual that could tear the fabric of reality.

## Session Breakdown

### [[Session 1 - The Missing Professor]] (Played)
The investigators learn of Ashford's disappearance, visit his study, interview witnesses, and discover the first major clue — an encoded letter hidden in a secret drawer.

### [[Session 2 - The Docks at Night]] (Played)
Following leads from the coded letter, the investigators stake out the Kingsport docks. They encounter [[Brother Ezra]], survive a chase through the fog, and discover crates marked with an occult symbol.

### [[Session 3 - The Inner Sanctum]] (Planned)
The investigators locate the cult's hidden sanctum and confront the [[Order of the Silver Twilight]] before their ritual can be completed.

## Key NPCs
- [[Professor Ashford]] — The missing antiquarian (the case's central figure)
- [[Inspector Crane]] — Police detective, nominally helpful but limited
- [[Mrs Whitmore]] — Ashford's loyal housekeeper
- [[Brother Ezra]] — Cult operative, dangerous
- [[Tommy Flanagan]] — Street-level informant

## Key Clues
- [[Coded Letter]] — Found in Ashford's study, references dock deliveries
- [[Strange Symbol]] — Appears on crates and in Ashford's notes
- [[Witness Statement]] — Dock worker testimony about late-night activity

## Themes
- The corruption of knowledge
- Institutional complacency in the face of the unknown
- The fragility of the rational worldview
