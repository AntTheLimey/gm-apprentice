# Witness Statement

## Overview
**Type:** Testimony — verbal account from a dock worker
**Source:** Harlan Briggs, a longshoreman at [[Kingsport Docks]]
**Obtained:** Session 2 (before the stakeout), through conversation at a dockside bar
**Recorded by:** Mal Bridges
**Status:** Unverified but consistent with other evidence

## The Statement

Harlan Briggs, a heavyset man in his fifties who has worked the Kingsport docks for thirty years, was persuaded to talk after Mal bought him several drinks. His account, paraphrased:

> "I seen things at night I don't talk about, and I don't ask questions. But since you're buying — yeah, there's been boats coming in late, after the regular shifts. Small launches, not the usual freighters. They dock at number seven and unload crates. Heavy crates. The men doing the unloading aren't regular dockhands — I don't recognize any of them, and I know every man who works these piers.
>
> There's one fella who gives me the creeps. Tall, thin as a rail, wears a black coat. He shows up to oversee the unloading and then they all disappear. The crates go into Warehouse Seven and I never see them come out the front. Maybe there's a back way, I don't know. I don't go near that warehouse after dark. Bad feeling about it.
>
> How long? Started maybe six months ago. Used to be once a month. Now it's every week or two. Whatever they're doing, they're doing more of it.
>
> No, I haven't told the police. What would I tell them? 'Some men unloaded some crates at a warehouse they have a lease on'? That's not a crime. And besides, a friend of mine — Danny Kowalski — he asked too many questions about Warehouse Seven back in the spring. Had an accident on the pier a week later. Fell, they said. Danny was sure-footed as a cat. I ain't stupid."

## Information Revealed
1. **Timeline:** The nighttime deliveries have been occurring for approximately six months, with increasing frequency. This predates Ashford's involvement (he only acquired the manuscript in September).
2. **Description of Brother Ezra:** Matches the "tall, thin man in a black coat" seen by [[Mrs Whitmore]] and encountered by the investigators. Confirms [[Brother Ezra]] is a regular presence at the docks.
3. **Escalation:** The deliveries are becoming more frequent, consistent with preparations for a major event (the ritual on October 20).
4. **Danger:** Danny Kowalski's "accident" suggests the cult eliminates people who get too curious. The investigators are now in genuine danger.
5. **Warehouse 7:** Crates go in but don't come out the front. Either there's a back exit or the contents are being moved through another route (underground?).

## Connections
- Corroborates the [[Coded Letter]]'s references to dock deliveries
- Provides additional description of [[Brother Ezra]]
- Confirms the [[Order of the Silver Twilight]]'s use of [[Kingsport Docks]] as a logistics point
- The "back way" out of Warehouse 7 suggests underground tunnels connecting to the cult's hideout

## Reliability
Briggs appears to be telling the truth. His account is consistent with:
- The investigators' own observations at Dock 7 (Session 2)
- The description of Ezra from [[Mrs Whitmore]]
- The cargo manifests implied by the [[Coded Letter]]

His reluctance to talk and his fear of retaliation suggest he is not exaggerating.

## GM Notes
The Witness Statement serves primarily as corroboration and atmosphere. It confirms what the investigators already suspect (the cult is using the docks) and adds detail (the escalating frequency, Danny Kowalski's fate). It establishes the personal cost of investigating the Order and raises the stakes.

The statement does not provide a directional clue to the inner sanctum's location. The mention of crates not coming out the front of the warehouse is suggestive of an underground route, but Briggs doesn't know where they go. This is evidence of the cult's operations, not a path to their hideout.
