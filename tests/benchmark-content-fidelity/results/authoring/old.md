## Narrative Recap

The rain came down on [[Saltmarsh]] like a held breath finally let go, and the party arrived to a town that did not want them. At the harbour office, [[Crane|Inspector Crane]] stonewalled every question, his answers as flat and grey as the water beyond the windows — but [[Vex]] caught what he meant to hide: ledger dates that had been falsified, voyages logged for tides that never ran. Whatever the harbourmaster was covering, it had a shape now, even if its name was still out of reach.

The thread led, as such threads do, to the [[Drowned Lantern]]. There [[Oona Felk]] found them first — an ex-smuggler turned landlord, frightened and tired, who wanted the [[Cartel]] gone from her town badly enough to whisper. Four men, she said. A back room. Midnight. So [[Mara]] and [[Tomas]] peeled off to tail them through the wet streets, shadowing the crew to a meeting that, when it came, was missing the one face that mattered: the [[Tidewright]] never showed. Only lieutenants, speaking in the careful half-sentences of men who answer to something worse than themselves.

It might have stayed a quiet eavesdrop, but [[Bram]] misjudged the dark and sent a lantern crashing, and the back room erupted. In the brawl the party snatched what they could — a crate manifest — and broke for the open air, fleeing across the rain-slick quay roofs while [[Tomas]] turned an ankle on the slates and limped the last stretch on grit and fear alone. The manifest, when they finally read it by stolen light, named its cargo plainly and meant nothing at all: "tide-engine parts." Words without a thing behind them.

Morning brought the bill. [[Crane|Inspector Crane]] was found dead, his lungs packed with sand — drowned on dry land, the [[Cartel]]'s signature pressed into a man who had simply been seen speaking to strangers. The party understood the message even as it chilled them: nothing in Saltmarsh goes unwatched, and a single conversation can be a death sentence. They are left holding a manifest for a machine no one can describe, certain only that the [[Tidewright]] is building something beneath this drowned and watchful town — and that the tide, whatever it is, is already coming in.

## Quick Bullets

- The party reached [[Saltmarsh]] in heavy rain and hit a wall in [[Crane|Inspector Crane]], who stonewalled them at the harbour office.
- [[Vex]] spotted falsified ledger dates, the first hard evidence of the harbourmaster's cover-up.
- [[Oona Felk]] — ex-smuggler, now landlord of the [[Drowned Lantern]] — tipped off the party out of fear and a wish to be rid of the [[Cartel]]; she joins as an ally.
- [[Mara]] and [[Tomas]] tailed four [[Cartel]] men to a midnight back-room meeting, but the [[Tidewright]] never appeared — only lieutenants.
- [[Bram]] blew the stealth by knocking over a lantern, triggering a brawl; the party seized a crate manifest and fled across the quay rooftops, [[Tomas]] twisting an ankle in the escape.
- The manifest lists mysterious "tide-engine parts" — the party has no idea what a tide-engine is.
- [[Crane|Inspector Crane]] was found dead the next morning with sand in his lungs; the party believes the [[Cartel]] killed him for meeting with them.
- Unresolved: the nature of the tide-engine, the identity and plans of the [[Tidewright]], and the danger that the [[Cartel]] is watching the party's every move.
