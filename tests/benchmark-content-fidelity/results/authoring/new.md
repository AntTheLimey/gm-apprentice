## Narrative Recap

Rain hammered the boards of [[Saltmarsh]] as the party came ashore, and the mood was tense before a single word was spoken. At the harbour office they found [[Inspector Crane]] waiting to stonewall them, offering nothing but closed doors and flat denials — until [[Vex]] caught what his answers were meant to bury: the dates in the office ledger had been falsified. Whatever was moving through this port, someone had taken pains to make the paperwork lie.

The party split to follow the thread. [[Mara]] and [[Tomas]] peeled off to tail four men back to the [[Drowned Lantern]], where its landlord [[Oona Felk]] — an ex-smuggler with no love left for the [[Cartel]] — slipped them a frightened word of warning, wanting the Cartel gone but plainly afraid of what wanting it might cost her. The trail led to a midnight meeting in a back room, but the [[Tidewright]] never showed; only lieutenants gathered in the lamplight. The watching went wrong when [[Bram]] fumbled his approach and knocked a lantern from its hook, and the room erupted into a brawl.

Out of the scramble the party seized a crate manifest, its cargo listed as "tide-engine parts." They fled across the quay roofs with the document in hand, [[Tomas]] twisting his ankle in the run but keeping his feet long enough to get clear. The night ended with the manifest theirs and its meaning entirely opaque — no one among them could say what a tide-engine was, only that the Cartel was paying to bring its pieces into Saltmarsh.

Morning brought the cost. [[Inspector Crane]] was found dead, sand packed in his lungs, and the party did not need to be told how to read it: the [[Cartel]] had learned he met with them, and made sure he would meet no one again. They are left holding a manifest they cannot decipher, an ally in [[Oona Felk]] who is more scared than they are, and the cold certainty that whatever the tide-engine is, the people building it kill to keep it quiet.
