## Description

Harbour-master of [[Saltmarsh]] for nineteen years, a heavy, slow-spoken man who keeps a ledger of every vessel in and out of the harbour. He has lately taken to falsifying the dates in it. After his daughter sailed on the *Merrow's Daughter* and did not return, he has paid [[The Brine Cartel]] a third of his salary and asked no questions about what they unload after dark.

## Notes

When the investigators meet him, Crane is courteous and unhelpful in equal measure. He believes, correctly, that the Cartel will kill him if he talks, and he believes, wrongly, that they will spare him if he stays silent.

## Description

The only tavern on the eastern quay, a low building of tarred timber that floods to ankle depth at the spring tides. Its landlord is a former smuggler named [[Oona Felk]], who serves a sour ale and keeps a back room that [[The Brine Cartel]] uses for meetings the harbour-master is not invited to.

## Notes

Anyone who watches the Lantern for a full night will notice that the same four men arrive separately and leave together, always within the hour after the midnight bell.

## Description

The faction that controls every illegal cargo moving through [[Saltmarsh]]. Lately their cargo has stopped being ordinary contraband. They answer to a figure they call the [[Tidewright]], whom none of the rank-and-file has met.

## Notes

Their discipline is absolute and their punishments are inventive; the last informant washed up with his lungs full of sand, which is not how drowning works.
