## Description

Crane is the harbour-master of Saltmarsh and has been for nineteen years.
He is a heavy, slow-spoken man who keeps a ledger of every vessel in and
out of the harbour, and who has lately taken to falsifying the dates in it.
His daughter sailed on the *Merrow's Daughter* and did not come back; since
then he has paid the [[The Brine Cartel]] a third of his salary and asked no
questions about what they unload after dark.

When the investigators meet him, Crane will be courteous and unhelpful in
equal measure. He believes, correctly, that the Cartel will kill him if he
talks, and he believes, wrongly, that they will spare him if he does not.

## Description

The Drowned Lantern is the only tavern on the eastern quay, a low building
of tarred timber that floods to ankle depth at the spring tides. Its
landlord, a former smuggler named Oona Felk, serves a sour ale and keeps a
back room that the [[The Brine Cartel]] uses for meetings the harbour-master is not
invited to.

Anyone who watches the Lantern for a full night will notice that the same
four men arrive separately and leave together, always within the hour after
the midnight bell.

## Description

The Brine Cartel controls every illegal cargo that moves through Saltmarsh,
and lately their cargo has stopped being ordinary contraband. They answer to
a figure they call the Tidewright, whom none of the rank-and-file has met.
Their discipline is absolute and their punishments are inventive; the last
informant washed up with his lungs full of sand, which is not how drowning
works.
