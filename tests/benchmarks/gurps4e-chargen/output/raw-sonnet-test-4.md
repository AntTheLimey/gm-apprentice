## Character Notes

### Concept and Background

Callum Hardie joined the Royal Engineers at 18, served two tours in Afghanistan, then passed SAS selection at 26. He completed the Special Forces Medical Technician (SFMT) qualification at the Royal Centre for Defence Medicine, earning a Physician-level credential that exceeds standard paramedic training. He can manage penetrating trauma, perform limited surgery, administer anaesthesia, and handle the full range of casualties expected on a tier-one operation.

His Arabic is functional rather than fluent — good enough for tactical questioning, reading road signs, and maintaining a thin cover story, not good enough to pass as a native speaker or handle nuanced negotiation.

The MI6 contact (named "Ellis" in Hardie's phone as a plumber) emerged from a joint UKSF/SIS operation in 2021. Ellis feeds Hardie targeting information and occasionally asks for returns on that investment. Neither of them enjoys this arrangement, but both consider it useful.

### Roleplay Notes

**Combat Reflexes** means Hardie never freezes and always gets his full +1 to active defenses. He acts before surprise is a factor.

**Overconfidence (CR 12)** is the mechanical expression of SAS culture — when he thinks he has the tactical edge, he needs to roll CR 12 to not push further than prudent. On a failed roll, he'll underestimate a threat or bypass a precaution.

**Post-Combat Shakes** and **Nightmares** provide the human cost of his career. He functions perfectly in the field. It's in the quiet moments — debriefs, hotel rooms, waiting — that the cracks show.

**Physician-13** means Hardie succeeds on a roll of 13 or less when treating patients under normal conditions. With medical equipment bonuses (+1 from advanced bag), effective 14. In field conditions with time pressure, GMs should apply situational modifiers per B345 (Time Pressure, Poor Conditions), but his baseline capability is that of a competent junior doctor with a trauma specialisation.

**Surgery-10** allows limited surgical intervention. At IQ-2 on a Very Hard skill, he succeeds roughly half the time under ideal conditions and rarely under fire — which is realistic for a medic rather than a surgeon. In a well-equipped field hospital with assistance, Hardie can attempt procedures at reduced penalty. Without proper equipment, apply the situational modifiers from B345. This represents realistic SFMT capability: life-saving emergency procedures rather than elective surgery.

### The MI6 Contact — Mechanical Detail

Contact (Ellis, MI6 Handler) [3 pts]: Skill 15, frequency 9 or less, Somewhat Reliable.

When Hardie contacts Ellis, the GM rolls 9 or less (3d6) to determine if Ellis is available and willing to help. A successful roll means Ellis provides intelligence, access, or an introduction at the equivalent of a skill 15 roll. "Somewhat Reliable" means Ellis may occasionally provide outdated information or have competing loyalties — he won't actively burn Hardie, but he's an intelligence officer first.
