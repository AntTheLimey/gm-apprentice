**22 SAS Regiment, D Squadron — Modern Military Operative**
175 points | TL8 | Realistic Campaign

---

## Character Background

Gareth Talbot, known in the regiment as "Gaz," joined the British Army at 18, served four years in the Parachute Regiment (2 PARA), then passed selection for 22 SAS at 23. Now 29, he has completed operational tours in Afghanistan (Helmand Province), Iraq, and several undisclosed counter-terrorism and direct-action missions in West Africa. He is sniper-qualified, demolitions-trained, and holds an EMT qualification from his Sabre Squadron's combat medical cell. He is valued for his steadiness under fire and situational awareness, though his superiors note he occasionally overestimates what a four-man patrol can accomplish.

## Campaign Hooks

- **Operational Flashback**: A specific sound or smell (rotor wash, burning plastic, a particular accent) can trigger a Flashback roll. On a failed CR 12, Gaz is mentally elsewhere for 1d seconds — a critical window in a firefight.
- **The Man He Left Behind**: During a contact in Helmand, a teammate was KIA before Gaz could reach him. He recounts the story rarely but the Vow and Nightmares both trace to this incident. A GM can use this as a pressure point.
- **Overconfidence and the Four-Man Patrol**: Gaz has a tendency to assess that his patrol can handle a task that warrants a full troop. This has worked out so far — but the GM should engineer one situation per arc where it creates a real problem.
- **Chain of Command Tension**: Military Rank 2 gives him authority, but Duty (EH) means he can be called back from civilian life at any time. A GM can create friction by issuing orders that conflict with his Sense of Duty to teammates or his Code of Honor.
- **Bloodlust ROE Violation**: On a failed CR 15, Gaz moves to neutralise a threat that was in the process of surrendering or was already down. In a realistic campaign this is a potential war crimes/investigation storyline.
