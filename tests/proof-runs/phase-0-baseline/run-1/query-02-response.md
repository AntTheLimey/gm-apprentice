## Horatio Vane

**Concept:** A once-respected antiquarian who traded in rare occult manuscripts long enough to glimpse what the words actually mean — and has spent the last three years trying to forget.

**Appearance:** Gaunt man in his mid-fifties. Well-cut but rumpled clothing, always slightly too warm for the weather. Ink stains on both hands. One eye drifts faintly inward when he is tired, which is always. Carries a leather satchel he never lets touch the floor.

**Portrayal:** Drops his voice to an urgent near-whisper whenever the conversation turns to anything unusual. Stands with his back to the wall. Scans exits before sitting. Occasionally loses the thread of a sentence mid-utterance, then picks it up slightly too loudly — as though he forgot he was trying to be quiet.

**Voice:** Clipped and precise until anxiety overtakes him, at which point speech becomes fast, over-qualified, and circular.

### AIMS

**Agenda**
- *Immediate:* Determine whether the investigators are being watched or are genuine
- *Short-term:* Offload a specific tome he acquired three months ago
- *Long-term:* Achieve enough peace of mind to sleep through the night again

**Instinct:** Freeze, then flee. Under acute pressure, switches to calculated deception.

**Moves**
1. Contacts a long-standing acquaintance to quietly investigate anyone asking after him
2. Moves his sleeping quarters each week between three different addresses
3. Has drafted a letter describing what he knows, hidden inside a Tibetan prayer box

**Secrets**
- *Surface:* Clearly frightened. Sells only from catalogues now; shop has been dark for months
- *Investigation:* His specialty was Mythos-adjacent manuscripts. Three years ago he catalogued a private library and never published a word about it
- *Deep:* He witnessed a ritual completion. He knows two spell names he has never spoken aloud
