## CoC 7e Chase Rules

### Setting Up the Chase

1. **Chase track** — Draw a line of abstract locations (typically 10-20 spaces). Place the pursuer and quarry at their starting positions on the track.
2. **Speed** — Each participant's MOV score determines how many spaces they move per round. Higher MOV means more spaces covered.
3. **Hazards** — The Keeper places hazards at intervals along the track: fences, traffic, locked doors, crowds, etc.

### Each Chase Round

1. Each participant moves a number of spaces equal to their MOV.
2. Optionally, a participant may attempt a **CON roll** to gain +1 extra space. Failure means no bonus movement and possible fatigue.
3. If a hazard is encountered, roll the appropriate skill to navigate it. Failure costs 1 space and may cause damage.
4. Participants may spend their action on **attacks**, **skill use**, or **creating obstacles** for those behind them.

### Chase Resolution

- **Caught** — The pursuer occupies the same space as the quarry.
- **Escaped** — The quarry reaches the end of the track, or opens a gap too large for the pursuer to close.
- **Complications** — Bystander injuries, property damage, and attracting police attention are all possible side effects.

### Keeper Notes

- A chase begins when one party flees and another pursues (the "Flee" action in a combat round).
- Chases are the primary way to resolve a foot or vehicle pursuit without converting it into straight combat.
- Keep it dramatic: hazards are the main tool for creating tension and equalising speed differences.
