# CoC 7e Chase Rules

CoC 7e uses a structured, abstract chase system with a linear track to resolve foot chases, vehicle pursuits, and other fleeing/pursuit scenarios.

---

## Setting Up the Chase

1. **Chase track**: Draw or visualise a line of locations — typically 10 to 20 spaces. Place the pursuer(s) and quarry at their starting positions on the track.
2. **Speed (MOV)**: Each participant's MOV stat determines how many spaces they move per round. Higher MOV means more spaces covered.
3. **Hazards**: The Keeper places hazard spaces at intervals along the track — obstacles such as fences, traffic, locked doors, or crowds that must be navigated.

---

## Each Chase Round

Each round of a chase proceeds as follows:

1. **Move**: Each participant moves a number of spaces equal to their MOV.
2. **Sprint attempt (optional)**: Any participant may attempt a CON roll to gain +1 bonus space that round. Failure means no extra movement and possible fatigue penalties.
3. **Navigate hazards**: If a participant reaches a hazard space, they must roll the appropriate skill (e.g., Athletics/Jump for a fence, Drive for traffic). Failure costs 1 space and may cause damage.
4. **Actions**: Participants may spend their action on attacks, using skills, or creating new obstacles for those behind them.

---

## Ending the Chase

| Outcome | Condition |
|---------|-----------|
| **Caught** | The pursuer occupies the same space as the quarry |
| **Escaped** | The quarry reaches the end of the track, or opens a gap too large for the pursuer to close |
| **Complications** | Bystander injuries, property damage, or attracting police/unwanted attention |

---

## Key Points for the Keeper

- **Fleeing from combat**: A character who takes the "Flee" action in a combat round initiates a chase. This is their action for that round (they cannot also attack).
- **MOV values**: Most human investigators have MOV 7, 8, or 9 (derived from STR, DEX, and SIZ on the 3-18 scale). A typical human has MOV 8. Age reduces MOV.
- **Hazards are the drama**: The skill checks at hazard spaces are where the tension lives. Tailor hazards to the environment — a rooftop chase has different hazards from a car pursuit through 1920s Boston.
- **Attacks during a chase**: A participant can attempt attacks or skill actions during their turn, but this replaces movement for that round (or is subject to Keeper ruling on partial movement).
- **Vehicles**: Vehicle chases work the same way but substitute Drive (Automobile) or Pilot skill for navigation rolls. Vehicle MOV is typically much higher than human MOV.

## Quick Reference Card

| Step | What happens |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | Move MOV spaces along track |
| 2 | Optional: CON roll for +1 space (fail = no bonus, possible fatigue) |
| 3 | Hazard encountered? Roll appropriate skill; fail = -1 space, possible damage |
| 4 | Optional action: attack, skill use, or create obstacle |
| End | Pursuer on same space = caught; quarry off track / unclosable gap = escaped |
