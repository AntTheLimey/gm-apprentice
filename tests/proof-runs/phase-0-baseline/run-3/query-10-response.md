## Maren Scull

**Concept:** A mid-tier information broker who knows something incriminating and is desperately trying to look like she doesn't.

**Appearance:** A compact woman in her mid-forties with close-cropped grey hair and a habit of checking the exits every few minutes. One coat sleeve is always tugged lower than the other — hiding something on her wrist.

**Portrayal:** Answers every question with a clarifying question first ("Why would you want to know that?"). When pressed, she shifts the subject by producing practical help — a name, a direction, a small favour — to make investigators feel they've gotten something worth leaving over.

**Voice:** Careful and quiet. Speaks in short, complete sentences. Never uses first-person possessives when denying knowledge — says "That's not known" rather than "I don't know."

---

**AIMS:**

- **Agenda:**
  - *Immediate:* Satisfy these investigators just enough to get them out of her shop and off her tail.
  - *Short-term:* Destroy or hand off the ledger (or letter, or evidence — fit to your scene) before whoever she's protecting discovers she still has it.
  - *Long-term:* Extract herself from an obligation she took on years ago that has grown far more dangerous than she anticipated.

- **Instinct:** Fawn first, then flee. Under direct confrontation she defaults to apparent cooperation — giving real-but-small truths to protect the large one. If she feels genuinely cornered (named as a direct witness, threatened with arrest) she will bolt.

- **Moves (if the PCs don't act):**
  1. Within the hour: sends a coded message to her contact warning them investigators are asking questions.
  2. By end of day: moves the incriminating item to a secondary location.
  3. Within 24 hours: arranges to be visibly elsewhere (a social alibi) when the contact acts on her warning.

- **Secrets:**
  - *Surface (casual):* She's nervous. She asks too many clarifying questions and keeps glancing at a locked strongbox under the counter.
  - *Investigation (active effort):* She received payment recently — out of proportion to her stated income. Her coat sleeve hides a distinctive tattoo or brand linking her to a faction, guild, or event the investigators should recognise.
  - *Deep (significant investigation):* She isn't protecting a criminal out of loyalty or greed — she's protecting someone she loves (a sibling, a former partner, a child) who is being used as leverage. She wants out. She can be flipped, but only if the investigators can guarantee that person's safety.

**Stat Block Note (system-agnostic):**

Maren is not a combatant. Her relevant capabilities are:
- **Detect deception / read motive:** Expert — she's spent decades in this trade.
- **Lie convincingly:** Skilled — she doesn't lie outright, she redirects. Catching her in a direct falsehood is hard because she rarely tells one.
- **Physical confrontation:** Poor. She will not fight. She will run, stall, or call for help.
- **Network access:** Good — she can get information within her city district within a few hours if she chooses to.

**GM Notes — running her at the table:**

- **The tell:** Every time she deflects with a question, she is protecting something specific. Track what topics trigger the behaviour — each one is a thread.
- **The flip:** She will cooperate fully if the investigators demonstrate they can protect the leveraged person, or if they make clear her contact already knows about this conversation (removing the benefit of warning them).
- **The hook for later:** If the investigators leave without finding her secret, she will make one more move. She'll send them a scrap of useful information anonymously — enough to advance their investigation, not enough to expose her. She's trying to steer them toward her contact without pointing the finger herself.
- **If she runs:** she goes to ground with a contact, not home. Home is watched. She knows where the evidence is and she's the fastest route to it — catching her is worth the chase.
