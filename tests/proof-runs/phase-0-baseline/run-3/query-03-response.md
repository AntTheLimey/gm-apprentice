## New Occupation Stat Blocks

These five occupations are unique to the Regency setting and have no base CoC 7e equivalent.

**Gentleman** — Landed gentry, lives by wealth and privilege.
EDU×2+APP×2 | CR 50–90 (Band 4: Gentry)
Skills: Charm, Dancing, Etiquette, Fashion, Gaming, Ride, one weapon skill, one hobby.

**Gentlewoman** — Gentry woman; accomplishments define social standing.
EDU×2+APP×2 | CR 50–90 (Band 4: Gentry)
Skills: Art/Craft (Embroidery/Fine Art/Instrument/Singing), Dancing, Etiquette, Fashion, Language (Other), Persuade, Ride, one hobby.

**Nouveau Riche** — Wealthy by trade or industry; seeks gentry acceptance.
EDU×4 | CR 50–90 (Band 4: Gentry)
Skills: Accounting, Appraise, Art/Craft (Business), Dancing, Fast Talk, Persuade, Ride, one weapon skill.

**Servant (Footman)** — Male household servant; errands and public duties.
INT×2+POW×2 | CR 10–30 (Band 1: Laborer)
Skills: Animal Handling, Art/Craft (Any), Dodge, Etiquette, Fast Talk, Listen, Ride, Spot Hidden.

**Servant (Housemaid)** — Female domestic servant; cleaning and personal service.
INT×2+POW×2 | CR 10–30 (Band 1: Laborer)
Skills: Art/Craft (Cooking/Sewing), Dodge, Etiquette, Fashion, Fast Talk, Listen, Spot Hidden, Stealth.

## Occupational Bands (Credit Rating by Social Class)

| Band | Name | CR Range | Occupations |
|------|------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Laborer/Servant | 9–30% | Burglar, Butler/Valet/Maid, Criminal, Driver (Carriage), Miner, Missionary, Sailor, Servant (Footman), Servant (Housemaid), Smuggler, Soldier/Marine, Zealot |
| 2 | Shopkeeper/Craftsperson | 20–60% | Actor, Antique Dealer, Archaeologist, Artist, Author, Book Dealer, Boxer, Craftsperson, Entertainer, Farmer, Gambler, Journalist, Librarian, Museum Curator, Musician, Occultist, Pharmacist, Prostitute, Shopkeeper, Spy, Undertaker, Zookeeper |
| 3 | Professional | 30–70% | Accountant, Antiquarian, Architect, Clergy, Doctor, Explorer, Lawyer, Military Officer, Professor, Researcher |
| 4 | Gentry/Nouveau Riche | 50–90% | Gentleman, Gentlewoman, Nouveau Riche |
| 5 | Aristocracy | 90–100% | Not routinely available as a starting occupation. Rename Gentleman/Gentlewoman to Nobleman/Noblewoman with CR 90–100% for aristocratic play. |

**Special notes:**
- Butler/Valet/Maid CR is capped at 40% even though it sits in Band 1, reflecting the elevated status of senior household staff.
- Band 2 artistic occupations (Actor, Artist, Entertainer, Musician) may raise CR to 70% with Keeper permission for celebrated performers.

## Skill Point Formulas

All Regency occupations use the standard CoC 7e formulas from the base occupation list:

| Formula | Typical Use |
|---------|-------------|
| EDU × 4 | Academic, professional (Doctor, Lawyer, Professor, Researcher, Antiquarian) |
| EDU × 2 + APP × 2 | Social, gentry (Gentleman, Gentlewoman, Entertainer, Actor) |
| EDU × 2 + DEX × 2 | Manual, dexterous (Boxer, Burglar, Craftsperson) |
| EDU × 2 + STR × 2 | Physical (Soldier/Marine, Miner) |
| EDU × 2 + (STR or DEX) × 2 | Mixed physical (Sailor, Military Officer, Spy) |
| EDU × 2 + POW × 2 | Willpower (Clergy, Occultist, Zealot) |
| INT × 2 + POW × 2 | Servant occupations (Footman, Housemaid) |

## Notes for Keepers

- Occupations not listed in the Regency overlay (e.g. Accountant, Sailor, Boxer) use the nearest base CoC 7e stat block from `occupations.md` with period-appropriate skill substitutions at Keeper discretion (replace Drive Auto with Ride, remove Electrical Repair, etc.).
- Gentry-suitable occupations marked with * may be taken as hobby careers: the character uses the Gentleman or Gentlewoman occupation skill point formula and CR band, but draws skills from the hobby occupation's list.
- Investigator groups spanning Band 1 and Band 4 will experience significant social friction — use this as a feature of Regency play, not a problem to be smoothed over.
