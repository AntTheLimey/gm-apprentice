# Score Opportunity: The Butcher's Ledger

**Target:** The Billhooks (Tier II, Strong — Coalridge)
**Score Type:** Deception / Stealth hybrid
**Difficulty:** Standard (6 coin potential payoff, 2 base rep)

---

## The Setup

The Billhooks run their labor rackets out of a legitimate front: **Harker's
Meat & Rendering**, a slaughterhouse and cold-storage facility in Coalridge.
The operation is run by Tarvul's eldest son **Coran**, who keeps a physical
ledger — the "black book" — recording every protection payment, extortion
target, and name of every Bluecoat on the Billhooks' payroll.

A disgruntled former Billhooks enforcer named **Petra Mull** has quietly
approached the crew. She was cut out of her share after a job went wrong
and now wants revenge on Coran specifically. She knows the ledger is kept
in Coran's office above the cutting floor, and that Wednesday nights the
facility runs with a skeleton crew while Tarvul and Coran attend a standing
meeting with The Hive.

The score: retrieve the ledger. It's worth 6 coin to a buyer — either
the Red Sashes (who'd love leverage over Bluecoat officers) or an
anonymous Inspectors contact — and dealing a reputational blow to the
Billhooks in either case.

---

## Score Details

**Objective:** Steal Coran's ledger from the office above Harker's cutting
floor during the Wednesday night skeleton-crew window.

**The window:** Roughly two hours while Tarvul and Coran are at their Hive
meeting. The remaining crew on-site: four workers (non-combatants), two
Billhooks enforcers acting as guards, and a watchman on the roof.

**Buyers and payoff options:**

| Buyer | Coin | Rep | Consequence |
|-------|:---:|:---:|-------------|
| The Red Sashes | 6 | +1 | Red Sashes gain leverage over Bluecoats; Billhooks suspect them |
| Anonymous Inspectors contact | 4 | +2 | Inspectors begin investigating Billhooks; Bluecoat names get burned |
| Use it directly for leverage | 0 coin | — | Crew gains +1 status with Billhooks (fear-based); future blackmail potential |

---

## Plan Options for Players

**Stealth** — Point of infiltration: the canal-side drainage grate behind
the rendering vats leads to the subfloor. The smell is appalling; the route
avoids the main floor entirely.

**Deception** — Method: posing as a late-night delivery crew from a tallow
merchant. Gets past the front gate; still need to deal with the guard near
the office stairs.

**Assault** — Point of attack: draw both enforcers to the street with a
staged disturbance; hit the building while it's light. High heat, potential
for killing involved (+2 heat modifier).

---

## Key Clocks

**Tarvul Returns** (6-clock, danger, hidden)
Tarvul's meeting ends early if the Hive business wraps fast. Each loud
consequence or alarm ticks this clock. When it fills: Tarvul arrives with
four armed Billhooks — desperate position for anyone still inside.

**Coran's Office Lock** (4-clock, progress)
The office has a heavy padlock and a bolted inner door. Standard thieves'
tools work, but each segment represents one obstacle overcome: outer lock,
inner bolt, desk drawer, and locating the ledger among the papers.

**Watchman Spooked** (4-clock, danger, visible)
The roof watchman gets edgy if he sees anything off. When full, he fires a
signal shot — alerts all of Coalridge within the block, adds +2 heat and
advances Tarvul Returns by 2.

---

## Key NPCs

**Coran Billhooks** — Tarvul's calculating son. He manages the books and
the day-to-day intimidation operation. Less brutal than his father, more
strategic. Doesn't take kindly to being embarrassed. If the crew takes the
ledger, Coran becomes personally motivated to identify and punish them
(faction clock: "Find the thieves", 6-clock, advances between sessions).

**Petra Mull** — Former enforcer, burned by the Billhooks. She's the
crew's inside contact. She knows the floor plan and the schedule, but she
has her own agenda: she wants Coran's personal stash of coin (kept in the
same office) and will expect the crew to let her take it. This is about
400 coin she reckons she's owed — and if the crew refuses, she goes quiet
and becomes a liability.

**Dav the Watchman** — Roof guard, nervous type. He's being paid extra
to be here on a night he's usually off; he suspects something is up. He'll
raise the alarm at the first sign of real trouble, but can be bought off or
scared off relatively easily (wants an excuse to leave).

---

## Complications (GM toolkit — pick or roll)

1. **The ledger is already gone.** Coran moved it earlier today — it's
   now at Tarvul's home in a locked chest. The score becomes a two-stage
   problem. (Clock: "Locate the ledger", 4-clock.)
2. **A Bluecoat drops by.** One of the Bluecoats on the payroll arrives
   for his weekly coin pickup, unscheduled. He doesn't know the crew is
   inside. Getting out requires avoiding him, bribing him, or adding a
   very problematic body to the evening.
3. **Petra's contact is also here.** Someone from the Red Sashes has had
   the same idea — they're already inside, going for the ledger. The crews
   have conflicting objectives; one of them is going to leave empty-handed
   unless they deal.
4. **The rendering vats are running.** A faulty valve means the subfloor
   route is flooded with hot tallow runoff — impassable without heat
   protection or a creative solution.

| Friend/contact (Petra) actively aiding entry | +1d |

A Stealth plan with Petra's help starts at roughly 3d — expect a Risky
opening position, which is exactly right for a standard score against a
Tier II gang.

## Payoff and Heat Summary

| Outcome | Coin | Rep | Heat Notes |
|---------|:---:|:---:|------------|
| Clean extraction, ledger secured | 6 | 2 | 0–2 base (smooth/contained) |
| Ledger secured, alarm raised | 6 | 2 | 4+ (loud) + possible +2 killing |
| Ledger not found, abort | 0 | 0 | 0–2; Coran suspects someone cased the place |
| Ledger secured + Petra's stash taken | 6 + side coin | 2 | As above; Petra becomes an enemy |

Billhooks faction status: reduce by 1 regardless of how clean the score
is. They know someone hit them. If the ledger reaches the Inspectors,
reduce by 2 and start a Billhooks faction clock: "Purge the payroll list"
(8-clock) as they attempt damage control.

## Sequel Hooks

- Coran begins a personal vendetta clock. If it fills before the crew
  neutralises him, he hires the Silver Nails to hunt them.
- The burned Bluecoat names from the ledger create a new faction dynamic:
  an Inspector contact becomes available as a +1 status asset, but several
  corrupt Bluecoats now have reason to make the crew's life difficult.
- Petra disappears after the score. Two sessions later, a rumour surfaces
  that she's been "processed" at the slaughterhouse. Tarvul found her.
