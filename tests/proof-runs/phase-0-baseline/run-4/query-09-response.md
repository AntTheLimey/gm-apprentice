# Score Opportunity: The Cold Room at Slaughterhouse Nine

## Overview

The Billhooks use a licensed slaughterhouse in Coalridge — Slaughterhouse Nine — as both a legitimate business front and the site of their most private operations: debt collection by extreme means, storage of blackmail material, and processing of assets taken from rivals. Tarvul keeps a locked cold room beneath the main floor where he stores two things: a ledger of outstanding "loans" to half the ward bosses in Coalridge, and the current whereabouts of a person the crew has good reason to want found (a missing contact, a kidnapped ally, a stolen asset — tailor to the table). Coran, the elder son, runs the facility day-to-day. Bryl, the younger, shows up unpredictably and is harder to read than his brother.

---

## The Score

**Target:** The Billhooks (Tier II, Strong)
**Location:** Slaughterhouse Nine, Coalridge
**Primary Objective:** Retrieve the ledger and/or locate the missing person
**Secondary Objective (optional):** Plant evidence to redirect Bluecoat attention onto the Billhooks

**Suggested Plan Types:**

| Plan | Detail | Engagement Modifier |
|------|--------|---------------------|
| Stealth | Through the drainage channel at the rear, below floor level | +1d bold approach, -1d hostile turf |
| Deception | Posing as city meat inspectors from the Health Directorate | +1d good detail, -1d Coran has seen real inspectors recently |
| Assault | Coordinated distraction fire at the front while a second team hits the cold room | +1d bold, -1d Billhooks are numerous and Tier II |

The stealth approach is the cleanest opener but the drainage channel smells like what it is and produces noise. The deception approach has the highest ceiling (Coran is vain and wants to look legitimate) and the highest floor (a failed Consort or Sway roll triggers immediate violence rather than a gradual escalation). Assault is loud and will draw heat.

---

## Key NPCs

**Coran (Billhooks lieutenant, runs the floor)**
- Want: Keep operations looking clean enough to avoid Inspectors
- Won't cross: Anything that embarrasses the family in front of Tarvul
- Approach: Pride and status work on him. Offering a bribe insults him; offering information about a rival does not.
- If deceived successfully: leads the "inspectors" personally, which keeps him occupied and away from the cold room
- If suspicious: sends for Bryl, which escalates the clock

**Bryl (Billhooks lieutenant, irregular presence)**
- Want: Prove he is not the soft one
- Won't cross: Direct challenge to his nerve in front of crew members
- Approach: Volatile. He does not de-escalate. If he appears, treat it as a complication regardless of plan type.
- Trigger for appearance: Any loud noise, the alarm whistle, or a messenger sent by Coran

**Floor Workers (cohort, scale: gang)**
- Eight to twelve laborers on shift, most of them coerced or in debt to the Billhooks. Not loyal fighters, but they will raise the alarm.
- If given a clear exit and a reason to look away, most will take it (Command or Sway, risky/standard effect)

**The Prisoner / Locked Asset (if relevant)**
- Located in the secondary cold room, not the ledger room. Retrieving both objectives in one run requires splitting the party or making additional rolls — an intentional pressure point.

---

## Location

**Slaughterhouse Nine, Coalridge**
Atmosphere: blood-iron-coal smoke, perpetual damp

**Ground Floor — Public Abattoir**
- Active during day shift, reduced crew at night; smell and noise provide cover
- Hook chains run the length of the ceiling on iron rails: useful for movement, also for noise
- The foreman's booth near the main entrance has a speaking tube to the cold room below

**Below-Floor — The Cold Room Passage**
- Reached via a heavy iron trapdoor behind the rendering vat or via the drainage channel
- Low ceiling, gas lamps on a single valve (turning it off plunges the passage into darkness)
- Two doors: ledger room (padlocked, Coran has the key, there is no copy) and secondary holding room

**Exterior — Coalridge Alley**
- Narrow, overlooked by workers' tenements; residents have learned not to watch
- The drainage channel exit is 40 feet from the nearest Billhooks lookout post

## Clocks

| Clock | Type | Segments | Starts Ticking |
|-------|------|:--------:|----------------|
| Coran's Suspicion | Danger | 4 | First lie or out-of-place action |
| Bryl Arrives | Danger | 4 | Coran sends a message or loud noise |
| Cold Room Alarm Triggered | Danger | 6 | Trapdoor opened without disabling the wire |
| Ledger Retrieved | Progress | 4 | PCs working the padlock / searching the room |
| Missing Person Extracted | Progress | 6 | Locating and moving the prisoner safely |

**The wire on the trapdoor:** a simple trip-wire connected to a bell in Coran's office. Spotting it requires a Survey roll (risky/standard at night, controlled/standard if PCs cased the place during downtime). Disabling it is Finesse (controlled/great if spotted first, risky/standard if not). A failed disable ticks Cold Room Alarm 3 segments immediately.

## Complications (mid-score)

Roll or choose one when a 4-5 comes up in a pivotal moment:

1. **A Billhooks enforcer recognizes one PC** from a previous entanglement. He does not act immediately — he is watching, waiting to see how this plays out. A clock starts: "He Reports to Tarvul" (4-clock).
2. **The ledger is in cipher.** It can be taken, but reading it requires a long-term project (Study, 6-clock during downtime) or a contact with knowledge of the Billhooks' code.
3. **A second crew is already here.** The Gray Cloaks are also after something in the cold room — they are not allies, but they share a common enemy tonight. A tense negotiation is possible; so is violence; so is each crew pretending the other is not there.
4. **Coran calls a ledger check.** Bad timing. He will expect to review the ledger in the next ten minutes. The progress clock on Ledger Retrieved is now also a racing clock.
5. **Bryl has a hostage.** A street kid he caught near the drainage channel. He has not hurt the child yet, but he will use them as leverage the moment he knows something is wrong.

## Downtime Consequences (Post-Score)

**If the ledger is taken:**
- Billhooks status shifts to -2 (Hostile) or -3 (War) depending on how the score went
- The ward bosses named in the ledger owe the crew a favour — or a threat — worth exploring in free play
- Tarvul will dispatch Bryl to find out who did it; a Billhooks clock starts: "Tarvul Identifies the Crew" (8-clock)

**If the missing person is extracted:**
- Relevant faction or contact moves toward +1 status with the crew
- Depending on what the person witnessed in that cold room, a new long-term project or faction thread opens

**If the secondary objective (evidence plant) is completed:**
- +1 heat (messy work) but the Bluecoats open a file on the Billhooks
- The Inspectors' clock "Build Case Against Billhooks" advances 2 segments — which cuts both ways

**Heat baseline:** Hostile turf (+1), standard operation base (2). Total minimum: 3 heat. Add +2 if killing occurred, +1 if high profile.

## Engagement Roll Notes

- **Stealth via drainage channel:** start at 1d, +1d bold approach, -1d hostile turf = 1d. Risky opening is likely; desperate possible on a bad roll.
- **Deception as inspectors:** start at 1d, +1d good detail (real documentation forged in advance), -1d Coran has recent experience with real inspectors = 1d. Same math, but a controlled result here means Coran personally escorts them, which is both an asset and a leash.
- **Linked plan option:** a PC runs a distraction in the alley (loud fight, small fire) as a setup maneuver. If successful, +1d to the main engagement roll. If the setup PC rolls 1-3, the noise brings Bryl early — tick Bryl Arrives 2 segments before the main score begins.

## Flavour and Tone

The Billhooks are not subtle. Their violence is theatrical and instrumental — they want people to know what happened, because fear is the point. The slaughterhouse setting is not accidental. Tarvul chose it because the smells explain everything and the sounds explain nothing. A score here should feel cramped and slick underfoot, with the constant background noise of the floor above masking and unmasking sounds in equal measure. The cold room is quiet in a way that the rest of the building is not. That contrast is the dread. Lean into it.
