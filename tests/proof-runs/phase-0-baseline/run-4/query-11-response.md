# The Universal Faction Turn

The Universal Faction Turn is a cross-system post-session procedure for advancing every active faction in the campaign world. It sits at **Step 2** of the Post-Session Update Checklist (defined in `world-evolution.md`) and answers the central question: what did each faction do while the PCs were busy?

The core premise is that the world does not wait. Every antagonist faction has a plan that advances whether the PCs act or not. The PCs don't start the story — they *interrupt* it.

---

## The Five Questions

Run all five questions for each active faction, in order:

### 1. What is the faction's current goal?
State it in concrete terms. "Control the docks." Not "expand influence." Specificity is required — vague goals produce vague fiction.

### 2. What would the faction do if the PCs didn't exist?
Identify the faction's next step on its own timeline, independent of player action. Then classify the impact of that step:

| Impact | Meaning | If PCs miss it |
|--------|---------|----------------|
| Critical | Irreversible turning point | World shifts permanently |
| Significant | Major advantage, recoverable with effort | Cost of catching up increases |
| Minor | Incremental progress | Lost opportunity, not disaster |
| Flavour | World texture, life goes on | No mechanical or narrative consequence |

This classification is the GM's guide to pacing. Not every faction move needs to be a crisis — flavour-level activity keeps the world feeling alive without constantly raising stakes.

### 3. Did the PCs affect this faction?
Look for direct and indirect interference from this session. A PC who burned down a warehouse may not have known it belonged to the faction. Count it anyway.

### 4. What changes?
Apply the outcome based on PC involvement:
- **No interference:** the faction advances one step toward its goal
- **Interference:** the advance is altered, delayed, accelerated, or derailed — depending on the nature and severity of PC action

### 5. What becomes visible to the PCs?
Decide how, or whether, the faction's move surfaces in the fiction:
- **Directly** — PCs witness it firsthand
- **Through allies** — a contact brings word
- **Through rumour** — overheard or reported secondhand
- **Not at all** — the faction moves in the dark until a later trigger

---

## When to Run It

The Universal Faction Turn runs after every session as part of the Post-Session Update Checklist. It runs for every active faction, not just the ones the PCs interacted with this session.

---

## System-Specific Overrides

The Universal Faction Turn is the default for any system without its own faction mechanics. System-specific modules override **Step 4 (What changes?)** when applicable:

**FitD (Forged in the Dark)**
Replace the generic "advance one step" with clock mechanics:
- PCs opposed faction: tick back 1–2 segments
- PCs ignored faction: advance 1–2 (plan proceeds)
- PCs actively helped faction: advance 2–3
- Tier and Hold changes apply: significant resource loss moves Hold Strong → Weak or reduces Tier by 1; consolidation moves Hold Weak → Strong. Tier changes are rare (every 5–10 sessions).
- 4+ Heat means nearby factions notice; entanglement results may create new faction threads.

**CoC 7e (Call of Cthulhu)**
Replace clocks or mechanical counters with **narrative arc stages**. CoC does not use ticks or segments. Each antagonist group has a plan with named stages (e.g., for a summoning cult: Preparation → Gathering → Ritual → Culmination). The faction advances one stage or holds based on PC interference and the narrative logic of the scene.

**D&D 5e (2024)**
Faction changes are framed through influence and territory shifts:
- PCs cleared a location or killed a faction leader → propose power vacuum; determine which faction moves in
- PCs allied with a faction → propose expansion (territory, recruits, resources)
- PCs angered a faction → propose escalation (bounty hunters, political pressure, military response)
Each faction turn should produce 1–2 potential quest hooks for future sessions.

**GURPS / Generic**
Run the Universal Faction Turn as written, without modification.

---

## Output Expectations

All faction turn proposals are recommendations awaiting GM approval. Never silently update campaign state. Present all Step 2 proposals together with the rest of the post-session checklist results, and wait for explicit GM confirmation before filing.

When writing faction advances, follow these standards:
- Be decisive: "The cult dispatches two agents within 48 hours" — not "the cult may send agents"
- Name everyone: "Inspector Brennan starts asking questions" — not "a law enforcement figure investigates"
- Write scenes, not summaries: "A flat-faced stranger appears near the boarding house twice" — not "agents begin surveillance"

## Where This Fits

The Universal Faction Turn is Step 2 of 6 in the Post-Session Update Checklist:

1. Thread State Updates
2. **Faction Turns** ← this procedure
3. Consequence Surfacing
4. Foreshadowing Review
5. Discovery State Updates
6. World State Changes

After all six steps are complete, present the full set of proposals to the GM, wait for confirmation, then file changes using the Filing Protocol.
