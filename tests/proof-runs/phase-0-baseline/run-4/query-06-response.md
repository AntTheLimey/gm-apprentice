## Feature Descriptions

**Lay On Hands (Level 1):** Healing pool equal to 5 × your Paladin level in HP. Each use restores any amount up to the pool's remaining total. Also lets you spend 5 HP from the pool to cure one disease or neutralize one poison.

**Spellcasting (Level 1):** Half-caster using CHA. Prepares spells from the Paladin spell list equal to your Prepared column total. Spell slots are shared with any multiclass spellcasting.

**Weapon Mastery (Level 1):** Can use the Mastery property of Simple and Martial weapons you are proficient with.

**Fighting Style (Level 2):** Choose one fighting style (e.g., Defense, Dueling, Great Weapon Fighting, Protection, Blessed Warrior, Blind Fighting — per your GM's available list).

**Paladin's Smite (Level 2):** When you hit with a melee or ranged weapon attack, you can expend a spell slot to deal extra radiant damage (2d8 per slot level, +1d8 per slot above 1st). This replaces the old Divine Smite and is now a reaction-triggered ability rather than free.

**Channel Divinity (Level 3):** Gain 2 uses (recharge on Short or Long Rest). Specific uses depend on your Oath subclass. Two uses becomes three at Level 11.

**Subclass — Oath (Level 3):** Choose your Sacred Oath, which defines your Channel Divinity options, bonus oath spells (always prepared), and higher-level features. SRD subclass is **Oath of Devotion** (see below).

**Extra Attack (Level 5):** Attack twice when you take the Attack action.

**Faithful Steed (Level 5):** You can cast *Find Steed* once without expending a spell slot. The steed also gains benefits while you are mounted on it.

**Aura of Protection (Level 6):** Allies within 10 ft (including yourself) add your CHA modifier (minimum +1) to all saving throws as long as you are conscious.

**Abjure Foes (Level 9):** As an action, expend a Channel Divinity use to frighten enemies. Creatures you choose within 60 ft must succeed on a WIS save or be Frightened until the end of your next turn.

**Aura of Courage (Level 10):** Allies within 10 ft (including yourself) are immune to the Frightened condition as long as you are conscious.

**Radiant Strikes (Level 11):** Your weapon attacks deal an extra 1d8 radiant damage on a hit.

**Restoring Touch (Level 14):** When you use Lay On Hands to restore HP to a creature, you can also end one of the following conditions on it: Blinded, Charmed, Deafened, Frightened, Paralyzed, or Stunned.

**Aura Expansion (Level 18):** Your Aura of Protection and Aura of Courage expand from 10 ft to 30 ft.

## SRD Subclass: Oath of Devotion

Oath spells are always prepared and don't count against your total.

**Channel Divinity Options:**
- **Sacred Weapon:** As a Bonus Action, imbue one weapon with holy power. For 1 minute, the weapon is magical (if it wasn't), emits bright light 20 ft / dim 20 ft further, and you add your CHA modifier to attack rolls with it.
- **Holy Nimbus (Level 20 capstone):** As an action, emit an aura of sunlight for 1 minute. Undead and fiends within 30 ft take 10 radiant damage at the start of their turns; you have Advantage on saves against spells cast by such creatures.

## Build Notes

- The Paladin is one of the strongest martial/support hybrids in 2024. CHA governs both Aura of Protection (a party-wide save bonus) and spellcasting.
- Paladin's Smite is now a reaction trigger (not free action), preventing accidental nova spending — but the damage output remains excellent with higher-level slots.
- Aura of Protection at Level 6 is frequently cited as the best defensive feature in the game for a party.
- Half-caster multiclassing: a Paladin 2/Sorcerer X build is a classic "Sorcadin" combining Smite nova damage with full-caster slots.
