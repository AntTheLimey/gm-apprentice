## Design Notes

**Why DX 14?** The cat burglar archetype lives or dies on physical skills.
DX 14 (80 pts) is expensive but gives Stealth 14 at base, Climbing 14 at
base, and Brawling/Knife 14 without investment. Every point in DX pays
dividends across the whole skill list. (B11, B67)

**Why Per 14 (+10 pts)?** Separate from IQ, Per governs Observation and
Search — the skills that spot cameras, find hidden safes, and notice the
guard who just changed his route. At IQ 12 / Per 12 she would miss critical
details; Per 14 ensures those don't happen. (B17)

**Why Flexibility over Double-Jointed?** At 150 pts the 10-point saving
matters. Flexibility [5] gives +3 to Climbing and Escape; Double-Jointed
[15] gives +5. The extra +2 on skills already at 15 and 13 respectively is
marginal compared to buying Night Vision, Perfect Balance, and more skills
(chargen-kit-thief.md guidance).

**Lockpicking at 11:** Low, but with Electronic Lockpicks (+2 = 13) and the
job prep phase providing full concentration and no time pressure, a 13 is
workable. Criminals who rush locks get caught. This reflects Sylvie's
patient approach. If the GM uses extended contests or time penalties, the
player should invest in more Lockpicking at advancement.
