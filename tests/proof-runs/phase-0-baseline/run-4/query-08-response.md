# Fortune Rolls in Blades in the Dark

Fortune rolls are one of the four core roll types in Blades in the Dark (alongside action rolls, resistance rolls, and downtime rolls). Their purpose is to **disclaim GM decisions** — when the GM doesn't want to simply dictate an outcome and there's no player action roll appropriate, a fortune roll lets the dice determine how things fall.

## The Basic Mechanic

Roll a pool of d6s equal to the relevant **trait rating** plus any applicable bonuses. Read the single highest die.

| Result | Outcome |
|:---:|---------|
| Crit (two+ 6s) | Exceptional / extreme effect |
| 6 | Good / standard effect |
| 4–5 | Mixed / limited effect |
| 1–3 | Bad / poor effect |

If there's no relevant trait (rating 0), roll 2d6 and take the **lowest** result — this cannot produce a critical.

## What Trait to Roll

The trait rolled depends on what's being determined:

- **An NPC or faction acting:** roll their relevant attribute rating or Tier
- **An environmental factor:** roll a number of dice appropriate to the potency/scale of that factor (GM judgment)
- **A specific outcome with partial factors:** build the pool by adding/subtracting dice for relevant advantages and disadvantages

There is no fixed universal trait — fortune rolls are context-defined.

## Common Uses

**Gathering Information (no obstacle, not common knowledge)**
When PCs want information and there's no obstacle to overcome with an action roll, a fortune roll determines quality of information found. Effect level maps directly: great effect = exceptional detail, standard = solid answer, limited = partial/incomplete.

**Engagement Roll (score opening)**
The engagement roll at the start of every score is a fortune roll. It starts at 1d and is modified by plan quality, target vulnerabilities, allies, and opposition. The result sets the opening position (controlled, risky, or desperate) — it does not require an action from the PCs.

| Result | Opening Position |
|:---:|-----------------|
| Crit | Controlled; first obstacle already overcome |
| 6 | Controlled |
| 4–5 | Risky |
| 1–3 | Desperate |

**NPC/Faction Outcomes**
When an off-screen NPC or faction acts and the GM wants impartial resolution — "does the Bluecoat patrol catch the runner?" — roll the relevant Tier or attribute.

**Downtime Activities**
Downtime rolls use the same progression as fortune rolls: acquiring assets, long-term projects, recovery, and heat reduction all follow the 1-3/4-5/6/crit outcome table. These are technically "downtime rolls" but mechanically identical to fortune rolls.

## Key Distinction from Action Rolls

Fortune rolls have **no position and no player agency** — there is no resisting, no stress expenditure to affect the outcome, and no devil's bargain available. They are purely GM tools for impartial resolution of events outside the PCs' immediate control. When a player wants to *do something* to influence an outcome, that's an action roll, not a fortune roll.

## Summary

Fortune rolls are a lightweight "let the dice decide" mechanism. Build a pool based on the relevant trait or factor, roll, read the highest die, and interpret the result on the four-tier scale (bad / mixed / good / exceptional). They handle everything from score opening positions to off-screen NPC activity to information quality — any time the GM wants to outsource a decision to chance rather than fiat.
