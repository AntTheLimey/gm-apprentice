## Previously On...

*The following is a speculative recap based on Session 3's prep plan. Update after play.*

Haunted by what they had already seen, the investigators followed the trail to its darkest source: a deconsecrated church in Kingsport's Old Quarter, where something old and wrong waited beneath the stones. [[Rev. Marsh]] drew on his knowledge of local ecclesiastical history to identify [[St Anselm's Church]] — the only "old church" that fit the coded letter's clues. They entered through the crypt.

What they found below was not built by human hands, or not recently: stonework older than the church above it, carved with the eye-within-crescent symbol that had followed them since [[Ashford's Study]]. They found the library. They found [[Professor Ashford]] — alive, but barely himself, hunched over translations he had been forced to make, his mind half-gone.

They also found [[Brother Ezra]], and the members of the [[Order of the Silver Twilight]]'s inner circle preparing for a ritual that was not supposed to happen. What followed was dangerous, desperate, and costly. But the ritual was stopped. Whether Ezra and the Grand Master survived is a question that may echo into this session.

## Active Threads

### Priority Threads

**1. Ashford's recovery (or death)**
If rescued, Ashford is in a fragile mental state (SAN: 15 before the final session — possibly lower now). His recovery — physical, psychological, and legal — is the emotional core of the resolution. His wealthy family, his university colleagues, his housekeeper [[Mrs Whitmore]], and the police all have claims on what happens next.

**2. The Grand Master's identity and fate**
The Order's leader was unnamed throughout Sessions 1-3. Session 3's GM notes suggested the reveal could happen at the climax. If the Grand Master survived or escaped, they remain a loose thread. If revealed as a known figure (the university chancellor, a civic leader, someone the investigators trusted), the fallout is a major story beat for Session 4.

**3. Inspector Crane's accountability**
[[Inspector Crane]] was bribed by the Order for years. His deliberate inaction nearly cost Ashford his life. The investigators have evidence of this (the Order's resources include Crane's cooperation, and the investigators now know enough to make the case). Does Crane face justice? Does he cooperate to save himself? Does he remain in place?

**4. The cult's remnants**
The inner circle is broken, but the Order had 30+ outer-circle members who were unknowing participants. Some will scatter. Some may try to reconstruct. The stone tablets recovered from the docks, and the Revelations of Glaaki itself, are still dangerous objects — who controls them now?

**5. The pit beneath the ritual chamber**
The ritual was stopped, but the pit was not closed. Something moved in the darkness below. Ashford saw it fully and it broke him. The investigators may have glimpsed it. This thread does not resolve cleanly — it is the wound in the world that remains.

## Touchpoint Plan

### A-Plot: The Ashford Aftermath (all PCs)
The external narrative: bringing down what remains of the Order, deciding what happens to the evidence (the tablets, the book), getting the authorities involved, and managing the fallout. This is the resolution arc for the case.

### Dr. Eleanor Voss — B-Plot Featured PC

**Touchpoint 1: Ability Showcase**
Voss's Medicine skill is front and center in Ashford's care. She is likely his attending physician. A medical scene early in Session 4 establishes her professional connection to the outcome.

**Touchpoint 2: Moral Dilemma (High Impact — B-Plot)**
Ashford's sister arrives from Boston and demands to know what happened to Henry. She is grieving and searching for someone to blame. Voss must decide what to tell her: the truth (which is unprovable and may get Voss committed if she tells it poorly), a sanitized version (which protects her but abandons the truth), or something that opens an investigation (which implicates the Order members who remain at large, including possibly figures with legal protection).
No clean answer. This is the defining B-plot scene.

**Touchpoint 3: Decision Callback**
If Voss documented her observations about Brother Ezra's eyes and unusual physiology (Sessions 2 and 3), someone finds those notes — a colleague, a reporter, or a member of the Order. Suddenly her professional record has become a liability.

### Rev. Marsh — C-Plot

**Touchpoint 4: Arc Advancement Clue (toward Stage 3)**
In the aftermath, Marsh visits St Anselm's Church during daylight — the surface level, not the chambers — and finds that the Diocese has a file on the property going back to 1895. The file contains an early 20th century letter from a minister who descended into the crypt "on a dare" and came back with nightmares. The letter, written before the Order ever used the place, describes the sound beneath the stones. Marsh recognizes what this means: the pit predates the Order. The horror was there first. His faith now has to contend with something that predates the church that sits above it.

### Mal Bridges — C-Plot

**Touchpoint 5: Character Moment**
Mal writes his final report to Ashford's sister. The scene is brief — perhaps a single page of what he writes vs. what he crosses out. No combat, no horror. Just a Pinkerton man trying to explain to a grieving woman why he can't tell her the whole truth.

## Scene Design

*Scenes below are pitched for GM review. Each is described briefly; approve, tweak, or reject before committing.*

---

### Scene Pitch 1: "The Morning After"

**Summary:** The investigators are in the immediate aftermath of Session 3 — tired, shaken, possibly injured — dealing with the practical questions of what to do with Ashford, the stone tablets, and whatever is left of the scene at St Anselm's.
**Scene type:** Social / investigation
**Dramatic objective:** Ground the players in the stakes; establish Ashford's condition; force the first practical decision (who knows about this? what do they tell Crane?).
**Key entities:** [[Professor Ashford]], investigators, the stone tablets from Session 2, [[St Anselm's Church]] (surface level)
**Touchpoints delivered:** Voss Ability Showcase (medical care), group A-plot foundation
**Branching:**
- Do they take Ashford to a hospital? (Voss's natural call — but it opens the official record)
- Do they go back to St Anselm's to close off the scene or retrieve evidence?
- Do they contact Crane, or avoid him?

---

### Scene Pitch 2: "The Confession (Conditional)"

**Summary:** [[Inspector Crane]] appears — either summoned by the investigators, or arriving because someone called in a disturbance near St Anselm's. He knows. He's been expecting this. Crane is caught between self-preservation and a genuine (if belated) desire to do right by Ashford.
**Scene type:** Social / confrontation
**Dramatic objective:** Give the investigators the chance to turn Crane, expose him, or both. Establish whether institutional authority is a resource or obstacle in Session 4.
**Key entities:** [[Inspector Crane]], investigators
**Touchpoints delivered:** A-plot institutional strand; Mal's C-plot (his evidence file)
**Branching:**
- Hard Persuade with evidence (bribe history + everything investigators know) converts Crane into a cooperative but limited ally — he can authorize an arrest, provide legal cover, but his credibility is damaged.
- Failing or skipping this scene leaves Crane in place as a passive obstacle who doesn't interfere but doesn't help.
- The investigators could arrest or expose him directly — but this burns the bridge and leaves them without official cover for anything they're about to do.

---

### Scene Pitch 3: "Ashford's Sister" (B-Plot — Dr. Voss)

**Summary:** Margaret Ashford arrives from Boston. She is articulate, composed, and looking for explanations. She goes to the hospital (or Ashford's home) and finds Dr. Voss by Ashford's bedside.
**Scene type:** Social / moral dilemma
**Dramatic objective:** Force Dr. Voss's defining B-plot choice: what does she tell this woman?
**Key entities:** Margaret Ashford (new NPC — needs stub), [[Professor Ashford]], [[Dr. Eleanor Voss]]
**Touchpoints delivered:** Voss B-plot Moral Dilemma (high impact); Ability Showcase for Medicine
**Branching:**
- Full truth → Margaret believes her, or doesn't. If she believes her, she becomes a motivated ally. If she doesn't, it puts Voss's credibility at risk.
- Sanitized truth → Voss spares Margaret the horror, closes off that avenue, but something about what she says doesn't quite line up later.
- Clinical truth only ("Henry suffered a significant mental and physical trauma; he needs rest and specialist care") → Margaret is not satisfied. She will dig.

---

### Scene Pitch 4: "What the Church Remembers" (C-Plot — Rev. Marsh)

**Summary:** Marsh visits the Diocese offices to report the damage to St Anselm's and discovers the historical file — including the 1903 letter from a minister who heard the sound beneath the stones before the Order ever arrived.
**Scene type:** Investigation / quiet horror
**Dramatic objective:** Begin Marsh's Stage 3 arc escalation. The horror predates human involvement.
**Key entities:** [[Rev. Marsh]], [[St Anselm's Church]], diocesan archivist (minor NPC), the 1903 letter (new document)
**Touchpoints delivered:** Marsh C-plot Arc Advancement Clue
**Branching:**
- Marsh takes the letter and says nothing → private knowledge, personal burden
- Marsh reports it to the others → opens a new thread about the pit's true age and nature
- Marsh burns or destroys the letter → a choice with its own weight (complicit in burial of knowledge, very much against his nature)

---

### Scene Pitch 5: "The Grand Master's Last Move"

**Summary:** The Grand Master — whose identity was revealed in Session 3 — makes a final play: either fleeing (with something dangerous), attempting to destroy the investigators' credibility from a position of institutional power, or one last direct threat. This is the scene that closes the antagonist arc.
**Scene type:** Social / chase / confrontation (GM's choice of emphasis)
**Dramatic objective:** Give the investigators a final chance to bring the antagonist to justice or allow them to escape — with real consequences either way.
**Key entities:** The Grand Master, investigators, [[Inspector Crane]] (if converted), surviving Order inner circle
**Touchpoints delivered:** A-plot climax resolution; Mal Ability Showcase (investigation/fast talk)
**Branching:**
- Arrested with evidence → institutional win; Order formally dismantled; some secrets become public
- Escapes → clean break with threads left dangling into a potential future arc
- Confrontation turns ugly → Brother Ezra enters if still alive

---

### Scene Pitch 6: "Mal's Report" (Character Moment — C-Plot)

**Summary:** Mal sits down — wherever he is — and writes his final report to Ashford's sister, summarizing what he found and why the professor went missing. This scene can be a brief interlude between larger scenes, or a closing image for the session.
**Scene type:** Character moment (solo, but witnessed by party)
**Dramatic objective:** Give Mal's player a quiet, personal beat that closes the case from his professional perspective.
**Key entities:** [[Mal Bridges]], Margaret Ashford (offscreen)
**Touchpoints delivered:** Mal C-plot Character Moment
**Branching:**
- Brief roleplay — what does Mal actually write? What does he leave out? No mechanical stakes, just characterization.

---

### Contingency Scene: "Brother Ezra's Return"

**Trigger:** The investigators discover Ezra survived Session 3, or Ezra finds them before they find him.
**Summary:** A tense final encounter with Ezra, now weakened (binding degrading toward November 1) but not yet harmless. This scene is most effective as a reversal — the investigators, who feared him, now have a chance to face him when he is no longer quite the monster he was.
**Scene type:** Horror / confrontation
**Dramatic objective:** Close the Ezra thread. His fate — arrested, destroyed, or escaped and fading — should feel like a direct consequence of what the investigators did in Sessions 2 and 3.
**Key entities:** [[Brother Ezra]], investigators
**Touchpoints delivered:** Decision Callback (investigators chose to expose themselves at the docks in Session 2; Ezra's recognition of them was the consequence)
