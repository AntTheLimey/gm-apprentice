## Reginald Osric Tench

**Concept:** A former antiquarian dealer whose brushes with genuine Mythos
artefacts have shattered his faith in the rational order — now he hoards
information and suspects everyone, including himself.

**Appearance:** Late fifties, thin as a scarecrow, with liver-spotted hands
that never quite stop moving. Wears a waistcoat regardless of season, all
pockets stuffed with folded notes. His shop on Arkham's Garrison Street has
newspapers taped across the lower half of every window.

**Portrayal:** Never stands with his back to a door. Scans newcomers from
forehead to shoes before speaking. Speaks in a low, rapid murmur and
finishes other people's sentences — incorrectly — because he cannot bear
the suspense of not knowing what they are about to say.

**Voice:** Drops his volume at the exact moment a sentence becomes
important, so the listener has to lean in. Punctuates claims with "or so
they'd have you believe" even when discussing the weather. Rarely uses
proper nouns for Mythos-adjacent things, preferring "the matter with the
brass box" or "that Dunwich business."

### AIMS Profile

**Agenda**
- *Immediate:* Determine whether the investigators are genuine collectors,
  fed operatives, or something worse before deciding how much — if anything
  — to share.
- *Short-term:* Offload a particular catalogued lot (a pre-Columbian jade
  tablet, Lot 7-C) to someone trustworthy before its previous owners track
  it back to him.
- *Long-term:* Compile a complete provenance record of every Mythos artefact
  he has knowingly handled, seal it in a solicitor's trust, and retire to
  somewhere coastal where "the geometry is still ordinary."

**Instinct**
Freeze-then-flee. Under immediate threat he goes statue-still for two or
three seconds — then bolts for the nearest exit he has already mapped.
He has memorised the layout of every building he regularly visits.

**Moves** (if PCs do not engage him)
1. Sells Lot 7-C to a private buyer in Providence — removing the one piece
   of evidence that links the investigators' case to Tench.
2. Sends an anonymous letter to the Miskatonic library, flagging a specific
   call number as "dangerous to the uninitiated" — his way of warning
   someone without exposing himself.
3. Begins quietly liquidating his remaining stock and transfers the lease on
   his shop to a nephew, preparing to disappear.

**Secrets**

| Layer | Secret |
|-------|--------|
| Surface | Keeps a loaded Webley in the left-hand drawer of his desk; has clearly rehearsed the motion of reaching for it. |
| Investigation | Eight years ago he sold what he believed was an ornate Polynesian idol to a Boston collector. Three weeks later the collector was dead; the idol was returned to Tench anonymously, postage from no city he has found on a map. He still has it, wrapped in oilcloth, in the back storeroom. |
| Deep | His Cthulhu Mythos knowledge came at a cost: he performed one minor ritual — documented in a journal he keeps behind a false panel — during a period of breakdown following the idol incident. He genuinely does not know whether it worked or what it might have set in motion. |

### Relationships

- **Miskatonic University Library (Professor Armitage's department):**
  Cautious mutual respect. Tench occasionally donates marginalia-heavy
  volumes he deems "too interesting to sell"; the library staff consider
  him eccentric but reliable on provenance questions. He would go to them
  only as a last resort — too public.
- **The Boston collector's widow (Mrs. Hargrove):** She blames Tench for
  her husband's death. Has twice sent lawyers to his shop. Tench pays
  a former policeman thirty dollars a month to watch for her agents.
- **A fence named Corley Dash:** Tench's intermediary for any item he
  wants moved quickly and quietly. Dash does not ask questions; Tench
  prefers it that way. If Dash is pressured, he will give up Tench
  immediately.
- **The investigators:** Unknown quantity. This is the problem.

### GM Notes — Running Tench

**The core tension:** Tench has exactly the information the investigators
need. He will not give it freely because he has watched what happens to
people who know what he knows. Every scene with him is a negotiation over
trust, and his price is always some form of security — evidence that the
investigators know how dangerous this is and will not be cavalier with
what he tells them.

**Sanity tells:** At SAN 28 he is a functioning paranoid, not a gibbering
wreck. He is sharp, well-read, and professionally accomplished. The cracks
show in: obsessive checking of exits, inability to finish a thought about
the idol without changing the subject, and a habit of writing things down
on paper rather than saying them aloud in public. If investigators push
him hard (a SAN roll at a critical moment, or witnessing something in their
presence), he may tip into temporary insanity — catatonic stillness for
1D6 rounds, then flight.

**The oilcloth bundle:** If investigators find the idol in the storeroom,
it radiates a faint, sourceless wrongness (Spot Hidden at Hard difficulty
to notice the room has grown a few degrees colder). Examining it without
preparation costs 0/1D3 SAN. Tench watches anyone who goes near that room.

**Leverage points:**
- Promise genuine protection (hard to deliver, but he will negotiate)
- Demonstrate you already know about the idol (bypasses hours of fencing)
- Mention Mrs. Hargrove's name — he will immediately want to know why
- Show genuine competence against something Mythos-adjacent (he will
  respect investigators who have survived)

**If the investigators are clumsy or threatening:** Tench goes silent,
thanks them politely, and the shop is shuttered and empty the next morning.
He is not brave enough to be a martyr, but he is experienced enough to
vanish when he needs to.
