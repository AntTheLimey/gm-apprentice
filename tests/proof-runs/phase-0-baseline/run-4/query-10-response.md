## Maris Aldren — The Reluctant Witness

**Concept:** A mid-level functionary who knows exactly what happened and is desperate not to get involved.

**Appearance:** Soft hands, ink-stained cuffs, and a persistent habit of checking the nearest exit whenever someone new enters the room.

**Portrayal:** Never answers directly — restates your question back at you slightly differently as if buying time. Laughs a beat too late at things that aren't funny.

**Voice:** Clipped, transactional. Lots of "I couldn't say" and "you'd have to ask someone else about that." Drops into over-formal language when pressed ("I really couldn't speak to those particular details").

---

### AIMS Profile

**Agenda**
- *Immediate:* Get the investigators out of here without saying anything that can be used against them.
- *Short-term:* Confirm that the person they're really afraid of doesn't find out they spoke to investigators at all.
- *Long-term:* Keep a steady, unremarkable life. They didn't ask for any of this.

**Instinct (under pressure):** Fawn first, then flee. They will agree with whatever the investigators say to end the conversation, then immediately try to leave or redirect. They do not fight. They do not freeze — they are practiced at looking calm while panicking.

**Moves (if investigators push)**
1. Offers a small true fact that sounds significant but costs them nothing, to appear cooperative.
2. Drops the name of a third party — someone tangentially involved — to shift focus away from themselves.
3. If cornered: claims they need to fetch something / check on someone / take an urgent message, and physically moves away.

**Secrets**
- *Surface (casual):* Visibly nervous; checks over their shoulder; repeats themselves when flustered.
- *Investigation (active effort):* They were present when the key event happened, but positioned as a bystander — an errand, a delivery, passing through.
- *Deep (significant effort):* They've already spoken to someone on the antagonist's side. Not because they're loyal — because they were threatened. They're not complicit; they're cornered.

---

### Relationship Web (1-2 connections — minor NPC)

- **The antagonist (or their proxy):** Threatened Maris into silence after the event. Maris doesn't work for them; they're simply scared.
- **A second witness or colleague:** Someone Maris trusts who may know more — and who Maris might reluctantly point toward if they feel safe enough.

---

### Running Notes

**The key tension:** Maris *wants* to tell the truth. They're not a villain. Every lie costs them visibly. Reward players who create trust or safety — a private conversation, a guarantee of anonymity, proof that investigators can actually protect them — with genuine information.

**The tell:** When Maris is about to repeat a prepared lie, they pause and look at their hands. They've rehearsed the lie; the pause is the rehearsal firing. Players who notice this can call it out.

**If investigators press too hard too fast:** Maris shuts down entirely — goes monosyllabic, claims ignorance, ends the conversation. The scene becomes about re-earning trust rather than extracting information.

**Escalation lever:** If the antagonist's name is spoken aloud in earshot of Maris, their behaviour shifts noticeably — sharper, quieter, more careful. That reaction is information even if no words are exchanged.
