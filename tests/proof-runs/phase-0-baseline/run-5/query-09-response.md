# Score Opportunity: The Coalridge Butchery Job

**Target:** The Billhooks (Tier II, Strong)
**District:** Coalridge
**Tone:** Noir, brutal, industrial

---

## The Situation

The Billhooks run their operation out of a working butchery in Coalridge —
Tarvul's Rendering Works, a legitimate slaughterhouse fronting labor extortion,
body disposal, and rack-rate loan sharking directed at the factory workers who
pass through the district. The gang is known for extreme violence: they leave
evidence of what they do to people who don't pay, and that evidence tends to be
anatomical.

Tarvul, the vicious patriarch, is aging. His sons Coran and Bryl are both
ambitious and distrustful of each other. The crew has an opening — a disgruntled
Billhooks lieutenant named **Soot** (a factory-scarred woman who ran collections
in the eastern refineries) was passed over for a promotion that went to Bryl
despite Soot being responsible for doubling the territory's income. She's not
ready to betray the gang, but she's angry enough to let something slip over
cheap gin.

---

## The Score

**What's available (GM chooses one or more as the session's prize):**

**Option A — Bleed the ledger (Standard score, 6 coin)**
The Billhooks keep an extortion ledger: names, amounts, and leverage notes on
every factory owner and worker captain paying protection. Stealing it strips the
gang of weeks of extortion income and gives the crew something to sell (to the
Inspectors, to a rival, or to the workers themselves). Tarvul keeps the ledger
in the rendering works office — a back room above the kill floor, accessible
through the main building or via the loading dock at the rear canal.

**Option B — Disrupt the labor racket (Big score, 8 coin)**
The Billhooks strong-arm the Coalridge Labor Dispatch, a hiring hall where
factory workers queue for shift assignments. The gang takes a cut from every
placement; the dispatcher is their puppet. A daring crew could replace the
dispatcher (permanently or just for a night), siphon the week's intake, and
embarrass the gang in front of the workers they terrorize. Higher risk: the
hiring hall is public-facing and on Billhooks turf, so Bluecoat entanglement
is likely.

**Option C — Tarvul's personal stash (Major score, 10+ coin)**
Word through the canals is that Tarvul hoards coin in the rendering works
basement — behind a false wall in the brine vault where carcasses are salted.
The basement is also where the Billhooks dispose of "problems." It is guarded
around the clock by two of Tarvul's most loyal men, and Tarvul himself sleeps
in a room directly above it.

---

## Key NPCs

**Tarvul** — Vicious patriarch. Early 60s, missing three fingers on his left
hand. Surprisingly quiet until violence becomes necessary, then precise and
economical about it. Loyalty only to the Billhooks as an institution; he would
sacrifice either son if the gang demanded it. *Line he won't cross:* letting an
outsider see the basement.

**Coran (eldest son)** — Runs street-level enforcement. Loud, reckless,
resents his father's caution. Spends his nights at a fighting pit in Charhollow.
Can be manipulated through his pride — tell him Bryl said something unflattering
and he'll do something stupid.

**Bryl (younger son)** — Recently promoted, runs the financial side of the
extortion. Meticulous and paranoid. Keeps his own set of books separate from the
ledger, hidden at a rented room in Nightmarket. If the crew discovers this, it's
leverage — Bryl is skimming from Tarvul.

**Soot (lieutenant, contact hook)** — The passed-over lieutenant. Finds the
crew through a mutual acquaintance. She won't directly betray the gang but will
confirm the ledger location and tell them Coran is away on a given night. Her
price: the crew creates a situation that looks like Bryl was responsible for
whatever goes wrong. She wants him gone, not dead.

## Location: Tarvul's Rendering Works

**Type:** Industrial building, Coalridge
**Atmosphere:** Wet iron, coal smoke, low constant noise

**Sensory:** Sight — soot-blackened brick, pale electroplasmic lamp above the
loading dock, blood-dark runoff channeled into the canal. Sound — the low grind
of the rendering machinery (runs at night to avoid Bluecoat attention to the
smell). Smell-Touch — tallow, brine, the sweet-rot of old meat.

**Key Features:**

- **Kill floor (ground level):** Open, slippery, poor visibility due to hanging
  carcasses. The rendering machinery runs on a timer; getting caught in it is a
  Harm 3 consequence. Two workers present most nights (paid to not notice things).
- **Office (upper level):** Where the ledger is kept. Thin floorboards — anything
  loud on the kill floor is audible from above. A 4-segment danger clock: "Night
  Shift Workers Raise the Alarm."
- **Loading dock (rear):** Opens on a narrow canal side-arm. A gondola could
  approach quietly. The dock lock is old and brittle (Finesse or Wreck, Risky).
- **Brine vault (basement):** Locked with a heavy iron bar plus a padlock. Lit
  by a single electroplasmic lantern. Cold. Smells strongly of salt and something
  else. The two guards rotate in 3-hour shifts; one sleeps on a cot just inside
  the door.

**Secrets:**
- The basement contains remains. There is at least one ghost forming down there
  — the Billhooks have been careless. A character with Attune may sense it before
  they open the vault door (controlled position to detect it; risky to interact
  with it unprepared). Disturbing the vault without addressing the ghost creates
  a wild card: the ghost lashes out at whoever is closest.
- Bryl visited the office two nights ago and took something from the ledger.
  The remaining pages have a gap — he's already trying to cover the skimming
  trail.

**Hazards:**
- Two night-shift workers (non-combatant, will run or hide; will not fight
  unless cornered, but will talk to the Bluecoats after)
- Two Billhooks guards in the basement (Tier II gang members, gang quality)
- Tarvul himself is on-site if the score goes loud (he sleeps above the vault;
  the sound of the machinery starting or stopping wakes him)

  machinery stops abnormally)

  the basement or makes a loud action near it)

## Faction Consequences

- **Billhooks (–1 status):** Default outcome of any successful score against
  them. Tarvul puts out the word that the crew is a problem.
- **Billhooks (–2 status / war clock):** If vault is robbed or Tarvul is harmed
  — the Billhooks will come looking. Start a 6-clock: "Billhooks Hunt the Crew."
- **Inspectors (+1 status):** If the ledger is sold to the Inspectors rather
  than leveraged personally. They get names; they owe the crew a quiet debt.
- **Coalridge workers (minor +1):** If the hiring hall is disrupted and the crew
  makes it known who did it — word travels on the factory floor.

## Sequel Hooks

- **Bryl's separate books** (Nightmarket rented room) — a follow-up score
  against a softer target, high yield, and it destabilizes the Billhooks further
- **The basement ghost** — if it wasn't dealt with, it's still forming; a Spirit
  Warden patrol notices something wrong; who investigates?
- **Soot's ambition** — if Bryl goes down (arrested, shamed, or worse), Soot
  steps up. She could become a recurring contact, a patron, or a rival depending
  on how the crew treated her
- **Tarvul's retaliation** — the old man doesn't panic; he asks around quietly,
  identifies the crew, and sends Coran to make a very visible example of someone
  the crew cares about

## GM Running Notes

Cut to the score immediately after the engagement roll — don't narrate the
approach. The rendering machinery noise is the score's atmosphere and its
pacing mechanism: it masks sound (players may feel safe) but also masks the
sound of Tarvul moving around upstairs. When the 4-5 result on a roll calls
for a consequence, reach first for "the machinery stops unexpectedly" or
"one of the night-shift workers wanders into the wrong room" before anything
that simply blocks progress.

Soot is a devil's bargain waiting to happen: if any PC leans hard on her for
more information before the score, offer an extra die in exchange for the
complication that she's told Bryl something to protect herself.

Tarvul should feel patient and inevitable if he appears. He doesn't shout. He
asks one question. Then he acts.
