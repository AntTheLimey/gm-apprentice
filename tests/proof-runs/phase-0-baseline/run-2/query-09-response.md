# Score Opportunity: The Knackerman's Ledger

**Target:** The Billhooks (Tier II, Strong — brutal gang in Coalridge; labor rackets, butchery front)
**Score Type:** Stealth / Social hybrid
**Premise:** Tarvul, the Billhooks' vicious patriarch, keeps a handwritten ledger of every factory foreman, labor inspector, and Bluecoat on the gang's payroll. The crew has learned the ledger exists and that it's stored in the back office of the Knackerman's Yard — the Billhooks' slaughterhouse front in Coalridge. Getting it could be used to:
- Extort the bribed officials directly (long-term income)
- Sell it to the Inspectors (reputation boost, favors from law)
- Hand it to a rival gang (faction leverage against the Billhooks)
- Hold it over Tarvul as leverage for territory or a ceasefire

The opportunity has opened because Tarvul's elder son Coran has taken the ledger out of the vault temporarily to update it — he'll have it in the office for the next two nights before locking it back in the strongbox behind the rendering floor.

## The Location: Knackerman's Yard

**Type:** Industrial building, Coalridge
**Atmosphere:** Bloody, loud, close

**Sensory:**
- *Sight:* Pale electroplasmic lamps on iron hooks. Meat hooks hang empty or swaying. Coal-black smoke from the rendering furnace tinges everything.
- *Sound:* Cleavers on block, the squeal of chains, the low moan of the furnace. Shouted orders in a guttural Skovlan dialect.
- *Smell/Touch:* Copper blood and tallow fat coat the air. The floor is slick with runoff. Every surface is cold.

**Key Features:**

- **Rendering Floor (ground level):** Main work area. Six Billhook workers and two armed enforcers present during a shift. The furnace door doubles as an emergency corpse-disposal unit. Crates stacked high give good cover but the workers know every shadow.
- **Back Office (raised mezzanine):** Coran's working space. A scarred desk, a locked strongbox (currently empty — ledger is out), and a window overlooking the floor below. The ledger sits under a false bottom in a document crate marked "Dockers Guild Permits."
- **Cold Store:** A side room kept frigid by a small electroplasmic chiller unit. Three large iron hooks. One currently holds what may or may not be a human body wrapped in canvas. Spirit activity is notably higher here — the chiller disrupts the ghost field in unpredictable ways.
- **Loading Dock (rear):** Two guards on rotation, shift changes at 3rd and 6th bell. The drainage channel emerges here.

**Inhabitants:**
- **Coran** (Billhooks lieutenant, calculating son): In the office most of the night, reviewing accounts. He's less brutal than his father but more paranoid — he checks the floor twice an hour.
- **Six workers:** Ordinary laborers bound to the Billhooks by debt. They will not fight for the gang but they will raise an alarm.
- **Two enforcers:** Armed with cleavers and short blades. Loyal to Tarvul personally, not Coran — a fact worth knowing.
- **One errand boy:** Runs messages between the Yard and a Billhook safe house two streets away. He leaves and returns unpredictably.

**Secrets:**
- The canvas-wrapped thing in the Cold Store is a city assessor who went missing last month. Coran ordered it. This is a significant piece of leverage — or a liability.
- The two enforcers resent Coran and have been quietly approached by Bryl (the other son) to watch and report. They are not fully loyal to anyone right now.
- A Gondolier contact once used the drainage channel for a different job and left a mark — the Gondoliers know this access point exists.

## Complications (mid-score)

1. **The errand boy returns early** with a message from Tarvul — Coran must call the floor together in 10 minutes, giving him a reason to look through his papers.
2. **A ghost stirs in the Cold Store.** The electroplasmic chiller malfunctions (rolls Desperate for any Attune attempt nearby). The spirit is the assessor — confused, terrified, and drawn to whoever is holding the ledger.
3. **One of the two enforcers spots something.** He doesn't sound the alarm immediately — he wants to know why someone is here. He could be bribed, bluffed, or neutralized, but he's testing the crew's read of the situation.

## Connections

- **Sequel hook (sell to Inspectors):** An Inspector named Harlan Brogan has been quietly building a case against Tarvul for three seasons. He'll pay well for the ledger — but he'll also want to know how the crew got it, which is its own problem.
- **Sequel hook (hold over Tarvul):** Tarvul is not a man who accepts extortion. Using this for leverage opens a War clock with the Billhooks (8-clock) rather than a simple faction drop.
- **Sequel hook (the ghost):** If the spirit of the assessor was disturbed, it may have attached to a PC or followed the ledger out of the Yard. The Path of Echoes has been looking for this particular ghost — they know what it knows.

## GM Notes

**Tone:** This score should feel workmanlike and dangerous rather than glamorous. The Knackerman's Yard is a real place of labor and death — the violence the Billhooks deploy is intimate and physical. Let that atmosphere press on the PCs throughout.

**Coran as a character:** He's the interesting one here. Tarvul is a force of nature; Coran is calculating and quietly ambitious. If the crew establishes any relationship with him (even adversarial), he's a better long-term asset than his father. He might be willing to be turned — eventually.

**The enforcers' split loyalty** is a detail worth surfacing through a Consort or Survey roll during the score. It won't resolve the encounter on its own, but it provides a Set Up opportunity for a PC who finds it.

**Don't prep plots.** The location, clocks, and NPCs are the situation. Cut to the engagement roll, let the fiction drive, and trust flashbacks to handle the prep the players wish they'd done.
