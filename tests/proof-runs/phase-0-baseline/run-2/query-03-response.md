# Regency Cthulhu — Occupations

The Regency period (1811–1820) uses its own occupation list that replaces the standard CoC 7e list. Skill point formulas (EDU×4, EDU×2+APP×2, etc.) remain as per the base system.

**Bold** = suitable for gentry. † = new occupation with a dedicated stat block (see below). * = also playable as a hobby career for a Gentleman or Gentlewoman (use that occupation's skills but the Gentleman/Gentlewoman CR band).

## New Occupations (Regency-Specific Stat Blocks)

These five occupations are unique to Regency play and have no direct base-game equivalent.

**Gentleman** — Landed gentry, lives by wealth and privilege.
EDU×2+APP×2 | CR 50–90 (Band 4: Gentry)
Skills: Charm, Dancing, Etiquette, Fashion, Gaming, Ride, one weapon skill, one hobby.

**Gentlewoman** — Gentry woman; social standing defined by accomplishments.
EDU×2+APP×2 | CR 50–90 (Band 4: Gentry)
Skills: Art/Craft (Embroidery/Fine Art/Instrument/Singing), Dancing, Etiquette, Fashion, Language (Other), Persuade, Ride, one hobby.

**Nouveau Riche** — Wealthy by trade or industry; seeks gentry acceptance.
EDU×4 | CR 50–90 (Band 4: Gentry)
Skills: Accounting, Appraise, Art/Craft (Business), Dancing, Fast Talk, Persuade, Ride, one weapon skill.

**Servant (Footman)** — Male household servant; errands and public duties.
INT×2+POW×2 | CR 10–30 (Band 1: Laborer)
Skills: Animal Handling, Art/Craft (Any), Dodge, Etiquette, Fast Talk, Listen, Ride, Spot Hidden.

**Servant (Housemaid)** — Female domestic servant; cleaning and personal service.
INT×2+POW×2 | CR 10–30 (Band 1: Laborer)
Skills: Art/Craft (Cooking/Sewing), Dodge, Etiquette, Fashion, Fast Talk, Listen, Spot Hidden, Stealth.

## Occupational Bands

Regency society sorts occupations into five bands by Credit Rating, which matters for social interaction and access to polite society.

| Band | Name | CR Range | Example Occupations |
|------|------|----------|---------------------|
| 1 | Laborer/Servant | 9–30% | Burglar, Butler/Valet/Maid, Criminal, Driver (Carriage), Miner, Missionary, Sailor, Servant (Footman), Servant (Housemaid), Smuggler, Soldier/Marine, Zealot |
| 2 | Shopkeeper/Craftsperson | 20–60% | Actor, Antique Dealer, Archaeologist, Artist, Author, Book Dealer, Boxer, Craftsperson, Entertainer, Farmer, Gambler, Journalist, Librarian, Museum Curator, Musician, Occultist, Pharmacist, Prostitute, Shopkeeper, Spy, Undertaker, Zookeeper |
| 3 | Professional | 30–70% | Accountant, Antiquarian, Architect, Clergy, Doctor, Explorer, Lawyer, Military Officer, Professor, Researcher |
| 4 | Gentry/Nouveau Riche | 50–90% | Gentleman, Gentlewoman, Nouveau Riche |
| 5 | Aristocracy | 90–100% | Not routinely playable; rename Gentleman/Gentlewoman to Nobleman/Noblewoman and use CR 90–100% |

**Notes:**
- Butler/Valet/Maid CR is capped at 40% (reflects senior household staff status, not full Band 2).
- Band 2 artistic occupations may raise CR to 70% with Keeper permission (reflecting celebrity performers or acclaimed artists).

## Building a Character

For skill point allocation, use the formula column from the base `occupations.md`:

- EDU×4 occupations: Accountant, Architect, Author, Doctor, Journalist, Lawyer, Librarian, Nouveau Riche, Professor, Researcher
- EDU×2+APP×2 occupations: Actor, Antiquarian, Artist, Entertainer, Gentleman, Gentlewoman, Musician, Prostitute
- EDU×2+STR×2 or DEX×2 occupations: Boxer, Burglar, Criminal, Driver, Farmer, Miner, Sailor, Smuggler, Soldier/Marine
- INT×2+POW×2 occupations: Servant (Footman), Servant (Housemaid)

Occupations not listed in the Regency file use the nearest base equivalent with period-appropriate skill substitutions (Keeper approval required). For example, an Antique Dealer uses the base Antiquarian skill list; a Museum Curator uses the base Librarian or Professor list with appropriate swaps.

Personal interest points (INT×2) apply as normal and may be spent on any skill except Cthulhu Mythos.
