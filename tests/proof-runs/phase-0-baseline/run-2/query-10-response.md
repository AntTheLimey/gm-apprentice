## Marta Velde — "The Cooperative One"

Concept: A minor functionary who is being far too helpful, knows more than she admits, and is quietly terrified the investigators are getting warm.

---

### 3-Line Core (use this if you have 10 seconds)

**Appearance:** Mid-40s, over-dressed for the setting (as if she put on her best clothes for this conversation), hands that won't stay still — smoothing her jacket, adjusting a ring, picking at a cuff.

**Portrayal:** Answers every question before you finish asking it. Volunteers information that wasn't requested. Laughs a beat too late at nothing funny.

**Hook:** She was present the night of the incident. She's already decided what story she's telling and is working very hard to tell it.

---

### AIMS Profile (use this to play her past the first exchange)

**Agenda**
- *Immediate:* Get through this conversation without contradicting herself
- *Short-term:* Make the investigators satisfied enough to stop looking at her
- *Long-term:* Keep her involvement in the incident from destroying her livelihood / marriage / standing (pick what fits your campaign)

**Instinct (under pressure):** Fawn — smile wider, offer more, deflect with helpfulness. She does not fight or flee; she buries you in cooperation until you forget what you asked.

**Moves** (what she does if the scene drags out):
1. Offers tea, a seat, something to look at — breaks eye contact with activity
2. Redirects suspicion toward a third party ("Have you spoken to the caretaker? He was there all evening")
3. If cornered: produces a partial truth that sounds like a full confession ("I did see him that night, yes — I should have said so immediately, I'm sorry") — releases pressure without revealing the real secret

**Secrets**
- *Surface (casual):* She's nervous. Something is making her uncomfortable.
- *Investigation (active effort — Spot Hidden / Insight / Notice):* Her timeline has a 45-minute gap she keeps paper-over with different details each time.
- *Deep (significant effort — research, pressure, or a failed roll with consequences):* She didn't witness the incident — she caused the conditions that made it possible, through negligence or a quiet deal she made months ago. She has been managing the fallout ever since.

---

### Voice and Mannerism

**Speech pattern:** Completes your sentences for you (usually slightly wrong). Uses "Of course" and "Naturally" as nervous filler. Never says "I don't know" — always "I couldn't say for certain, but..."

**Mannerism:** Adjusts her ring constantly when lying. Stops adjusting it when telling the truth. (Give the players one chance to notice this — if they do, it becomes a reliable tell for the rest of the scene.)

---

### Running the Scene

**If players are direct and aggressive:** She becomes more flustered, more helpful, more obviously hiding something — but she doesn't crack. She'll end the scene by saying she has an appointment and needs to go, very sorry, very cooperative, completely unreachable afterward.

**If players are patient and indirect:** She relaxes slightly, makes a small error — a date that doesn't match, a name she shouldn't know yet. Let the players catch it.

**If players catch the ring tell:** She notices them noticing. Pause. She looks at her hand. Folds it in her lap. Now she knows they know she's lying. This shifts the scene entirely — she either shuts down completely or makes a calculated offer.

**Fail forward on a missed roll:** She doesn't give up useful information, but she gives up an *emotional* tell — she tears up briefly when a specific name is mentioned, or goes very still when a specific location comes up. The information is still in the scene; it just requires a different approach.

**For CoC 7e:** APP 65, EDU 55, Charm 55%, Fast Talk 60%, Psychology 35% (she reads people, not well). SAN intact — she's rationalised her role as minor.

**For D&D 5e:** Commoner base stats; Persuasion +3, Deception +3, Insight +1. No combat capability. Treat as an information source, not a combat encounter.

**For GURPS 4e:** Acting-12, Fast-Talk-11, Diplomacy-12. Disadvantage: Cowardice (12). She will not risk personal danger to protect her secret.

**For FitD:** Status 1 functionary. 1d Sway, 1d Consort. Has a clock "Cover Story Holds" (4 segments, 2 already filled by previous investigators).
