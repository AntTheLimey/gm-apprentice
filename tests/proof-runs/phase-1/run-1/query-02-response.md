## Cornelius Mather Vane

**Concept:** A 64-year-old antiquarian dealer of rare books and pre-Columbian artefacts who witnessed something he was never meant to see inside a client's sealed library three years ago — and has been slowly unravelling ever since.

**Appearance:** A tall, once-distinguished man with a white Van Dyke beard he now neglects to trim. Wears a heavy woollen overcoat regardless of season, pockets stuffed with folded paper. One of his shirt cuffs is always buttoned; the other floats loose. His shop smells of cedar oil and old fear.

**Portrayal:** Speaks in sudden, emphatic whispers — normal conversational volume, then drops to barely audible for anything he considers important. Constantly repositions himself so his back is to a wall. If he can't achieve this, he becomes visibly distracted and short with whoever he's talking to.

**Voice:** Starts sentences normally, trails off, restarts with "No — what I mean is —" before the real point. Asks "Do you understand what I'm telling you?" after every significant disclosure, then looks immediately regretful for asking.

---

### AIMS Profile

**Agenda**
- *Immediate:* Get rid of the Dunsany folio. He acquired it legally, has no idea what's in it, but something about it feels wrong and he wants it out of the shop before tonight.
- *Short-term:* Convince himself (and anyone who'll listen) that what he saw in the Hargreave library was a trick of the light and old age. He is not succeeding.
- *Long-term:* Leave the trade. Sell the inventory, close the shop, move somewhere with no libraries, no old books, no clients who ask questions in Latin.

**Instinct:** Freeze, then flee. Under genuine threat Vane does not fight — he goes very still, says "Quite, quite," and then is gone out the back before the scene resolves. He has a practiced exit from every room he regularly occupies.

**Moves** (if investigators don't engage)
1. He contacts a solicitor to begin drawing up a quiet sale of shop inventory — triggers in 2 weeks if PCs haven't purchased the folio or his trust.
2. He writes an anonymous letter to the Arkham Advertiser hinting at "irregularities" in the Hargreave estate — triggers in 1 month; this will attract the wrong attention.
3. He destroys the contents of his private ledger — names, sale records, client addresses for 40 years of discreet trade — if he believes someone dangerous is looking for it. He'll do this without warning.

**Secrets**
- *Surface (casual):* Something's bothering him. He's jumpy, the shop's been in disorder for weeks, and he's drinking more than usual. "Business worries," he says.
- *Investigation (active effort):* Three years ago he was asked to catalogue the private library of Hargreave House. He quit the commission after one day, returned the advance, and has never discussed it. Hargreave himself died six months later under circumstances ruled accidental.
- *Deep (significant work):* He has a locked back room. In it: a photograph he took at Hargreave House (Architecture; Library Use to contextualise); a hand-copied fragment in his own shaking handwriting from a text he refuses to name; and — most dangerously — a sales ledger showing he sold several items from that library to four other clients before he understood what he was dealing with. He doesn't know what the items do. Some of those clients have since gone mad or disappeared.

---

### Relationships

- **Hargreave Estate (executor, Arthur Peel):** Peel has been sniffing around asking what Vane saw. Vane is terrified of him but also isn't sure Peel is the threat — he might be another victim.
- **Professor Elaine Drummond (Miskatonic University, Art History):** A longtime professional contact and occasional friend. Vane trusts her more than anyone and has almost confided in her twice. He keeps not doing it. He believes telling her would put her in danger.
- **The Four Clients:** He sold artefacts from the Hargreave library to four individuals. He knows where three of them live. He checks a different newspaper each morning looking for their names in the obituary columns.
- **Investigator party (potential):** The first investigators to treat him gently, show expertise, and not press him too hard will receive his fragile cooperation. Push too fast and he'll freeze and vanish. Return on a second visit and he'll have more to say — he spends the intervening time deciding he wants to trust someone.

### GM Notes

**Pacing:** Vane is a slow-burn source. He rewards patient investigators. First meeting: 3-Line NPC level of access. Second meeting (after investigators have demonstrated competence or trustworthiness): AIMS level. Third meeting, or if investigators connect him to current threat: full disclosure — or he runs.

**The ledger** is the scenario's key secondary MacGuffin. It contains 40 years of discreet sales; somewhere in it is documentation connecting three different players in the current threat. Getting it requires either Vane's genuine trust or a break-in (Stealth vs his Listen 45%, Locksmith for the safe).

**Sanity loss for his revelation:** if Vane describes what he saw at Hargreave House, investigators roll SAN 0/1D4 (contextual dread, not entity exposure) — they're reacting to a credible, terrified man describing something they can't yet explain.

**If Vane dies:** he becomes more useful. His solicitor has instructions to forward the ledger to Professor Drummond if he dies "under unusual circumstances." This is not a coincidence — Vane wrote those instructions the day after the Hargreave visit. He has been preparing for his own death for three years.
