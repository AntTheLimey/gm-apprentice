# Score Opportunity: The Coalridge Cut

## Overview

The Billhooks (Tier II, Strong) run brutal labor rackets and protection schemes
across Coalridge, operating under cover of a legitimate butchery front. Tarvul,
the vicious patriarch, has just squeezed a new extortion arrangement out of a
ring of factory foremen — protection coin collected weekly from six factories
along Ironworks Lane. His sons Coran and Bryl ride the collection circuit
together, making them predictable and vulnerable.

**Score premise:** Hit the Billhooks' weekly collection run and take the coin
before it reaches Tarvul's counting house. Secondary opportunity: seize or
destroy the ledger of factory contacts the Billhooks use to run the racket —
a long-term blow to their hold over Coalridge industry.

---

## Score Sheet

**Target:** The Billhooks — Tier II, Strong  
**Score type:** Robbery (with sabotage option)  
**Scale:** Standard  
**Location:** Ironworks Lane, Coalridge district  
**Payoff:** 6 coin (standard score) + potential faction damage clock  
**Heat:** 2 base (contained/standard) +1 hostile turf (Billhooks control
Coalridge) = **3 heat**. Add +2 if killing involved.  
**Rep:** 2 base, no tier modifier (same tier as crew) = **2 rep**

---

## The Setup

Coran and Bryl ride the collection circuit every seven-night. They travel with
two heavies and a cart to carry the coin. The route runs from the furnace-floor
of Ashgate Ironworks down to the end of the lane, taking roughly two hours.
The collection is always done by full dark — the brothers aren't stupid enough
to carry coin in daylight.

**The opportunity window:** The gap between the last factory (Dulvane & Sons
Foundry, at the lane's end) and Tarvul's butcher shop is eight blocks through
a maze of narrow alleys and loading bays. This stretch has no Bluecoat patrols
— the Billhooks bought that absence — and is poorly lit. The cart must pass
under the Ember Bridge culvert, a natural chokepoint.

**The complication:** A third Billhook enforcer, Vorse (a Level 2 threat), is
watching from the tenement rooftop above the culvert — he's not on the cart,
he's early warning. The crew won't know this unless they case the route in
advance.

## Key Scenes and Obstacles

### Scene 1 — The Culvert

**Type:** Robbery / Assault  
**Agenda:** Take the coin before the cart reaches the butcher shop.  
**Opening:** Rain hammers Ironworks Lane. The cart's iron wheels grind on
wet cobblestones. One lantern hangs from the cart-frame, throwing a narrow
yellow arc. The culvert arch is low and black, slick with condensate.

**Who Is Present:**
- Coran Billhook: riding the cart, hand near a knife, watching the shadows.
  Wants: get the coin home fast; any delay makes him suspicious.
- Bryl Billhook: walking alongside the cart, relaxed, joking with one heavy.
  Wants: reach the shop so he can eat. Less dangerous than Coran in a fight;
  more likely to run for help.
- Two Billhook heavies: simple muscle, obedient, will fight hard but surrender
  if both brothers are down.
- Vorse (on rooftop — hidden unless cased): watching from above. Raises the
  alarm clock on any sign of trouble.

**Environment:**
- The culvert arch: low enough that a standing person on top could drop
  onto the cart; tight enough that the cart must slow to pass through.
- Loading bays on both sides: large sliding doors, dumpsters, stacked crates.
  Good cover; also good for a flanking approach.
- A broken electroplasmic lamp at the near end of the culvert: pools of deeper
  dark here. Potency bonus for stealth actions in this zone.

**Complications:**
- Vorse on the roof (if not neutralised): ticks the Alarm clock 2 on the
  first sign of violence. If the clock fills, Billhook backup (6-clock
  "Enforcer Crew Arrives") begins.
- A factory night-shift worker stumbles out of the Dulvane Foundry side door
  mid-score. Civilian witness. Devil's Bargain opportunity: ignore them for a
  bonus die, but they report to the Bluecoats by morning (+1 heat).
- Coran carries the ledger in a locked satchel, not in the coin chest. The
  crew must realise this and act separately to take it.

**Outcomes:**
- Full success: Coin seized, cart disabled, brothers flee. Ledger optional
  grab (Study or Hunt roll to locate it on Coran).
- 4-5: Coin secured but one heavy is still fighting, or Vorse got a segment
  off — partial alarm raised (+1 heat).
- Failure / 1-3: Coran escapes with the coin. Bryl and the heavies remain.
  Or: coin secured but Vorse's alarm is fully raised — backup clock starts.

**Connections:** If the ledger is taken, the GM may offer a follow-on score
targeting the six factory foremen (liberating them from the racket, or
installing the crew's own protection arrangement).

**System Notes:**
- Skirmish / Wreck most effective against the heavies. Prowl or Finesse to
  reach Vorse on the roof undetected. Hunt to locate the ledger satchel on
  Coran before he bolts.
- Risky/Standard is the default position for the ambush. If Vorse is not
  neutralised first, downgrade to Risky/Limited.
- Coran resists (Command or Skirmish) at Effect 2. Bryl has a worse result
  on resistance rolls — he's built for speed, not a stand-up fight.

### Scene 2 — The Counting House (Optional: Escalation)

If the crew wants to hit Tarvul directly rather than the collection run, or
if they pursue a linked plan (disrupt the collection as a setup, then raid
the counting house while it's emptied of coin and Tarvul is angry and
distracted):

**Type:** Stealth / Sabotage  
**Agenda:** Destroy the racket's records and embarrass Tarvul in front of
his lieutenants.  
**Location:** The back of the Billhook butcher shop, Coalridge. Tarvul's
office above the cold-room. The front is a working butchery — hooks, cleavers,
the smell of blood and offal.  
**Key threat:** Tarvul himself is Level 3. Encountering him directly is
Desperate position unless the crew has created significant advantage.

## Faction Consequences

**If the crew succeeds:**
- Billhooks clock "Retaliate Against [Crew]" (6-clock) begins advancing
  between sessions. When full: Billhooks send a dedicated enforcer crew;
  crew status with Billhooks drops to -2 (Hostile).
- If the ledger is taken or destroyed: Billhooks' hold over Coalridge
  weakens. Their Hold may shift Strong → Weak after 2-3 sessions of the
  crew pressing this advantage (GM discretion, Faction Turn rules).
- Rival factions notice. The Lampblacks and Fog Hounds hear the Billhooks
  got hit; crew rep in Coalridge goes up even if no one knows who did it.

**If the crew fails or is driven off:**
- Billhooks' status clock with the crew ticks toward -1 (Interfering).
  Tarvul puts the word out: someone is watching his runs.
- Heat +1 on top of standard (Billhooks pressure the Bluecoats they own).

| Factor | Mod |
|--------|:---:|

| Hostile turf (Coalridge is Billhook territory) | -1d |
| Bold, aggressive plan (e.g. full assault at culvert) | +1d |
| Overly complex/contingent plan | -1d |
| **Minimum for a clean Assault/Deception** | **2d** |

A result of 4-5 (Risky opening) is expected. A critical sets the crew at the
top of the culvert with Vorse already spotted. A 1-3 means Vorse has already
seen them moving into position — the Alarm clock starts at 2.

## GM Notes

Run Coran as the dangerous one — he keeps his knife hand loose and his eyes
moving. Bryl is the talker; if the crew uses Sway or Command on the brothers,
Bryl breaks first. Tarvul never comes out personally unless his sons are in
mortal danger (Level 3, not worth the crew facing him on an unplanned basis).

The real prize is the ledger — it turns one score into an ongoing leverage
story. If the crew takes it, they now know which foremen pay the Billhooks.
That's a power source and a moral choice: protect the foremen, install their
own racket, or sell the ledger back to the Billhooks for coin and a temporary
status bump.

Keep Vorse invisible unless the crew specifically Cases the route (Survey
roll, 4-5 result reveals the route is being watched but not by whom, 6
identifies Vorse on the roof). Rewarding preparation without punishing the
crew that skips it — Vorse is a Danger clock, not a gotcha.
