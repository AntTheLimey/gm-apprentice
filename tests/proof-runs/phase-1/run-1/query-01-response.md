# CoC 7e Chase Rules

## Overview

CoC 7e uses a structured, abstract chase system built around a **chase track** — a series of mapped locations or waypoints. It replaces raw speed calculations with a spatial, action-oriented framework where hazards and skill rolls create drama.

---

## Setting Up the Chase

1. **The Chase Track** — The Keeper draws a line of locations (typically 10–20 spaces). The pursuer and quarry are placed at their starting positions on the track.
2. **Speed (MOV)** — Each participant's MOV score determines how many spaces they move per round. Higher MOV = more spaces per round.
3. **Hazards** — The Keeper places obstacles at intervals on the track: fences, traffic, locked doors, crowds. These require skill rolls to navigate.

---

## Each Chase Round

1. Each participant **moves their MOV** in spaces along the track.
2. **Optional sprint** — Attempt a CON roll to gain +1 space. Failure means no bonus movement, and may cause fatigue.
3. **Hazards** — If a participant hits a hazard space, they roll the appropriate skill (Jump for a fence, Drive for traffic, etc.) to navigate it. Failure costs 1 space and may deal damage.
4. **Actions** — On their turn, a participant may also spend their action to:
   - Attack a nearby participant
   - Use a skill to create an obstacle for those behind them
   - Attempt any other relevant stunt

---

## Ending the Chase

| Outcome | Condition |
|---------|-----------|
| **Caught** | Pursuer occupies the same space as the quarry |
| **Escaped** | Quarry reaches the end of the track, or opens a gap large enough that the pursuer cannot close it |
| **Complications** | Bystander injuries, property damage, or attracting police/witness attention |

---

## Key Reminders for Keepers

- **Initiation** — A chase begins the moment one party **flees** (this counts as their action for the round under the "Flee" action in combat).
- **CON as stamina** — CON rolls fuel burst movement and represent endurance. Investigators with low CON tire quickly.
- **Hazards as pacing** — Space your hazards to create tension peaks. A clear stretch followed by a sudden crowd scene or a locked gate is far more dramatic than constant rolls.
- **Keep it brief** — Like CoC combat, chases should be decisive. 3–6 rounds is a good target.
