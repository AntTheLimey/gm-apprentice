## Marta Delvane

**Concept:** A mid-level clerk who knows far more than she should and is desperately trying to look ordinary.

**Appearance:** Neat but over-pressed clothes — every crease is deliberate. She keeps one hand in her coat pocket throughout the conversation, fingers moving slightly, like she's counting something.

**Portrayal:** She answers questions with a slight pause before every sentence, as if measuring each word. She agrees too quickly with things she should push back on, then quietly contradicts herself thirty seconds later.

**Voice:** Overly helpful phrasing — "Of course, naturally, whatever you need" — but the information she offers is always just slightly beside the point.

---

### AIMS Profile (if she becomes recurring)

**Agenda**
- *Immediate:* Get the investigators off her trail without giving anything away
- *Short-term:* Destroy a document or warning an associate before investigators find them
- *Long-term:* Protect someone she loves who is deeply compromised

**Instinct:** Fawn — she defaults to excessive cooperation, then retreats when the cooperation might actually expose something.

**Moves**
- Volunteers a small, true, harmless detail to seem transparent
- Steers conversation toward a colleague who is also involved but more expendable
- Sends a message (letter, phone call, errand boy) to an associate within the hour if the investigators leave

**Secrets**
- *Surface:* She's been crying recently — reddened eyes she blames on allergies
- *Investigation:* Her desk / files contain a key reference missing from the official record — something scratched out or a gap in dates
- *Deep:* She's covering for a family member (sibling, child, spouse) who committed or witnessed the central crime. Her loyalty isn't to a faction — it's to a person, which makes her sympathetic and potentially an ally if that person is already compromised.

---

### Running Her at the Table

**Two tells to deploy immediately:**
1. She is never quite still — the hand in the pocket, a pen turning end-over-end, counting.
2. She answers *around* direct questions: "When did you last see him?" → "He usually comes in on Thursdays, very punctual normally."

**Flip her into an ally:** If the investigators demonstrate they are trying to protect rather than prosecute her person of interest, she breaks. She's not a villain — she's a cornered person making bad choices out of love.

**If investigators push hard:** She does not crack under pressure; she goes quiet and says she needs to speak to someone first. She does not bluff, lie baldly, or run — those feel false to her. She stalls.
