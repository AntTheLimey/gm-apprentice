# MIRELA VOSS
**Cat Burglar** — 150 points

Mirela is a precision second-story thief. She doesn't break locks — she reads them. Raised in a family of travelling circus performers, she pivoted her acrobatic training and sleight of hand into high-end residential burglary. She doesn't do crews, doesn't do violence, and doesn't do jobs she hasn't scouted herself. Her problem is she can't stop working even when she's flush.

## Combat Summary

Mirela does not fight. Combat is failure.

- **Dodge:** 9 (unmodified) / **11** with successful Acrobatics roll
- **Knife:** skill 14; sw-2 cut (1d-2 cut) / thr imp (1d-2 imp), Reach C,1
- **Brawling:** skill 14; Parry 10; thr-1 cr damage
- **Tactical note:** If caught, she uses the first turn to create distance (Move or Step + Retreat for +3 Dodge), second turn to disengage via Escape or Acrobatics, third turn to flee. She will not draw the knife unless her life is genuinely in danger — it leaves forensic evidence.
- **Against surprise:** No freeze (she has no Combat Reflexes, but Per 13 and Danger Sense equivalent via Observation skill means she spots threats early — avoid the situation entirely)

## Character Notes for the GM

**Hooks:** Kleptomania is the engine — she occasionally lifts things she wasn't paid for, and that can blow a clean job or produce unexpected leverage. Her Secret (criminal record) means a police procedural campaign can put real pressure on her at any time. The Code of Honor creates interesting friction when a client turns out to be targeting someone who doesn't deserve it.

**Growth paths:** Buying off Loner would mark a major arc shift toward trusting a crew. Adding Luck [15] is the obvious first advancement. Picking up Forgery or Disguise opens the spy end of the spectrum. Combat Reflexes and a higher Acrobatics would make her a more cinematic rooftop thief.
