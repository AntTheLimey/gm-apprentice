Occupations marked **bold** are suitable for gentry characters; those marked \* can also be used as hobby careers for a Gentleman or Gentlewoman (applying that occupation's skills but the Gentleman/Gentlewoman Credit Rating band).

## New Occupation Stat Blocks (Regency-specific)

**Gentleman** — Landed gentry; lives by wealth and privilege.
Formula: EDU×2+APP×2 | CR 50–90 (Band 4: Gentry)
Skills: Charm, Dancing, Etiquette, Fashion, Gaming, Ride, one weapon skill, one hobby.

**Gentlewoman** — Gentry woman; accomplishments define social standing.
Formula: EDU×2+APP×2 | CR 50–90 (Band 4: Gentry)
Skills: Art/Craft (Embroidery/Fine Art/Instrument/Singing), Dancing, Etiquette, Fashion, Language (Other), Persuade, Ride, one hobby.

**Nouveau Riche** — Wealthy by trade or industry; seeks gentry acceptance.
Formula: EDU×4 | CR 50–90 (Band 4: Gentry)
Skills: Accounting, Appraise, Art/Craft (Business), Dancing, Fast Talk, Persuade, Ride, one weapon skill.

**Servant (Footman)** — Male household servant; errands and public duties.
Formula: INT×2+POW×2 | CR 10–30 (Band 1: Laborer)
Skills: Animal Handling, Art/Craft (Any), Dodge, Etiquette, Fast Talk, Listen, Ride, Spot Hidden.

**Servant (Housemaid)** — Female domestic servant; cleaning and personal service.
Formula: INT×2+POW×2 | CR 10–30 (Band 1: Laborer)
Skills: Art/Craft (Cooking/Sewing), Dodge, Etiquette, Fashion, Fast Talk, Listen, Spot Hidden, Stealth.

| 4 | Gentry/Nouveau Riche | 50–90% | Gentleman, Gentlewoman, Nouveau Riche |
| 5 | Aristocracy | 90–100% | Not routinely playable. Rename Gentleman/Gentlewoman to Nobleman/Noblewoman with CR 90–100% |

Notes:
- Butler/Valet/Maid CR is capped at 40% even for senior household staff.
- Band 2 artistic occupations may raise CR to 70% with Keeper permission.

## Keeper Notes

- Occupations without full Regency stat blocks use the nearest base CoC 7e equivalent with period-appropriate skill substitutions (Keeper approval required).
- Skill point formulas for all occupations follow the base CoC 7e table: EDU×4, EDU×2+APP×2, EDU×2+DEX×2, EDU×2+STR×2, EDU×2+POW×2, INT×2+POW×2, etc.
- "Drive Auto" in base entries → "Drive (Carriage)" in Regency. "Firearms" → period-appropriate weapons (flintlock pistol, musket). Keeper applies common sense to any anachronistic skills.
