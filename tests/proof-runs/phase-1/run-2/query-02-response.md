## Cornelius Vane

**Concept:** A once-respected antiquarian dealer in rare books and esoteric
artefacts who made the catastrophic mistake of opening one too many sealed
caskets; now a trembling wreck convinced that the things he's catalogued
are looking back.

**Appearance:** Late 50s, stooped as though trying to shrink below sight-
lines. Steel-grey hair unwashed and slightly wild at the temples. Wears
fingerless gloves indoors — he says for the cold, but the knuckles beneath
are scarred from his own nails. His shop reeks of cedar oil and something
underneath it that visitors can't quite name.

**Portrayal:** Speaks in rapid, low bursts, then stops mid-sentence to
listen. Keeps his back to a wall wherever possible. When threatened or
cornered, becomes unnervingly calm — that is the version of him that has
already accepted the worst.

**Voice:** Answers every direct question with a qualifying clause — "That
depends on what you mean by..." or "It's not that simple, you have to
understand..." Trails off whenever he's about to say something important.
If pressed, he whispers the last few words.

## AIMS Profile

**Agenda:**
- *Immediate:* Sell off his remaining stock and close the shop. He wants
  to liquidate everything before "they" trace the provenance back to him.
- *Short-term:* Identify whether the investigators are connected to the
  parties who sold him the artefacts he now bitterly regrets buying. If
  they are, he will try to plant incriminating items on them and flee.
- *Long-term:* Disappear entirely — new name, coastal town, no more
  books older than 1890. He has come within one bad night of attempting
  this twice before and turned back both times. He cannot stop.

**Instinct:** Flee — but only after assessing whether fleeing is more
dangerous than staying. Under serious pressure he freezes, then floods
with contradictory rationalisations. He will betray anyone to protect
himself, and feel genuine guilt about it approximately thirty seconds
after it stops being useful.

**Moves (what he does if the PCs don't intervene):**

1. Within the week, contacts a fence named Gault to quietly move three
   particular items he will not name even to himself. If Gault is
   unavailable, Vane will attempt to destroy one of them — the smallest
   one — and will fail.
2. Sends an anonymous letter to a university department warning them off
   a line of inquiry into a specific estate sale — unsigned, typed,
   routed through a postal forwarding address he set up in 1927.
3. Begins sleeping at the shop rather than his lodgings, convinced
   (possibly correctly) that something has been in the lodgings already.

**Secrets:**

- *Surface:* His shop is in visible decline — shelves thinning, items
  not replaced, his manner too distracted to sell. Something is wrong
  with Cornelius Vane.
- *Investigation (Psychology or active questioning):* He acquired three
  artefacts from an estate sale two years ago that he has never catalogued,
  never displayed, and cannot bring himself to sell through legitimate
  channels. He has seen what they do in the dark.
- *Deep (Library Use + History, or breaking into his back office):* One
  of those artefacts is a carved stone cylinder seal of pre-Dynastic
  origin that does not match any civilisation on record. The other two
  are a hand-written journal in an unknown script and a taxidermied
  specimen that is not any known animal. He has Cthulhu Mythos 08% and
  doesn't know it — the knowledge arrived unbidden and he cannot unknow it.

## Relationships

- **Gault (fence, Dockside):** Trusts to a degree; fears slightly more.
  Gault moves things without asking questions. Vane suspects Gault has
  already looked inside the crate he left with him.
- **Professor Elspeth Harrow (University, Antiquities):** Former
  professional relationship, now avoided. Harrow wrote to him twice about
  the estate sale provenance. He has not replied. She is getting closer.
- **Mrs. Aldrich (lodging-house keeper):** Depends on her for normalcy;
  uses her as an anchor. She thinks he works too hard and occasionally
  leaves soup outside his door. He has not told her not to come upstairs.
- **The PCs:** Treats as potential threats until proven otherwise. Will
  offer real but partial information if convinced they stand between him
  and the entities he fears — he will not be fully honest even then.

## GM Notes

**Running Vane:** He rewards patience. Investigators who listen rather
than interrogate will get further. His information is genuinely useful —
he knows things he cannot explain and several he can. The challenge is
that he parcels everything out behind conditions and half-truths, so the
PCs must decide how much to trust a man who is clearly lying about the
framing even when the facts are accurate.

**The paranoia is real, not just flavour.** Vane has SAN 31. Any Mythos
encounter in his presence risks tipping him into temporary insanity (1D6
SAN loss = potential break). His erratic behaviour during play is not
affectation — he is a man whose grip on consensus reality is one bad
night away from failing entirely.

**The three artefacts** are deliberately left vague for Keeper use.
The cylinder seal, the journal, and the specimen are hooks, not answers.
The journal's script might be legible to a PC with Archaeology or a
specific Language skill. The specimen might match something in a Mythos
tome. The seal might be the key to a lock no one has found yet.

**If Vane survives the scenario**, he is not saved. He knows too much
for equilibrium. A possible arc: he becomes an unreliable recurring
contact — present, useful, steadily deteriorating, and eventually a
liability the investigators must make a difficult choice about.

**SAN award for helping Vane:** 0 — he is not a rescue, he is a warning.
