# Score Opportunity: The Bone Yard Butchers

**Target Faction:** The Billhooks (Tier II, Strong)
**District:** Coalridge
**Score Type:** Stealth / Assault hybrid
**Suggested Crew Tier:** I–II (challenging but achievable)

---

## The Situation

The Billhooks run a labor racket out of Coalridge through a front operation called Garsen's
Fine Cuts — a legitimate butchery supplying high-end restaurants in Brightstone. The real
operation happens in the back: Tarvul's sons Coran and Bryl use the cold-storage cellars to
hold debtors in brutal "persuasion sessions" until their families pay protection coin. The
cellars also serve as an unofficial treasury — Tarvul keeps the weekly protection payoffs
in a heavy iron strongbox before his man Delse walks them to the Billhooks' safehouse each
Dusky-night at the third bell.

A tanner named Orva Brek has come to the crew through a mutual contact. Her brother Sev is
in those cellars right now, three weeks overdue on his workshop fees. Orva can't raise the
coin — she's offering the only thing of value she has: the layout of the ground floor, which
she mapped during a delivery two years ago. She's also heard that Delse is skimming from the
take, and Tarvul hasn't noticed yet — a lever someone else could use.

---

## Score Frame

**Opportunity:** Hit the Garsen's Fine Cuts cellar on Dusky-night before the third bell.
Extract Sev Brek. Optionally take Tarvul's strongbox (escalates danger, opens future angles).

**Why now:** Delse's skimming will be discovered at the end-of-month count. When Tarvul
finds out, he'll tear apart anyone who was near the coin — including any crew seen near the
butchery. The window is seven days.

**Scale:** Two Billhooks sentries posted outside at night. Coran usually oversees the cellar
personally. Three to six prisoners in cells (possible complications: some won't leave,
one may be an informant for the Bluecoats). Bryl is elsewhere until after the third bell —
if the crew runs long, he arrives with four armed men.

## Key Location: Garsen's Fine Cuts

**Type:** Commercial ground floor + sub-basement cellars
**Atmosphere:** Rank, cold, industrial

**Sensory:**
- Sight: Electroplasmic lamp on the butchery sign, guttering low. Hanging carcasses visible
  through the grimy front window. The rear yard smells of fat and old blood.
- Sound: The grinding of the coal lifts from the factory two streets over. Muffled voices
  below the floor. A single bell tolling the hours from the Coalridge foundry.
- Smell-Touch: Rendered fat, iron tang, damp stone. The cellar stairs are slick.

**Key Features:**
- **Front counter and cold room:** ground level, two sentries rotate here. Carcasses provide
  cover, but knocking one loose is loud.
- **Cellar stairs (behind the cutting block):** narrow, single-file. Coran positions himself
  at the bottom when he hears noise above.
- **The cells (3 iron cage frames, padlocked):** Sev is in the middle cage, level 2 harm
  (Broken Rib). One other prisoner is delirious; one is a Bluecoat informant named Gell who
  will betray the crew's identities if not dealt with.
- **The strongbox (Tarvul's treasury, left wall):** heavy iron, Tier III lock. Contains 8
  coin and a ledger documenting the skimming (Delse's handwriting, names of the businesses
  paying protection — saleable blackmail or leverage against the Billhooks).

**Secrets:**
- The drainage tunnel leads under the spirit well. A ghost (Lesser, hostile, bound 40 years)
  lurks there. It can be Attune-d past, commanded to delay pursuit, or accidentally triggered
  to fill a danger clock.
- The ledger's contents implicate two Bluecoat captains who have been underreporting Billhooks
  arrests. This is worth -1 heat on a future score if handled correctly, or a war with the
  Bluecoats if it surfaces badly.
- Coran carries a master key for all cells on a chain at his belt.

## Key NPCs

**Coran (Billhooks lieutenant, Tarvul's elder son)**
- Want: Keep the operation clean until the end-of-month count; prove himself to his father.
- Will: Go loud fast. Will call for Bryl the moment he suspects trouble. Will not abandon the
  strongbox — he's responsible for it.
- Won't: Negotiate. He views negotiation as weakness.
- Position at start: In the cellar, doing the books by lamplight.

**Delse (courier, skimmer)**
- Want: To get out before Tarvul's count reveals the shortfall.
- Will: Betray the crew if it buys him cover. Cooperate if they offer a way out of Doskvol.
- Won't: Confront Coran or Tarvul directly — he's terrified of them.
- Social approach: Sway or Consort, risky (he's paranoid). Success: leaves the rear door
  unbolted and is "conveniently absent" during the score.

**Sev Brek (prisoner, Orva's brother)**
- Level 2 harm (Broken Rib). Can move but slowly and noisily without help.
- Has been here three weeks; knows the guard rotation from listening.
- Useful: will describe Coran's habits and the third bell timing if the crew makes contact
  before the score (Long-Term Project clock or downtime Consort).

**Gell (Bluecoat informant in the cells)**
- Poses as a laborer caught in a debt dispute.
- Will memorize faces and report to Inspector division within 24 hours of release.
- Can be bribed (3 coin, he'll "forget"), intimidated to silence (Command, risky — if he
  talks later, +2 heat), or left behind (he leaves eventually, +1 heat).

## Clocks

**"Interior Patrols Alert"** — 4-segment danger clock
Ticks when: noise above the floor (1), a sentry is killed rather than subdued (2), Coran
hears voices he doesn't recognize (1), a cell door is forced without the key (1).
When full: Coran retreats up the stairs and bars the cellar door while sending word to Bryl.
The crew is trapped below or must fight up a chokepoint.

**"Bryl's Return"** — 6-segment mission clock (time pressure)
Advances 1 segment per significant in-score delay (arguing, a failed roll with a lost
opportunity consequence, a PC going down). When full: Bryl arrives at the front with four
Billhooks. The job becomes a desperate fighting withdrawal.

**"Sev Can Walk"** — 4-segment progress clock (optional)
If the crew wants to stabilize Sev before moving him, each Tinker or Attune action ticks this
(1-3: one segment, 4-5: two, 6: three). Full clock = Sev moves quietly and at full speed.
Not required — the crew can drag him — but incomplete means -1 effect on any group action
involving his movement.

## Complications Table

Roll or select as needed when a 4-5 result calls for a complication beyond reduced effect:

1. Coran has a second Billhook posted in the cellar the crew didn't know about.
2. The ghost in the drainage tunnel has followed the crew up — it's now loose in the cellar
   and drawn to the fear of the prisoners.
3. A fire has been lit in the coal room above; smoke is filling the stairwell.
4. Sev refuses to leave without the prisoner in the third cage (an old friend — delirious but
   recognizable to him).
5. The strongbox is bolted to the floor, not just left sitting. Removing it is a Long-Term
   Project action mid-score.
6. Gell the informant sees the crew's faces and makes a quiet deal with Coran — they're
   working together before the crew realizes.

## Outcomes

**Full success (Sev extracted, crew escapes clean):**
- Orva pays the agreed fee (2 coin), +1 faction status with any faction Orva has connections
  to (GM's choice, e.g. the Fog Hounds if she has canal contacts).
- Heat: 2 (contained, standard). Billhooks faction status drops 1 (they know someone hit
  them; they don't know who yet).

**Partial (Sev extracted, alarm raised / strongbox left / Gell loose):**
- Same coin, but heat 4 (loud, chaotic). Billhooks begin a faction clock: "Find Who Hit
  Garsen's" (6-segment). When it fills, the crew gets a -2 status escalation rather than -1.

**Strong (Sev + strongbox + ledger secured):**
- +4 coin, +2 rep (score against a Tier II target). The ledger is a long-term project clock
  asset: "Leverage the Billhooks Ledger" (4-segment, Consort/Sway). When filled: choose one
  — neutralize Billhooks hostility for one score OR reduce heat by 3 on any future score
  involving the implicated Bluecoat captains.
- Delse disappears from Doskvol (removes the skimming complication from future Billhooks
  entanglements).

**Failure / Abort:**
- Crew retreats to downtime. No payoff. Heat 2. Coran now knows someone was scouting the
  cellar — Billhooks gain "Watching for Another Hit" 4-segment danger clock that activates
  on any future Billhooks score.

## Sequel Hooks

- **Tarvul's retaliation:** If the crew's identity leaks (Gell, Delse turns informant, or
  high heat), Tarvul puts a 4-coin bounty on the crew's heads via the Silver Nails. This is
  -2 status with Billhooks and a new threat faction clock.
- **The ledger:** The implicated Bluecoat captains are named Harwick and Stenn. Harwick is
  a legitimate lever; Stenn is violent and will act preemptively if he hears the ledger
  is in play.
- **The ghost:** If the drainage ghost was used or disturbed, the Path of Echoes has noticed
  unusual spirit activity in Coalridge. They'll approach the crew through a contact — they
  want to know what was down there, and they're willing to trade information.
- **Orva's debt:** Orva still owes the Billhooks' original workshop fee (2 coin). Tarvul
  won't forgive it just because Sev is gone. If she can't pay, she becomes a recurring
  complication — the Billhooks will lean on her to identify who helped her brother.
