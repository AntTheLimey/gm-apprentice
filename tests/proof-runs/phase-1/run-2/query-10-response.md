## Marta Voss

**Concept:** A harried mid-level records clerk who knows more than she is saying — and is terrified that admitting it will make her the next victim.

**Appearance:** Heavyset woman in her late forties, always clutching a leather folio even when it has nothing in it. A small ink stain on her left cuff that she keeps rubbing with her thumb.

**Portrayal:** Answers questions with slightly too much detail about the wrong thing, then cuts off abruptly. If directly pressed she laughs once, too loudly, then goes quiet.

**Voice:** Formal, clipped sentences. Uses "naturally" and "of course" as nervous filler — "Naturally I wouldn't know anything about that." Trails off mid-sentence when a topic gets dangerous.

---

### AIMS

**Agenda**
- *Immediate:* Get through this conversation without being identified as a witness.
- *Short-term:* Warn the person who asked her to stay quiet (her supervisor, Brother Ehrlen / equivalent) that the investigators are asking questions.
- *Long-term:* Keep her job, avoid becoming a loose end herself.

**Instinct:** Fawn first, then flee. She appeases authority figures reflexively, but will bolt if she feels cornered with no escape route.

**Moves** (fires on a timeline if PCs don't act)
1. After this scene: sends a note to her supervisor warning that investigators visited.
2. Within 24 hours: requests three days' leave and books a room at a relative's address across town.
3. If the supervisor reacts badly to her warning: she turns — approaches the PCs herself, terrified, with the one piece of hard evidence she withheld.

**Secrets**

| Layer | What |
|-------|------|
| Surface | She is visibly nervous and vague about dates/times |
| Investigation (active effort) | She witnessed someone destroy a document — she can describe the person but not name them |
| Deep (significant pressure/trust) | She has a copy of that document. She made it instinctively, then panicked and has been sitting on it ever since |

---

### Relationships

- **Her supervisor:** Fears him. He explicitly told her not to talk. She's loyal to him out of habit but that loyalty is cracking.
- **The victim / case subject:** Never met them personally, but processed their paperwork. Feels vague guilty-bystander guilt.
- **The PCs:** Threat and potential lifeline simultaneously — she hasn't decided which yet.

---

### Running Her at the Table

**First contact:** She volunteers too much about mundane details (the filing system, the office hours) while deflecting every question that touches the incident. A successful Insight / Notice / Read a Person roll reveals her eye contact breaks sharply on the word "Tuesday" (or whatever the key date is).

**Under direct pressure:** She shuts down and invokes procedure — "I'd need authorisation to discuss that." She is not hostile, just scared. Threatening her makes it worse; offering her safety or anonymity is the lever.

**The turn:** If the PCs protect her from the supervisor's retaliation (or if she learns he is sending someone after her), she becomes fully cooperative and hands over the hidden copy.

---

### Quick Stat Notes by System

**CoC 7e (quick):** Records Clerk. INT 65, EDU 70. Skills: Library Use 65%, Accounting 55%, Spot Hidden 45%, Persuade 30%. SAN 60 (she's not involved in anything supernatural — yet).

**D&D 5e (2024):** Use the **Commoner** stat block (MM). Add proficiency in Investigation (+3) and Insight (+1) reflecting years of paperwork. She has a folded document tucked inside her folio (the copy).

**GURPS 4e (quick, ~50 pts):** IQ 11, DX 9, HT 10. Advantages: Photographic Memory (relevant to why she copied the document). Disadvantages: Cowardice -10, Duty (Employer) -5. Skills: Accounting-12, Administration-13, Observation-10.

**FitD:** Role — Clerk (Tier I, attached to a Guild or government office). Notable actions: Study 2, Consult 1. Vice: Obligation (protecting her position). She has a 4-clock: "Disappear before they come for me."

---

### GM Notes

The key dramatic tension is that Marta is both an obstacle and a resource — the same fear that makes her stonewall the PCs is what will eventually flip her. Do not play her as hostile or combative. Play her as someone who has been rehearsing a set of safe answers and keeps nearly saying the real ones by accident.

If you need her to be a dead end (the PCs fail to crack her in this scene), fire Move 1 immediately — the supervisor's reaction in a later scene becomes a consequence of this encounter. If you want her as a thread to pull, let a single good roll crack her surface secret and let the PCs decide whether to push or build trust over time.

Record her now to avoid continuity errors. Minimum note: "Marta Voss — clerk, saw document destroyed, has a copy, warned her boss after PC contact."
