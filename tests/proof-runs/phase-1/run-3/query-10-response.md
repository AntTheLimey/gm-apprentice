## Marta Velk — The Reluctant Witness

**Concept:** A middle-aged records clerk who knows exactly what the investigators are looking for and is terrified of being caught knowing it.

**Appearance:** A compact woman in her 50s with close-cropped grey hair and bitten-down fingernails. Her left thumb is wrapped in a bandage that keeps getting replaced — she picks at the skin underneath whenever she's nervous.

**Portrayal:** Never sits still. Straightens papers, re-stacks objects, keeps hands occupied. Answers questions a beat too slowly, as if running each one through a filter first. When caught off-guard she laughs — a short, hollow sound that doesn't reach her eyes.

**Voice:** Hedges everything. "I *think* it was Tuesday." "As far as I know, nobody..." "That's not really my area." Finishes other people's sentences wrong on purpose, steering conversations away from dangerous ground.

---

### AIMS Profile

**Agenda**
- *Immediate:* Get the investigators out of her office without giving them anything actionable. Appear helpful, be useless.
- *Short-term:* Destroy or misfiled the one document that could connect her to the cover-up before anyone thinks to request it formally.
- *Long-term:* Retire quietly in 18 months and move to be near her daughter. She has a number in her head — the amount she was paid — and she needs to outlast the statute of limitations.

**Instinct:** Fawn under pressure. She wants to be liked. When cornered she apologises, redirects, and offers something small and harmless to satisfy her interrogator. She has never fought anyone in her life and won't start now — but she will lie without hesitation if the lie is gentle enough.

**Moves (if PCs don't intervene)**
1. Misfiled the key document under a dead case number in the basement archive — buys her a week, maybe two.
2. Calls a mobile number she was given "for emergencies" tonight to warn whoever is pulling her strings that investigators are asking.
3. Takes two days of sick leave and disappears to her daughter's house if pressure continues, making herself temporarily unreachable.

**Secrets**
- *Surface (casual observation):* The bandage, the nervous energy, the over-helpful hollow laugh. She's clearly uncomfortable — she'll say it's been a stressful week.
- *Investigation (active questioning / Spot Hidden / Notice):* Her desk has a thin scratch mark along one drawer edge where it was forced at some point, and a replacement key on a ring that doesn't match the others. Someone else accessed her workspace, or she has something to hide in there.
- *Deep (evidence / hard persuasion / leverage):* Six months ago she was approached by a man she'd never seen before who knew things about a fifteen-year-old embezzlement she thought was buried. He asked one small favour: misfiled one document. She told herself it was a one-time thing. She has a personal phone she never mentioned, and a number she calls it from three times a year.

---

### Relationships

- **Her supervisor:** Reports to him, fears him mildly, doesn't trust him — she suspects he knows more than he lets on about the same matter and wonders if he was approached too.
- **The contact (unknown name):** Communicates via an intermediary number. Has met in person once. This relationship is the core of whatever the investigators are trying to unravel.
- **Her daughter (off-scene):** Genuinely loves her, no involvement. Represents what Marta is trying to protect. Leveraging this is the fastest way to break her composure — and the most morally complicated.

---

### Running Her at the Table

**If the investigators are polite and official:** She is cooperative-seeming, slightly over-helpful, and subtly steers them toward publicly available records. She will not lie about facts she's asked directly — she will simply not mention the ones they haven't thought to ask about.

**If pressed or accused:** She becomes quietly distressed, not angry. She does not threaten. She says things like "I really don't know what you're implying" and "I want to be as helpful as I can, truly." The more emotional the scene gets, the more helpful she performs, and the less she actually says.

**Breaking point:** Show her that you already know about the document, or mention her daughter's name in a context that implies danger to her. She doesn't crack all at once — she asks for a glass of water, takes a moment, and then says "If I tell you this... what happens to me?"

**The hook she offers:** She can give investigators a name — not the person at the top, but the intermediary she met once. That's her bargaining chip. She's been holding it in reserve without knowing why.

### Quick Stat Block (System-Agnostic Notes)

Marta is **not a physical threat.** Her relevant capabilities are:
- **Deception / Lie:** High. She's been practising for months.
- **Bureaucratic knowledge:** Very high — she knows this records system better than anyone alive.
- **Resistance to intimidation:** Moderate. She folds slowly and strategically, giving something small to stop the pressure rather than cracking completely.
- **Physical:** Average for her age. Fleeing is her only option in danger.

**System translations:**
- *CoC 7e:* Fast Talk 65%, Library Use 75%, Bureaucracy 70% (if using). SAN 50. No Cthulhu Mythos knowledge unless you want her entangled in something deeper.
- *D&D 5e:* Use the **Commoner** stat block (MM). Proficient in Deception and History. No combat relevance.
- *GURPS 4e:* ST 8, DX 10, IQ 12, HT 10. Skills: Bureaucracy-14, Fast-Talk-13, Research-13. Disadvantages: Cowardice, Secret (accessory to cover-up).
- *FitD:* Consort 2, Study 2, Sway 1. Stress threshold: 6. She has a 1-segment clock: "Give investigators the name" — it fills when she believes cooperation is safer than silence.

### GM Notes

The most useful thing about Marta is that she **genuinely did not understand the full scope** of what she was involved in when she agreed. She thought it was corporate embarrassment, maybe financial irregularity. If the investigators are pursuing something darker, her horror at realising what she's been protecting is a powerful scene.

She is not the villain. She is the door. The question is whether the investigators open her gently or kick her down.

**Continuity reminder:** Record her name and key details immediately. The document she misfiled, the intermediary's name she's withholding, and the mobile number are three separate threads that can pay off across multiple sessions.
