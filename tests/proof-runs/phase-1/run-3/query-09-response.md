# Score Opportunity: The Coalridge Ledger

## Overview

**Target:** The Billhooks (Tier II, Strong)  
**Location:** Coalridge — the Billhooks' territory, centred on their slaughterhouse front operation at the Harrowgate Meatworks  
**Score Size:** Standard (6 coin payoff)

---

## The Situation

The Billhooks run a brutal labor racket across Coalridge's factory districts, extorting wages from workers who can't afford to refuse. Their enforcer operation is fronted by the Harrowgate Meatworks — a legitimate slaughterhouse that doubles as a counting house, interrogation site, and storage for anything the Billhooks need to disappear. Tarvul, the vicious patriarch, keeps a physical ledger of every debtor, bribe paid to Bluecoat officers, and labor protection shake-down. That ledger is both a business record and a weapon — and someone else wants it.

**The Hook:** A Dockers union organizer named Sera Voss has made contact with the crew. She's been building a case against the Billhooks' labor extortion but needs the ledger to name the Bluecoat officers on the take. She's offering 4 coin up front and promises the Dockers' goodwill (+1 crew status with The Dockers) if the ledger reaches Inspector Helene Vael's desk without the Billhooks tracing it back to the crew. Vael is one of the few Inspectors not already in someone's pocket.

Complication: Coran, Tarvul's elder son, has been moving the ledger regularly — ever since a Gray Cloaks operative was found with a Billhook blade through her collarbone two weeks ago. He keeps it within arm's reach. Tarvul himself visits the Meatworks every second night for the accounts.

## Score Locations

### The Harrowgate Meatworks
**Type:** Industrial building, 3 floors  
**Atmosphere:** Bloody, cold, loud

**Sensory:** Sight — electroplasmic lamps sway over hanging carcasses, iron hooks gleam wet in pale blue light. Sound — the rhythmic thud of cleavers and the groan of rendering machinery on the ground floor, even after hours. Smell/Touch — rendered fat coats every surface, the air thick with offal and coal smoke; floors are tacky underfoot.

**Key Features:**
- **Counting Room (top floor):** Locked iron door, one window overlooking the yard. This is where Coran works. A heavy iron safe bolted to the floor holds coin; the ledger sits on the desk — Coran has it in hand or within reach, never inside the safe. **Interaction:** picking the safe is a false lead; the ledger is the target, not the coin.
- **Rendering Floor (ground floor):** Active even at night with 3-4 workers and 2 Billhook enforcers. The machinery creates noise that masks movement — or screaming. **Interaction:** the rendering vats are a consequence waiting to happen (level 3 harm, Severe).
- **The Cold Store (basement):** Where bodies go — animal and otherwise. At least two recent "labor disputes" are stored here. **Interaction:** ghosts cling to this space; occult plans benefit, but spirit activity draws Spirit Warden patrols by morning if disturbed.
- **Yard and drain access:** The rendering yard drains connect to Coalridge's sewer network. The grate is locked with a standard padlock. **Interaction:** Wrench or Skirmish to force; Tinker or Finesse to pick silently.

**Inhabitants:**
- Coran Billhook (in counting room, reviewing the week's accounts) — wants the books balanced and to prove himself to Tarvul; will flee before fighting if he's alone.
- 2 Billhook enforcers on the floor (Skirmish 2, scale: small gang) — loyal to Tarvul, not Coran; will not break without serious pressure.
- 4 workers (night shift) — not fighters; will scatter or cower.

**Secrets:** The Cold Store ghost — Olla Dresh, a factory worker the Billhooks disappeared three months ago — retains enough coherence to point toward the counting room if the crew can communicate with spirits. She cannot leave the basement.

---

### The Rusty Hook Tavern (Silkshore border)
**Type:** Tavern, street-level  
**Atmosphere:** Dim, smoky, mournful

Bryl Billhook drinks here most nights — away from the family operation, which he resents. He is the weak link: not vicious, not ambitious, just trapped. He can be turned with the right offer — or threatened with something that damages his reputation with Tarvul (evidence of his gambling debts to the Gray Cloaks, which would enrage his father).

**Social plan:** NPC price is enough coin to clear his debt to the Gray Cloaks (2 coin) and a promise the crew won't make him look complicit. NPC line: Bryl will not help if he thinks it gets Tarvul killed — he hates his father but fears the idea of being next in line.

## Complications (d6 mid-score table)

Roll one if a 4-5 lands and the GM needs a complication beyond Reduced Effect:

1. A Bluecoat officer on Billhook payroll arrives for his weekly envelope — he'll recognize a suspicious scene.
2. Tarvul arrives a night early; his tread is heard on the stairs.
3. One of the night workers recognizes a PC from a previous score.
4. Coran moves the ledger to the safe just before the crew reaches it — the safe is harder to crack quietly.
5. The Cold Store ghost Olla becomes agitated and visible; workers start shouting.
6. A Gray Cloaks operative is already casing the Meatworks — they want the ledger too, for different reasons.

## Outcomes

**Full success (ledger secured, clean):**  
- Payoff: 6 coin + 2 rep (above-Tier target: Billhooks are Tier II vs crew assumption of Tier I; adjust if crew is higher-Tier)  
- Dockers +1 status  
- Billhooks -1 status (now at -1: Interfering)  
- Heat: 2 (contained — ledger disappears, Billhooks don't know who took it)  
- Inspector Vael receives the ledger; the Bluecoat officers named in it are quietly transferred or pressured. Coran spends two weeks looking over his shoulder.

**Partial success (ledger secured, messy):**  
- Payoff: 6 coin  
- Heat: 4 (loud — Coran saw someone, or bodies left behind)  
- Billhooks -1 status and begin a "find the thieves" clock (6-clock, advances each downtime)  
- Dockers +1 status still applies if the ledger reached Vael

**Failure / abandoned:**  
- Payoff: 0  
- Heat: 2 (they were there)  
- Billhooks -1 status (Interfering — they're now watching for the crew)  
- Coran moves the ledger to Tarvul's home; a new score is required with higher stakes

**Unintended consequence — Spirit Warden clock filled:**  
Regardless of score outcome, Spirit Wardens arrive at dawn. If the crew is still present, they must leave immediately or be witnessed at the scene of an "arcane disturbance." Future occult plans near Coalridge start at -1d from Warden scrutiny.

## Connections and Sequel Hooks

- If the Bluecoat officers named in the ledger are brought down, **The Inspectors** (+1 status) gain a foothold in Coalridge — and the crew can claim credit quietly.
- The Gray Cloaks (see Complication 6) will approach the crew afterward if they were present: the Cloaks want to know what was in the ledger. This is a free play / social scene opportunity.
- Tarvul's revenge is slow and indirect — he'll start a long-term faction clock ("Find the Coalridge Thieves," 8 segments) advancing each downtime until the crew addresses it or the Billhooks are weakened.
- Olla Dresh's ghost is a loose thread. She knows more about what the Billhooks have "disappeared" than just her own death. A follow-on score into the Cold Store — or a Long-Term Project to lay her spirit to rest — seeds future play.

## GM Running Notes

**Tone:** Gritty, physical, smells-like-copper. The Meatworks is designed to unsettle even before violence starts — the hooks, the cold, the residual blood. Lean into sensory detail before the first roll. The Billhooks are not subtle; their response to intrusion is loud and escalating.

**Coran as a character:** He is not his father. He won't throw his life away defending the counting room alone, but he will alert the floor enforcers. If cornered and the crew offers him a way out of the shadow of the ledger (e.g., "we frame this as a robbery, you were overpowered"), he'll accept — at a cost. This is a devil's bargain opportunity: the crew gets the ledger without a fight, but Coran becomes a loose end who knows their faces.

**Billhooks Tier vs crew Tier:** If the crew is Tier II (same), treat all engagement rolls as standard. If Tier I, the Billhooks' scale advantage may reduce effect on direct confrontation to Limited unless the crew uses the environment cleverly (the machinery, the cold store, the drain access).

**Heat and the Dockers:** The Dockers care that the ledger surfaces through official channels, not that the Billhooks suffer publicly. A noisy score that draws Bluecoat attention before the ledger reaches Vael may spook Voss and forfeit the faction reward — even if the crew gets paid.
