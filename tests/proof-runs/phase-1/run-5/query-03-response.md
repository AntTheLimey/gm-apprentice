## New Occupation Stat Blocks (Regency-Specific)

**Gentleman** — Landed gentry, lives by wealth and privilege.
- Formula: EDU×2 + APP×2
- Credit Rating: 50–90 (Band 4: Gentry)
- Skills: Charm, Dancing, Etiquette, Fashion, Gaming, Ride, one weapon skill, one hobby skill.

**Gentlewoman** — Gentry woman; social standing defined by accomplishments.
- Formula: EDU×2 + APP×2
- Credit Rating: 50–90 (Band 4: Gentry)
- Skills: Art/Craft (Embroidery/Fine Art/Instrument/Singing), Dancing, Etiquette, Fashion, Language (Other), Persuade, Ride, one hobby skill.

**Nouveau Riche** — Wealthy through trade or industry; seeking gentry acceptance.
- Formula: EDU×4
- Credit Rating: 50–90 (Band 4: Gentry)
- Skills: Accounting, Appraise, Art/Craft (Business), Dancing, Fast Talk, Persuade, Ride, one weapon skill.

**Servant (Footman)** — Male household servant; errands and public duties.
- Formula: INT×2 + POW×2
- Credit Rating: 10–30 (Band 1: Laborer)
- Skills: Animal Handling, Art/Craft (Any), Dodge, Etiquette, Fast Talk, Listen, Ride, Spot Hidden.

**Servant (Housemaid)** — Female domestic servant; cleaning and personal service.
- Formula: INT×2 + POW×2
- Credit Rating: 10–30 (Band 1: Laborer)
- Skills: Art/Craft (Cooking/Sewing), Dodge, Etiquette, Fashion, Fast Talk, Listen, Spot Hidden, Stealth.

| 4 | Gentry/Nouveau Riche | 50–90% | Gentleman, Gentlewoman, Nouveau Riche |
| 5 | Aristocracy | 90–100% | Not routinely available as a PC. Rename Gentleman/Gentlewoman to Nobleman/Noblewoman and use CR 90–100%. |

Notes:
- Butler/Valet/Maid CR capped at 40% even within Band 1 (reflects senior household staff).
- Band 2 artistic occupations (Artist, Entertainer, Musician, etc.) may raise CR to 70% with Keeper permission.

## Using Base CoC 7e Occupations

Regency occupations not in the list above default to the nearest base CoC 7e equivalent with period-appropriate skill substitutions (Keeper approval required). Use the standard skill point formula table from `systems/coc-7e/occupations.md`.

## GM Notes

- **Hobby careers:** Occupations marked * can serve as hobby pursuits for Gentleman or Gentlewoman characters. The character uses the Gentleman/Gentlewoman skill list but gains skills from the hobby occupation — useful for the well-rounded gentleman scholar, artist, or smuggler.
- **Aristocracy (Band 5):** Reserved for NPCs or by special Keeper arrangement. Mechanically identical to Gentleman/Gentlewoman but CR 90–100%.
- **Social mobility as drama:** The gap between Band 3 Professional (CR 30–70%) and Band 4 Gentry (CR 50–90%) is the engine of Regency social friction. A successful Doctor or Lawyer who clears 70% CR is wealthier than many minor gentry but still excluded from certain social spaces — rich material for investigation scenarios.
