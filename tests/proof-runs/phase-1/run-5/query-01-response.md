# CoC 7e Chase Rules

CoC 7e uses a structured, abstract chase system built on a track of locations. Here is the full procedure.

---

## Setting Up a Chase

1. **Chase track**: Draw a line of locations (typically 10-20 spaces). Place the pursuer and quarry at their starting positions on the track.
2. **Speed**: Each participant's MOV characteristic determines how many spaces they move per round. Higher MOV = more spaces covered.
3. **Hazards**: The Keeper places hazards at intervals on the track — fences, traffic, locked doors, crowds, and similar obstacles.

A chase begins when one participant declares they are fleeing (using their action for the round as "Flee").

---

## Chase Round Sequence

Each chase round proceeds as follows:

1. **Move**: Each participant moves their MOV in spaces along the track.
2. **Sprint attempt (optional)**: A participant may attempt a CON roll to gain +1 extra space. Failure means no extra movement and may cause fatigue.
3. **Hazards**: If a participant enters a hazard space, they must roll the appropriate skill (e.g., Jump for a fence, Drive for traffic, Athletics for a crowd) to navigate it.
   - Success: pass through normally.
   - Failure: lose 1 space (and potentially take damage, at the Keeper's discretion).
4. **Actions**: Participants may spend their action on:
   - Attacking the other party (ranged weapon, thrown object, ramming vehicle, etc.)
   - Using a skill to create an obstacle for those behind them
   - Another relevant stunt

---

## Chase Resolution

| Outcome | Condition |
|---------|-----------|
| **Caught** | Pursuer occupies the same space as the quarry |
| **Escaped** | Quarry reaches the end of the track, or opens a gap so large the pursuer cannot close it |
| **Complications** | Bystander injuries, property damage, attracting police attention — apply as appropriate |

---

## Key Rules Summary

- **MOV is the primary stat.** An investigator with higher MOV has a natural advantage; chasing or fleeing a creature with MOV 10+ is extremely dangerous.
- **CON sprinting is optional risk.** Taking the CON roll each round can widen or close the gap but risks fatigue accumulation.
- **Hazards are narrative tools.** The Keeper uses them to raise tension and force meaningful skill rolls, not to automatically end the chase.
- **Actions still happen.** Characters are not helpless mid-chase — shooting, throwing obstacles, and stunts all remain available as the round's action.

---

## Keeper Guidance

Chases should be run cinematically: describe what the characters see as they move through spaces, and place 2-4 hazards to create decision points. Most foot chases resolve in 3-6 rounds. Vehicle chases can run longer and should include Drive rolls for hazard navigation.

If investigators are fleeing a Mythos creature with a higher MOV, make the escape about finding cover, a locked door, or a barrier the creature cannot pass — not outrunning it on the track, which will usually be impossible.
