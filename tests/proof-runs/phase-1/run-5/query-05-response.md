## Design Notes

### The Physical Path

The chargen-kit-thief.md identifies two build paths for thief/spy
characters. Mira is squarely on the physical path: DX 13 as the
foundation, Per 13 for situational awareness, IQ 11 for technical
skills, HT 11 for endurance. This delivers strong physical skill
levels while keeping the budget honest.

**DX 13 [60 pts]** raises every physical skill on the sheet. Stealth 15,
Climbing 14 (effective 17 vs tight spaces with Flexibility), Acrobatics
13, Escape 13 (effective 16 with Flexibility) — all solid for a
150-point character.

**Per 13 [10 pts above IQ]** is worth the secondary modification
investment. Observation 14 means she reads a room before entering it.
Search 13 means she finds the hidden wall safe, not just the obvious one.

### Advantage Choices

**Flexibility [5]** (B56) is the most efficient physical infiltration
advantage available. It grants +3 to both Climbing and Escape. On
Climbing 14, that's effective 17 when squeezing through tight access
shafts or using unorthodox routes. On Escape 13, that's effective 16
when she needs to slip handcuffs or squeeze through a narrowing duct.
At 5 pts it outperforms buying either skill up by one extra level.

**Night Vision 5 [5]** (B71): Five levels at 1 pt/level eliminate
darkness penalties in all but pitch-black conditions. Combined with
the Night Vision Goggles (NV 9) in her kit, Mira operates freely
in any darkness condition.

**Perfect Balance [15]** (B74): Near-mandatory for a character who
works at height. The +1 to DX-based skills on narrow or unstable
surfaces stacks with every physical skill she has. The +6 vs
knockdown matters when four floors up on a ledge in a wind.

**Luck [15]** (B66): The safety net. At 150 points there is no buying
out every risk. One failed Lockpicking roll in a timed window, one
critical failure on Stealth — Luck converts a catastrophe into a
second chance, once per hour of real time.

**Note on Flexibility vs Double-Jointed:** Double-Jointed [15] gives
+5 to Climbing and Escape (vs Flexibility's +3) and also applies to
Erotic Art. At 15 pts vs 5 pts it costs three times as much for a
smaller marginal improvement. At 150 points, Flexibility is the
correct choice. At 200 points, swap in Double-Jointed.

### Skill Investment Logic

**Stealth [8 pts, level 15]** is slightly lower than the maximum
efficient investment to save points for breadth. At 15, most routine
patrols don't spot her; a critical failure (roll 17 or 18 only at
this level) is still possible but rare.

**Climbing [4 pts, level 14]** looks modest, but Flexibility's +3
bonus applies whenever she's climbing through restricted spaces (which
is most building climbing — pipes, ductwork, facade details). For open
wall climbing without handholds she's at 14; for her natural element
she's at 17.

**Lockpicking [8 pts, level 13]** with Work by Touch technique
(Skill-2 = 11 in darkness): she can pick locks blind, a routine
operational requirement. For well-lit conditions with tools she's at
13; in darkness without looking she's at 11. The technique starts at
the default Skill-4 (-4 penalty); 2 points buys it to Skill-2 (-2
penalty), a meaningful improvement.

**Acrobatics [4 pts, level 13]**: The +2 Dodge bonus on a successful
Acrobatics roll makes this worth the hard-difficulty investment. On a
chase across rooftops or a sudden confrontation, rolling Acrobatics
first and getting Dodge 11 (or 10 at light encumbrance) changes
outcomes.

**Combat skills are deliberately minimal**: Brawling DX+1 [2 pts]
and Knife DX+0 [1 pt]. Mira's doctrine is to not be in combat.
Every point spent on combat above functional is a point not spent
on the skills that prevent combat from being necessary.

### Disadvantage Notes

**Secret [-20]** (Criminal; Imprisonment) is the defining pressure.
She has no criminal record in official databases — yet — but that
changes the moment a detective gets lucky or a job goes wrong. The
GM's primary hook is the investigation closing in.

**Greed (CR 12) [-15]** triggers when a target has more value than
she was hired to take, or when an unplanned opportunity presents
itself. She makes a Will 11 roll to resist. The GM should present
these temptations; acting on Greed is how she gets into trouble.

**Overconfidence (CR 12) [-5]**: She may underestimate a security
upgrade, take a job that exceeds her current resources, or assume
she can improvise where she should have planned. Roll Will vs 11 to
recognize she's in over her head.

**Loner (CR 12) [-5]**: Prefers solo work. In team scenarios, -2 to
reaction rolls from colleagues who want coordination. She'll resist
accepting help even when she needs it.

### Scaling

**At 100 pts:** Drop Perfect Balance (save 15), reduce Night Vision
to 2 (save 3), drop Loner and replace with a lighter disad (save 0
net), trim Stealth to [4] = DX+1=14 (save 4), Climbing to [2] = DX+0=13
(save 2). Still a functional operative, less capable on height-based
infiltration.

**At 200 pts:** Add Double-Jointed [15] replacing Flexibility (net +10),
Combat Reflexes [15], boost DX to 14 (+20), raising Stealth to 17,
Climbing to 16, and adding Pickpocket (DX/H) at 14 and Shadowing
(IQ/A) at 12. This is a cinematic-grade heist specialist.

### Sample Scenario: The Nightwatch Gallery

A private collector's penthouse on the 18th floor. Security: card-access
elevator, magnetic door locks, motion sensors on display cases, one
roving guard.

- **Scouting:** Research (11) pulls building permits and floor plans.
  Observation (14) + Binoculars maps the guard's patrol timing from a
  rooftop across the street. Audio bugs (planted during a building
  inspection cover visit) track interior movement.
- **Entry:** Climbing (14, effective 17 via drainpipe) up the service
  side. Traps (12) identifies motion sensor placement. Electronics
  Operation Security (12) loops the card-reader signal or bypasses the
  magnetic lock.
- **Interior:** Stealth (15) gets her past the guard. Lockpicking (13)
  opens the display case; Work by Touch (11) handles it if the lights
  are off. Search (13) locates the secondary concealed safe.
- **Exit:** Grapnel to the adjacent roof. If anything goes wrong, Luck
  covers the first catastrophe; Acrobatic Dodge (roll 13, then Dodge
  11) covers the second. If physically cornered, Brawling creates one
  opening — then she runs.
