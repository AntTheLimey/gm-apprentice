## Mira Halsten — The Reluctant Witness

**Concept:** A mid-level clerk at the relevant institution who knows more than she's said and is desperately trying to stay invisible.

---

### 3-Line (Use Right Now)

**Appearance:** A slight woman in her 40s, hair pinned severely back, who keeps both hands flat on whatever surface is near her — desk, counter, thighs — as if steadying herself.

**Portrayal:** Answers every question by restating it slightly differently before she responds. Gives the answer to the question before yours, not the one you just asked. Never looks at the thing she's referring to.

**Hook:** She witnessed something she wasn't supposed to witness three weeks ago and has been quietly moving money out of her savings ever since.

---

### Investigation Scene Mechanics (Grab-and-Go)

**What she volunteers:** Nothing. She'll confirm facts the investigators already have, word for word, no additions.

**What a good roll / good roleplay gets:**
- *First success:* She was working late the night in question. So was someone who shouldn't have been.
- *Second success:* She filed a report about what she saw. The report is gone from the records.
- *Third success / trust established:* She has a copy. She made one because she suspected this might happen.

**What makes her clam up:** Direct accusations, raised voices, anyone who seems connected to her employer, anyone who writes things down in front of her.

**What breaks her open:** Evidence that the investigators are already in danger too. Misery loves company; fear loves shared stakes.

---

### AIMS (If She Becomes Recurring)

**Agenda:**
- *Immediate:* Get through this conversation without her name going anywhere
- *Short-term:* Stay employed until she has enough saved to leave the city
- *Long-term:* Disappear somewhere no one knows her

**Instinct:** Freeze, then fawn. Her first move under pressure is to become maximally agreeable while giving nothing real.

**Moves (if PCs don't intervene):**
1. Destroys her personal copy of the report within 48 hours if she thinks investigators are getting close
2. Requests a transfer to a branch in another district by end of the week
3. If denied the transfer, goes silent — won't speak to anyone outside her apartment

**Secrets:**
- *Surface:* She's been distracted and jumpy for weeks (colleagues have noticed)
- *Investigation:* She filed an incident report that has since vanished from the records
- *Deep:* The person she saw that night is someone the investigators will recognise — and that person saw her too

---

### Voice at the Table

Use the "formal diction + restating" pattern:
> "You're asking whether I was present on the fourteenth. The fourteenth. I... I work late sometimes. Most of the staff do."

Never give a clean yes or no. Always pivot via paraphrase.

---

### Stat Block (System-Agnostic)

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Role | Witness / Reluctant informant |
| Threat | None direct; becomes dangerous if she destroys evidence |
| Persuasion difficulty | Hard (she wants to cooperate; she's more afraid than hostile) |
| Intimidation effect | Counterproductive — she shuts down entirely |
| Key skill | Administrative knowledge; access to records |

---

### GM Notes

Mira is not an antagonist. She is someone caught between institutional loyalty and personal survival. The goal of the scene is to make her feel safe enough to help — or to give investigators enough to find the evidence without her.

**Scene escalation:** If investigators push too hard and Mira bolts, she can still be recovered — but she'll destroy the copy of the report first. Make the players feel the cost of impatience.

**If she survives and trusts the investigators:** She becomes a low-tier recurring asset — someone with legitimate access who will do small favours in exchange for protection.
