## Oswald Fenn

**Concept:** A once-respected antiquarian whose transactions in cursed objects have stripped him of sleep, trust, and most of his sanity — but not, infuriatingly, his expertise.

**Appearance:** A slight man in his early sixties who dresses too warmly for the season — overcoat even indoors, collar up. Thinning white hair, always damp at the temples. Ink-stained hands that never fully keep still; when he speaks to you, he also watches the door.

**Portrayal:** Never sits with his back to a room entrance. Qualifies every statement ("allegedly", "so far as I've been able to determine", "though I may be wrong, I pray I am wrong"). Starts sentences, stops, looks over his shoulder, restarts. Generous with information once he decides you're not *them* — but that decision takes time, and you have to earn it.

**Voice:** Low, clipped sentences that accelerate when he's frightened. Frequent false starts. Has a habit of trailing off mid-sentence and resuming two beats later as though nothing happened. "The catalogue came through — I shouldn't keep it, you understand, but I — the provenance dates to 1743, which means Hargreave couldn't have — no. Start again."

## AIMS Profile

**Agenda**
- *Immediate:* Offload the Hargreave folio to someone competent enough to handle it and paranoid enough not to be careless. Ideally the investigators. He will not keep it another night.
- *Short-term:* Confirm whether the men who have been watching his shop are agents of the Manthorne Society, a rival dealer, or something worse. He needs to know before he can plan.
- *Long-term:* Achieve enough distance from the objects he has trafficked over twenty years to allow his remaining sanity to stabilise. He suspects this is not possible but cannot stop trying.

**Instinct**
Flee, then rationalise the fleeing as strategy. If cornered, Oswald becomes very still and very precise — he defaults to recitation of facts as a coping mechanism, which can look like calm but is actually dissociation. Under genuine threat he will bargain with anything he has: information, money, the folio, the investigators' names.

**Moves** (fires on schedule if investigators don't engage)
1. *This week:* Packages the Hargreave folio and posts it anonymously to Miskatonic University — removes it from his shop, solves nothing, creates a new problem.
2. *Next week:* Begins liquidating his most dangerous inventory at below-market prices to the first serious buyer. He will not vet them. This is very bad.
3. *Following month:* Closes the shop, takes a room at a boarding house under a false name. Disappears from any network that could help or warn the investigators.

**Secrets**
- *Surface:* Clearly frightened of something. Excessive security on a dusty antiquarian shop: new locks, boarded rear window, a dog he doesn't seem to like.
- *Investigation (Psychology 40% or direct question after trust):* Has lost nine points of Sanity in the past three months from handling a single item. He knows something is wrong with the folio but cannot identify what, which frightens him more than if he could.
- *Deep (Library Use + cross-referencing his records):* Oswald has knowingly sold at least three Mythos-adjacent objects over his career, convincing himself they were harmless curiosities. One reached a collector who died under strange circumstances eighteen months ago. He has suppressed this. The Manthorne Society — a loose network of collectors with genuine occult awareness — blames him and is watching to see whether he'll repeat the pattern.

## Relationships

- **The Manthorne Society:** Adversarial surveillance. They consider him a dangerous amateur who distributes hazardous materials through ignorance. They won't act unless he does something irreversible.
- **Professor Helena Voss (Miskatonic University):** Cautious colleague. She has warned him twice to stop handling certain categories of object. He respects her enough to be ashamed of ignoring her advice. She doesn't know about the folio yet.
- **Gerald Fenn (nephew):** Dependent. Works in the shop, knows nothing of the dangerous side of the trade. Oswald has been trying to get him to leave for a job in Boston. Gerald thinks his uncle is having a breakdown.
- **The Hargreave Folio:** Object. The thing he needs to be rid of. A manuscript catalogue of a 17th-century collection with marginalia in an unknown hand — the marginalia are the problem.

## GM Notes

**Running Oswald at the table:** He is not obstructive — he wants to help, he wants to be rid of the folio, and he is genuinely frightened. The paranoia is the obstacle. Give him one concrete reason to trust each investigator individually before he opens up fully. The Psychology skill (45%) means he reads people reasonably well; if an investigator tries to deceive him or rushes him, he notices and retreats.

**The folio as a prop:** The marginalia should be partially legible — a few phrases the investigators can parse, most in a hand that resists translation. Oswald can read fragments but refuses to read it aloud. If asked why, he says: "I tried once. I won't describe what I heard in the room afterwards." SAN loss for extended study: 0/1D4.

**If the investigators don't help him:** The first Moves fire. The folio ends up at Miskatonic, creating a second location problem. Oswald disappears. He resurfaces three sessions later in worse shape: SAN 28, Cthulhu Mythos 22%, and now actively delusional about the investigators' motives.

**Sanity loss (true nature):** If the investigators learn that Oswald knowingly sold dangerous objects and concealed a death, they may lose 0/1 SAN from the implications — not from Oswald himself, but from what it says about the world they're moving through.

**Escalation hook:** The Manthorne Society's watchers will approach the investigators separately within a session of them meeting Oswald. They have information he doesn't — and their own agenda for the folio.
