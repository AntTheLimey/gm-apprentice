## Feature Descriptions

**Lay On Hands (Level 1):** A pool of HP equal to 5 × Paladin level. As an action, touch a creature to restore any number of HP from the pool, or expend 5 HP to cure one disease or poison. Replenishes on a Long Rest.

**Spellcasting (Level 1):** Prepares spells from the Paladin list using CHA as the spellcasting ability. Half caster — spell slots go up to 5th level. Spell slots are shared with any multiclass caster levels.

**Weapon Mastery (Level 1):** Can use the Mastery property of a number of eligible weapons. Mastery options (Cleave, Graze, Nick, Push, Sap, Slow, Topple, Vex) vary by weapon.

**Fighting Style (Level 2):** Choose one fighting style. Options include Blessed Warrior, Blind Fighting, Defense, Dueling, Great Weapon Fighting, Protection, and others.

**Paladin's Smite (Level 2):** When you hit with a melee weapon attack, you can expend a spell slot to deal extra Radiant damage — 2d8 per spell slot level (1st level = 2d8, 2nd = 3d8, etc.). Undead and fiends take an additional 1d8. Replaces the old Divine Smite; this version is still a spell slot expenditure but the feature is now a defined class ability rather than a free-form slot dump.

**Channel Divinity (Level 3):** Gain 2 uses (3 at level 11), recharging on a Short or Long Rest. The specific effects depend on your subclass Oath. Generic uses available to all Paladins are granted through the subclass.

**Subclass — Oath (Level 3):** Choose your Sacred Oath, which grants Channel Divinity options, additional prepared spells (always prepared, don't count against your limit), and higher-level features. The SRD subclass is **Oath of Devotion**.

**Extra Attack (Level 5):** Attack twice when you take the Attack action.

**Faithful Steed (Level 5):** Can cast *Find Steed* without expending a spell slot. The steed also gains certain benefits while bonded to you.

**Aura of Protection (Level 6):** You and friendly creatures within 10 ft add your CHA modifier (minimum +1) to all saving throws while you are conscious. This is one of the strongest defensive auras in 5e.

**Abjure Foes (Level 9):** As an action, expend a Channel Divinity use to frighten a number of creatures you can see within 60 ft (CHA save negates). Frightened creatures are also incapacitated while the condition lasts. Ends when the creature takes damage or the duration expires.

**Aura of Courage (Level 10):** You and friendly creatures within 10 ft can't be Frightened while you are conscious. Stacks with Aura of Protection (both apply in the same 10 ft radius at this level).

**Radiant Strikes (Level 11):** Your melee weapon attacks deal an additional 1d8 Radiant damage on every hit (no resource cost).

**Restoring Touch (Level 14):** When you use Lay On Hands to restore HP to a creature, you can also end one of the following conditions on that creature: Blinded, Charmed, Deafened, Frightened, Paralyzed, or Stunned. No additional HP cost.

**Aura Expansion (Level 18):** The radius of Aura of Protection and Aura of Courage (and any other Paladin auras) expands from 10 ft to 30 ft.

## SRD Subclass: Oath of Devotion

Sacred Oath spells are always prepared and don't count against your Prepared Spells total.

**Channel Divinity — Sacred Weapon:** As a bonus action, imbue a weapon with positive energy for 1 minute. The weapon becomes magical (if it isn't), adds your CHA modifier to attack rolls, and sheds bright light in a 20 ft radius.

**Channel Divinity — Holy Nimbus (Level 20 / capstone):** As an action, emit an aura of sunlight for 1 minute. Enemies that start their turn in the aura take Radiant damage; undead and fiends have disadvantage on attacks against you.

## GM Notes

- The Paladin's **Aura of Protection** is broadly considered one of the most impactful single class features in D&D 5e. In a party context, a CHA-focused Paladin (CHA +5) adds +5 to every party member's saves within 30 ft at level 18. This substantially reduces the danger of many high-CR threats.
- **Paladin's Smite** is now an explicit feature rather than an implied slot expenditure. Mechanically similar to 2014, but the 2024 version clarifies it is not a spell — it cannot be counterspelled.
- Paladins are **half casters**: they gain a spell slot every other level. Use the half caster table (not the full caster table shown at the end of the classes reference). Their highest available spell slot is 5th level.
- The 2024 **Weapon Mastery** system adds tactical variety — Paladins with a greatsword (Graze) or maul (Topple) can knock enemies prone to facilitate teammates' attacks.
