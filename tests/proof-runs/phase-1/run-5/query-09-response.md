# Score Opportunity: The Coalridge Tally

**Faction:** The Billhooks (Tier II, Strong — brutal labor-racket gang in Coalridge)
**Score Type:** Robbery / Assault hybrid
**Suggested Crew Tier:** Any (best at Tier I-II; higher-tier crews will find it almost trivial)

---

## Premise

The Billhooks run a protection racket across Coalridge's factory district.
Every week, Coran — Tarvul's eldest son and the gang's coin-counter —
collects payments from factory foremen in a battered ledger called the
Tally. The Tally is both an accounts book and a blackmail register:
it lists every foreman who's ever paid, every one who's ever resisted
(with the grim result), and the names of a handful of City Council
functionaries who take a quiet cut. It is the Billhooks' single most
valuable non-violent asset.

Word has reached the crew through a fence, a desperate factory foreman,
or a rival gang — the Tally is in transit tonight. Coran is moving it
from Tarvul's slaughterhouse base to a lockbox at the Coalridge Bluecoat
sub-station (one of the corrupt officers on the Billhooks' payroll stores
it for them). The move happens on foot: Coran, two Billhook heavies
with meat cleavers, and a covered handcart.

**The opportunity:** intercept the handcart, grab the Tally, and either
sell it, use it to extort the Billhooks, or leverage the Council names
inside.

---

## Score Framing (Table Setup)

Read aloud to players during Score Setup:

> "The smog sits low over Coalridge tonight. Somewhere up the hill a
> rendering vat has gone wrong — the air smells of singed fat and hot
> iron. Coran Billhook leaves the Slaughterhouse at the eleventh bell,
> pushing a handcart loaded with butcher's waste. Underneath the waste,
> wrapped in oilskin, is a ledger that could embarrass half the district
> foremen in the city. He's got two men with him — Vosker, who is large,
> and Brak, who is mean. They'll be at the sub-station before the next bell."

Players choose their **plan type** and **detail**, then roll engagement.

## Six Plan Types — Notes for the GM

| Plan | Detail Example | Engagement Modifier |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| Assault | "At the alley mouth past the Gasworks" | +1d (bold and physical, suits their Tier) |
| Stealth | "Through the factory rooftops, drop onto the cart" | Neutral (risky — Coran is watchful) |
| Deception | "Fake a Bluecoat checkpoint, demand to inspect the cart" | +1d if crew can source a uniform; -1d if no props |
| Transport | "Intercept via the canal — Coran has to cross a bridge" | +1d (cuts off retreat option) |
| Occult | "Send a ghost ahead to terrify the escort into bolting" | -1d (draws Spirit Warden attention in Coalridge) |
| Social | "Coran owes a personal debt to a contact the crew knows" | +1d if that contact exists; otherwise -1d |

## Key NPCs

**Coran Billhook** — Tarvul's eldest son. Calculating where his father is
brutal. He values the Tally as proof of his own importance within the
gang; losing it terrifies him more than physical harm. Wants to deal
if cornered. Line he won't cross: admitting to his father he lost it.
*(Deception and Social approaches can exploit his personal pride.)*

**Vosker** — Large, loyal, slow. Fights until he takes serious harm,
then looks to Coran for instruction. No initiative of his own.

**Brak** — Mean, quick to escalate, carries a short cleaver and a
bladder of lamp oil. If things go badly, Brak lights something on fire.
*(Devil's Bargain: the factory wall catches — witnesses, heat +2.)*

**Constable Hilda Drave** — The corrupt Bluecoat expecting the Tally
at the sub-station. If delivery is late, she sends two officers to
investigate after a quarter-bell. She is not loyal to the Billhooks —
she is loyal to the arrangement. She can be bribed, but she will
remember faces.

## Key Locations

**The Slaughterhouse Yard** (starting point)
- A working butchery with animals penned outside; the smell and noise
  provide cover. The yard gate is watched by one Billhook lookout.
- Opportunity: intercept before Coran leaves, in the Billhooks' own
  territory. High heat (+1 hostile turf), but easy access to the Tally
  before it reaches more complicated terrain.

**Hask Lane** (chokepoint)
- A narrow alley between two factory walls, lit by a single
  electroplasmic lamp that flickers and gutters. Pools of grey water.
  The handcart must slow to navigate a broken cobblestone section.
- Opportunity: the natural ambush point. One entry, one exit.
  The broken stones can be used to jam the cart.
- Hazard: Brak knows this lane is a choke — he watches the rooftops
  here and will light his oil if spooked.

**The Gasworks Bridge** (midpoint)
- A narrow iron footbridge over a stinking canal. Coran would have
  to cross alone with the cart while the heavies go around — the only
  point on the route where he is briefly separated.
- Opportunity: block the bridge. Coran is isolated. A Skirmish or
  Sway here resolves the score with minimal chaos.
- Hazard: a Docker night watchman makes rounds on the canal bank
  every fifteen minutes.

**Coalridge Bluecoat Sub-Station** (endpoint)
- A cramped brick building, two constables on duty, Drave inside.
  The lockbox is behind a heavy door.
- Opportunity: social or deception approach — arrive before Coran
  with a plausible story and claim the delivery. Requires either
  forgery or a convincing cover.
- Hazard: Tier III institutional resistance. If violence starts
  here, the crew is almost certainly fighting every Bluecoat in
  the district inside twenty minutes.

## Clocks

**Coran Reaches the Sub-Station** — 6-clock (Mission)
*Advances one segment per round of in-score time that passes without
the crew acting decisively. Full = window closed; Tally is locked away.*

**Brak Gets Suspicious** — 4-clock (Danger)
*Starts at 0. Advances when: the crew is spotted on the rooftops (1),
Coran pauses to look around (1 — on a 4/5 result during approach),
Brak sees a flicker of movement (1), or any loud noise on the approach.
Full = Brak lights his oil and starts shouting. Heat +2, alarm raised,
fighting in a burning space.*

**The Tally Is Secured** — 6-clock (Progress)
*The crew's objective. Filling this clock means they have the ledger
in hand and Coran cannot easily report the loss without admitting
what the Tally contains.*

## Complications (roll or select)

1. The handcart contains a live person — a factory foreman who
   refused to pay and is being delivered to Tarvul for a lesson.
   Rescuing him costs time and adds a witness.
2. A different crew — the Gray Cloaks — is also watching Coran
   tonight, for their own reasons. They will not intervene but they
   will observe. The crew may be identified to a third party.
3. Coran suspects something and has already sent Bryl (his brother)
   ahead via a different route with a decoy ledger. The real Tally
   is on Coran's person, not in the cart.
4. Constable Drave sent a reminder message via a street runner —
   the handover window has moved forward half a bell. The Mission
   clock starts at 2 instead of 0.
5. The Docker night watchman on the bridge is not a Docker — he
   is a Billhook scout placed specifically to watch for trouble
   tonight. Someone tipped Coran off that a crew was asking questions.

## Payoff and Heat

**Payoff on success:**

| What the Crew Takes | Rep | Coin |
|--------------------|----|-----|
| Tally only (sold to a fence) | 2 rep | 4 coin (standard score) |
| Tally + leverage over Billhooks | 3 rep | 2 coin (extortion opens long-term income) |
| Tally + Council names sold to a rival faction | 4 rep | 6 coin (big score, but makes a new enemy) |
| Tally destroyed on behalf of the foremen | 2 rep | 0 coin (favour economy only) |

**Heat:**

| Approach | Base Heat | Modifiers |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Quiet theft (deception/transport) | 2 | +1 high-profile target (Billhooks are Tier II) |
| Street ambush in Hask Lane | 4 | +1 high-profile, +1 hostile turf = 6 |
| Assault at Slaughterhouse Yard | 4 | +1 hostile turf, +2 if killing = up to 7 |
| Social at Sub-Station (clean) | 0 | +1 high-profile = 1 |

## Outcomes and Sequel Hooks

**Full success — Tally secured, no alarm raised:**
- Tarvul does not know what happened or who did it. Clock starts:
  "Billhooks Hunt for the Tally" (6-clock). Crew has a window to act
  on the ledger before the clock fills and the Billhooks turn violent.
- The Council functionaries named inside will pay to keep it quiet.
  Future social score opportunity against Whitecrown figures.

**Partial success — Tally secured, crew is identified:**
- Billhook status shifts to -1 (Interfering) immediately. Coran
  reports to Tarvul. Bryl starts asking questions in the crew's
  hunting grounds within one downtime. Faction clock: "Billhooks
  Retaliate" (4-clock) begins ticking.

**Failure — Tally not recovered, crew exposed:**
- Billhook status drops to -2 (Hostile). The crew's faces are
  known; Tarvul activates his labor-racket network to put pressure
  on any legitimate contacts the crew relies on. The Tally reaches
  the sub-station; its contents are now a Billhook-controlled weapon.

**Failure — Coran killed or seriously harmed:**
- Tarvul enters a grief-rage. Status -3 (War) within one downtime.
  The Billhooks are not tactically sophisticated but they are
  numerous and they do not forgive.

## GM Notes

**Tone:** Gritty, physical, industrial. The smell of rendering fat
and coal smoke should feature in every description. The Billhooks
are not clever enemies — they are relentless and brutal. Coran is
the smart one, and his vulnerability is ego.

**Position by plan:**
- Assault: Risky (they are ready for trouble, just not for you
  specifically)
- Deception: Risky → Controlled if the detail is convincing
- Stealth on rooftops: Risky (Brak watches high ground)
- Social at the bridge: Controlled if the crew has a legitimate
  connection to the factory district

**Effect by plan:**
- Any plan targeting Coran directly: Standard effect on the Tally
- Plans targeting the heavies first: Limited effect on the Tally
  (still have to deal with Coran)
- Deception at the sub-station: Great effect if the forgery holds;
  Limited if Drave is suspicious

**The Brak problem:** If Brak ignites his oil, treat the fire as a
4-clock Danger ("Factory Wall Catches") that ticks independently
of the score. The crew may need to choose between completing the
score and stopping a larger catastrophe — a classic FitD dilemma.
