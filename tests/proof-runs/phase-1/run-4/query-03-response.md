# Regency Cthulhu — Available Occupations

Regency play (1811–1820) uses a period-specific occupation list in place of
the standard CoC 7e list. Skill point formulas from the base occupation file
still apply; the Regency overlay replaces which occupations are available and
introduces five new stat blocks.

## New Occupations (Regency-Specific Stat Blocks)

**Gentleman** — Landed gentry, lives by wealth and privilege.
EDU×2+APP×2 | CR 50–90 (Band 4: Gentry)
Skills: Charm, Dancing, Etiquette, Fashion, Gaming, Ride, one weapon skill, one hobby skill.

**Gentlewoman** — Gentry woman; accomplishments define social standing.
EDU×2+APP×2 | CR 50–90 (Band 4: Gentry)
Skills: Art/Craft (Embroidery/Fine Art/Instrument/Singing), Dancing, Etiquette, Fashion, Language (Other), Persuade, Ride, one hobby skill.

**Nouveau Riche** — Wealthy by trade or industry; seeks gentry acceptance.
EDU×4 | CR 50–90 (Band 4: Gentry)
Skills: Accounting, Appraise, Art/Craft (Business), Dancing, Fast Talk, Persuade, Ride, one weapon skill.

**Servant (Footman)** — Male household servant; errands and public duties.
INT×2+POW×2 | CR 10–30 (Band 1: Laborer)
Skills: Animal Handling, Art/Craft (Any), Dodge, Etiquette, Fast Talk, Listen, Ride, Spot Hidden.

**Servant (Housemaid)** — Female domestic servant; cleaning and personal service.
INT×2+POW×2 | CR 10–30 (Band 1: Laborer)
Skills: Art/Craft (Cooking/Sewing), Dodge, Etiquette, Fashion, Fast Talk, Listen, Spot Hidden, Stealth.

## Occupational Bands

Social standing in Regency England is baked into the Credit Rating system
through five bands:

| Band | Name | CR Range | Representative Occupations |
|------|------|----------|---------------------------|
| 1 | Laborer/Servant | 9–30% | Burglar, Butler/Valet/Maid, Criminal, Driver (Carriage), Miner, Missionary, Sailor, Servant (Footman), Servant (Housemaid), Smuggler, Soldier/Marine, Zealot |
| 2 | Shopkeeper/Craftsperson | 20–60% | Actor, Antique Dealer, Archaeologist, Artist, Author, Book Dealer, Boxer, Craftsperson, Entertainer, Farmer, Gambler, Journalist, Librarian, Museum Curator, Musician, Occultist, Pharmacist, Prostitute, Shopkeeper, Spy, Undertaker, Zookeeper |
| 3 | Professional | 30–70% | Accountant, Antiquarian, Architect, Clergy, Doctor, Explorer, Lawyer, Military Officer, Professor, Researcher |
| 4 | Gentry/Nouveau Riche | 50–90% | Gentleman, Gentlewoman, Nouveau Riche |
| 5 | Aristocracy | 90–100% | Not routinely available as a starting occupation. Rename Gentleman/Gentlewoman to Nobleman/Noblewoman with CR 90–100% |

Notes:
- Butler/Valet/Maid CR is capped at 40% even though it sits in Band 1 (reflects the elevated status of senior household staff).
- Band 2 artistic occupations (Actor, Musician, etc.) may raise CR up to 70% with Keeper permission.

## How to Use This

1. **Occupations without a Regency stat block** use the nearest equivalent
   in the base `occupations.md` file, substituting period-appropriate skills
   with Keeper approval. For example, Doctor uses the base Doctor of Medicine
   stat block; Driver (Carriage) adapts Farmer/Rancher (dropping Drive Auto,
   adding Ride and Animal Handling).

2. **Hobby careers** (marked \*) can be taken as secondary pursuits by a
   Gentleman or Gentlewoman character. Use that occupation's skill list but
   keep the Gentleman/Gentlewoman CR band (50–90%).

3. **Gentry-eligible occupations** (marked **bold**) are those considered
   respectable for people of quality. A Military Officer or Antiquarian fits
   naturally in Regency society; a Criminal or Prostitute does not, even if
   the character holds a high CR.

4. **Personal interest points** (INT×2) go to any non-Cthulhu Mythos skill
   as normal — use them to layer on period flavor (Dancing, Etiquette,
   Fashion, Ride) regardless of occupation.
