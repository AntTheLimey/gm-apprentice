## Cornelius Waite Drabble

**Concept:** A respected antiquarian dealer whose sanity has been quietly
dissolving for three years — ever since he translated a margin note in a
folio he should never have purchased.

**Appearance:** Late fifties, gaunt, with the yellow complexion of a man
who barely sleeps. His suits were once expensive; now they are pressed
obsessively but worn at the elbows and collar. He wears white cotton
gloves indoors at all times, claiming it's to protect old paper. His shop
reeks of cedar oil and something medicinal he drinks from a flask.
One eye twitches when he hears a door open unexpectedly.

**Portrayal:** Speaks very quietly and precisely, as though choosing
each word to minimise the information it carries. He answers questions
with questions. When genuinely frightened he switches to rapid, run-on
sentences that circle a topic without landing on it. Physically, he
positions himself with his back to the wall and one eye on exits.

**Voice:** Drops his voice to near-silence for the most alarming statements
— the opposite of what you'd expect. "Oh, that — yes, that was in the
Hansley collection —" *leans in, barely audible* "— you should give it
back."

### AIMS

**Agenda**
- *Immediate:* Find out how much the investigators already know. If they
  know too little, he will not tell them anything. If they know too much,
  he will try to warn them off — for their own sake, he tells himself.
- *Short-term:* Sell or destroy a specific item in his locked back room
  before it draws further attention. He has already turned away two
  buyers he cannot identify.
- *Long-term:* Reach a state he calls "untangled" — having passed on or
  destroyed every artefact in his collection that has a connection to the
  Mythos. He does not believe he will survive the process.

**Instinct**
Freeze, then fawn. His first response to threat is stillness and silence;
if pressed he becomes helpful to the point of self-erasure, agreeing with
everything until he can find an exit. He does not fight and does not run
— both draw attention.

**Moves (if investigators don't intervene)**
1. *This week:* Approaches a fence he trusts to quietly sell the locked
   item to a private collector overseas — someone anonymous, someone far
   away.
2. *Next fortnight:* If the sale falls through, he burns the item in his
   back yard and takes a week in a seaside hotel to see if anything
   follows the smoke.
3. *If the cult finds him first:* Disappears from the shop. Neighbours
   report seeing him load two suitcases into a cab at 3 a.m.

### Secrets

**Surface** *(casual interaction)*
Clearly nervous about something, but dismisses it as overwork and
"the usual nonsense of the trade — forgeries, disputes over provenance."
The white gloves never come off indoors.

**Investigation** *(Library Use / Psychology on a Hard roll or
Spot Hidden in the shop)*
The locked back room contains one item that is not catalogued anywhere
in his stock records. It arrived eighteen months ago in a lot from
a deceased estate and he has not been able to bring himself to appraise
it properly. He has made three attempts. After each one he burned the
notes he made.

**Deep** *(find his private journal / Persuade on an Extreme roll /
confrontation at low SAN moment)*
Three years ago he translated a seven-word marginal annotation in a
Latin folio — a directory entry, essentially, for a ritual. He has no
idea what the ritual does. He never completed the translation. The
annotation itself was in a script he could not identify, and he read it
phonetically, and now he hears those seven syllables in the sound of
running water. He has a Cthulhu Mythos score of 12 and he knows exactly
what that means for the ceiling on his sanity.

### Relationships

- **The investigators:** Cautious potential ally. Will trade scraps of
  information for evidence that they are competent and discreet. Will
  not trade anything that makes his situation worse.
- **Margaret Tulse (his assistant, mid-twenties):** He trusts her with
  the shop's routine business and nothing else. He is terrified she will
  touch the wrong thing. He has considered dismissing her for her own
  protection and cannot bring himself to do it.
- **The two unidentified buyers:** Men who contacted him by letter,
  then in person. Both knew the item was in his back room without being
  told. He does not know their names. One left a calling card with
  nothing printed on it.
- **Professor Elaine Harwick (Miskatonic):** Former colleague, estranged.
  She is the only person he believes might understand what the margin
  note means. He has not contacted her because he does not want her to
  become involved. He carries her address in his wallet.

### GM Notes

**Running Drabble at the table**

He is at his best as an information broker who gives everything in the
wrong order. He will confirm a detail the investigators have already
discovered before he will volunteer a new one. This makes him feel
believable rather than convenient: the players piece him together
rather than receiving a briefing.

His SAN of 38 is load-bearing. If investigators push him hard — if they
show him something Mythos-adjacent, or if combat breaks out in the shop
— consider calling for a SAN roll (0/1D4 for witnessing violence at his
age; higher for anything supernatural). A failed roll produces a bout
of temporary madness: he locks himself in the back room and refuses to
speak, which can itself become a puzzle.

**The locked item**

Its nature is left deliberately open for the Keeper. Suggestions that
preserve mystery without over-defining it:
- A bronze hand mirror whose reflection shows the room as it will look
  in one hour
- A folio of woodcuts whose figures have been annotated in a hand that
  matches no known printer
- A set of weights and measures in an unidentifiable metal, calibrated
  to a unit that does not correspond to any historical system

Whatever it is, it should be something that *could* be explained as
a curiosity — right up until it isn't.

**Escalation hooks**

- If investigators buy or take the locked item, the two unidentified
  buyers turn their attention to the investigators instead. Drabble
  is briefly relieved, then horrified on their behalf.
- If Drabble is killed before he can warn the investigators about the
  item, Margaret Tulse finds his private journal and contacts them.
  She knows nothing about what it contains. She just knows he wrote in
  it every night for three years and stopped twelve days ago.
- If the investigators introduce Drabble to Professor Harwick, the
  reunion scene should be uncomfortable — she knows more than he
  admitted to her, and she knows what the seven syllables mean.
