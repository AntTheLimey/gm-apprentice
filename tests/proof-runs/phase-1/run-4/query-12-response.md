**Previously on The Ashford Case:**

The investigators came to Kingsport at the request of Dr. Albert
Wilmarth to find his colleague, Professor Henry Ashford — a
respected antiquarian who had gone missing after a frightening
change in behavior. At Ashford's ransacked study, Mal Bridges
found a coded letter hidden in a false drawer; Rev. Marsh decoded
it using his knowledge of obscure ciphers, revealing that Ashford
had discovered a hidden chamber beneath "the old church" where
a secret society, the [[Order of the Silver Twilight]], planned
a summoning ritual before the new moon.

Following the letter's clues to the docks, the investigators
staked out Warehouse 7 and encountered [[Brother Ezra]] — a
gaunt, unsettling figure who moved with inhuman speed. A chase
through the fog-shrouded waterfront ended with the investigators
escaping, shaken, but in possession of two stone tablets carved
with the same eye-within-crescent symbol that had been haunting
the investigation. Dr. Voss saw Ezra's face clearly and lost
three points of Sanity to what she witnessed. Mal took a minor
bruise. Rev. Marsh gained Cthulhu Mythos points from examining
the tablets.

Ezra now knows the investigators' faces.

*(Assumed from Session 3 intended outcome: The investigators
identified St Anselm's Church as the location of the inner
sanctum, descended into the hidden chambers, and confronted the
cult before or during its ritual preparations. Ashford was found

1. **[[Professor Ashford]]'s condition** — Even if rescued,
   Ashford is severely mentally compromised (SAN ~15, extended

   can speak, but he is not the man his colleagues knew. His

   [[Order of the Silver Twilight]] is fractured but not

   chancellor, the mayor, another trusted figure) may retaliate,
   flee, or accelerate a backup plan.

3. **Brother Ezra's binding renewal** — His binding ritual must
   be renewed on November 1st. If the investigators disrupted
   the October 20th ceremony, Ezra's powers may be degrading —
   but he has eleven days before the deadline. He is still

   taking bribes to ignore dock activity. The investigators

   this is still outstanding. It may have significance.

8. **The ritual's partial completion** — If the ritual was
   interrupted rather than prevented, something may have been
   partially summoned or weakened in the dimensional barrier
   beneath the church. Environmental signs may linger: unusual
   cold, wrong-shaped shadows, objects displaced.

### Watch (stale thread risk — not yet 3 sessions but tracking)

9. **Dr. Wilmarth's standing request** — Ashford's Miskatonic
   colleague who hired the investigators has heard nothing.
   How does the academic world learn what happened?

## Planned Scenes

*These scenes are designed for the post-Session 3 aftermath context.
They assume the ritual was disrupted and Ashford was recovered but broken.*

---

### Scene 1: The Reckoning (Aftermath and Stabilization)

**Type:** Social / investigation hybrid
**Objective:** Establish the immediate post-event state: where is Ashford, how
are the investigators, what does Kingsport know?
**Entities:** [[Professor Ashford]], [[Dr. Voss]], [[Rev. Marsh]],
[[Kingsport Docks]] area or a local boarding house
**Setup:** The investigators are somewhere safe — or as safe as Kingsport
gets. Ashford is with them, barely coherent. He keeps murmuring passages
in a language that isn't Latin. His eyes fix on empty corners of the room.

**Branching:**
- Voss can attempt a clinical assessment (Medicine roll) — success reveals
  his SAN loss is severe but he is not immediately dangerous to himself
  or others. Hard success reveals he is hovering around a triggering
  threshold and needs careful handling.
- Marsh can try to reach him on a personal level. If successful (Persuade
  or Psychology), Ashford surfaces briefly — enough to confirm one key
  fact about the Order (GM's choice from Ashford's secrets: who the Grand
  Master is, what is in the pit, whether the ritual can be re-attempted).
  Then he slips back. This moment costs Marsh 0/1 SAN.
- Mal is assessing the situation tactically: does anyone know they were
  at the church? Did anyone see them leave? He can attempt a Streetwise
  or Spot Hidden check to determine if they were followed.

**Voss B-plot moment:** She is the medical authority here. The question of
whether to bring Ashford to a proper facility — with all the documentation
and exposure that entails — surfaces for the first time. No resolution
required yet. Just the first pull.

### Scene 2: The Statement (Medical Ethics and Institutional Pressure)

**Type:** Social / investigation
**Objective:** Dr. Voss's B-plot crisis: the question of what gets written
down, who sees it, and what it means for Ashford's future.
**Entities:** [[Dr. Voss]], [[Professor Ashford]], Dr. Wilmarth (via
letter or telephone), [[Mrs Whitmore]]
**Setup:** If the investigators contact Dr. Wilmarth (which is expected —
he hired them), he will want to come to Kingsport immediately. He's
alarmed but not aware of the full scope. He will ask for a medical
assessment. Voss has to decide what she tells him.

**Branching:**
- Tell Wilmarth the full truth → He is shaken but becomes a genuine ally.
  He can access Miskatonic resources, can provide cover for Ashford's
  "nervous breakdown," and has the institutional authority to take
  responsibility for the Revelations. Risk: if the Grand Master has
  academic connections, Wilmarth may be watched.
- Sanitized account → Buys time. Ashford goes to a private sanatorium
  under Voss's care. No exposure of the Order yet. Risk: the story
  will be harder to control as things develop.
- Mrs Whitmore arrives to check on Ashford. Her presence grounds the
  scene. She produces the silver medallion if the investigators haven't
  received it yet. Her grief at seeing Ashford's state is a quiet gut-punch.

**Moral dilemma (B-plot touchpoint for Voss):** Ashford can describe
what happened to him in fragments that would be devastating as formal
testimony. Getting that testimony on record would expose the Order. But
a formal psychiatric statement also risks Ashford being committed,
and his testimony would invite scrutiny of the whole investigation.
Does Voss protect her patient or protect the investigation?

---

### Scene 3: The Bribe (Confronting Crane)

**Type:** Social / investigation
**Objective:** Bring [[Inspector Crane]] to account — or use him.
**Entities:** [[Inspector Crane]], [[Mal Bridges]], possibly the desk
sergeant and/or state police contact
**Setup:** Mal has known since Session 1 that Crane was dragging his feet.
Now the investigation has established why — the Order's monthly envelope.
Mal can arrange a private meeting with Crane: in his office, at a neutral
location, or by ambush.

**Branching:**
- Confront Crane with evidence → Hard Psychology roll to read him.
  Crane's guilt is real. If the investigators have the bribery evidence
  (cash envelopes, the file of ignored dock incidents), Crane can be
  turned. A successful Persuade roll (modified by evidence quality) flips
  him from obstacle to reluctant ally. He can provide: the names of Order
  outer-circle members who attended lodge meetings on record, a formal
  statement about the dock activity he was paid to ignore, and police
  protection for Ashford at the hospital.
- Expose Crane without approach → Go to the desk sergeant or state police
  directly. Crane is investigated. He cooperates quickly to avoid
  prosecution. Result: same information, but Crane is destroyed (three
  years from retirement, finished). He is also more likely to be
  thorough in his cooperation because he has nothing left to protect.
- Use Crane → Mal keeps the bribery information as leverage. Crane remains
  in place, now working for the investigators informally. Dangerous —
  Crane is weak, and weak people under pressure become unpredictable.

**C-plot touchpoint for Mal:** Whatever he decides about Crane, the moment
forces him to articulate what kind of investigator he is. The Pinkerton
who sold out informants, or the one who didn't?

**Embedded beat:** At some point during or after the Crane scene, Mal
should be able to write or phone Ashford's sister. She hired him. She
deserves to know her brother is alive, even if Mal hasn't figured out
yet how to explain the rest.

---

### Scene 4: The Question of the Texts

**Type:** Investigation / roleplay
**Objective:** Force a group decision about the forbidden materials.
**Entities:** All PCs, [[Professor Ashford]] (if lucid enough to have an
opinion), Dr. Wilmarth
**Setup:** In the investigators' possession: two stone tablets, Ashford's
translation notes, and potentially the complete Revelations of Glaaki
(if they brought it out of the sanctum). This material is evidence, a
weapon, a temptation, and a hazard.

**The decision:**
- **Destroy everything** → No more Mythos exposure risk. The evidence
  that would have proven the Order's crimes is gone. The Grand Master,
  if he surfaces, has nothing to recover. Permanent — and each PC must
  confirm it, because Marsh and Voss will have different reactions.
- **Preserve and secure** → Somewhere safe. Where? The investigators
  must name a location. There is no fully safe location. Whatever they
  choose can be found.
- **Deliver to Miskatonic** → Wilmarth is the natural recipient. He is
  a genuine scholar who will treat the material seriously. Risk: he
  becomes a target. Also: the Grand Master may be part of the Miskatonic
  community.
- **Use as evidence** → Present the tablets and translation notes to
  the state police or a journalist. This is the most exposed option and
  the one most likely to produce immediate legal action against the Order.

**Ashford's voice:** If Rev. Marsh or Dr. Voss has made any contact
with Ashford, he may have a lucid moment during this scene — enough
to cast a vote. He whispers "burn it." Then: "No. Don't. God help me,
don't." His inability to be consistent is its own tragedy.

### Contingency Scene A: Brother Ezra's Return

**Trigger:** If the investigators remain in Kingsport without moving
quickly to resolve the Crane situation and secure Ashford, or if Mal's
check in Scene 1 reveals they were followed.

**Type:** Horror / chase
**Objective:** Ezra has not given up. He comes for Ashford — not the
investigators specifically, but he will go through them.
**Setup:** Night. The investigators' location has been compromised.
Ezra arrives alone — he doesn't bring cultists, because there are
barely any left. This makes him more dangerous, not less.

**Branching:**
- His binding is degrading (November 1 approaches). He is still faster
  than a normal human but no longer operating at Session 2 levels.
  DEX 85 → 70, Move 12 → 10. Perceptive investigators may notice he
  seems slightly less sharp.
- Environmental advantages available: fire (his unnaturally tough skin
  does not protect against fire), the ritual circle symbols (if the
  investigators took rubbings or photographs of the floor inscriptions,
  Rev. Marsh may know something that can stop him temporarily), or
  simply light and noise in a place where Crane can be forced to respond.
- SAN check on encountering him: 0/1d4 (as established in his file).
  Dr. Voss has already had this experience — she knows what she saw.
  This makes the encounter different for her: not new horror but
  confirmed horror.

**Escape or subdue:** Killing Ezra is possible but very difficult in
a fair fight. His HP is 15, but with armor and his speed, a direct
confrontation is likely to cost someone. Environmental trapping,
fire, or using the degraded binding against him are all viable.

---

### Contingency Scene B: The Grand Master Revealed

**Trigger:** If the investigators follow the thread of the inner circle's
identity — whether through Crane's information, records from the
lodge, or Ashford's testimony.

**Type:** Social / revelation
**Objective:** The Grand Master's identity lands as a dramatic reveal.

**GM Notes for naming the Grand Master:**
The Order of the Silver Twilight file suggests: the university
chancellor, the mayor, or another trusted figure. The most dramatically
satisfying choice is someone the investigators have interacted with
and trusted. Options from the vault:

- **Dr. Albert Wilmarth** — Ashford's own colleague who hired the
  investigators. This makes the revelation devastating: he was watching
  everything from the start. Wilmarth is named in connection with
  Ashford but never given a full vault file. This could be him.
  (High impact, recontextualizes Sessions 1-3 entirely.)
- **The mayor or a prominent Kingsport businessman** — Less personal
  but gives the Order political protection and a clear motive for Crane's
  compliance. Easier to expose and easier to arrest.
- **Judge Marcus Abernethy** — Named in the Order's inner circle as
  providing legal protection. He hasn't appeared in play but could be
  introduced in Session 4 as the face the investigators finally put to
  the organization.

**Whatever the identity:** The reveal should happen through evidence
and deduction, not a villain monologue. The investigators piece it
together. The GM confirms it through the reaction of other NPCs when
they confront or name the person.

## Session End Objectives

Good stopping points for Session 4, in order of dramatic strength:

1. **Strong ending:** The investigators secure Ashford, expose Crane,
   identify the Grand Master, and make a decision about the texts —
   then face the consequence of one of those decisions, whether Ezra
   arriving or the Grand Master going to ground or Wilmarth reacting
   to the truth.

2. **Solid ending:** Ashford is secured. Crane is confronted. The Grand
   Master is named but not yet confronted. The texts are in limbo.
   Strong cliffhanger: the investigators realize the Grand Master knows
   they know.

3. **Minimum viable:** Ashford is stabilized and the investigators have
   a clear next step toward either Crane or the Grand Master. SAN
   costs have been paid. The threat is contained but not resolved.

## Audit Notes

### Agency Audit

**Reviewed scenes for agency violations:**

- Scene 1 (Reckoning): No PC actions assumed. Ashford's behavior is
  described as observed (he murmurs, his eyes fix on corners) without
  directing PC responses. Branches are choice-based. Clean.

- Scene 2 (Statement): The moral dilemma is presented as a choice with
  genuine multiple paths. Wilmarth's arrival is contingent (investigators
  contact him) not forced. Clean.

- Scene 3 (Crane): Multiple approaches available with different
  consequences. Crane's response is contingent on evidence and roll
  outcomes, not predetermined. Clean.

- Scene 4 (Texts): All options carry real weight without a predetermined
  "right answer." Ashford's confused response adds pathos but does not
  direct the PCs. Clean.

- Contingency A (Ezra): Scene has ≥2 contingencies for resolution
  (environmental, escape, confront). No PC emotions assumed. Clean.

- Contingency B (Grand Master): Designed around evidence-gathering and
  NPC reactions, not a scripted reveal scene. Clean.

**One flag:** The Grand Master choice (Dr. Wilmarth or another figure)
