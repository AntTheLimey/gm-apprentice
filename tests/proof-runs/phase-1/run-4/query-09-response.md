# Score: "The Coalridge Tithe"

**Type:** Robbery / Assault hybrid  
**Target:** The Billhooks — Tier II, Strong. Brutal gang in Coalridge; labor rackets and a butchery front.  
**District:** Coalridge  
**Score Size:** Standard (6 coin potential)

---

## The Opportunity

The Billhooks collect their weekly labor tithe every Graywheel night — enforcers led by Coran (Tarvul's eldest son) make the rounds through the factory district, shaking down foremen and collecting cash from the shops that operate under Billhooks "protection." The route ends at a converted slaughterhouse on Smelt Row, where the coin is counted and secured in an iron lockbox before Tarvul's own men transport it to the gang's stronghold at dawn.

A desperate dockworker named **Hollis** — formerly muscle for the Dockers, now freelancing — has watched the route for three weeks. He'll sell the information for two coin and a guarantee his name stays clean. The window is narrow: if the crew waits past Graywheel, Tarvul changes the route after an anonymous tip (a danger clock already ticking).

**The Job:** intercept the tithe before it reaches the slaughterhouse, relieve Coran's escort of the lockbox, and disappear into the industrial fog before the Billhooks know who hit them.

---

## Score Details

**Potential Payoff:** 6 coin (standard score vs. Tier II target)  
**Rep:** 2 base +1 (Billhooks are same Tier as most new crews, no modifier; +1 if crew is Tier I)  
**Exposure (base heat):** 4 — loud, chaotic if the escort fights; 2 if the crew keeps it clean

**Engagement Roll modifiers to suggest:**

| Factor | Mod |
|--------|:---:|
| Bold / direct assault approach | +1d |
| Hollis's inside info on the route | +1d |
| Billhooks Tier II vs typical starting crew Tier I | +1d |
| Coran is a vicious fighter; escort is six strong | -1d |
| Hostile turf (Coalridge is Billhooks territory) | -1d |

**Starting pool:** 1d base + likely net +1d = **2d** engagement roll for a standard assault approach.

---

## Key NPCs

**Coran** — Tarvul's eldest son. Mid-20s, heavy-built, scarred jaw. Enjoys the work. Carries a hook-bladed cleaver at his hip (Billhooks signature weapon). He won't run — he'll call the crew's bluff and attack if they show a moment of hesitation. Goal during the score: protect the coin and make an example.  
*Position if cornered: will try to create distance and yell for backup rather than die for coin.*

**Bryl** — Coran's younger brother, not on this escort but knows the route. If Coran is humiliated, Bryl will take it personally and start asking questions in Coalridge about who was on Smelt Row. This is a slow-burn clock, not an immediate threat.

**Hollis** — The informant. Nervous, grateful, has a family in Charhollow. If the crew burns him or the Billhooks connect him to the job, he disappears (and the crew loses a potential future contact in the docks labor network).

**Marta** — A foreman's wife who witnesses the ambush from an upper-floor window. She's not loyal to the Billhooks but she's not brave either. She will talk to whoever frightens her most afterward.

---

## Location: Smelt Row, Coalridge

**Atmosphere:** Choking, industrial, lightless except for the orange glow of furnace vents.

**Sensory:**
- Sight: Soot-blackened brick walls, puddles of rust-colored run-off, electroplasmic streetlamps spaced far apart — pools of cold blue light with long shadows between.
- Sound: The distant rhythm of a night-shift forge; somewhere above, a ventilation wheel squeals on a rusted axle.
- Smell/Touch: Warm metal and old blood from the slaughterhouse. The air is damp and slightly sweet in a way that turns the stomach.

**Key Features:**
- *The Choke Point* — a narrow alley where Smelt Row bends between two factories. One way in, one way out. Ideal ambush site; also a trap if backup arrives.
- *The Loading Bay* — wide double doors on the slaughterhouse's street face. Two Billhooks guards wait here for the escort. If the attack happens too close, those guards will hear it.
- *The Iron Bridge* — a short footbridge over a run-off canal. Cutting the escort here means any chase goes into an unfamiliar industrial maze — good for the crew, bad for Coran.

---

## Clocks

**"Coran's Escort Overwhelmed"** — 4-clock (Progress). Fill to neutralize the escort and seize the lockbox.

**"Alarm Reaches the Slaughterhouse"** — 6-clock (Danger). Ticks on loud rolls, failed Prowl checks, bad consequences. Fills = Billhooks backup arrives mid-score. At 4 segments: the guards at the loading bay move to investigate.

**"Bryl Learns Who Did This"** — 8-clock (Faction, hidden). Ticks between sessions. When full: Billhooks have a name, faction status drops to -2 (Hostile) if not already.

---

## Plan Approaches

**Assault** — Hit the escort on the Iron Bridge. Controlled start if the crew surprises them; risky if Coran spots the setup. Heat: 4–6 depending on how messy.

**Deception** — Pose as Bluecoats or inspectors conducting a random stop. Gets the escort to lower weapons before the grab. +1d engagement; detail: forged papers or a uniform cohort. Heat: 2 if nobody dies.

**Stealth** — Shadow the route and cut the coin purse before it reaches Coran's hands — intercept a courier earlier in the evening before the escort consolidates. Quieter, smaller take (4 coin, minor score), but heat drops to 0.

---

## Complications (draw from these as consequences)

1. The lockbox is chained to Coran's wrist. Cutting it free is a desperate Finesse roll — or someone has to deal with the wrist.
2. Hollis showed up to watch. He panics when the violence starts and knocks over a stack of slag buckets — **tick Alarm clock 2**.
3. The lockbox contains less coin than expected (4 instead of 6) — Tarvul skimmed before the route, or diverted some to Ironhook Prison for a payoff.
4. Marta's window opens. She looks down. She makes eye contact with one of the crew.
5. One of the escort is a young kid — can't be more than fifteen — handed a hook and told to look mean. What does the crew do?

---

## Downtime Consequences

- **If the crew was quiet:** +1 status with any faction that resents the Billhooks (potentially the Lampblacks, or labor groups in Charhollow).
- **If the crew was loud:** Billhooks status drops 1 immediately. Tarvul starts a **"Find the crew" 6-clock** (Faction).
- **If Hollis is burned:** lose a future contact asset. Tick the Bryl clock +2.
- **If Coran is killed:** Tarvul escalates immediately. **"Find the crew" becomes a 4-clock**. Bryl clock irrelevant — Tarvul is personally hunting them.

---

## Sequel Hooks

- Tarvul's lockbox contains a note referencing a deal with the **Bluecoats** — someone in the watch is on the Billhooks' payroll and tipping them to rival crew activity.
- Hollis, if protected, can point the crew toward a Billhooks stash in Dunslough — a flooded storefront used to stage stolen goods from the factory rackets.
- Bryl, if he learns the crew's name, could be turned — he's tired of living under Tarvul's thumb and might trade information for a way out of Coalridge.
