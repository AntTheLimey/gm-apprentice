## Edvard Voss — The Reluctant Witness

**Concept:** A mid-level functionary who knows something incriminating and is desperately trying to look like he doesn't.

**Appearance:** Heavy-set man, fifties, with a clerk's soft hands and a new bruise on his jaw he keeps touching. His collar is buttoned wrong — one hole off — like he dressed in a hurry.

**Portrayal:** Never answers the first question asked. Pivots to a different subject, then circles back with a too-casual "oh, and about that..." Keeps checking the nearest exit.

**Voice:** Over-explains unimportant details ("I was at the market — the one on the south side, not the new one, the fish smell there is unbearable —") then goes completely flat when anything relevant comes up: "I don't know anything about that."

---

### AIMS Profile

**Agenda**
- *Immediate:* Get the investigators out of his space without revealing what he saw.
- *Short-term:* Convince the person he is afraid of that he kept his mouth shut.
- *Long-term:* Get out of the city before whatever he witnessed catches up to him.

**Instinct:** Fawn first, then freeze. He does not fight and he does not run — he smiles and apologises and tells you what you want to hear, which is not the same as the truth.

**Moves (fires if PCs don't act):**
1. Sends a note to the person he fears, telling them investigators are asking questions.
2. Makes arrangements to leave — withdraws savings, gives away his cat.
3. Disappears from his usual haunts. After this, finding him requires investigation.

**Secrets**
- *Surface:* Something is clearly wrong — the bruise, the nervous energy, the non-answers.
- *Investigation:* He witnessed a meeting between two people who aren't supposed to know each other. He was paid to forget about it. The bruise is a reminder.
- *Deep:* He didn't just witness it — he was supposed to deliver a message and read it first. He knows the contents. He has a copy hidden somewhere.

---

### Relationships

- **The Person He Fears:** employer or power-holder; gave him the bruise; holds something over him (debt, family, past crime).
- **His Upstairs Neighbour:** the one person he trusts; if the PCs are smart they find her first — she noticed his change in behaviour and is quietly worried.
- **The Investigators (potential):** he wants to trust them, and that tension is playable — push too hard and he shuts down; give him a reason to feel safe and he cracks.

### GM Notes — Running Edvard at the Table

**The core beat:** Edvard is not evil. He is afraid. The investigators should feel the tragedy of a person grinding himself down trying to protect a secret that is protecting someone who doesn't deserve it.

**Pressure dial:**
- Low pressure → he stonewalls pleasantly. Very helpful about everything except the thing that matters. Offers tea.
- Medium pressure → cracks appear. He catches himself, goes pale, over-corrects. The bruise gets touched a lot.
- High pressure → one of two outcomes: (a) he breaks and tells them everything in a rush, making them promise three times to keep him safe, or (b) he bolts and they have to find him later in worse circumstances.

**The copy of the message:** this is your plant for a later scene. If the investigators search his lodgings (with or without permission), they can find it. Don't volunteer this — let them earn it.

**If the investigators protect him:** he becomes a reliable minor ally. He knows the administrative underbelly of wherever this is set — permits, ledgers, who-knows-who. Useful.

**If they push too hard and he runs:** that's a scene. He leads them somewhere he thinks is safe. It isn't.
