# CHAN WAI-KEUNG
**Hong Kong Martial Arts Instructor / Bodyguard** — 200 points
TL8 | Modern Realistic | Disadvantage limit -50 pts | Quirk limit -5 pts

*Chan Wai-Keung spent fifteen years studying Wing Chun under Sifu Lam
in Kowloon before opening his own school in Mong Kok. He funds the
school by taking private bodyguard contracts through a discreet agency
that serves Hong Kong's business elite. Patient, professional, and
dangerously efficient at close range.*

## Background Notes

**Style — Wing Chun (Ving Tsun)**: Chan's martial art is a close-range
striking style emphasising centreline control, economy of motion, and
simultaneous attack-and-defence. Mechanically this is represented by
Karate (striking/parrying) and Judo (trapping, grappling, and sweeping),
with techniques reflecting Wing Chun's signature tools: the elbow, the
choke, and precise targeting of the face and throat.

**Trained by a Master**: Sifu Lam Chun-Fai, now retired to Shenzhen,
trained Chan intensively for fifteen years. This is a cinematic advantage
and requires GM approval in a realistic campaign. The GM may rule it
represents exceptionally high-quality training rather than superhuman
ability — the mechanical effect (halved Rapid Strike penalties) reflects
a master's efficiency of movement rather than movie physics.

**Bodyguard Role**: Observation, Tactics, Body Language, and Driving
cover the professional core of close-protection work. Intimidation and
Savoir-Faire reflect the need to deter threats verbally and operate in
elite social environments without drawing attention. Stealth supports
advance reconnaissance of venues.

**Enemy**: A Triad enforcer named Bak "White Dragon" Siu-Lung blames
Chan for the death of a gang associate during a protection job two years
ago. Bak appears occasionally (9 or less) with a small crew — equal in
combat capability to Chan — to settle the score.

**Duty**: Chan's agency places him on 24-hour call (12 or less) for
active contracts. Bodyguard work is inherently dangerous (Extremely
Hazardous), doubling the base value to -20.
