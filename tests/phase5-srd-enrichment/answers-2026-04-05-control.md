## CoC/BRP

### Q1: Dodge base chance
Dodge starts at half the investigator's DEX stat. So DEX 60 = 30% base Dodge. It's a combat reaction skill, pushed only in specific circumstances, and improves through experience like other skills.

### Q2: 1920s doctor NPC
Dr. Eleanor Marsh, 38, GP turned coroner's consultant. STR 45, DEX 50, INT 75, POW 60, CON 55, APP 65, EDU 80, SAN 55, HP 10. Skills: Medicine 70%, First Aid 65%, Science (Biology) 55%, Spot Hidden 50%, Library Use 60%, Persuade 55%. Motivation: obsessive truth-seeker, dismisses the supernatural — until she can't.

### Q3: Innsmouth hook
Innsmouth is a decaying Massachusetts fishing town reeking of brine and rot. Its residents have a distinctive "Innsmouth look" — bulging eyes, grey skin, stooped gait. Streets are too quiet; curtains twitch. Investigators might be drawn by: a missing relative who visited, an inheritance tied to the Marsh family, or rumours of pre-Columbian gold jewellery surfacing at auction. The horror is gradual — something is wrong before you can name it. The Esoteric Order of Dagon controls everything. Nobody leaves easily.

### Q4: Archaeologist profession
Core: Archaeology 60%+, History 50%+, Library Use 50%+, Spot Hidden 40%+. Support: Navigate 40%, Climb 40%, First Aid 35%, one Language (Ancient) 40%+. Recommended bonuses: Appraise and Persuade for academic/social encounters. Focus personal interest skills on a regional speciality (Egypt, Mesoamerica, etc.) for flavour and scenario hooks. Archaeology opens mythos tomes naturally — keep SAN topped up early.

### Q5: Dockside creature
The Deep One is perfect — amphibious, intelligent, and culturally embedded in port communities. They lurk beneath piers, recruit through cult entanglement and interbreeding. Alternatively, a Dimensional Shambler fits a claustrophobic warehouse scene: it phases through walls, STR 80, leaves investigators with nowhere to run. For atmosphere before combat, use a Star Vampire — invisible until it feeds, suddenly a screaming crimson shape. Deep Ones reward slow dread; Shamblers reward sudden panic.

## FitD

### Q1: Bluecoats tier
The Bluecoats are Tier III — a significant municipal force, well-resourced and connected to city government, but still beneath the Tier IV/V powers like the Unseen or Immortal Emperor's apparatus.

### Q2: Skovlander fence
Runa Ashveld. Broad-shouldered woman, burn scars up her left arm, always chewing dried licorice root. Deals in looted Skovlan military gear — bayonets, field kits, officer coats — plus forged refugee papers on the side. Quirk: refuses to sell to anyone who can't name a Skovlan city from before the Unity War.

### Q3: Crow's Foot
A dense, crumbling residential district — narrow canals, laundry overhead, gambling dens at street level. Feels like a pressure cooker. The Lampblacks and Red Sashes have been grinding each other down in a low-level gang war for years, with the Bluecoats content to let them. Crow's Foot is excellent for scores that require escalating faction entanglement — whatever you do, you'll step on someone's turf.

### Q4: Con artist playbook
The Slide is the con artist playbook. Key abilities: Temptation — compel someone by appealing to their vice (no roll). Rook's Gambit — take +2d on a deceptive gambit, but suffer level 3 harm on a failure. Like Looking Into a Mirror — detect lies automatically; can't be surprised. A Little Something on the Side — earn extra coin on downtime via a side hustle.

### Q5: Silkshore conflict
The Silkshore red lamp district sits between the Iruvian Consulate (wanting leverage over the pleasure trade for political intelligence) and the Gondoliers (who control waterway access and quietly run extortion on brothels). A score could center on the Consulate hiring the crew to steal blackmail material from a Gondolier-protected madam — forcing the crew to choose which powerful faction they're willing to antagonize long-term.

## D&D 5e

### Q1: Basilisk
Basilisk — CR 3, AC 15 (natural armour). HP 52 (8d8+16). Its Petrifying Gaze is a DC 12 Con save; failure progresses through Restrained to Petrified over two failed saves. Notably, meeting its gaze in a reflection still triggers the save at advantage.

### Q2: CR 3 cult leader
AC 13 (chain shirt), HP 27, Spd 30 ft. Key stats: WIS 16 (+3), CHA 14 (+2). Spells (DC 13): Inflict Wounds, Hold Person, Spiritual Weapon, Blindness/Deafness. Tactics: opens with Hold Person on the biggest threat, drops Spiritual Weapon as a bonus action, stays behind cultist mooks and uses Sacred Flame at range. Uses Healing Word to keep minions up.

### Q3: Ruined temple
Cracked flagstone floors choked with strangler-fig roots. Collapsed columns create difficult terrain. Hazards: unstable ceiling (DC 12 DEX save or 2d6 bludgeoning on triggered tremor), 10-ft pit under rotted wood (DC 14 Perception, 1d6 fall). PCs find a desecrated altar stained with old blood, locked iron coffer (DC 15 Thieves' Tools) containing a spell scroll of Silence, and the lingering smell of burnt offerings — someone was here recently.

### Q4: Warlock summary
Warlocks draw power from a Patron (Fiend, Archfey, Great Old One, etc.). Very few spell slots — typically 2 at low levels — but always at highest available level, and recharge on Short Rest. Bread-and-butter: Eldritch Blast (upgradeable with Invocations). Invocations are passive upgrades. Pact Boon at level 3 customises playstyle (blade, tome, chain). Plays as versatile striker/utility; unique because of short-rest recovery and patron roleplay hook.

### Q5: CR 5 encounter
1 Green Hag (CR 3) + 4 Bandits (CR 1/8) + 1 Bandit Captain (CR 2). Total XP ~1,500 adjusted (Medium-Hard). The Hag uses Illusory Appearance and Invisible Passage to divide the party; the Captain and bandits are enthralled bodyguards. Hag's Horrifying Visage (DC 11 WIS) can panic low-save characters. Works because the hag is a tactical threat without one-shotting PCs, while mooks keep all four players engaged.
