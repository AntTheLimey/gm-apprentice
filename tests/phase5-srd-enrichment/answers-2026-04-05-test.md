## CoC/BRP

### Q1: Dodge base chance
Dodge base is DEX x 2% (BRP) or half DEX in CoC 7e's official character sheet (numerically the same — a DEX of 12 gives Dodge 24%). You choose Dodge OR Parry each round; successive dodges suffer a cumulative -30% penalty. Dodging bullets is Difficult (skill halved) and limited to one per round.

*Source: systems/coc-7e/skills.md and systems/coc-7e/mechanics.md*

### Q2: 1920s doctor NPC
Dr. Eleanor Marsh. Concept: A quietly competent Arkham physician hiding a morphine dependency and a patient who knows too much. Appearance: Grey-streaked hair pinned tightly back, ink-stained cuffs, always smells faintly of carbolic acid. Portrayal: Measured and professional until pressed, then clipped and evasive. Characteristics: STR 9, CON 12, SIZ 10, INT 15, POW 11, DEX 11, APP 13, EDU 17. Derived: HP 11, MP 11, SAN 55, Luck 55, Build 0, MOV 8, DM none. Key Skills: Medicine 70%, First Aid 65%, Persuade 55%, Psychology 50%, Science (Biology) 45%, Library Use 50%, Spot Hidden 40%, Language (Own) 85%, Language (Latin) 30%. AIMS: Immediate — keep her patient sedated and silent; Long-term — maintain her practice's respectability. Secret: One patient witnessed something at the Docks and hasn't been coherent since. She's keeping him sedated rather than reporting it.

*Source: npc-generation.md, systems/coc-7e/professions.md, systems/coc-7e/mechanics.md*

### Q3: Innsmouth hook
Innsmouth is a crumbling Massachusetts seaport whose people bear "the Innsmouth look" — bulging eyes, narrow heads, scaly skin — the result of generations of interbreeding with Deep Ones, amphibious humanoids who dwell beneath nearby Devil Reef. The Esoteric Order of Dagon controls the town. What draws investigators: a missing relative who came here researching family genealogy, a cache of strange gold jewelry traced to local merchants, or a government tip about contraband that goes mysteriously cold. The horror escalates from social unease (the staring townsfolk, the shuttered Gilman House) to existential dread when investigators discover the lineage runs in their own blood.

*Source: systems/coc-7e/setting-lovecraft.md, systems/coc-7e/creatures.md*

### Q4: Archaeologist profession
The BRP professions list doesn't have a dedicated Archaeologist entry, but the Scholar profession is the closest fit (or swap skills with Keeper approval). Core: Knowledge (Archaeology) 60%+, Knowledge (History) 50%, Knowledge (Anthropology) 40%, Language (Other) x2 — field languages, Library Use / Research 50%+, Spot Hidden 45%, Navigate 35%, First Aid 40%, Persuade 40%. Wealth: Average to Affluent. Swap one Language for Science (Geology) if the campaign involves ancient ruins. Knowledge (Occult) is an excellent secondary investment.

*Source: systems/coc-7e/professions.md*

### Q5: Dockside creature
Deep Ones are the ideal fit. Described by Lovecraft as amphibious humanoids with greyish-green skin, white bellies, webbed hands, bulging eyes, and gills, they can emerge from harbour waters at night. They dwell in underwater cities, worship Dagon, and have been interbreeding with coastal humans for generations. The dockside horror angle: they aren't attacking randomly — they've come for a specific hybrid-blooded dockworker whose transformation is nearly complete, or to reclaim a stolen idol. No ORC stat block exists for Deep Ones, but mechanically treat them as strong, amphibious humanoids (STR/CON above average, MOV aquatic 8+, claws 1D6+dm). SAN loss: 0/1D6 by analogy with Ghouls.

*Source: systems/coc-7e/creatures.md, systems/coc-7e/setting-lovecraft.md*

## FitD

### Q1: Bluecoats tier
The Bluecoats are Tier III, Strong hold. They are Doskvol's city watch — corrupt, brutal, and widely on criminal payrolls. Their example faction clock is "Major crackdown on Crow's Foot" (6-clock), making them a direct mechanical pressure point on crews operating in that district.

*Source: systems/fitd/factions.md — Institutions section*

### Q2: Skovlander fence
Halla Brenne — a broad-shouldered woman with close-cropped grey hair, a burn scar along her left jaw, and callused hands she never quite hides. A refugee-turned-trader, she speaks in clipped sentences and never barters twice on the same number. She flinches at Bluecoat bells but never runs. She deals in war surplus: Skovlan military hardware, black-powder weapons the Imperium declared contraband after the Unity War, and the occasional field-stripped ghost-suppression kit. Her quirk: she insists on payment in Skovlan coin stamped before the Occupation — won't explain why. Nightmarket's commercial bustle makes her easy to encounter at a covered stall between the spice merchants and a candle-maker.

*Source: systems/fitd/setting-doskvol.md — Nightmarket district; factions.md world-context*

### Q3: Crow's Foot
Crow's Foot is a poor, gang-contested district — the canonical starting area for a new crew ("lots of opportunity and conflict"). Three factions actively fighting: The Crows (Tier II, Strong) — protection, gambling, smuggling under Lyssa. The Lampblacks (Tier II, Weak) — extortion, arson under Bazso Baz, at war with Red Sashes. The Red Sashes (Tier II, Weak) — Iruvian sword-school turned gang under Mylera Klev. Three Tier II factions in direct conflict creating a three-way pressure environment. The Lampblacks/"Destroy the Red Sashes" 8-clock is a ready-made ticking faction threat.

*Source: systems/fitd/setting-doskvol.md, systems/fitd/factions.md*

### Q4: Con artist playbook
The Slide is the natural fit — "a subtle manipulator and spy" whose XP trigger is addressing a challenge with deception or influence. Starting actions: Sway 2, Consort 1. Key abilities: Cloak & Dagger (+1d in disguise, surprise potency on reveal), Mesmerism (target forgets the interaction), Like Looking into a Mirror (always know when lied to), A Little Something on the Side (+2 stash each downtime), Rook's Gambit (2 stress to roll best action while doing something else). Starting items: fine disguise kit, forged document, trance powder.

*Source: systems/fitd/playbooks.md — Slide section*

### Q5: Silkshore conflict
Silkshore is the vice and entertainment district — "taverns, theaters, brothels." The Fog Hounds (Tier I, Strong) manage illicit trade in the canals; The Hive (Tier IV, Strong) controls the drug/vice economy. Built-in tension: The Hive is running an "Absorb the Fog Hounds" 6-clock. Score options: steal a Hive shipment financing the takeover, burn the Hive's leverage over a Silkshore brothel owner, or disrupt the Hive's relationship with a bribed official. The Gondoliers (Tier III, Strong) add a third layer — they control canal transport.

*Source: systems/fitd/setting-doskvol.md, systems/fitd/factions.md*

## D&D 5e

### Q1: Basilisk
CR 3, AC 15, HP 52, Speed 20 ft. Medium Monstrosity. Its sole action is Bite. The classic threat is its Petrifying Gaze: creatures that meet its eyes must make Constitution saves or begin turning to stone, fully Petrified on a second failed save.

*Source: systems/dnd-5e-2024/monsters.md — CR 3 entry*

### Q2: CR 3 cult leader
Use the Cultist Fanatic stat block as base: Humanoid S, AC 13, HP 44, Pact Blade + Spells. "Brother Varek, Voice of the Ashen Eye" — dark-pact warlock/priest hybrid. CHA-primary; proficient Deception, Persuasion, Religion. Spells: Cantrips — Eldritch Blast, Toll the Dead; 1st — Bane, Command, Inflict Wounds; 2nd — Hold Person, Spiritual Weapon. Tactics: Opens with Hold Person on most dangerous PC. Spiritual Weapon as bonus action. Commands a second target to "kneel." Uses cultist minions as screen. Retreats when below 10 HP, triggering a pre-set dark ritual clock. AIMS: Immediate — sacrifice a captive; Long-term — summon the cult's patron entity.

*Source: systems/dnd-5e-2024/monsters.md, systems/dnd-5e-2024/spells.md, npc-generation.md*

### Q3: Ruined temple
"The Shattered Nave" — Columns lean at broken angles against a sky choked with vines. The altar ahead is split in two, a black stain spreading from its centre. Something skitters in the dark above you. Environment: Cracked floor (Difficult Terrain, DC 12 Acrobatics or fall Prone), collapsed ceiling (obscures vision, Half Cover), defaced mural (DC 14 Religion reveals the original deity). Hazards: Unstable column (push triggers DC 13 STR save, 2d8 bludgeoning + Restrained), poisoned offering bowl (DC 12 Perception, DC 13 CON or Poisoned 1 hour). PCs find: a chained prisoner, cultist ritual journal (three clues), desecrated holy symbol worth 150 gp.

*Source: content-generation.md, systems/dnd-5e-2024/conditions-rules.md*

### Q4: Warlock summary
The Warlock made a pact with a powerful entity for magical power. Primary stat CHA. Pact Magic: very few slots (just 2 at most levels) but recharge on Short Rest. Key features: Eldritch Invocations (permanent passive upgrades), Magical Cunning at level 2 (recover half slots as Magic action), Mystic Arcanum at 11+ (free 6th-9th level casts per Long Rest). How it plays: spam Eldritch Blast as reliable at-will, save slots for control spells (Hold Person, Hypnotic Pattern), Short Rest after every two fights. SRD subclass: Fiend Patron.

*Source: systems/dnd-5e-2024/classes.md — Warlock section*

### Q5: CR 5 encounter
XP budget: ~1,200 Hard, ~1,800 Deadly for 4 level-3 PCs. "The Corrupted Shrine Guard" — 1 Wight (CR 3, 700 XP) + 4 Cultists (CR 1/8, 25 XP each). Adjusted XP ~1,600 (Hard-Deadly). Why: Wight's Life Drain reduces HP maximum creating real jeopardy. Cultists give easy early kills. Wight targets isolated PCs while cultists swarm heaviest-armoured. No regeneration; goes down to fire/radiant cleanly giving divine PCs a spotlight.

*Source: systems/dnd-5e-2024/monsters.md*
