## Character Background

Marcus Chan (陳明峰) grew up in Kowloon, training under a Wing Chun sifu from age
twelve. By his early twenties he had opened his own dojo in Mong Kok while
moonlighting as personal protection for mid-tier business executives and visiting
celebrities. His reputation is strictly professional: he completes the contract,
never asks about the client's business, and keeps his mouth shut.

He cross-trained in Filipino Escrima (Arnis) after a case where a client was
targeted by a knife-wielding attacker — he was embarrassed by the gap in his
arsenal and spent two years fixing it. He still teaches exclusively Wing Chun at
the dojo but carries a folder "for training purposes."

His police contact, Inspector Chan Fai (no relation), is a useful source of
advance warning about persons of interest, but Marcus keeps the relationship
arm's-length — he mistrusts institutional entanglements on principle.

**GM Hooks:**
- The Duty disadvantage means his agency can activate him into hazardous contracts
  without much notice; he doesn't always get to pick his clients
- Overconfidence has gotten him into trouble before — he tends to
  underestimate prepared, armed opposition
- Code of Honor means he can be used against his interests by an unscrupulous
  client who knows he won't break the contract
- His dojo is a recurring social hub that can draw trouble (triad connections
  among students, rival schools, etc.)

## Style Notes (Martial Arts supplement)

**Primary Style: Wing Chun Kung Fu**
The style's characteristic simultaneous attack and defence is reflected in
Karate as the core striking skill. Wing Chun's centreline theory maps well to
the Elbow Strike and close-range grapple techniques. The Style Familiarity perk
(MA49) means Marcus can use Wing Chun-school techniques without the -1 style
unfamiliarity penalty.

**Secondary Style: Escrima / Arnis (Filipino)**
Expressed through the Knife skill with Style Familiarity perk. In Escrima the
same hand positions and techniques apply whether holding a blade, a stick, or
empty-handed — this cross-training reinforces Karate at close range.

**Trained by a Master (B93, MA48):**
This 30-point advantage is the keystone of Marcus's build. At 200 points it
represents the culmination of over two decades of serious, consistent training
under qualified instruction. In mechanical terms it:
1. Allows Karate/Judo to parry weapons without penalty
2. Grants access to the full technique list in the MA supplement
3. Permits cinematic esoteric skill purchases if the campaign ever shifts tone
   (the GM may allow Power Blow, Pressure Points, etc. as future advancement)

**Techniques Purchased:**
- **Kicking** (MA75, Hard): Bought from Karate-2 (default 14) to Karate+0 (16)
  for 2 pts. Reflects Wing Chun's low kicks and Escrima-influenced leg attacks.
- **Choke Hold** (MA69, Hard): Bought from Judo-2 (default 12) to Judo-0 (14)
  for 2 pts. A bodyguard tool — subdue without permanent harm.
