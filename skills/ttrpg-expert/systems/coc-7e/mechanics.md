# Call of Cthulhu 7e — Core Mechanics

## Overview

Lovecraftian horror investigation game built on the Basic
Roleplaying (BRP) percentile system. Published by Chaosium Inc.
Investigators are ordinary people confronting cosmic horrors
beyond human comprehension. The game emphasises investigation,
atmosphere, and the slow erosion of sanity over combat prowess.

## BRP Core Mechanics (ORC License)

The following mechanics are from the Basic Roleplaying:
Universal Game Engine, available under the ORC License from
Chaosium Inc.

### Characteristics

Seven core characteristics, each rated on a 3-18 scale (human
average 10-11). Generated by rolling 3D6 for STR, CON, POW,
DEX, and CHA; 2D6+6 for INT and SIZ. Each characteristic has
an associated characteristic roll equal to the stat multiplied
by 5, used when no specific skill applies.

| Characteristic | Roll | Governs |
|----------------|------|---------|
| STR (Strength) | Effort (STRx5) | Melee damage, lifting, carrying |
| CON (Constitution) | Stamina (CONx5) | Endurance, poison/disease resistance, HP |
| SIZ (Size) | -- | Height/weight/bulk, HP, damage modifier |
| INT (Intelligence) | Idea (INTx5) | Learning, memory, experience bonus |
| POW (Power) | Luck (POWx5) | Willpower, magic aptitude, power points |
| DEX (Dexterity) | Agility (DEXx5) | Initiative, balance, reflexes |
| CHA (Charisma) | Charm (CHAx5) | Likability, first impressions |

The optional Education (EDU) characteristic (rolled 2D6+6)
measures breadth of formal or practical learning and generates
a Knowledge roll (EDUx5). CoC 7e uses APP (Appearance) in
place of CHA, and EDU is standard rather than optional.

### Derived Values

| Value | Formula |
|-------|---------|
| Hit Points | (CON + SIZ) / 2, rounded up |
| Major Wound Level | 1/2 HP, rounded up |
| Power Points | Equal to POW |
| Damage Modifier | STR+SIZ on lookup table (see below) |
| Experience Bonus | 1/2 INT, rounded up |
| Movement (MOV) | 10 units per round (human default) |

**Damage Modifier Table (key ranges):**

| STR+SIZ | Modifier | STR+SIZ | Modifier |
|---------|----------|---------|----------|
| 2-12 | -1D6 | 33-40 | +1D6 |
| 13-16 | -1D4 | 41-56 | +2D6 |
| 17-24 | None | 57-72 | +3D6 |
| 25-32 | +1D4 | Each +16 | +1D6 |

Full damage modifier applies to melee/brawl attacks. Thrown
weapons use half the positive modifier (round up). Firearms
and self-propelled missiles receive no damage modifier.

### Skill System

Skills are rated 0-100%+ on a percentile scale. Each skill
has a base chance representing untrained ability. Skills fall
into six categories: Combat, Communication, Manipulation,
Mental, Perception, and Physical. Skill specialties (e.g.,
Melee Weapon (Sword)) are treated as separate skills.

**Skill proficiency benchmarks:**

| Rating | Level | Meaning |
|--------|-------|---------|
| 00-05% | Novice | Complete amateur |
| 06-25% | Neophyte | Beginner, occasionally succeeds |
| 26-50% | Amateur | Hobby-level, handles routine tasks |
| 51-75% | Professional | Can make a living with the skill |
| 76-90% | Expert | Advanced expertise, respected |
| 91%+ | Master | World-class, near-miraculous |

Training cannot raise a skill above 75%; further improvement
requires successful use under stress (experience checks).

### Resolution System

Roll D100 equal to or under the skill rating to succeed. Five
degrees of outcome:

| Result | Threshold | Effect |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Critical | <= 1/20 of skill | Best possible result; max weapon damage, ignores armor |
| Special | <= 1/5 of skill | Exceptional result; bonus effects (knockback, impale, etc.) |
| Success | <= skill rating | Adequate result |
| Failure | > skill rating | Task not accomplished |
| Fumble | Highest 1/20 of failure range (96-00 typical); 00 always fumbles | Dramatic mishap |

A roll of 01-05 always succeeds for any skill with a base
chance of 5%+. Rolls of 96+ always fail (except resistance
rolls with 10+ point advantage).

**Difficulty Modifiers:**

| Difficulty | Adjustment |
|------------|------------|
| Automatic | No roll needed |
| Easy | Skill doubled |
| Average | No modification |
| Difficult | Skill halved |
| Impossible | No roll, or 01% at GM discretion |

Situational modifiers (equipment, environment, familiarity)
are applied as +/- percentages after difficulty adjustment.

### Resistance Table

Used when characteristics oppose each other (STR vs SIZ to
force a door, POT vs CON for poison, POW vs POW for
willpower contests). The formula:

**Chance = 50% + (active x 5) - (passive x 5)**

Equal values give 50%. Each point of difference shifts the
chance by 5%. A difference of 10+ is automatic success/failure
(GM may allow 01 or 00 to override).

### Opposed Skill Rolls

Three BRP methods for skill-vs-skill contests:

1. **Highest successful roll**: Both roll; highest roll that
   still succeeds wins. Ties broken by higher skill rating.
2. **Subtraction**: Passive skill rolled first; if successful,
   its full value is subtracted from the active skill's rating
   before the active roll.
3. **Resistance table**: Divide both skills by 5 and resolve
   as a single resistance roll.

### Combat Basics

Combat rounds are 12 seconds. Characters act in DEX rank
order (highest first). Each round: Statements, Powers, Action,
Resolution.

**Actions per round (choose one):**
- Attack (melee or ranged)
- Move (up to 30m if only defending)
- Noncombat action (use a skill, draw weapon, etc.)
- Engage or Disengage from melee

**Defenses (reactive, any number per round with penalties):**
- Parry (weapon/shield skill roll; blocks damage)
- Dodge (Dodge skill roll; evades attack entirely)

Each successive parry or dodge after the first takes a
cumulative -30% penalty. A critical parry deflects even a
critical attack; the Attack and Defense Matrix cross-references
attacker and defender success levels to determine the outcome.

Optional hit locations (D20 roll): legs (1-8), abdomen (9-11),
chest (12), arms (13-18), head (19-20).

### Experience and Advancement

When a skill is used successfully in a stressful situation,
mark an experience check. Between adventures, roll D100 for
each checked skill: if the roll exceeds the current skill
rating (plus experience bonus = 1/2 INT), the skill increases
by 1D6 points.

Skills above 100% still improve but require rolling over 100
on the experience roll (experience bonus becomes essential).
POW increases when successfully matched against equal or
higher POW on the resistance table; increase is 1D3-1.

## CoC-Specific Mechanics (beyond BRP)

The following are specific to Call of Cthulhu and are
summarised at the conceptual level.

### Sanity System

Call of Cthulhu's signature mechanic. Sanity tracks mental
stability on a percentile scale (initial value POW x 5, as
in BRP's optional Sanity rule, but extensively developed).
Encountering Mythos entities or phenomena triggers sanity
checks; failed checks reduce current sanity. Exposure to
Mythos knowledge permanently lowers the ceiling on recoverable
sanity. At zero sanity or after a sufficiently large single
loss, the investigator suffers a bout of madness. The system
creates a compelling doom spiral where the most knowledgeable
investigators are also the most fragile.

### Luck Mechanic

CoC 7e treats Luck as a spendable resource (separate from the
BRP Luck characteristic roll). Investigators can spend Luck
points to nudge failed rolls, creating a depleting safety net
that heightens tension as the scenario progresses.

### CoC Skill Emphasis

CoC uses the BRP skill framework but curates a specific skill
list emphasising investigation: Spot Hidden, Library Use,
Listen, Psychology, and others have deliberately low base
values, encouraging specialisation and teamwork among
investigators.
