# Call of Cthulhu 7e -- Skills Reference

Complete CoC 7e skill list with base chances, usage notes, and
specialties. Used during character creation (allocating skill
points) and play (quick lookup of what a skill covers and its
base chance).

CoC 7e uses its own curated skill list rather than the full
BRP generic catalogue. Skills use CoC 7e naming conventions
(e.g., "Archaeology" not "Knowledge (Archaeology)"). The
resolution system uses bonus/penalty dice rather than flat
difficulty modifiers, and success levels are Regular, Hard
(half skill), and Extreme (one-fifth skill).

## Skill Roll Success Levels

| Result | Threshold | Meaning |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Critical | 01 | Best possible outcome |
| Extreme | <= 1/5 of skill | Exceptional success |
| Hard | <= 1/2 of skill | Strong success |
| Regular | <= skill | Standard success |
| Failure | > skill | Task not accomplished |
| Fumble | 96-00 (skill < 50%) or 00 (skill >= 50%) | Dramatic mishap |

## Bonus and Penalty Dice

CoC 7e replaces flat modifiers with bonus and penalty dice.
Roll an extra tens die alongside the normal D100:

- **Bonus die**: Keep the lower tens die (better roll).
- **Penalty die**: Keep the higher tens die (worse roll).

Multiple bonus/penalty dice stack up to 2. If both types
apply, they cancel one-for-one. The Keeper assigns bonus or
penalty dice based on circumstances (favourable conditions,
good planning = bonus; adverse conditions, poor tools =
penalty).

## Proficiency Benchmarks

| Rating | Level | What it Means |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| 01-05% | Novice | Complete amateur, roll every time |
| 06-25% | Beginner | Knows the basics, sometimes succeeds |
| 26-50% | Competent | Handles routine tasks reliably |
| 51-75% | Professional | Expert level, makes a living with it |
| 76-90% | Expert | Advanced expertise, respected authority |
| 91%+ | Master | World-class, near-miraculous ability |

## Combat Skills

**Dodge** -- Base: DEX/2. Evade incoming attacks. One free
dodge per round; each additional dodge after the first incurs
a penalty die. Can dodge melee attacks and (with a penalty
die) the first visible ranged attack per round. Cannot dodge
unseen attacks. Does not use up the investigator's action.

**Fighting (Brawl)** -- Base: 25%. Unarmed combat: punches,
kicks, head butts, grapples. Also covers improvised weapons
(chair legs, bottles). Can be used to "fight back" as a
defensive reaction against melee attacks.

**Fighting (specialisation)** -- Base: varies. Melee weapon
categories such as Fighting (Sword), Fighting (Axe), etc.
Uses the same fight-back mechanic as Brawl.

**Firearms (Handgun)** -- Base: 20%. Accuracy with pistols
and revolvers. No damage bonus applies to firearms. Includes
basic maintenance and loading.

**Firearms (Rifle/Shotgun)** -- Base: 25%. Accuracy with
long arms including bolt-action rifles, sporting rifles, and
shotguns. Shotgun damage varies by range bracket.

**Firearms (specialisation)** -- Base: varies. Other firearm
categories as needed: Submachine Gun (15%), Machine Gun (10%),
Heavy Weapons (10%).

**Throw** -- Base: 20%. Hit a target with a thrown object:
grenades, dynamite, rocks, improvised missiles. Range is
based on thrower's STR.

## Communication Skills

**Charm** -- Base: 15%. Win someone over through personal
warmth, physical attractiveness, or seduction. Effects tend
to be lasting but require time and genuine interaction. Use
for building rapport, making friends, and romantic persuasion.

**Fast Talk** -- Base: 05%. Quick verbal deception, misdirection,
or confusion. Effects are temporary -- the target may realise
they were conned within minutes. For lasting persuasion, use
Charm or Persuade. Useful for getting past a guard, quick cons,
and creating momentary distractions.

**Intimidate** -- Base: 15%. Frighten, bully, or coerce
through threats (verbal or physical). Forces compliance but
breeds resentment. The target obeys from fear, not agreement.
May backfire if the target is in a position of power or
outnumbers the investigator.

**Persuade** -- Base: 10%. Convince someone through reasoned
argument, negotiation, or debate. Effects are lasting -- the
target genuinely changes their mind. Takes longer than Fast
Talk but the result is more durable. Used for negotiations,
legal arguments, and changing beliefs.

## Investigation and Research Skills

**Accounting** -- Base: 05%. Interpret financial records,
detect embezzlement, trace money flows, understand business
structures. Useful for following paper trails and uncovering
financial motives.

**Anthropology** -- Base: 01%. Study of human cultures past
and present. Identify cultural artefacts, understand unfamiliar
customs, recognise the origins of ritual practices. Can provide
context for cult activities and strange beliefs.

**Appraise** -- Base: 05%. Estimate the value, age, and
authenticity of objects. Identify fakes, assess antiques,
evaluate art. Can reveal hidden significance in seemingly
ordinary items.

**Archaeology** -- Base: 01%. Knowledge of ancient civilisations,
excavation techniques, and artefact identification. Date ruins,
identify architectural styles, interpret ancient inscriptions.
Essential for Mythos investigations involving ancient sites.

**History** -- Base: 05%. Knowledge of recorded history,
historical events, and their significance. Research historical
figures, identify period-appropriate details, spot anachronisms.
Useful for providing context to current events.

**Law** -- Base: 05%. Knowledge of legal codes, procedures,
and rights. Navigate the legal system, identify criminal
activity, understand contracts and property law. Helps with
police interactions and court proceedings.

**Library Use** -- Base: 20%. Locate information in libraries,
archives, newspaper morgues, and filing systems. Find specific
books, navigate catalogues, locate obscure references. An
attempt typically takes several hours. This is the primary
research skill in CoC 7e.

**Natural World** -- Base: 10%. Knowledge of plants, animals,
weather patterns, and natural phenomena. Identify species,
predict weather, recognise when something in nature is wrong
or unnatural. Useful in rural and wilderness scenarios.

**Occult** -- Base: 05%. Knowledge of folk magic, superstitions,
secret societies, and esoteric traditions. Does NOT cover
genuine Mythos knowledge (that is Cthulhu Mythos). Covers
astrology, tarot, spiritualism, Theosophy, and similar belief
systems. Useful for identifying cult paraphernalia and
distinguishing genuine threats from charlatanism.

**Science (specialisation)** -- Base: 01%. Scientific knowledge
in a specific field: Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Geology,
Astronomy, Botany, Cryptography, Forensics, Mathematics,
Meteorology, Pharmacy, Zoology, etc. Each specialisation is a
separate skill. Requires specifying the field.

## Interpersonal and Perception Skills

**Listen** -- Base: 20%. Hear and interpret sounds: overhear
conversations, detect approaching footsteps, notice unusual
noises. Both active (straining to hear) and passive (the
Keeper rolls secretly). Opposed by Stealth.

**Psychology** -- Base: 10%. Read another person's emotional
state, motives, and truthfulness through body language,
speech patterns, and behaviour. Detect lies, identify hidden
agendas, and assess NPC reliability. The CoC equivalent of
BRP's Insight skill.

**Spot Hidden** -- Base: 25%. Notice hidden objects, spot
concealed doors, detect lurking figures, find clues at a scene.
Both active (searching) and passive (the Keeper rolls secretly).
Opposed by Stealth and Disguise. The primary observation skill
in CoC 7e.

**Track** -- Base: 10%. Follow a trail over ground: footprints,
broken vegetation, tyre tracks, blood drops. A roll covers a
period of tracking. Conditions affect difficulty: rain or hard
surfaces warrant penalty dice; snow or soft ground warrant
bonus dice.

## Technical and Manipulation Skills

**Art/Craft (specialisation)** -- Base: 05%. Create works of
art or construct objects by hand. Specialisations include:
Acting, Calligraphy, Cooking, Forgery, Drawing, Painting,
Photography, Sculpture, Woodwork, Writing, and others. Each
is a separate skill. Photography is particularly useful for
documenting evidence.

**Disguise** -- Base: 05%. Alter appearance through costume,
makeup, posture, and voice. Opposed by Spot Hidden or
Psychology. Impersonating a specific known individual or
someone of a vastly different body type warrants a penalty die.

**Drive Auto** -- Base: 20%. Operate an automobile in normal
conditions. Day-to-day driving is automatic; rolls are only
needed for chases, dangerous road conditions, or evasive
manoeuvres.

**Electrical Repair** -- Base: 10%. Repair and jury-rig
electrical devices: wiring, fuse boxes, generators, radios.
In the 1920s this covers the relatively new technology of
household and industrial electrification.

**Locksmith** -- Base: 01%. Open locks without the key, crack
safes, and understand security mechanisms. Requires tools; work
without proper picks warrants a penalty die.

**Mechanical Repair** -- Base: 10%. Fix and modify mechanical
devices: engines, clocks, plumbing, industrial machinery.
Covers basic automobile repair.

**Navigate** -- Base: 10%. Find the way using maps, compass,
stars, or intuition. Roll during extended journeys, in
unfamiliar territory, or in conditions of poor visibility.

**Operate Heavy Machinery** -- Base: 01%. Drive or operate
large mechanical equipment: cranes, steam shovels, tractors,
trains. Each type may count as a separate specialisation.

**Pilot (specialisation)** -- Base: 01%. Control aircraft,
boats, or other specialised vehicles. Each type is a separate
skill: Pilot (Aircraft), Pilot (Boat), etc.

**Sleight of Hand** -- Base: 10%. Manipulate small objects
deceptively: pick pockets, palm objects, cheat at cards,
perform stage magic. Opposed by Spot Hidden. Useful for
planting or stealing evidence.

## Physical Skills

**Climb** -- Base: 20%. Climb surfaces with handholds: walls,
trees, cliffs, drainpipes. Ladders and easy climbs are
automatic. Penalty die for wet, crumbling, or featureless
surfaces. A fumble means a fall.

**Jump** -- Base: 20%. Leap for height or distance. A standing
jump covers roughly one's own height horizontally. A running
start extends this significantly. Also reduces falling damage
on a success.

**Ride** -- Base: 05%. Ride and control a mount (horse, camel,
etc.). Normal riding is automatic; rolls needed for galloping,
jumping obstacles, riding in combat, or controlling a spooked
animal.

**Stealth** -- Base: 20%. Move quietly and avoid detection.
Opposed by Listen and Spot Hidden. Moving quickly warrants a
penalty die. Noisy equipment or surfaces also impose penalties.

**Survival (specialisation)** -- Base: 10%. Find food, water,
and shelter in a specific environment: Desert, Sea, Arctic,
Forest, etc. Build fires, identify hazards, avoid exposure.
Each environment is a separate specialisation.

**Swim** -- Base: 20%. Move through water. Calm water is
automatic for anyone with Swim above base. Rough water or
strong currents require rolls. Heavy equipment or armour
imposes penalty dice.

## Medical Skills

**First Aid** -- Base: 30%. Stabilise injuries, stop bleeding,
bandage wounds, treat shock. Heals 1 HP if applied within one
round of injury (one hour for non-combat situations). One
attempt per injury. Does not treat disease or poison.

**Medicine** -- Base: 01%. Diagnose and treat serious injuries,
diseases, and poisoning. Perform surgery. Heals 1D3 HP with
proper facilities. Can treat conditions beyond First Aid's
scope. Requires medical equipment; working without it warrants
a penalty die.

**Psychoanalysis** -- Base: 01%. Treat mental illness and
psychological trauma. In CoC terms: a successful Psychoanalysis
roll can restore SAN points (1D3 per month of treatment). Can
also snap an investigator out of temporary insanity during a
session. The primary tool for recovering lost sanity.

## Language Skills

**Language (Own)** -- Base: EDU. Speak and read the
investigator's native language. Routine communication is
automatic. Rolls needed for obscure dialects, archaic forms,
deciphering damaged texts, or understanding highly technical
material.

**Language (Other: specify)** -- Base: 01%. Speak and read a
foreign language. Each language is a separate skill. At 30%+
an investigator can hold basic conversations. At 50%+ they
can read academic texts. At 70%+ they are fluent.

## Special Skills

**Credit Rating** -- Base: 00%. Represents social standing,
financial resources, and perceived respectability. Set during
character creation within the range specified by the
investigator's occupation. Determines spending level, access
to loans, and social influence. Not improved through normal
experience -- changes only through major narrative events.
Used as a social skill: high Credit Rating opens doors that
Persuade cannot.

| Credit Rating | Wealth Level | Meaning |
|---------------|-------------|---------|
| 0 | Penniless | Homeless, no resources |
| 1-9 | Poor | Meagre income, basic dwelling |
| 10-49 | Average | Comfortable middle class |
| 50-89 | Wealthy | Significant resources |
| 90-98 | Rich | Vast wealth, major influence |
| 99 | Super-rich | Essentially unlimited funds |

**Cthulhu Mythos** -- Base: 00%. Knowledge of the true
cosmic horrors underlying reality. This skill is unique in
CoC 7e:

- **Cannot be raised by spending skill points** during
  character creation or by experience checks during play.
- **Only increases** through reading Mythos tomes, witnessing
  Mythos rituals, or encountering Mythos entities directly.
- **Permanently reduces maximum Sanity**: Max SAN = 99 minus
  Cthulhu Mythos skill. An investigator with 15% Cthulhu
  Mythos can never have more than 84 SAN.
- **Provides limited benefits**: Can be rolled to remember
  Mythos lore, identify Mythos creatures or artefacts, and
  understand Mythos-related phenomena.
- **Creates the doom spiral**: The more an investigator
  learns, the less sane they can ever be. This is the central
  dramatic tension of Call of Cthulhu.

## Luck (Derived Stat, Spendable Resource)

Luck in CoC 7e is not a skill but a derived attribute that
functions as a spendable resource:

- **Starting value**: Roll 3D6 x 5 (separate from other
  characteristics).
- **Spending**: After a failed skill roll, an investigator
  may spend Luck points equal to the difference between the
  roll and the skill rating, turning the failure into a
  success. Example: skill 45%, rolled 52 -- spend 7 Luck
  points to succeed.
- **Restrictions**: Cannot spend Luck on SAN rolls, damage
  rolls, or pushed rolls.
- **Depletion**: Spent Luck is gone. As Luck depletes, the
  investigator loses their safety net, raising tension.
- **Recovery**: Typically 1D10 Luck recovered between
  sessions (Keeper's discretion).
- **Group Luck**: The investigator with the lowest current
  Luck sets the threshold for group Luck rolls.

## Pushing Rolls

When a skill roll fails, the player may ask to "push" the
roll -- one re-attempt with increased risk:

- The player must describe how they try harder or differently.
- The Keeper decides if pushing is possible.
- If the pushed roll also fails, the consequences are much
  worse than the original failure (injury, alerting enemies,
  offending an NPC, etc.).
- Each roll can only be pushed once.
- Combat rolls and SAN rolls cannot be pushed.

## Experience and Improvement

When a skill succeeds under stress, mark an experience check.
Between adventures, roll D100 for each checked skill: if the
roll exceeds the current skill rating, the skill increases
by 1D10 points.

Cthulhu Mythos never receives experience checks. It only
increases through Mythos encounters and tome study.
