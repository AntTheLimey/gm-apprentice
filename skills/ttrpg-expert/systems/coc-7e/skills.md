# Call of Cthulhu 7e — Skills Reference

Complete BRP skill list with base chances, usage notes, and
specialties. Used during character creation (allocating skill
points) and play (quick lookup of what a skill covers and its
base chance).

## Skill Categories

Skills fall into six categories, each linked to characteristics
for optional category bonuses:

| Category | Covers | Primary Stat |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Combat | Weapons and fighting maneuvers | DEX |
| Communication | Interpersonal, language, persuasion | INT |
| Manipulation | Precise hand-eye coordination, crafting | DEX |
| Mental | Knowledge, analysis, medicine | INT |
| Perception | Observation, awareness, tracking | INT |
| Physical | Athletics, stealth, movement | DEX |

## Proficiency Benchmarks

| Rating | Level | What it Means |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| 01-05% | Novice | Complete amateur, roll every time |
| 06-25% | Neophyte | Beginner, knows enough to be dangerous |
| 26-50% | Amateur | Hobby level, can handle routine tasks |
| 51-75% | Professional | Can make a living with this skill |
| 76-90% | Expert | Advanced expertise, reasonable chance at Difficult tasks |
| 91%+ | Master | Few attain this, near miracles under routine conditions |

## Combat Skills

**Artillery (various)** — Base: per weapon. Fire massive field
pieces and vehicular weapons (cannons, trebuchets). Requires
crew to operate; solo use is Difficult or Impossible.

**Brawl** — Base: 25%. Unarmed combat: punches, kicks, head
butts. Can parry Brawl or Grapple attacks but parrying limb
takes minimum weapon damage. Using Brawl against a grappled
foe is Easy.

**Energy Weapon (various)** — Base: per weapon. Fire hand-held
directed energy weapons (lasers, blasters). Futuristic settings
only. Cannot be dodged. Shield blocks are Difficult.

**Firearm (various)** — Base: per weapon (typically 15-30%).
Accuracy with firearms plus handling and basic maintenance.
Specialties: Machine Gun, Pistol, Revolver, Rifle, Shotgun,
Submachine Gun. No damage modifier applied. Can make aimed
attacks.

**Grapple** — Base: 25%. Wrestling and submission holds.
Success grabs the target; maintain with Grapple each round.
Grapple effects (next round): change hold, disarm, immobilise
limb, injure, knockdown, strangle, throw.

**Heavy Weapon (various)** — Base: per weapon. Operate portable
explosive or large direct-fire weapons (bazookas, rocket
launchers, flamethrowers). Usually employ autofire or
explosive ordnance.

**Martial Arts** — Base: 01%. Specialised fighting style used
in combination with Brawl, Melee Weapon, Missile Weapon, or
Grapple. If the roll is under both skills, roll base damage
twice (modifier not doubled). Specialties by tradition:
boxing, karate, kung fu, fencing, etc.

**Melee Weapon (various)** — Base: per weapon (typically 10-25%).
Wield a weapon class in hand-to-hand combat. Specialties by
weapon class: Axe, Club, Dagger, Flail, Hammer, Hand, Mace,
Polearm, Staff, Sword. Multiple parries at cumulative -30%.

**Missile Weapon (various)** — Base: per weapon (typically
05-15%). Hand-held self-propelled weapons: bows, crossbows,
thrown axes, javelins, slings. Half damage modifier applies;
self-propelled weapons (crossbows) get no damage modifier.

**Parry (various)** — Base: per weapon. Block incoming attacks
with a weapon or shield. Successive parries at cumulative -30%.
Cannot parry attacks from vastly larger foes (double+ SIZ).

**Shield** — Base: per shield (typically 10-20%). Block attacks
with a shield. Can also attack with the shield for crushing/
knockback damage. Shield blocks are parries.

## Communication Skills

**Bargain** — Base: 05%. Haggle over prices, negotiate
compromises. Use opposed rolls for important deals. Bargaining
is about compromise; use Fast Talk or Persuade for winner-
take-all arguments.

**Command** — Base: 05%. Inspire and coordinate subordinates.
Success adds 1/5 Command as complementary bonus to all
subordinates each round they follow orders. Primarily combat
but extends to negotiations.

**Disguise** — Base: 01%. Use posture, costume, and voice to
appear as someone else. Opposed by Spot or Insight. Theatrical
makeup and alterations add up to +20%. Impersonating a specific
known person or vastly different body type is Difficult.

**Etiquette (various)** — Base: 05%. Know and follow social
rules and codes of a particular group. Specialties by group.
Roll when first interacting with an important group or on
significant occasions.

**Fast Talk** — Base: 05%. Quick persuasion or bluffing. Effects
are temporary: a few minutes and a successful Idea roll later,
the target may come to their senses. For permanent persuasion,
use Persuade. Retrying after a failed Communication roll is
Difficult.

**Language (various)** — Base: Own INT x5 (or EDU x5), Other
00%. Verbal communication in a specific language. Routine
conversation in own language is Automatic. Roll only for
difficult concepts or unusual situations. Understanding
obscure dialects or ancient varieties is Difficult.

**Perform (various)** — Base: 05%. Artistic expression through
performance: acting, dancing, playing an instrument, singing,
ritual. Specialties: Act, Dance, Juggle, Orate, Play
Instrument, Recite, Ritual, Sing. Perform (Ritual) may
augment magic.

**Persuade** — Base: 15%. Convince someone through logic,
debate, empathy, or intimidation. Effects last indefinitely
(unlike Fast Talk). Convincing someone against core beliefs
is Difficult. Extended Persuade may take hours or days.

**Status** — Base: 15%. Reputation, social class, professional
credentials. Throw weight around for favours, loans, or
intimidation. Only operates within your area of influence;
outside that area is Difficult. Not improved by normal
experience; the Keeper awards increases for notable deeds.

**Teach** — Base: 10%. Train others in a skill. Teacher must
have twice the student's rating or 50%, whichever is lower.
Success trains at normal rate; special trains 1D6, critical
trains 1D6+2.

## Manipulation Skills

**Art (various)** — Base: 05%. Create works of art. Specialties:
Calligraphy, Drawing, Painting, Photography, Sculpture,
Writing, etc. Critical or special yields a work of lasting
value.

**Craft (various)** — Base: 05%. Construct or make things.
Specialties: Blacksmithing, Carpentry, Ceramics, Cooking,
Leatherworking, Locksmithing, etc. Using Craft without
proper tools is Difficult.

**Demolition** — Base: 01%. Store, transport, set, and detonate
explosives for specific effects (collapse a building safely,
blow a safe without harming contents). At 60%+, normal uses
automatically succeed. Without proper tools is Difficult.

**Fine Manipulation** — Base: 05%. Hand-eye coordination:
picking locks, disarming traps, tying knots, disassembling
devices. Without appropriate tools is Difficult.

**Heavy Machine (various)** — Base: 01%. Operate machines
larger than a person: forklifts, tanks, cranes, locomotive
engines. Specialties by vehicle type. Can be used as weapons
doing 3D6 to 6D6.

**Repair (various)** — Base: 15%. Fix damaged equipment or
make slight modifications. Specialties: Electrical, Electronic,
Mechanical, Structural, etc. Success restores 1D3 HP to the
item.

**Sleight of Hand** — Base: 05%. Manipulate small objects
deceptively: picking pockets, cheating at cards, stage magic.
Opposed by Spot. Once palmed, use Hide to conceal from a
dedicated search.

## Mental Skills

**Appraise** — Base: 15%. Estimate an object's worth. Failure
means no idea; fumble yields false information. Special or
critical provides additional details about provenance or
history.

**First Aid** — Base: 30%. Stop bleeding, bandage wounds, set
broken limbs, revive unconscious persons. Takes a full combat
round; cannot be done in active combat. Success heals 1D3 HP
to a single wound, special 2D3, critical 1D3+3. Can restore
a character at 0 or negative HP to life if brought to 1+ HP.
Only one attempt per wound.

**Gaming** — Base: INT+POW%. Play games of skill and chance.
Opposed roll vs other players. To cheat, use Sleight of Hand
or Fast Talk, opposed by Spot or Insight.

**Knowledge (various)** — Base: 05% (common) or 00% (obscure).
Academic study or experience in a field. Specialties: Accounting,
Anthropology, Archaeology, History, Law, Occult, Religion,
Folklore, Linguistics, Literature, Philosophy, Streetwise,
Group (subspecialty), Region (subspecialty), etc.

**Literacy (various)** — Base: 00% (historical) or equal to
Language (modern settings). Read and write in a specific
language. In modern settings, equals Language skill. Understanding
obscure dialects or ancient texts is Difficult.

**Medicine** — Base: 05%. Diagnose and treat serious injuries,
diseases, poisonings, and perform surgery. Success heals as
First Aid or doubles healing rate to 2D3/week, or halts ongoing
poison/disease damage. Special heals 2D3 HP or 1D3 characteristic
points. Each use applies to a single malady.

**Psychotherapy** — Base: 01% (modern) or 00% (historical).
Evaluate and treat mental illness. With Sanity rules: success
after a month of treatment restores 1D3-1 SAN (special 1D3,
critical 1D3+1). Cannot raise SAN above POW x5 minus Blasphemous
Lore penalties.

**Science (various)** — Base: 01%. Scientific knowledge and
methodology. Specialties: Astronomy, Biology, Chemistry,
Cryptography, Geology, Mathematics, Natural History,
Pharmacology, Physics, Psychology, Zoology, etc. Requires
specialised equipment; without it is Difficult or Impossible.

**Strategy** — Base: 01%. Guide forces in battle, military
logistics, and tactical analysis. Opposed rolls between
leaders determine engagement outcomes. Using Strategy when
outnumbered or poorly supplied is Difficult.

**Technical Skill (various)** — Base: 05%. Operate and maintain
advanced devices: computers, robots, clockwork, traps, siege
engines. Specialties: Clockwork, Computers, Cybernetics,
Electronics, Robotics, Sensors, Traps, etc.

## Perception Skills

**Insight** — Base: 05%. Read another person's emotional state,
motives, and character through body language and speech patterns.
Opposed by Etiquette, Fast Talk, or Persuade. Using Insight on
close friends or family is Easy.

**Listen** — Base: 25%. Hear and interpret sounds. Normal range
for a polite-volume human voice is 15 metres. Both active and
passive: the Keeper may roll secretly. Opposed by Stealth.

**Navigate** — Base: 10%. Find the way using compass, charts,
stars, or intuition. Roll every four hours of a journey. Maps
or compass add up to +20%. Without instruments in featureless
terrain (sea, desert, darkness) is Difficult.

**Research** — Base: 25%. Locate information in libraries,
archives, or networks. An attempt takes four hours (faster
with electronic methods). Failure should still yield something
to keep the game moving; total dead-ends are boring.

**Sense** — Base: 10%. Taste, touch, smell, and other non-
visual/non-auditory senses. Identify a mysterious odour, feel
a hairline fracture, notice a faint taste. Both active and
passive. Strong or familiar stimuli are Easy; faint or
unfamiliar are Difficult.

**Spot** — Base: 25%. Search, notice details, spot hidden
objects or ambushes, recognise a disguise. Both active and
passive. Opposed by Hide, Disguise, or Sleight of Hand.
In complete darkness, Spot is Impossible.

**Track** — Base: 10%. Follow a trail over ground. A roll takes
1D3 minutes. Rainy night is Difficult; bright snowy day is
Easy. If trail is lost, finding it again is Difficult.
Advanced equipment adds up to +20%.

## Physical Skills

**Climb** — Base: 40%. Climb surfaces with hand/footholds.
Climbing rate is 1/5 MOV. Ladders are Automatic; very tall
ladders/trees are Easy. Without feet is Difficult. Roll every
minute or 10 metres. Fumble means fall.

**Dodge** — Base: DEX x2%. Evade incoming attacks. Choose Dodge
OR Parry each round, not both (unless fully defensive).
Multiple dodges at cumulative -30%. Dodging bullets/arrows is
Difficult and only possible once per round (first visible
shot only).

**Drive (various)** — Base: 20% (familiar) or 01% (unfamiliar).
Control ground vehicles. Day-to-day driving is Automatic.
Specialties: Automobile, Carriage, Motorcycle, etc.

**Fly** — Base: DEX x4 (winged) or 1/2 DEX (other). Manoeuvre
in flight without a vehicle. Short uneventful flights are
Automatic. See Aerial Combat for modifiers.

**Hide** — Base: 10%. Conceal oneself or an object from view.
Opposed by Spot. Adequate cover essential; scant cover is
Difficult; no cover is Impossible. Camouflage adds up to +20%.
Moving more than 1 metre/round requires both Hide and Stealth.

**Jump** — Base: 25%. Leap for height or distance. Standing:
own height horizontally, half height vertically. Running
start: roughly 3 metres. Special +50%, critical doubles.
Also reduces falling damage by 1D6 per success level.

**Pilot (various)** — Base: 01%. Steer vehicles through air,
water, or space. Specialties: Airplane, Boat, Helicopter,
Submarine, etc. Day-to-day piloting is Automatic. Roll under
adverse or extraordinary conditions.

**Projection** — Base: DEX x2%. Optional skill for targeting
power-based attacks. Only used in powered-character campaigns.

**Ride (various)** — Base: 05%. Ride and handle a specific type
of mount. Specialties by animal. Both Ride and combat skill
must be 50%+ for unimpeded mounted combat; use the lower of
the two for attacks.

**Stealth** — Base: 10%. Move quietly without drawing attention.
Moving more than 1/2 MOV is Difficult. Opposed by Spot or
Listen. Noisy armour or bulky equipment may penalise.

**Swim** — Base: 25%. Move through water. Calm water: roll once
per turn. Rough water: roll each round. ENC subtracts from
Swim rating. Underwater melee uses lower of combat or Swim
skill; only impaling weapons are effective (others do 1D3-1);
water adds 3 armour points.

**Throw** — Base: 25%. Hit a target with a thrown object not
designed for throwing (improvised weapons, grenades, dynamite).
Range equals thrower's STR in metres; beyond that is Difficult.
Half damage modifier applies.

## Complementary Skills and Augments

One related skill may add 1/5 its rating as a complementary
bonus to another skill roll. Only one complementary skill may
apply per roll; bonuses do not stack.

Alternatively, roll a complementary skill as an augment: if
successful, adjust the primary skill's difficulty one step
easier (e.g. Difficult becomes Average). If the augment fails,
the primary skill shifts one step harder.

Cannot combine complementary bonus and augment on the same roll.
