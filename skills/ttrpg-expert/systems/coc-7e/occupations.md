# Call of Cthulhu 7e -- Occupations

CoC 7e occupation templates with skill allocations, skill point
formulas, and Credit Rating ranges. Used during character
creation to guide skill point distribution and during prep to
build NPCs with appropriate skill sets.

CoC 7e uses the term "occupation" (not "profession" as in
generic BRP). Each occupation defines the investigator's job
or calling before the scenario begins.

## How Occupations Work

An occupation provides:

- **Eight occupation skills** (including Credit Rating) that
  receive occupation skill points.
- **A skill point formula** based on characteristics (most
  commonly EDU x 4, but varies by occupation).
- **A Credit Rating range** indicating the occupation's
  economic and social standing.

Personal interest points (INT x 2) are allocated separately
and can go to any skill except Cthulhu Mythos.

Occupations are starting points, not limits. Investigators
grow beyond their occupation through experience. Swap skills
that do not fit the era or setting with Keeper approval.

## Skill Point Formulas

Different occupations use different formulas for calculating
occupation skill points:

| Formula | Typical Occupations |
|---------|-------------------|
| EDU x 4 | Academic and knowledge-based (Professor, Librarian, Author) |
| EDU x 2 + APP x 2 | Social and appearance-dependent (Dilettante, Entertainer) |
| EDU x 2 + DEX x 2 | Skilled manual work (Mechanic, Criminal) |
| EDU x 2 + STR x 2 | Physical labour (Soldier, Police Officer) |
| EDU x 2 + (STR or DEX) x 2 | Mixed physical (Private Investigator, Police Detective) |
| EDU x 2 + POW x 2 | Willpower-driven (Clergy, Occultist) |

Note: All characteristic values used are the percentile (x5)
values, not the raw 3-18 values.

## Occupations A-Z

**Antiquarian** -- Collector and dealer in rare books, maps,
and artefacts. Knowledgeable about history and the provenance
of old things.
Skill Points: EDU x 4. Credit Rating: 30-70.
Occupation Skills: Appraise, Art/Craft (any), History,
Library Use, Language (Other), one interpersonal skill
(Charm, Fast Talk, Intimidate, or Persuade), Spot Hidden,
plus one of: Archaeology, Law, Navigate, or Science (any).

**Artist** -- Painter, sculptor, musician, or other creative
professional. May be impoverished or celebrated.
Skill Points: EDU x 2 + DEX x 2 or EDU x 2 + POW x 2.
Credit Rating: 9-50.
Occupation Skills: Art/Craft (any), two other Art/Craft or
one Art/Craft plus a related skill, History, Listen, Natural
World or Spot Hidden, Psychology, plus one interpersonal
skill (Charm, Fast Talk, Intimidate, or Persuade).

**Author** -- Writer of fiction, non-fiction, journalism, or
poetry. Well-read and observant.
Skill Points: EDU x 4. Credit Rating: 9-30.
Occupation Skills: Art/Craft (Writing), History, Library Use,
Language (Own), Natural World or Occult, one interpersonal
skill (Charm, Fast Talk, Intimidate, or Persuade), Psychology,
plus any one other skill.

**Clergy** -- Priest, minister, rabbi, or other religious
leader. Counsellor and community figure.
Skill Points: EDU x 2 + POW x 2. Credit Rating: 9-60.
Occupation Skills: Accounting, History, Library Use, Listen,
Language (Other), one interpersonal skill (Charm, Fast Talk,
Intimidate, or Persuade), Psychology, plus any one other skill.

**Criminal** -- Burglar, forger, confidence trickster, or
other lawbreaker. Streetwise and cautious.
Skill Points: EDU x 2 + DEX x 2 or EDU x 2 + STR x 2.
Credit Rating: 5-65.
Occupation Skills: Disguise or Electrical Repair, one of
Fighting or Firearms, Locksmith or Mechanical Repair, Sleight
of Hand, Spot Hidden, Stealth, one interpersonal skill (Charm,
Fast Talk, Intimidate, or Persuade), Psychology.

**Dilettante** -- Person of independent wealth who pursues
interests as hobbies rather than employment. Well-connected
and widely travelled.
Skill Points: EDU x 2 + APP x 2. Credit Rating: 50-99.
Occupation Skills: Art/Craft (any), Firearms (any), Language
(Other), Ride, one interpersonal skill (Charm, Fast Talk,
Intimidate, or Persuade), plus any three other skills.

**Doctor of Medicine** -- Physician, surgeon, or medical
specialist. Highly educated with access to medical facilities.
Skill Points: EDU x 4. Credit Rating: 30-80.
Occupation Skills: First Aid, Language (Latin), Medicine,
Psychology, Science (Biology), Science (Pharmacy), plus any
two of: Accounting, Library Use, Persuade, or another Science.

**Drifter** -- Wanderer, hobo, or itinerant worker. Tough
and resourceful but marginalised.
Skill Points: EDU x 2 + (STR or DEX) x 2 or EDU x 2 + APP x 2.
Credit Rating: 0-5.
Occupation Skills: Climb, Jump or Swim, Listen, Natural World,
Navigate, one interpersonal skill (Charm, Fast Talk, Intimidate,
or Persuade), Stealth, plus any one other skill.

**Engineer** -- Designs, builds, or maintains mechanical,
electrical, or structural systems.
Skill Points: EDU x 4. Credit Rating: 30-60.
Occupation Skills: Art/Craft (Technical Drawing), Electrical
Repair, Library Use, Mechanical Repair, Operate Heavy Machinery,
Science (Engineering or Physics), plus any two of: Accounting,
Climb, Drive Auto, or Science (any).

**Entertainer** -- Actor, singer, dancer, comedian, or other
performer. Charismatic and expressive.
Skill Points: EDU x 2 + APP x 2. Credit Rating: 9-70.
Occupation Skills: Art/Craft (Acting), Disguise, two
interpersonal skills (Charm, Fast Talk, Intimidate, or
Persuade), Listen, Psychology, plus any two other skills.

**Farmer/Rancher** -- Works the land or raises livestock.
Practical, self-reliant, and connected to the community.
Skill Points: EDU x 2 + (STR or DEX) x 2. Credit Rating: 9-30.
Occupation Skills: Art/Craft (Farming) or Natural World,
Drive Auto or Ride, one of Fighting or Firearms, First Aid,
Mechanical Repair, Natural World, Track, plus any one other
skill.

**Journalist** -- Reporter, columnist, or photojournalist.
Curious, well-connected, and persistent.
Skill Points: EDU x 4. Credit Rating: 9-30.
Occupation Skills: Art/Craft (Photography), History, Library
Use, Language (Own), one interpersonal skill (Charm, Fast Talk,
Intimidate, or Persuade), Psychology, plus any two of:
Accounting, Law, Stealth, or another Language.

**Lawyer** -- Attorney, barrister, or legal scholar. Skilled
in argument and procedural knowledge.
Skill Points: EDU x 4. Credit Rating: 30-80.
Occupation Skills: Accounting, Law, Library Use, two
interpersonal skills (Charm, Fast Talk, Intimidate, or
Persuade), Psychology, plus any two other skills.

**Librarian** -- Keeper of books and archives. Quietly
knowledgeable with access to extensive records.
Skill Points: EDU x 4. Credit Rating: 9-35.
Occupation Skills: Accounting, Library Use, Language (Other),
Language (Own), plus any four of: Art/Craft (any), History,
Science (any), or another Language.

**Mechanic** -- Repairs and maintains automobiles, machinery,
or other equipment. Practical and resourceful.
Skill Points: EDU x 2 + DEX x 2. Credit Rating: 9-40.
Occupation Skills: Art/Craft (any), Climb, Drive Auto,
Electrical Repair, Mechanical Repair, Operate Heavy Machinery,
plus any two of: Accounting, Locksmith, or Science (any).

**Military Officer** -- Commissioned officer in the armed
forces. Trained in leadership and strategy.
Skill Points: EDU x 2 + (STR or DEX) x 2. Credit Rating: 20-70.
Occupation Skills: Accounting, Firearms (any), Navigate, two
interpersonal skills (Charm, Fast Talk, Intimidate, or
Persuade), Psychology, plus any two of: Drive Auto, Fighting
(Brawl), First Aid, Law, Language (Other), or Survival (any).

**Missionary** -- Religious worker serving in foreign lands.
Devoted, culturally aware, and resilient.
Skill Points: EDU x 2 + APP x 2. Credit Rating: 0-30.
Occupation Skills: Art/Craft (any), First Aid, Mechanical
Repair, Medicine, Natural World, one interpersonal skill
(Charm, Fast Talk, Intimidate, or Persuade), plus any two
of: Language (Other), Listen, Navigate, or Survival (any).

**Nurse** -- Medical professional providing patient care.
Practical, compassionate, and observant.
Skill Points: EDU x 4. Credit Rating: 9-30.
Occupation Skills: First Aid, Listen, Medicine, one
interpersonal skill (Charm, Fast Talk, Intimidate, or
Persuade), Psychology, Science (Biology or Chemistry),
Spot Hidden, plus any one other skill.

**Occultist** -- Student of hidden lore, esoteric traditions,
and arcane knowledge. Often eccentric.
Skill Points: EDU x 2 + POW x 2. Credit Rating: 9-65.
Occupation Skills: Anthropology or History, Library Use,
Language (Other), Occult, one interpersonal skill (Charm,
Fast Talk, Intimidate, or Persuade), plus any three of:
Archaeology, Art/Craft (any), Science (any), or Spot Hidden.

**Parapsychologist** -- Investigator of psychic phenomena,
hauntings, and unexplained events. Straddles the line between
science and superstition.
Skill Points: EDU x 4. Credit Rating: 9-30.
Occupation Skills: Anthropology, Art/Craft (Photography),
History, Library Use, Occult, Psychology, one interpersonal
skill (Charm, Fast Talk, Intimidate, or Persuade), plus any
one of: Language (Other), Science (any), or Spot Hidden.

**Police Detective** -- Investigator within a police department.
Access to official resources and authority.
Skill Points: EDU x 2 + (STR or DEX) x 2. Credit Rating: 20-50.
Occupation Skills: Art/Craft (Acting) or Disguise, Firearms
(any), Law, Listen, one interpersonal skill (Charm, Fast Talk,
Intimidate, or Persuade), Psychology, Spot Hidden, plus any
one of: Accounting, Drive Auto, First Aid, or Stealth.

**Police Officer** -- Uniformed law enforcement. Authority,
physical capability, and community presence.
Skill Points: EDU x 2 + (STR or DEX) x 2. Credit Rating: 9-30.
Occupation Skills: Drive Auto, Fighting (Brawl), Firearms
(any), First Aid, Law, one interpersonal skill (Charm, Fast
Talk, Intimidate, or Persuade), Psychology or Spot Hidden,
plus any one other skill.

**Private Investigator** -- Works outside official channels.
Resourceful, persistent, and often operating in grey areas.
Skill Points: EDU x 2 + (STR or DEX) x 2. Credit Rating: 9-50.
Occupation Skills: Art/Craft (Photography), Disguise, Law,
Library Use, one interpersonal skill (Charm, Fast Talk,
Intimidate, or Persuade), Psychology, Spot Hidden, plus any
one of: Accounting, Fighting (any), Firearms (any), Locksmith,
or Stealth.

**Professor** -- University academic and researcher. Highly
educated with access to institutional resources and students.
Skill Points: EDU x 4. Credit Rating: 20-70.
Occupation Skills: Library Use, Language (Other), Psychology,
plus any four skills appropriate to the field of study, and
one interpersonal skill (Charm, Fast Talk, Intimidate, or
Persuade).

**Sailor** -- Merchant marine, naval rating, or fisherman.
Tough and experienced on the water.
Skill Points: EDU x 2 + (STR or DEX) x 2. Credit Rating: 9-30.
Occupation Skills: Electrical Repair or Mechanical Repair,
Fighting (Brawl), Firearms (any) or Throw, First Aid,
Navigate, Pilot (Boat), Spot Hidden or Survival (Sea),
Swim.

**Soldier** -- Enlisted military personnel or veteran.
Disciplined and combat-trained.
Skill Points: EDU x 2 + (STR or DEX) x 2. Credit Rating: 9-30.
Occupation Skills: Climb or Swim, Dodge, Fighting (any),
Firearms (any), First Aid, Stealth, Survival (any), plus any
one of: Drive Auto, Language (Other), Mechanical Repair,
Navigate, or Throw.

**Spy** -- Intelligence operative or covert agent. Skilled
in infiltration, deception, and tradecraft.
Skill Points: EDU x 2 + (STR or DEX) x 2 or EDU x 2 + APP x 2.
Credit Rating: 20-60.
Occupation Skills: Disguise, Firearms (any), Listen, Language
(Other), one interpersonal skill (Charm, Fast Talk, Intimidate,
or Persuade), Psychology, Sleight of Hand or Locksmith, Stealth.

**Tribal Member** -- Member of a traditional or indigenous
community. Connected to ancestral knowledge and practices.
Skill Points: EDU x 2 + (STR or DEX) x 2. Credit Rating: 0-15.
Occupation Skills: Climb, Fighting (Brawl) or Throw, Listen,
Natural World, Occult, Spot Hidden, Survival (any), Swim.

## Creating Custom Occupations

1. Choose a title and describe the occupation briefly.
2. Select a skill point formula appropriate to the occupation's
   primary characteristics.
3. Choose eight occupation skills (one must be Credit Rating).
4. Set an appropriate Credit Rating range.
5. Get Keeper approval.

Existing occupations can be adapted by swapping skills to fit
the era or setting. A 1920s Bootlegger might use the Criminal
template with Drive Auto added. A Modern-era Hacker might
use the Engineer template with Computer Use replacing
Operate Heavy Machinery.
