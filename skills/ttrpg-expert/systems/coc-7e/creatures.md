# Call of Cthulhu 7e -- Creatures Reference

**Important note on Lovecraft entities:** For iconic Mythos
creatures (Deep Ones, Shoggoths, Mi-Go, etc.), no official
CoC stat blocks are provided here -- those are Chaosium's
intellectual property. The Lovecraft entries below contain
only literary descriptions from public domain fiction. Use
the Ghoul [ORC] and other ORC creatures as mechanical
baselines, adapting stats as needed for your scenario. A
Ghoul-equivalent stat block can serve as a starting point for
many humanoid Mythos creatures; scale characteristics up for
larger or more powerful entities.

BRP ORC sample creatures with stat blocks, plus public domain
Lovecraft entities with literary descriptions. Used during
prep (encounter building) and play (stat lookups).

Each entry is labelled with its source:
- **[ORC]** -- Stats and descriptions from the BRP UGE ORC
  Content Document.
- **[Lovecraft]** -- Descriptions drawn from H.P. Lovecraft's
  published fiction (public domain, pre-1929). Names, physical
  descriptions, behaviours, and locations only. No game stats
  are provided for these entries as they are not part of the
  ORC content.

## Reading ORC Stat Blocks

- **Characteristics**: Rolled range and average in parentheses.
- **HP**: Usually (CON+SIZ)/2.
- **MOV**: Movement rate (Walk, Swim, Fly, Slither).
- **DM**: Average damage modifier.
- **Armour**: Natural protection and type.
- **Attacks**: Weapon/natural attack, skill %, damage, special.
- **Skills**: Relevant skills for a typical encounter.
- **SAN Loss**: Sanity cost on seeing the creature (if Sanity
  rules are used). Format: loss on success / loss on failure.

## Natural Animals [ORC]

### Alligator/Crocodile [ORC]
STR 4D6+12 (25), CON 3D6+6 (19), SIZ 4D6+12 (25), POW 3D6
(11), DEX 2D6 (7). HP 22, MOV Walk 7/Swim 6, DM +2D6.
Armour: 5-pt hide. Attack: Bite 50%, 1D10+dm.
Skills: Dodge 30%, Hide 50%, Stealth 75%.
Alligators are smaller (-1D6 STR/SIZ, Bite 1D8+dm).

### Bear [ORC]
STR 3D6+10 (21), CON 2D6+6 (13), SIZ 3D6+10 (21), POW 3D6
(11), DEX 3D6 (11). HP 17, MOV Walk 12/Swim 2, DM +2D6.
Armour: 3-pt fur. Attacks: Bite 25%, 1D10; Claw 25%, 1D6+dm.
Two attacks per round (two claws, or one claw + one bite).
Grizzlies: +1D6 STR/SIZ, +1 armour, +10% combat. Polar
bears: +2D6 STR/SIZ, +3 armour, +20% attacks, Swim 80%.

### Dog [ORC]
STR 2D6 (7), CON 3D6 (11), SIZ 1D6+1 (5), POW 2D6 (7),
DEX 2D6+6 (13). HP 8, MOV Walk 10/Swim 6, DM -1D4.
Attack: Bite 30%, 1D6+1/2dm. Wild dogs form packs of 1D8+3.
For larger dogs, use Wolf stats.

### Horse [ORC]
STR 3D6+18 (29), CON 2D6+6 (13), SIZ 4D6+12 (26), POW 3D6
(11), DEX 3D6 (11). HP 20, MOV Walk 12/Swim 2, DM +2D6.
Armour: 1-pt hide. Attacks: Kick 20%, 2D8+dm; Bite 10%, 1D6.
Untrained horses flee from blood, fire, gunshots. Warhorses:
+2 STR/CON/SIZ, hold ground on Ride check.

### Lion [ORC]
STR 2D6+12 (19), CON 3D6 (11), SIZ 3D6+6 (17), POW 2D6+6
(13), DEX 2D6+12 (19). HP 14, MOV Walk 10/Swim 2, DM +1D6.
Armour: 2-pt skin. Attacks: Bite 40%, 1D10; Claw 60%,
1D6+dm; Rip 80%, 2D6+dm. One claw + one bite per round;
if both hit, rakes with hind claws next round.

### Shark [ORC]
STR 3D6+12 (23), CON 2D6+9 (16), SIZ 3D6+12 (23), POW 3D6
(11), DEX 2D6+3 (10). HP 23, MOV Swim 10, DM +2D6.
Armour: 5-pt skin. Attacks: Bite 75%, 2D6+dm; Fin Slash 50%,
2D3+1/2dm. If bite hits, shark continues biting (Easy) each
round. Giant shark: STR/SIZ 6D6+24, +5D6 DM.

### Snake, Constrictor [ORC]
STR 3D6+12 (23), CON 2D6+6 (13), SIZ 5D6 (18), POW 3D6
(11), DEX 2D6+6 (13). HP 16, MOV Slither 6/Swim 4, DM +1D6.
Armour: 1-pt skin. Attacks: Bite 65%, 1D4+1/2dm; Constrict
40%, 1D6+dm per round. Constriction requires STR vs STR to
escape. Giant constrictor: +2D6 STR/SIZ, 4-pt hide.

### Snake, Venomous [ORC]
STR 2D4 (5), CON 2D6 (7), SIZ 2D4 (5), POW 1D8 (5),
DEX 3D6 (11). HP 6, MOV Slither 6/Swim 4, DM -1D6.
Attack: Bite 70%, 1D2+1/2dm (impaling) + poison.
Skills: Hide 80%, Stealth 90%.

### Tiger [ORC]
STR 3D6+12 (23), CON 3D6 (11), SIZ 3D6+6 (17), POW 3D6
(11), DEX 2D6+12 (19). HP 14, MOV Walk 12, DM +1D6.
Armour: 2-pt skin. Attacks: Claw 70%, 1D8+dm; Bite 45%,
1D10+1/2dm; Rip 80%, 2D8+dm. Two claws + one bite per round
(bite 5 DEX ranks after claws). If both claws hit, rips with
hind claws next round.

### Wolf [ORC]
STR 2D6+6 (13), CON 3D6 (11), SIZ 2D6+1 (8), POW 3D6 (11),
DEX 2D6+6 (13). HP 10, MOV Walk 10, DM none.
Armour: 2-pt fur. Attack: Bite 30%, 1D8+1/2dm.
Skills: Listen 75%, Sense 90%, Track 80%.
Wolves hunt in packs (12-40+), fighting cooperatively to
knock targets down then swarm.

## Fantasy Creatures [ORC]

STR 3D6+6 (17), CON 3D6 (11), SIZ 4D6+12 (26), INT 2D6+6
(13), POW 3D6 (11), DEX 3D6+3 (14), CHA 3D6 (11).
HP 19, MOV 12, DM +1D4 (melee) / +2D6 (kick/charge).
Armour: 1-pt hide (can wear additional armour).
Attacks: Composite Bow 45%, 1D8+1+1/2dm; Lance 35%,
1D10+1+dm; Bastard Sword 40%, 1D10+1+dm; Kick 50%, 1D6+dm.
Skills: Appraise 50%, First Aid 40%, Knowledge (History) 50%.
Wise, pastoral, fine craftsmen and fierce warriors.

### Dragon [ORC]
STR 20D6 (70), CON 10D6 (35), SIZ 20D6 (70), INT 10 (fixed),
POW 4D6+6 (20), DEX 3D6 (11). HP 53, MOV Walk 4/Fly 10,
DM +8D6. Armour: 12-pt scales.
Attacks: Breath Flame 75%, 4D6+fire (3m radius, POW in metres
range, costs 1D6 PP); Bite 65%, 3D6+dm; Claw 50%, 1D6+dm;
Tail 50%, 1D6+1/2dm (sweep).
Two attacks per round (breath or bite, plus claw or tail).
SAN Loss: 0/1D8+. Intelligent dragons (INT 4D6) know magic.

HP 10, MOV 11, DM none. Armour: 1-pt (soft leather).

Armour: None. Attacks: Claw 40%, 1D6; Weapon (as weapon type).

(17), POW 3D6+6 (17), DEX 3D6+6 (17), CHA 3D6+6 (17).

sunlight, garlic, holy symbols (at Keeper's discretion).

STR 3D6+12 (23), CON 3D6+6 (17), SIZ 3D6+6 (17), INT 2D6+6

STR 3D6+6 (17), CON 3D6+6 (17), SIZ 2D6+6 (13), INT --
(fixed 1), POW 1 (fixed), DEX 1D6 (4). HP 15, MOV 4.

## Lovecraft Entities [Lovecraft]

The following entries are drawn entirely from H.P. Lovecraft's
published fiction (pre-1929, public domain). They present his
literary descriptions only — physical appearance, behaviour,
associated locations, and thematic role. No game statistics are
provided; these are not Chaosium game content.

### Cthulhu [Lovecraft]
**Source**: "The Call of Cthulhu" (1928).
**Description**: A vast being of vaguely anthropoid outline,
with an octopus-like head whose face is a mass of feelers, a
scaly, rubbery body, prodigious claws on hind and fore feet,
and long narrow wings behind. Cthulhu is of mountainous size
and sleeps in the sunken city of R'lyeh beneath the Pacific
Ocean. Even in sleep, Cthulhu sends dreams to sensitive minds.
When the stars are right, R'lyeh rises and Cthulhu stirs. His
mere presence causes madness.
**Themes**: Cosmic indifference, the insignificance of humanity,
forbidden knowledge, deep time.

### Dagon [Lovecraft]
**Source**: "Dagon" (1919).
**Description**: An immense, Polyphemus-like creature that
rose from the sea. Vast and loathsome, it was a scaly, whale-
like thing that slithered across a slimy monolith-dotted shore.
Associated with deep ocean worship and the degeneration of
coastal communities.
**Themes**: Isolation, oceanic dread, encounters with the
incomprehensible.

### Deep Ones [Lovecraft]
**Source**: "The Shadow over Innsmouth" (1931 — written 1927).
**Description**: Amphibious humanoid creatures with greyish-
green skin, white bellies, webbed hands and feet, bulging
eyes, and gills. They dwell in vast cities beneath the ocean
and breed with humans, producing hybrid offspring that
gradually transform from human appearance into full Deep One
form. They worship Dagon and Cthulhu, offering gold and fish
in exchange for human mates.
**Themes**: Hereditary degeneration, hidden ancestry, coastal
decay, xenophobia.

### Elder Things [Lovecraft]
**Source**: "At the Mountains of Madness" (1936 — written 1931).
**Description**: Barrel-shaped beings roughly six feet long with
starfish-shaped heads and bases, five-ridged bodies, fan-like
folding wings, and clusters of tentacles. An incredibly ancient
race that arrived on Earth long before humanity, they built
great stone cities in Antarctica and created the shoggoths as
servants. They were scientists and artists of extraordinary
ability.
**Themes**: Deep time, the vastness of pre-human history,
science as hubris.

### Ghouls [Lovecraft]
**Source**: "Pickman's Model" (1927).
**Description**: Rubbery, loathsome creatures with canine faces,
mould-caked skin, and a hunched, roughly humanoid posture.
They inhabit warrens beneath graveyards and ancient cities,
feeding on human corpses. Some were once human. They are not
necessarily hostile but are deeply alien in their customs and
appetites.
**Themes**: The thin boundary between civilisation and
savagery, hidden worlds beneath the familiar.

### Mi-Go (Fungi from Yuggoth) [Lovecraft]
**Source**: "The Whisperer in Darkness" (1931 — written 1930).
**Description**: Pinkish, fungoid, crustacean-like beings about
five feet long with many-jointed limbs, clusters of pyramided
fleshy rings as heads, and membranous bat-like wings. They mine
rare minerals in the hills of Vermont and can fly through
interstellar space. They are capable of surgically removing
human brains and preserving them in metal cylinders for
transport across the cosmos.
**Themes**: Alien infiltration, rural isolation, the horror
of losing one's body.

### Nyarlathotep [Lovecraft]
**Source**: "Nyarlathotep" (1920).
**Description**: The Crawling Chaos, a being of a thousand
forms. In his first appearance he is described as a tall,
swarthy man resembling an ancient Egyptian pharaoh, who
travels from city to city demonstrating strange scientific
instruments that drive audiences to madness and riot. Unlike
other cosmic entities, Nyarlathotep walks among humanity and
takes apparent delight in driving mortals to despair.
**Themes**: The seductive face of cosmic horror, the
corruption of knowledge, entropy.

### Shoggoths [Lovecraft]
**Source**: "At the Mountains of Madness" (1936 — written 1931).
**Description**: Immense, protoplasmic masses capable of
shaping any limbs or organs at will — a shapeless congeries
of bubbling, protoplasmic matter, roughly fifteen feet in
diameter. Originally created by the Elder Things as mindless
slaves, they eventually developed intelligence and rebelled.
Their cry of "Tekeli-li!" echoes through the ruins of
Antarctic cities.
**Themes**: Creation turned against creator, mindless
servitude, formlessness as horror.

### Yog-Sothoth [Lovecraft]
**Source**: "The Dunwich Horror" (1929).
**Description**: An entity that is coterminous with all time
and space. In "The Dunwich Horror," Yog-Sothoth is the
progenitor of the Whateley twins — one nearly human, one
monstrous. The entity itself is described as a congeries of
iridescent globes, yet is also described as the gate and the
key, knowing the gate and being the gate. It exists outside
normal space-time and can grant forbidden knowledge to those
who summon it.
**Themes**: Forbidden procreation, hidden nature, the
permeability of dimensional barriers.

### The Colour Out of Space [Lovecraft]
**Source**: "The Colour Out of Space" (1927).
**Description**: Not a creature in any conventional sense but
an entity of pure alien colour — a hue not found in the
normal spectrum. It arrived in a meteorite that struck a farm
near Arkham, Massachusetts, and gradually drained the life
from the land, plants, animals, and finally the inhabitants.
It appeared to feed on life force itself, leaving behind grey,
brittle, crumbling remains.
**Themes**: Environmental corruption, slow inevitable decay,
the impossibility of understanding alien life.
