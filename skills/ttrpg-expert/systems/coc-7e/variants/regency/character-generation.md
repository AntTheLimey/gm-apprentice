# Regency Cthulhu — Character Generation

Regency (1811-1820) overlay for CoC 7e. Use alongside base
`../../character-generation.md` for core attribute generation,
derived values, and age modifiers. This file covers Regency-
specific social economics only.

---

## Occupational Bands and Credit Rating

In Regency, Credit Rating determines social class via occupational
bands. Check your occupation, find its band, then invest enough
skill points to meet the minimum CR for that band. You may spend
occupational or personal interest points on CR up to the band
maximum. See `occupations.md` for the full band table.

## Estate Standards

Roll 1D6. Ignore results above the investigator's means:
CR 50-69 (Poor Gentry): results 1-3. CR 70-82 (Average Gentry):
results 1-5. CR 83+ (Wealthy/Aristocracy): any result.

| 1D6 | Standard | Description |
|:---:|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Poor | Small estate or overcrowded with children. Small manor, few tenants. |
| 2 | Comfortable | Not much larger than poor but provides better income. Quaint. |
| 3 | Charming | Good social standing. Not large enough for sizable gatherings. |
| 4 | Large | True stately home. Can hold large events but not too many. |
| 5 | Impressive | Impeccably maintained. Must host at least one large gathering per year. |
| 6 | Grand | Very large house with expansive grounds. Among the wealthiest locally. |

## Reputation

Optional rule for gentry and those in high society.

**Starting Reputation** = (Credit Rating + Etiquette) ÷ 2. Maximum 99.

| Reputation | Society's Opinion | Effect |
|:----------:|-------------------|--------|
| 0 | Irredeemably wicked | Extreme difficulty rolls |
| 1-39 | A bad sort | Penalty die on social rolls |
| 40-59 | Respectable | No modifier |
| 60-98 | A credit to the Ton | Bonus die on social rolls |
| 99 | Positively angelic | Two bonus dice or automatic pass |
