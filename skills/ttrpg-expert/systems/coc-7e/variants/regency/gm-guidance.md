# Regency Cthulhu — GM Guidance

How running Regency (1811-1820) sessions differs from standard
1920s CoC. Use alongside base `../../session-procedures.md` for
core investigation structure (node-based, Three Clue Rule).

---

## Etiquette vs Credit Rating

The single biggest mechanical shift in Regency play.

- **Etiquette (INT/5)** replaces Credit Rating for social influence rolls
- **CR** still governs wealth and living standard — not social doors
- Use **Etiquette** for: introductions, forms of address, gaining acceptance in polite society, influencing gentry
- Use **CR** for: purchasing, demonstrating wealth, establishing living standard
- A wealthy merchant (high CR, low Etiquette) is snubbed at the assembly
- A poor gentleman (low CR, high Etiquette) is welcomed regardless

When a player asks "can my character talk their way in?" — ask which door they're at. A merchant's front door: CR. A countess's drawing room: Etiquette.

## Social Class as Investigation Barrier

Class structures create natural investigation obstacles and shortcuts.

- Gentry don't address servants directly — go through butler or housekeeper
- Lower-class characters can't approach gentry without a formal introduction
- Mixed-class parties: split by class for different investigation angles at the same location
- **Servants' hall** is a rich information source — servants overhear everything. Only accessible to lower-class investigators or via their own household staff
- Letters of introduction open doors; without one, even a gentleman may be turned away
- Tradesman characters have access to homes gentry never visit (back entrances, workshops, docks)

Run class barriers as nodes, not walls: there's always another angle, just a different one.

## The Season

The London social season (roughly February–July) is the primary gentry gathering.

- Balls, assemblies, card parties, morning calls — investigation opportunities equivalent to 1920s clubs and speakeasies
- Country house parties (summer/autumn) provide isolated settings ideal for horror scenarios
- Social events have strict protocols — arriving late, leaving early, or behaving badly costs Reputation (see `character-generation.md`)
- Out of season: London is thin of company. Useful for keeping investigators away from resources and witnesses

Use events as scene frames: the ball is the location, the guests are the witnesses, the ballroom is the crime scene.

## Investigation Without Modern Tools

No forensics, phones, or psychiatry. Adjust your clue design accordingly.

- **No forensics or fingerprinting** — rely on Library Use, personal contacts, Spot Hidden, and testimony
- **No telephones** — communication by letter (days of delay between London and country). Express riders or personal travel for urgency
- **Travel by carriage** — see `equipment.md` for speeds. London to Bath is a full day; cross-country trips take multiple days. Distance is a real obstacle
- **No Psychoanalysis** — use Reassure (APP/5) for Sanity recovery; see `skills.md`
- **Medicine is limited** — no modern diagnostics. Leeches and laudanum are standard. Medicine rolls for wounds; little else is treatable
- **Newspapers exist but are slow and expensive** — The Morning Post and The Times are the main sources. Information in print is days old

Design clues around what exists: gossip, letters, account books, physical evidence observed by witnesses.

## Period Narration Tips

Small details reinforce the period and make Etiquette rolls feel earned.

- Address people by title and surname in company — never first name unless intimate friends. "Mr Darcy", "Miss Bennet", "Lady Catherine"
- Getting forms of address wrong is an automatic Etiquette failure, no roll needed
- Describe costume using terms from `equipment.md` glossary — empire line gowns, cravats, spencers, pelisses
- **Dancing is social currency** — a dance is a conversation; a refused dance without cause is an insult; a second dance with the same partner signals attachment
- **Reputation matters** — track social consequences using the Reputation mechanic in `character-generation.md`
- Weather and walking are significant — ladies walk for exercise and social visibility; gentlemen ride. Both are observation opportunities

When in doubt: narrate what people are wearing, who is watching, and what everyone already knows about the investigators.
