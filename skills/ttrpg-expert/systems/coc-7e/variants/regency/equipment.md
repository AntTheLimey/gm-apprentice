## Costume Glossary

Period terms for narration. Use these when describing what characters wear.

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Bonnet | Peaked women's headgear, worn on back of head, often tied with ribbon |
| Breeches | Male waist-to-knee garment, fastened with buttons or buckles |
| Chemise | Plain short-sleeved white cotton undergarment (also "shift") |
| Chemisette | Sleeveless under-blouse filling neckline of empire line gown |
| Cravat | Men's necktie; white linen, silk, or cambric; many tying styles |
| Empire line | High-waisted gown with long flowing skirt and fitted bodice |
| Fichu | Triangular shawl worn across shoulders, preserves modesty |
| Frock coat | Tailored men's coat with tails; "dress coat" most common |
| Mittens (mitts) | Fingerless gloves with half-thumb, various fabrics |
| Mob cap | Soft headgear for married women, mothers, widows, servants |
| Pantaloons | Ankle-length trousers, held with stirrups, popularised by Brummell |
| Pelisse | Long-sleeved high-waisted full-length outdoor coat |
| Petticoat | Worn over chemise and stays; hem decorated with ruffles or lace |
| Reticule | Small drawstring handbag; essential with empire line fabrics |
| Spencer | Short high-waisted jacket complementing empire line |
| Stays | Type of corset; "short stays" ending below bust most common |
| Stockings | Silk or wool hose, held up with garters |
| Turban, draped | Fashionable women's headgear, could be decorated with feathers and jewels |
| Waistcoat | Single or double-breasted male garment; silk, satin, or wool |
