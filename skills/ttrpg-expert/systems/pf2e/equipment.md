# Pathfinder 2e (Remaster) — Equipment

Currency, weapons, armor, shields, gear, and fundamental runes from
Pathfinder Player Core (Remaster). Game numbers (prices, damage, traits,
Bulk) are reproduced; all descriptions are paraphrased.

## Currency

| Coin | Abbr | Value |
|------|------|-------|
| Copper piece | cp | 1/10 sp |
| Silver piece | sp | standard unit (10 cp) |
| Gold piece | gp | 10 sp / 100 cp |
| Platinum piece | pp | 10 gp / 100 sp |

Silver is the baseline unit. Gems, art, and raw crafting materials trade at
full Price; most other goods sell for half. 1,000 coins count as 1 Bulk.

**Starting wealth:** a new PC begins with 15 gp to spend on gear.

## Bulk

Bulk measures how heavy and awkward an item is to haul. Rough guide: 5–10 lb
is 1 Bulk, a few ounces is negligible, in between is light.

- **L** = light; ten light items equal 1 Bulk (round fractions down).
- **—** = negligible; ignore unless carrying huge quantities.
- Carry up to **5 + Str modifier** Bulk freely. Beyond that you are
  encumbered (clumsy 1, −10 ft Speed). Hard cap is **10 + Str modifier**.

## Key Weapon Traits

| Trait | Effect (paraphrased) |
|-------|----------------------|
| Agile | Multiple-attack penalty is −4/−8 instead of −5/−10. |
| Finesse | May roll Dexterity instead of Strength to attack in melee (damage still Strength). |
| Deadly (dX) | On a crit, add one die of the listed size (more dice with greater/major striking). |
| Fatal (dX) | On a crit, your damage die becomes the listed size and you add one extra die of it. |
| Forceful | Each repeat attack this turn gains bonus damage as momentum builds. |
| Sweep | +1 to attack if you already struck a different target with it this turn. |
| Reach | Strike foes up to 10 ft away. |
| Thrown (X ft.) | Can be hurled; adds your Strength to damage like a melee hit. |
| Versatile (B/P/S) | May deal the listed alternate damage type instead of its normal one. |
| Two-hand (dX) | Wielded in two hands, its damage die becomes the listed size. |
| Propulsive | Adds half your Strength to ranged damage (full if negative). |
| Backswing | After a miss, gain +1 to your next attack with it this turn. |
| Parry | Spend an action to raise it for +1 circumstance AC until your next turn. |
| Disarm / Trip / Shove | Attempt that Athletics maneuver with the weapon even without a free hand. |
| Nonlethal | Knocks out rather than kills; strike to kill at a −2 penalty. |
| Free-hand | Occupies no hand and can't be Disarmed, but can't attack if that hand is busy. |
| Volley (X ft.) | −2 penalty against targets within the listed close range. |
| Concealable | +2 to Stealth to hide or conceal the weapon. |

## Armor

Unarmored characters and mages typically wear **explorer's clothing**
(1 sp; +0 AC, +5 Dex cap, L Bulk, Cloth, comfort) — it uses unarmored
proficiency but can hold potency runes.

AC while wearing armor = 10 + Dex modifier (capped) + proficiency +
armor's item bonus + other. Meeting an armor's Strength value removes its
check penalty and reduces the Speed penalty by 5 ft.

half plate, and full plate are heavy. Chain armor is flexible and noisy;
full plate has bulwark (flat +3 to Reflex vs area damage instead of Dex).

## Shields

A shield only grants its AC bonus while Raised (an action). Its Hardness
reduces damage on a Shield Block; the shield is broken at its Broken
Threshold (BT) and destroyed at 0 HP.

| Shield | Price | AC | Speed | Bulk | Hardness | HP (BT) |
|--------|-------|-----|-------|------|----------|---------|
| Buckler | 1 gp | +1 | — | L | 3 | 6 (3) |
| Wooden shield | 1 gp | +2 | — | 1 | 3 | 12 (6) |
| Steel shield | 2 gp | +2 | — | 1 | 5 | 20 (10) |
| Tower shield | 10 gp | +2/+4 | −5 ft. | 4 | 5 | 20 (10) |

A tower shield gives +4 AC if you also Take Cover behind it. A buckler
frees the hand; other shields must be strapped and held. A shield can't be
runed, but you can bolt on a shield boss or shield spikes (each 5 sp,
1d6 B/P) to attack with it.

## Fundamental Runes

Runes are how mundane gear scales into magic equipment. A weapon needs a
**weapon potency** rune before it can hold a **striking** rune; armor needs
an **armor potency** rune before a **resilient** rune. Prices climb steeply
with item level; these are the must-have upgrades martial characters chase.

**Weapon potency** — item bonus to attack rolls plus property-rune slots:

| Rune | Item Level | Effect |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Weapon potency (+1) | 2 | +1 to attack, 1 property-rune slot |
| Weapon potency (+2) | 10 | +2 to attack, 2 slots |
| Weapon potency (+3) | 16 | +3 to attack, 3 slots |

**Striking** — extra weapon damage dice (same die size as the weapon):

| Rune | Item Level | Damage Dice |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Striking | 4 | 2 dice |
| Greater striking | 12 | 3 dice |
| Major striking | 19 | 4 dice |

So a *+2 greater striking longsword* rolls 3d8 on a hit and adds +2 to
attack rolls.

**Armor potency** — item bonus to AC plus property-rune slots:

| Rune | Item Level | Effect |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Armor potency (+1) | 5 | +1 AC, 1 property-rune slot |
| Armor potency (+2) | 11 | +2 AC, 2 slots |
| Armor potency (+3) | 18 | +3 AC, 3 slots |

**Resilient** — item bonus to all saving throws:

| Rune | Item Level | Saves |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Resilient | 8 | +1 |
| Greater resilient | 14 | +2 |
| Major resilient | 20 | +3 |

## Alchemical Items

Bombs, elixirs, mutagens, and poisons make up the largest slice of the
equipment set — hundreds of files such as `acid-flask-lesser`,
`elixir-of-life-lesser`, and `antidote-lesser`, each keyed by tier
(lesser/moderate/greater/major/true). A 1st-level PC can typically start
with lesser bombs (acid flask, alchemist's fire; 3 gp each), lesser elixirs
(antidote, antiplague, minor elixir of life; 3 gp each), and simple tools
(glow rod, smoke ball; 3 gp each). Full entries live in GM Core, not Player
Core; look them up by name in the equipment data for exact effects and DCs.
