# Pathfinder 2e (Remaster) — Archetype Feats

Archetypes let any character branch outside their class by spending
class-feat slots on a shared feat family. This file curates the core
multiclass archetypes — one per Player Core / Player Core 2 class — with a
dedication entry plus two or three signature follow-ups each. Effects are
paraphrased summaries; consult the sourcebook for full rules text.

## How archetypes work

- **Dedication first.** Every archetype opens with a **Dedication** feat
  (a level-2+ class feat with the `dedication` and `archetype` traits).
  Multiclass dedications gate on the target class's key attribute at
  **14+** (e.g., Strength 14 for Barbarian, Charisma 14 for Bard). Taking
  a dedication grants a slice of that class: trained proficiencies, a class
  DC, and a signature feature or spellcasting foundation.
- **The two-more-feats rule.** Once you take a dedication, you must earn
  **at least two more feats from that same archetype** before you may take
  a *different* multiclass dedication. This stops characters from
  collecting dedications without committing.
- **Follow-ups need the dedication.** Every later feat in an archetype
  lists its Dedication as a prerequisite. "Basic" feats hand you a low-level
  class feat; "Advanced" feats grant a higher one, treating your effective
  class level as half your character level for meeting prerequisites.
- **Multiclass vs. standalone.** *Multiclass* archetypes (below) mirror the
  core classes and let you graft on spellcasting or martial tricks.
  *Standalone* archetypes (Assassin, Pirate, Blessed One, and hundreds
  more) grant themed abilities with no matching class — same dedication and
  two-feats rules apply.

In the tables, "Dedication" in the Prereq column means the archetype's own
dedication feat; a named feat means that specific earlier feat is required.

## Spellcasting multiclass archetypes

Each grants a slim version of the class's casting: a small repertoire or
prepared list, trained spell attack/DC, and access to the tradition.
Basic → Expert → Master Spellcasting feats (levels 4/12/18) widen your slots
over time.

### Bard (occult, Charisma)

| Feat | Level | Prereq | Effect |
|------|-------|--------|--------|
| **Bard Dedication** | 2 | Cha 14 | Cast occult spells off a 2-cantrip repertoire; trained in Occultism, Performance, and spell attack/DC; pick a muse for its feats only. |
| **Basic Muse's Whispers** | 4 | Dedication | Take a 1st- or 2nd-level bard feat. |
| **Basic Bard Spellcasting** | 4 | Dedication | Open bard archetype spell slots; add an occult spell to your repertoire at each new rank. |

### Cleric (divine, Wisdom)

| Feat | Level | Prereq | Effect |
|------|-------|--------|--------|
| **Cleric Dedication** | 2 | Wis 14 | Prepare 2 divine cantrips daily; trained in Religion + your deity's skill and spell attack/DC; bound by that deity's anathema. |
| **Basic Dogma** | 4 | Dedication | Take a 1st- or 2nd-level cleric feat. |
| **Basic Cleric Spellcasting** | 4 | Dedication | Open cleric archetype spell slots and prepare deity spells in them. |

### Druid (primal, Wisdom)

| Feat | Level | Prereq | Effect |
|------|-------|--------|--------|
| **Druid Dedication** | 2 | Wis 14 | Prepare 2 primal cantrips daily; learn Wildsong; pick an order for its feats; trained in Nature and spell attack/DC; bound by druidic anathema. |
| **Order Spell** | 4 | Dedication | Gain your order's initial focus spell and a focus pool you refill by communing with nature. |
| **Basic Druid Spellcasting** | 4 | Dedication | Open druid archetype spell slots. |

### Sorcerer (bloodline tradition, Charisma)

| **Basic Blood Potency** | 4 | Dedication | Take a 1st- or 2nd-level sorcerer feat. |
| **Basic Sorcerer Spellcasting** | 4 | Dedication | Open sorcerer archetype spell slots; add a bloodline-tradition spell at each new rank. |

### Witch (patron tradition, Intelligence)

| Feat | Level | Prereq | Effect |

| **Basic Witch Spellcasting** | 4 | Dedication | Open witch archetype spell slots; add two spells of each new rank to your familiar. |

## Martial and skill multiclass archetypes

These graft weapons, combat features, or skill tricks onto any build.
"Resiliency" feats stack extra Hit Points per archetype feat you hold.

### Barbarian (Strength)

| Feat | Level | Prereq | Effect |
|------|-------|--------|--------|
| **Barbarian Dedication** | 2 | Str 14 | Trained in Athletics and barbarian class DC; gain Rage (−1 AC while raging); pick an instinct, bound by its anathema but without its perks. |
| **Barbarian Resiliency** | 4 | Dedication | +3 HP for every barbarian-archetype feat you hold, growing as you add more. |
| **Basic Fury** | 4 | Dedication | Take a 1st- or 2nd-level barbarian feat. |
| **Advanced Fury** | 6 | Basic Fury | Take a barbarian feat, counting your barbarian level as half your character level. |

### Champion (Strength)

| **Champion Resiliency** | 4 | Dedication | +3 HP per champion-archetype feat you hold. |
| **Champion's Reaction** | 6 | Dedication | Gain the champion reaction tied to your chosen cause. |

## Coverage note

This curates 15 of roughly 326 Player Core and Player Core 2 archetype
feats — the multiclass dedications for the core classes plus their most-used
follow-ups. The remaining archetypes (standalone concepts like Assassin,
Blessed One, Beastmaster, Aldori Duelist, and every higher-tier
spellcasting and combat feat) live in the ORC dataset under
`data/feats/archetype/`.
