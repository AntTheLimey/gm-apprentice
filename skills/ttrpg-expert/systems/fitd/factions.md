# Forged in the Dark -- Factions of Doskvol

*GM activities: Prep, Track, Build*

Factions are the living power structures of the city. Each has
a Tier (0-VI), hold (weak/strong), and long-term goals tracked
with clocks. The GM advances faction clocks during PC downtime.
PCs may directly affect faction clocks through their scores.

## Faction Status Quick Reference

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| +3 Allies | Will help even at cost; expect the same |
| +2 Friendly | Will help if no serious problems |
| +1 Helpful | Will help if no cost |
| 0 Neutral | Default starting status |
| -1 Interfering | Look for free trouble |
| -2 Hostile | Actively seek to hurt you |
| -3 War | Go out of their way, even at cost |

## The Underworld

### The Hive
- **Tier**: IV, Strong
- **Description**: The most powerful criminal organization in
  the city. They control much of the drug and vice trade and
  have deep connections to corrupt officials.
- **Notable NPCs**: Djera Maha (leader, ruthless and
  calculating)
- **Operations**: Drug trade, vice dens, protection rackets,
  political corruption
- **Typical actions**: Squeeze smaller gangs, expand territory,
  bribe officials

### The Crows
- **Tier**: II, Strong
- **Description**: An old gang that controls much of Crow's
  Foot. They run protection rackets, smuggling, and illegal
  gambling. Under the leadership of Lyssa, they're aggressive
  and territorial.
- **Notable NPCs**: Lyssa (leader, fierce and ambitious),
  Roric (former leader, murdered)
- **Operations**: Extortion, gambling, smuggling
- **Typical actions**: Defend territory, eliminate rivals,
  expand into new rackets

### The Lampblacks
- **Tier**: II, Weak
- **Description**: A gang of rough criminals who operate in
  Crow's Foot. Former laborers turned to crime. Led by Bazso
  Baz, they're in a bitter rivalry with the Red Sashes.
- **Notable NPCs**: Bazso Baz (leader, charismatic brute),
  Pickett (lieutenant, cunning)
- **Operations**: Extortion, sabotage, arson, street crime
- **Typical actions**: Fight the Red Sashes, recruit muscle,
  expand influence

### The Red Sashes
- **Tier**: II, Weak
- **Description**: An Iruvian sword-fighting school turned
  criminal gang. Elegant and deadly, they deal in high-quality
  vice and have connections to Iruvian nobility. At war with
  the Lampblacks.
- **Notable NPCs**: Mylera Klev (leader, refined and ruthless)
- **Operations**: Drug dealing (refined product), dueling
  academy, vice trade
- **Typical actions**: Undermine the Lampblacks, cultivate
  noble patrons, import Iruvian goods

### The Billhooks
- **Tier**: II, Strong
- **Description**: A brutal gang that controls territory in
  Coalridge. Known for extreme violence and intimidation.
  They run labor rackets and protection schemes.
- **Notable NPCs**: Tarvul (leader, vicious patriarch),
  Coran and Bryl (sons, ambitious)
- **Operations**: Extortion, labor rackets, butchery (as
  front), disposal of bodies
- **Typical actions**: Intimidate rivals, control workers,
  expand through violence

### The Fog Hounds
- **Tier**: I, Strong
- **Description**: A small but capable crew of smugglers who
  operate around Silkshore and the canals. They specialize in
  discreet transport of people and goods.
- **Notable NPCs**: the leader (resourceful, well-connected)
- **Operations**: Smuggling, transport, information brokering
- **Typical actions**: Run contraband, ferry passengers, sell
  intelligence

### The Gray Cloaks
- **Tier**: II, Weak
- **Description**: An organization of thieves and spies. They
  operate quietly and value subtlety over brute force. Known
  for excellent intelligence gathering.
- **Notable NPCs**: the leader (mysterious, cautious)
- **Operations**: Burglary, espionage, blackmail, information
  trade
- **Typical actions**: Gather secrets, sell information, steal
  valuable items

### The Wraiths
- **Tier**: I, Strong
- **Description**: A gang of skilled burglars. They specialize
  in breaking into well-protected locations. Quiet and
  professional.
- **Notable NPCs**: the leader (methodical, patient)
- **Operations**: Burglary, infiltration, lockbreaking
- **Typical actions**: Case targets, execute precision heists

### The Unseen
- **Tier**: IV, Weak
- **Description**: A secretive organization of assassins and
  spies. Their existence is only rumored. They take contracts
  from the wealthy and powerful.
- **Notable NPCs**: Unknown (the organization's leadership is
  deeply hidden)
- **Operations**: Assassination, espionage, sabotage
- **Typical actions**: Execute contracts, maintain secrecy,
  manipulate from the shadows

### The Silver Nails
- **Tier**: I, Strong
- **Description**: A crew of mercenaries and soldiers of
  fortune. They hire out for dangerous jobs and have a
  reputation for competence.
- **Notable NPCs**: the leader (veteran, pragmatic)
- **Operations**: Mercenary work, bodyguard services, bounty
  hunting
- **Typical actions**: Take contracts, protect clients, hunt
  targets

## Institutions

### The Bluecoats
- **Tier**: III, Strong
- **Description**: The city watch and police force of Doskvol.
  Corrupt and brutal, they maintain order primarily through
  violence and bribes. Many officers are on criminal payrolls.
- **Notable NPCs**: Commander (politically shrewd)
- **Operations**: Law enforcement, patrol, arrest, collecting
  bribes
- **Typical actions**: Shake down criminals, take bribes, make
  arrests for political purposes

### The Inspectors
- **Tier**: III, Strong
- **Description**: The investigative branch of law enforcement.
  More competent and less corrupt than the Bluecoats, they
  build cases against criminal crews. They answer to the
  magistrates.
- **Notable NPCs**: various inspectors assigned to cases
- **Operations**: Investigation, surveillance, prosecution
- **Typical actions**: Build cases, question witnesses, pursue
  evidence, issue warrants

### The Spirit Wardens
- **Tier**: IV, Strong
- **Description**: The masked guardians who collect and destroy
  the ghosts of the dead. They enforce the cremation laws and
  are feared and respected by all. Their true nature and
  methods are mysterious.
- **Notable NPCs**: Unknown publicly (they wear masks)
- **Operations**: Ghost collection, cremation enforcement,
  arcane containment
- **Typical actions**: Respond to hauntings, enforce cremation,
  investigate spirit disturbances

### The Imperial Military
- **Tier**: VI, Strong
- **Description**: The armed forces of the Imperium. They
  garrison the city but are primarily concerned with external
  threats and maintaining the Emperor's authority. They don't
  typically interfere in local crime.
- **Notable NPCs**: various officers and commanders
- **Operations**: Defense, imperial enforcement, leviathan
  hunter escort
- **Typical actions**: Maintain order, guard key installations,
  project imperial power

### Ironhook Prison
- **Tier**: IV, Strong
- **Description**: The city's main prison. Overcrowded, brutal,
  and corrupt. Gangs operate inside as openly as outside.
  Prison claims and the incarceration system are key mechanics.
- **Notable NPCs**: the Warden, various guards
- **Operations**: Incarceration, punishment, forced labor
- **Typical actions**: Process inmates, extract information,
  accept bribes

## The Elite

### City Council
- **Tier**: V, Strong
- **Description**: The governing body of Doskvol. Composed of
  wealthy merchants and minor nobles. They set laws and
  policies, usually to benefit the rich.
- **Notable NPCs**: various council members
- **Operations**: Legislation, taxation, civic projects
- **Typical actions**: Pass laws, award contracts, levy taxes,
  pursue political agendas

### Lord Scurlock
- **Tier**: III, Strong
- **Description**: A reclusive noble of great age and dark
  reputation. Rumored to be a vampire or worse. He maintains
  a vast estate and has connections throughout the city's
  underworld and elite.
- **Notable NPCs**: Lord Scurlock himself
- **Operations**: Occult research, political manipulation,
  collecting rare artifacts
- **Typical actions**: Pursue arcane goals, manipulate factions,
  acquire occult objects

### The Dimmer Sisters
- **Tier**: III, Strong
- **Description**: A pair of reclusive occultist sisters who
  maintain a mysterious manor in Barrowcleft. They deal in
  ghosts, arcane artifacts, and forbidden knowledge.
- **Notable NPCs**: The sisters themselves
- **Operations**: Spirit trafficking, occult services, arcane
  research
- **Typical actions**: Collect spirits, sell occult services,
  pursue arcane experiments

### The Brigmore Witches
- **Tier**: I, Weak
- **Description**: A small coven of witches and occultists.
  They practice forbidden rituals and consort with dark powers.
  They keep to the shadows.
- **Notable NPCs**: the coven leader
- **Operations**: Ritual sorcery, spirit dealing, curse work
- **Typical actions**: Perform rituals, deal with spirits,
  offer occult services

## Labor and Trade

### The Dockers
- **Tier**: III, Strong
- **Description**: The longshoremen's guild. They control the
  docks and all shipping labor. Powerful through sheer numbers
  and control of commerce.
- **Notable NPCs**: the guild master (tough negotiator)
- **Operations**: Dock labor, shipping oversight, smuggling
  facilitation
- **Typical actions**: Strike, negotiate, provide or withhold
  labor, facilitate smuggling

### The Gondoliers
- **Tier**: III, Strong
- **Description**: The guild that controls all canal transport
  in the city. They know every waterway and tunnel. They also
  serve a secret role in managing the things that live beneath
  the canals.
- **Notable NPCs**: the guild master
- **Operations**: Canal transport, waterway maintenance, hidden
  services
- **Typical actions**: Transport people and goods, maintain
  canals, guard underwater secrets

### The Sparkwrights
- **Tier**: IV, Strong
- **Description**: The engineers and technicians who maintain
  the lightning barrier and the city's electroplasmic
  infrastructure. Highly valued and well-protected.
- **Notable NPCs**: the chief engineer
- **Operations**: Barrier maintenance, electroplasmic
  engineering, infrastructure
- **Typical actions**: Maintain the barrier, develop new
  technology, repair infrastructure

### The Leviathan Hunters
- **Tier**: V, Strong
- **Description**: The massive ships and their crews who venture
  into the void sea to hunt leviathans for their blood. The
  lifeblood of the city's economy. Dangerous, lucrative work.
- **Notable NPCs**: various ship captains
- **Operations**: Leviathan hunting, electroplasm processing,
  void sea exploration
- **Typical actions**: Launch hunting expeditions, process
  leviathan blood, trade electroplasm

## Religious

### The Church of the Ecstasy of the Flesh
- **Tier**: IV, Strong
- **Description**: The dominant religious institution. They
  preach that life is short and should be lived fully. In
  practice, they are politically powerful, wealthy, and
  often corrupt.
- **Notable NPCs**: various high priests and priestesses
- **Operations**: Religious services, political influence,
  charity (as cover), vice
- **Typical actions**: Exert political influence, accumulate
  wealth, preach and proselytize

### The Path of Echoes
- **Tier**: II, Weak
- **Description**: A heretical cult that worships the dead and
  seeks communion with spirits. They believe the dead carry
  sacred wisdom. Persecuted by the Spirit Wardens.
- **Notable NPCs**: the cult leader
- **Operations**: Spirit worship, forbidden rituals, recruiting
  converts
- **Typical actions**: Hold secret services, protect spirits
  from the Wardens, recruit new members

### The Reconciled
- **Tier**: I, Weak
- **Description**: A group of ghosts who have retained their
  sanity and identity after death. They seek peaceful
  coexistence with the living and resist the Spirit Wardens'
  destruction of all spirits.
- **Notable NPCs**: various ghost elders
- **Operations**: Self-preservation, negotiation with the
  living, hiding from Spirit Wardens
- **Typical actions**: Avoid the Wardens, seek allies among
  the living, maintain their community

## Using Factions in Play

### Starting Crew Relationships

During crew creation, faction status is established through:
- Hunting grounds (paying the claiming faction or not)
- Crew upgrades (+1 status with a helper, -2 with a harmed
  faction)
- Favorite contact (+1/-1 status with related factions)

### The Faction Turn

Between sessions, the GM:
1. Selects 2-4 factions currently relevant to the story
2. Advances their project clocks (fortune roll using Tier if
   uncertain)
3. Chooses 1-2 downtime maneuvers for each
4. Shares relevant news through PC contacts and vice purveyors

### Faction Clock Examples

- The Lampblacks: "Destroy the Red Sashes" (8-clock)
- The Bluecoats: "Major crackdown on Crow's Foot" (6-clock)
- Lord Scurlock: "Acquire the Kotar ghost artifact" (8-clock)
- The Hive: "Absorb the Fog Hounds" (6-clock)
