# Forged in the Dark -- Entanglements

*GM activities: Run, Track*

Entanglements represent the complex background of debts,
rivals, and complications in the crew's life. After each score,
once payoff and heat are determined, the GM generates an
entanglement.

## Heat Generation

Heat is acquired after every score based on exposure:

| Base Exposure | Heat |
|--------------|:----:|
| Smooth and quiet; low exposure | 0 |
| Contained; standard exposure | 2 |
| Loud and chaotic; high exposure | 4 |
| Wild; devastating exposure | 6 |

### Additional Heat Factors

| Factor | Heat |
|--------|:----:|
| High-profile or well-connected target | +1 |
| Situation on hostile turf | +1 |
| Crew is at war with another faction | +1 |
| Killing was involved (by anyone) | +2 |

Mark heat on the crew's heat tracker (9 boxes). When heat
reaches 9, gain 1 wanted level and clear heat. Excess heat
rolls over to the new track.

## Wanted Level

| Level | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| 0 | Minimal law enforcement attention |
| 1 | Regular attention; minor patrols |
| 2 | Active investigation; significant response |
| 3 | Serious pursuit; large-scale operations |
| 4 | Maximum response; the city's full force |

Higher wanted level means law enforcement sends higher quality
and scale forces. The only way to reduce wanted level is
through **incarceration** (see rules-reference.md).

## Entanglement Table

Roll dice equal to wanted level. If wanted level is 0, roll 2d
and keep the lowest result.

| Result | Heat 0-3 | Heat 4-5 | Heat 6+ |
|--------|----------|----------|---------|
| 1-3 | Gang Trouble or The Usual Suspects | Gang Trouble or Questioning | Flipped or Interrogation |
| 4-5 | Rivals or Unquiet Dead | Reprisals or Unquiet Dead | Demonic Notice or Show of Force |
| 6 | Cooperation | Show of Force | Arrest |

The GM chooses which option in each cell best fits the current
fiction. Bring the entanglement into play immediately or hold
until an appropriate moment.

**Entanglements manifest fully before the PCs can act.** The
PCs deal with the aftermath; they can't intercept and defuse
it before it happens. This is the cost of doing business in
the underworld.

## The 12 Entanglements

### Arrest

An Inspector presents evidence to a magistrate and sends a
detail to arrest you (a gang at least equal in scale to your
wanted level).

**Resolution options:**
- Pay coin equal to wanted level + 3 (bribe)
- Hand someone over for arrest (clears your heat)
- Try to evade capture

### Cooperation

A +3 status faction asks you for a favor.

**Resolution options:**
- Agree to do the favor
- Refuse: forfeit 1 rep per Tier of the friendly faction
- Refuse harder: lose 1 status with them

If you don't have a +3 faction status, you avoid entanglements
this time.

### Demonic Notice

A demon approaches the crew with a dark offer.

**Resolution options:**
- Accept their bargain (consequences TBD)
- Hide until it loses interest: forfeit 3 rep
- Deal with it another way

### Flipped

One of the PCs' rivals arranges for one of your contacts,
patrons, clients, or customers to switch allegiances due to
the heat on you. They're loyal to another faction now.

**Resolution:** This is a loss. Determine which contact or
client is flipped and adjust relationships accordingly. You
may attempt to win them back as a score or long-term project.

### Gang Trouble

One of your gangs (or other cohorts) causes trouble due to
their flaw(s).

**Resolution options:**
- Lose face: forfeit rep equal to your Tier + 1
- Make an example of one of the gang members
- Face reprisals from the wronged party

### Interrogation

The officers round up one of the PCs to question them about
the crew's crimes.

**Resolution options:**
- Pay 3 coin (bribe them off)
- Take the beating: suffer level 2 harm AND tell them what they
  want to know (+3 heat). You may resist each consequence
  separately.

### Questioning

The cops grab an NPC member of your crew or one of the crew's
contacts to question about your crimes.

**Resolution options:**
- Pay 2 coin to get them released
- Let it play out: make a fortune roll to see how much they
  talk (1-3: +2 heat, 4-5: +1 heat). Roll 2d for a normal
  person, 3-4d for an experienced underworld type, 0-1d for
  someone soft or loyal to the law.

### Reprisals

An enemy faction makes a move against you (or a friend,
contact, or vice purveyor).

**Resolution options:**
- Pay them 1 rep and 1 coin per Tier of the enemy (apology)
- Allow them to mess with you or yours
- Fight back and show them who's boss

### Rivals

A neutral faction throws their weight around. They threaten
you, a friend, a contact, or a vice purveyor.

**Resolution options:**
- Forfeit 1 rep or 1 coin per Tier of the rival
- Stand up to them: lose 1 status with them

### Show of Force

A faction with whom you have negative status makes a play
against your holdings.

**Resolution options:**
- Give them 1 claim
- Go to war: drop to -3 status

If you have no claims, lose 1 hold instead.

### Unquiet Dead

A rogue spirit is drawn to you -- perhaps a past victim.

**Resolution options:**
- Acquire the services of an occultist (downtime activity: roll
  NPC quality as fortune roll)
- Deal with it yourself (action roll, likely Attune)

### The Usual Suspects

The cops grab someone in the periphery of your crew. One
player volunteers a friend or vice purveyor as the person
most likely taken.

**Resolution options:**
- Pay 1 coin to get them released
- Let it play out: make a fortune roll (1-3: +2 heat, 4-5:
  level 2 harm to the NPC)

## Incarceration

When someone is convicted for your crew's crimes (crew member,
friend, contact, or framed enemy), your wanted level drops by
1 and you clear heat.

### Prison Sentences

| Wanted Level | Sentence |
|-------------|----------|
| 4 | Life or execution |
| 3 | A year or two |
| 2 | Several months |
| 1 | A month or two |
| 0 | A few weeks, or a beating (level 3 harm, no resistance) |

### Incarceration Roll

Roll dice equal to crew Tier:

| Result | Outcome |
|--------|---------|
| Critical | +3 rep, 1 prison claim, +1 faction status |
| 6 | 1 prison claim, +1 faction status |
| 4-5 | Keep your head down; no incident |
| 1-3 | Horrific; suffer a level of trauma |

### Prison Claims

| Claim | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| Allied Claim | Boon from an ally's faction (claim from different crew type, not turf) |
| Cell Block Control | Never take trauma from incarceration |
| Guard Payoff | +1d to Tier roll when incarcerated |
| Hardcase | Crew Tier advances cost 2 fewer coin |
| Parole Influence | Serve time as if wanted level were 1 lower |
| Smuggling | +2 load while incarcerated (take twice for 4); may carry coin for bribes |

### Prison Claim Map

```
[Parole Influence] --- [Smuggling] --- [Allied Claim]    [Cell Block Control]
        |                   |               |                     |
  [Guard Payoff]  --- [ PRISON ] --- [Guard Payoff] --- [Allied Claim]
        |                   |               |                     |
    [Hardcase]     --- [Smuggling] --- [Allied Claim] --- [Allied Claim]
```
