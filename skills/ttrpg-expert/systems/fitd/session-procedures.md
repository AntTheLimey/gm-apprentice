## The Core Loop

```
Free Play -> Score Setup -> Engagement Roll -> Score Execution
    -> Payoff & Heat -> Entanglements -> Downtime -> Free Play
```

## Typical Session Flow

1. **Free play** (15-20 min): roleplay, gather info
2. **Score setup** (5-10 min): target, plan type, detail
3. **Engagement roll** (2 min): opening position
4. **Score execution** (60-90 min): actions, flashbacks, clocks
5. **Payoff and heat** (5 min): rewards, consequences
6. **Entanglements** (5-10 min): complications
7. **Downtime** (20-30 min): two activities per PC
8. **Faction updates** (between sessions): advance clocks

## Score Setup

### Six Plan Types

| Plan | Detail | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| Assault | Point of attack | "Front gate at midnight" |
| Deception | Method | "Disguised as inspectors" |
| Stealth | Point of infiltration | "Through the sewers" |
| Occult | Arcane method | "Using the ghost key" |
| Social | Connection | "Lord Strangford's invitation" |
| Transport | Route and means | "Gondola through Nightmarket" |

Don't over-plan. Plan type + detail are sufficient. Flashbacks
handle everything else. Each player chooses load (light/normal/
heavy) — not individual items, just maximum access level.

## Engagement Roll

Fortune roll starting from 1d:

| Factor | Mod |
|--------|:---:|
| Bold/daring plan | +1d |
| Overly complex/contingent | -1d |
| Detail exposes vulnerability | +1d |
| Target strongest vs this approach | -1d |
| Friends/contacts aid | +1d |
| Enemies/rivals interfere | -1d |
| Other advantage (e.g. lower-Tier target) | +1d |
| Other disadvantage (e.g. higher-Tier) | -1d |

| Result | Opening Position |
|:---:|-----------------|
| Crit | Controlled; first obstacle overcome |
| 6 | Controlled |
| 4-5 | Risky |
| 1-3 | Desperate |

Cut to first serious obstacle. Don't narrate approach.
Engagement sets starting position only; subsequent rolls
follow normal position rules.

## Flashbacks

| Stress | Situation |
|:---:|-----------|
| 0 | Ordinary action, easy opportunity |
| 1 | Complex action or unlikely opportunity |
| 2+ | Elaborate action, special contingencies |

Cannot undo established facts; CAN recontextualize them
("I bribed that guard" is valid; "that guard was never there"
is not). Downtime activities in flashbacks cost 1 coin or
1 rep. May require action/fortune rolls. Failed flashbacks
produce present consequences (the bribe happened, but the
guard reported it — now there's a complication).

Best use: when the engagement roll goes badly, flashback to
a preparation you made "just in case." Stress is self-limiting
(max 9); raise cost to 2 if flashbacks trivialise problems.

## Linked Plans

1. **Setup maneuver:** diversion as part of plan. Success
   improves position/effect. Failure may give -1d engagement.
   Setup PC can rejoin main operation.
2. **Separate operation:** first generates opportunity for
   second. Full downtime between them.

## Teamwork

| Maneuver | Mechanic |
|----------|----------|
| Assist | 1 stress → +1d. One per roll. |
| Group Action | All roll same action; best counts. Leader: 1 stress per 1-3. |
| Protect | Face consequence for teammate. May resist. |
| Set Up | +1 effect or improved position on follow-up. |

## Downtime Phase

Resolved in order: payoff → heat → entanglements → activities.

### Payoff

**Rep:** 2 base, +1 per tier target above crew, -1 per tier
below (min 0). Zero if completely quiet.

**Coin:**

| Score Size | Coin |
|-----------|:---:|
| Minor | 2 |
| Small | 4 |
| Standard | 6 |
| Big | 8 |
| Major | 10+ |

Tithe to crime boss if applicable: crew Tier + 1 coin. Don't
pay → start patience clock; each fill = -1 faction status.

### Heat

Determine base exposure from the score:

| Exposure | Heat |
|----------|:---:|
| Smooth, quiet | 0 |
| Contained, standard | 2 |
| Loud, chaotic | 4 |
| Wild, out of control | 6 |

Add: +1 high-profile target, +1 hostile turf, +1 at war,
+2 killing involved. Mark on 9-box track. If full: +1 wanted
level, clear heat, excess rolls over.

### Downtime Activities

Two free per PC (one if at war). Extra = 1 coin or 1 rep each.
Friend/contact helping = +1d. Spend coin after roll to increase
result by one level per coin.

| Activity | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| Acquire Asset | Tier roll: 1-3 Tier-1, 4-5 Tier, 6 Tier+1, crit Tier+2 |
| Long-Term Project | Action roll to tick clock: 1-3 one, 4-5 two, 6 three, crit five |
| Recover | Healer rolls healing clock (same progression) |
| Reduce Heat | Action roll to clear heat (same progression) |
| Train | 1 xp (2 with upgrade), one track, once per track per downtime |
| Indulge Vice | Roll lowest attribute; clear stress = highest die. Overindulge if excess. |

Assets: special items, cohorts (temp), vehicles, services.
Re-acquiring = +1d. Alchemicals/poisons/bombs: +2 heat.

### Giving Up

Abandon score → go to downtime. Usually zero payoff. Heat
and entanglements still apply.

## Clock Guidance

| Segments | Use |
|:---:|-----|
| 4 | Moderate complexity |
| 6 | Complex, multi-step |
| 8 | Long or difficult |

Name after obstacle, not method ("Interior Patrols" not "Sneak
Past Guards"). Layer clocks for complex threats.

| Clock Type | Visibility | Advances When |
|------------|-----------|---------------|
| Progress | Visible | PCs succeed |
| Danger | Usually visible | Consequences, failures, time |
| Racing | Both visible | Both sides act |
| Linked | Visible | Unlocks next when filled |
| Tug-of-war | Varies | Events fill or empty |
| Mission | Visible | Time passes; full = window closes |
| Faction | Hidden | Between sessions (GM) |

## XP and Advancement

### PC XP

**During play:** 1 xp in attribute when making desperate action
roll under that attribute.

**End of session** (1 each, 2 if a lot):
- Playbook-specific trigger
- Expressed beliefs/drives/heritage/background
- Struggled with vice/trauma issues

Track fills → advance: new special ability (playbook) or new
action dot (attribute).

### Crew XP

**End of session** (1 each, 2 if major):
- Crew-specific trigger
- Contend with challenges above station
- Bolster reputation or develop new one
- Express crew goals/drives/inner conflict

Track fills → new crew ability OR two upgrade boxes. Each
advance gives every PC stash = Tier + 2.

## Devil's Bargain

Bonus die for complication regardless of outcome. Player can
reject and push (2 stress) instead — not both. Common: collateral
damage, sacrifice item, betray friend, offend faction, tick
clock, add heat, suffer harm.

## GM Guidance

**Score:** cut to action after engagement roll. Use
consequences, not blocks — on a 4/5 the PC succeeds with a
cost; on a 1-3 things go badly but avoid shutting down all
options. Let flashbacks do planning. Track the fiction (who
knows what? what's happening off-screen? which clocks tick?).
Describe position before each roll so players can make
meaningful choices about pushing, resisting, or trading.

**Downtime:** don't skip it — it provides pacing, character
development, and recovery. Entanglements are story hooks, not
punishments. Advance faction clocks between sessions; when
you turn attention to a neglected faction, catch them up.
Share the world through contacts and vice purveyors — rumours
and gossip lead to future scores. Don't undermine payoff.

## Faction Turn (FitD-Specific)

Called by `world-evolution.md` Step 2. Overrides Universal
Faction Turn with FitD mechanics.

**Clock advancement:**
- PCs opposed faction: tick back 1-2 segments
- PCs ignored faction: advance 1-2 (plan proceeds)
- PCs helped faction: advance 2-3

**Tier/Hold changes:**
- Lost significant resources: Hold Strong → Weak or Tier -1
- Consolidated power: Hold Weak → Strong
- Tier changes rare (every 5-10 sessions)

**Heat consequences:** 4+ heat = nearby factions notice.
Entanglement results may create new faction threads.

**Rep/turf:** score affecting territory → propose rep changes
and turf shifts. Seized turf creates new enemies automatically.

Ref: `systems/fitd/factions.md` for canonical tiers, holds, clocks, NPCs.
