# Forged in the Dark -- Character & Crew Generation

## Character Creation Summary

1. Choose a playbook
2. Choose a heritage
3. Choose a background
4. Assign four action dots
5. Choose a special ability
6. Choose one close friend and one rival
7. Choose your vice
8. Record your name, alias, and look

## Step 1: Choose a Playbook

A playbook is the sheet with specific rules for a character
type. It determines your special abilities and xp triggers.
Every playbook represents a scoundrel at heart -- playbooks
are areas of focus and preference, not unique skill sets.

Each playbook comes with three starting action dots already
placed. Your character will grow and change over time; who
they become is part of the fun.

## Step 2: Choose a Heritage

Your heritage describes where your family line is from. Write
a detail about your family life on the character sheet.

Heritage affects how NPCs perceive you and can be expressed
during play to earn xp.

## Step 3: Choose a Background

Your background describes what you did before joining the crew.
Choose a background and write a detail specific to your
character.

Background provides context for skills and contacts your
character may draw upon.

## Step 4: Assign Four Action Dots

Your playbook begins with three action dots. You add four more
(seven total). At the start of the game, no action rating may
have more than two dots (unless a special ability says
otherwise).

Assign your four dots as follows:
- Put one dot in any action reflecting your **heritage**
- Put one dot in any action reflecting your **background**
- Assign two more dots anywhere you please (max rating 2)

After creation, action ratings may advance up to 3. When the
crew unlocks the Mastery upgrade, actions can advance to 4.

### The 12 Actions

**Insight**: Hunt, Study, Survey, Tinker
**Prowess**: Finesse, Prowl, Skirmish, Wreck
**Resolve**: Attune, Command, Consort, Sway

## Step 5: Choose a Special Ability

Review the special abilities for your playbook and choose one.
If you can't decide, go with the first one on the list -- it's
placed as a good default choice.

### Special Armor

Some special abilities refer to special armor. Each character
sheet has three armor boxes (standard, heavy, and special). If
you have abilities that use special armor, tick its box when
activated. Special armor is restored at the beginning of
downtime. If you have no special abilities that use special
armor, that box can't be used.

## Step 6: Choose One Close Friend and One Rival

Each playbook has a list of NPCs your character knows. Choose
one as a close relationship (friend, lover, family relation)
and mark the upward triangle. Choose another as a rival or
enemy and mark the downward triangle.

## Step 7: Choose Your Vice

Every character is in thrall to a vice, which they indulge to
deal with stress. Choose a vice type and describe it with
specific details, including the name and location of your
vice purveyor.

| Vice | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Faith | Dedicated to an unseen power, forgotten god, ancestor |
| Gambling | Games of chance, betting on sporting events |
| Luxury | Expensive or ostentatious displays of opulence |
| Obligation | Devoted to a family, cause, organization, charity |
| Pleasure | Gratification from lovers, food, drink, drugs, art, theater |
| Stupor | Seeking oblivion in drugs, excessive drinking, fighting pits |
| Weird | Strange essences, rogue spirits, bizarre rituals or taboos |

## Step 8: Record Name, Alias, and Look

Choose a name from the sample list or create your own. Note
any alias or nickname used in the underworld. Record a few
evocative words describing your character's look.

## Review Your Details

Check the experience triggers for your playbook and the special
items available to your character type. You begin with access
to all items on your sheet -- you'll decide what you're carrying
later, when you choose your load for a score.

## Items and Load

You have access to all items on your character sheet. For each
operation, decide your load level. During the operation, check
boxes for items as you use them, up to your chosen load limit.

| Load | Level | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| 1-3 | Light | Faster, less conspicuous; blend in with citizens |
| 4-5 | Normal | Look like a scoundrel, ready for trouble |
| 6 | Heavy | Slower; look like an operative on a mission |
| 7-9 | Encumbered | Overburdened; can only move very slowly |

Some special abilities (like Mule or Rigging) increase load
limits. Some items count as two items for load (connected
boxes). Items in italics don't count toward load.

You don't select specific items during planning -- you declare
them during the score as needed.

## Crew Creation Summary

1. Choose a crew type
2. Choose an initial reputation and lair
3. Establish your hunting grounds
4. Choose a special ability
5. Assign crew upgrades
6. Choose a favorite contact

## Step 1: Choose a Crew Type

Your crew type determines the scores you'll focus on and your
special abilities. The crew type is also how you're known in
the underworld.

Your crew begins with:
- 2 coin in its coffers
- Tier 0 with strong hold
- 0 rep

## Step 2: Choose an Initial Reputation and Lair

Choose how the underworld sees your newly formed crew:

- Ambitious
- Brutal
- Daring
- Honorable
- Professional
- Savvy
- Subtle
- Strange

Reputation earns you xp when bolstered, so choose something
that signals the kind of action you want.

Also decide where the crew's lair is located. At Tier 0, it's
probably a very modest or abandoned sort of place.

## Step 3: Establish Your Hunting Grounds

Choose a small part of a district (three or four city blocks)
as your hunting grounds -- the area you usually target for
scores. The GM tells you which faction claims the area, then
you decide:

- Pay them 1 coin for room to work
- Pay them 2 coin and gain +1 status
- Keep your money and take -1 status

Choose one operation type from your crew's list as your
preference. When you prepare your preferred operation type on
your hunting grounds, you get +1d to gather information rolls
and a free additional downtime activity for that operation.

## Step 4: Choose a Crew Special Ability

Review the special abilities for your crew type and choose one.
If you can't decide, go with the first on the list. Everyone
should be excited about the choice -- it focuses the game in a
specific direction from the start.

## Step 5: Assign Crew Upgrades

Your crew has two pre-selected upgrades that suit your type.
Choose two additional upgrades (four total at start) from your
crew-specific or general upgrade lists.

When you assign upgrades, the GM establishes faction impacts:
- One faction helped you get an upgrade: +1 status (or spend
  1 coin for +2 status)
- One faction was harmed by your upgrade: -2 status (or spend
  1 coin for -1 status)

### Common Crew Upgrades

- **Boat House / Carriage House**: Vehicle and storage
- **Cohort**: A gang or expert NPC (costs two upgrade boxes)
- **Hidden Lair**: Secret, disguised location
- **Mastery**: Unlocks action rating 4 (costs four upgrade boxes)
- **Quality**: Improves quality of a specific item type for
  all PCs (Documents, Gear, Implements, Supplies, Tools, or
  Weapons)
- **Quarters**: Living space in the lair
- **Secure Lair**: Locks, alarms, traps (second upgrade adds
  arcane measures)
- **Training**: Earn 2 xp (instead of 1) when training a given
  xp track (Insight, Prowess, Resolve, or Playbook)
- **Vault**: Increases coin storage to 8 (second upgrade to 16)
- **Workshop**: Tools for tinkering and alchemy, plus a small
  library

## Step 6: Choose a Favorite Contact

Choose one contact from the crew sheet as a close friend or
partner in crime. The GM establishes faction impacts:
- One faction is friendly with this contact: +1 status
- One faction is unfriendly with this contact: -1 status

Optionally, increase intensity to +2 and -2 status instead.

## Cohorts

A cohort is a gang or expert NPC who works for your crew.
Recruiting a new cohort costs two upgrade boxes.

### Gangs

Choose a gang type:
- **Adepts**: Scholars, tinkerers, occultists, chemists
- **Rooks**: Con artists, spies, socialites
- **Rovers**: Sailors, carriage drivers, deathlands scavengers
- **Skulks**: Scouts, infiltrators, thieves
- **Thugs**: Killers, brawlers, roustabouts

A gang has scale and quality equal to your crew Tier. Elite
upgrades give +1d for a given type.

### Experts

Record the expert's specific area of expertise (Doctor,
Investigator, Occultist, Assassin, Spy, etc.). An expert has
quality equal to crew Tier + 1, scale always zero.

### Edges and Flaws

Give each cohort one or two edges and an equal number of flaws.

**Edges**: Fearsome, Independent, Loyal, Tenacious
**Flaws**: Principled, Savage, Unreliable, Wild

### Cohort Harm

| Level | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| Weakened | Reduced effect |
| Impaired | -1d to quality rolls |
| Broken | Can't do anything until recovered |
| Dead | Destroyed; replace at cost of Tier + 2 coin plus two downtime activities |

All cohorts heal one level during downtime. A PC spending a
downtime activity to help removes two levels instead.

## Starting Situation in Doskvol

The default setting is the haunted industrial city of Doskvol
(Duskwall). Key elements for new crews:

- The city is trapped behind lightning barriers that hold back
  the death-lands and hungry ghosts outside
- Leviathan blood (electroplasm) powers the city's industry
  and the lightning barriers
- The Spirit Wardens collect and destroy the ghosts of the dead
- The Imperial government, noble houses, and criminal factions
  all vie for power
- Your crew is at the bottom of the ladder (Tier 0) and must
  claw their way up through scores, reputation, and daring

New crews should establish relationships (positive and
negative) with at least two or three factions during creation,
giving the GM immediate hooks for entanglements and
opportunities.
