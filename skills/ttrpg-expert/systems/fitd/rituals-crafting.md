# Forged in the Dark -- Rituals & Crafting

*GM activities: Run, Prep*

## Rituals

To enact a ritual is to come into contact with abyssal forces
and entreat them to do your will. It is not without
considerable risk.

### Prerequisites

A PC must have the **Ritual** special ability (Whisper playbook)
to safely perform ritual sorcery. Without it, the study and
practice of rituals leaves you utterly vulnerable to the powers
you supplicate.

### Finding a Ritual Source

A PC with Ritual begins with one known ritual (defined during
creation). To learn a new ritual, first find a **source**:

- **Payoff from a score**: Steal a ritual book, plunder a
  temple, loot an occultist's library
- **Long-term project**: Consort with cultist friends, study
  ancient texts, or other methods

### Learning a Ritual

Once a source is found, undertake a **long-term project** to
learn the ritual. Most rituals require an **8-segment clock**.

The player and GM answer three questions to define the ritual:

1. **GM asks**: "What does the ritual do and how is it weird?"
   *Player answers.*
2. **Player asks**: "What must I do to perform the ritual, and
   what is its price?" *GM answers.* A ritual takes at least
   one downtime activity and inflicts stress according to its
   magnitude. If dangerous or troublesome, it requires an
   action roll (usually Attune). May also have additional costs
   (sacrifice, rare item, dire progress clock, etc.).
3. **GM asks**: "What new belief or fear does knowledge of this
   ritual instill in you?" *Player answers.*

### Performing a Ritual

**Requirements**: Ritual special ability, follow the method
established by the ritual questions.

**Time**: Most rituals take **one downtime activity**. The GM
may require two or more for powerful or far-reaching rituals.
Some may be partially performed during downtime and "primed"
for later manifestation.

**Stress cost**: Based on the **magnitude** of the forces
involved. The GM uses the magnitude table as a guideline (may
be higher or lower at discretion). Some claims and abilities
reduce ritual stress cost (e.g., Cult's Ancient Obelisk).

**Reducing stress cost**: Use the duration examples on the
magnitude table. More time = lower stress. Generally no less
than one hour.

**Consequences**: The GM may tick a progress clock when you
perform a ritual (advancing an arcane power's agenda or
approaching a dark outcome).

**Rolls**:
- If **dangerous**: Action roll (usually Attune) for
  consequences
- If **uncertain**: Fortune roll for how well it manifests;
  spend coin 1-for-1 to increase the fortune roll result
  (expensive or rare materials)
- If **both**: Both rolls may be required

**Variability**: Each performance is unique and may not work
the same way each time. The GM or players may re-ask the ritual
questions to establish new parameters if things get out of hand.

### Example Ritual

> Player: "The ritual wards a person so ghosts of their victims
> cannot find them. It's weird because the warded person
> sometimes weeps tears of black blood."
>
> GM: "Spend a downtime action to prepare a mixture of tobacco,
> dream smoke, and crematory ash from a victim, which the
> target smokes. You take at least 3 stress, which will be the
> quality for a fortune roll when challenged by a spirit. Take
> more stress to make it more potent."
>
> Player: "My new fear is what happens if the spirits figure out
> where the ward came from and turn their vengeance on me."

### More Example Rituals

> **Ward of Sealed Thresholds** (protection)
>
> Player: "The ritual wards a doorway so that spirits cannot
> pass through it. It's weird because the door sometimes
> whispers the names of ghosts that have tried to cross."
>
> GM: "Chalk glyphs around the doorframe using ground bone
> mixed with electroplasm. Spend a downtime action and take at
> least 2 stress. The ward's quality equals the stress spent,
> resisted by a spirit's Tier. The ward lasts until the glyphs
> are disturbed or a spirit of sufficient power forces through."
>
> Player: "I now believe that doors remember every death that
> has passed through them."

> **Compel the Unwilling Tongue** (binding)
>
> Player: "The ritual forces a ghost to answer three questions
> truthfully. It's weird because the ghost speaks through my
> mouth while my eyes go black."
>
> GM: "You need a personal possession of the ghost's former
> life and a circle drawn in electroplasmic salt. Spend a
> downtime action, take at least 3 stress. Roll Attune
> (desperate) — on a partial, the ghost answers but plants a
> false memory in you. On a miss, it escapes the binding."
>
> Player: "My new fear is that one day a ghost will answer
> through me and refuse to leave."

> **Eyes of the Echo** (divination)
>
> Player: "The ritual lets me see through the eyes of someone
> whose blood I possess. It's weird because while I watch, I
> feel their emotions as if they were mine."
>
> GM: "Burn the blood sample in a silver dish and inhale the
> fumes. Spend a downtime action, take at least 4 stress. The
> vision lasts for a scene. You can see and hear but not
> interact. If the target has arcane defenses, roll Attune
> against their Tier."
>
> Player: "I now fear that someone might do this to me — that
> I'm being watched through my own eyes."

> **Ghost Mantle** (transformation)
>
> Player: "The ritual lets me temporarily take on a ghost's
> ability to pass through solid matter. It's weird because my
> body becomes translucent and cold, and I leave frost on
> everything I touch."
>
> GM: "Consume a vial of refined electroplasm mixed with your
> own blood. Spend a downtime action, take at least 5 stress.
> The effect lasts for minutes, not hours. While transformed,
> you're vulnerable to spirit-bane and electroplasmic barriers.
> Start a 'Spiritual Corruption' 6-clock — tick it each time
> you use this ritual."
>
> Player: "I believe that the boundary between living and dead
> is thinner than anyone admits, and I've proven it."

### Sample Alchemicals and Gadgets

These use the inventing and crafting rules above. Each has at
least one drawback as the GM would assign.

**Spectral Lantern** (spark-craft device)
A modified lantern that emits a frequency of electroplasmic
light visible only to attuned individuals. Reveals ghostly
residue, spirit trails, and hidden arcane wards within its
glow. Minimum quality: Tier + 2.
*Drawbacks:* Conspicuous (the light draws attention from
supernatural entities), Unreliable (roll quality — on 1-3
it attracts a ghost rather than just revealing them).

**Ghost Smoke** (alchemical consumable)
A glass vial that, when shattered, releases a cloud of pale
vapor. Spirits within the cloud become briefly visible to
everyone, even those without arcane sight. Lasts about 30
seconds. Minimum quality: Tier + 1.
*Drawbacks:* Consumable (single use), Volatile (the smoke
irritates the lungs — take level 1 harm "Wracking Cough"
unless resisted).

**Resonance Blade** (modified weapon)
A bladed weapon treated with electroplasmic solution and
inscribed with harmonic frequencies. Gains potency against
spirits and ghosts — can harm them as if they were physical.
Minimum quality: Tier + 3.
*Drawbacks:* Rare (requires refined electroplasm and a
whisper's blessing), Complex (two crafting stages: blade
treatment and harmonic inscription).

**Echo Tuning Fork** (arcane implement)
A specially crafted tuning fork that, when struck, resonates
with the ghost field. The user can "hear" nearby spirits as
distinct tones — different pitches for different types
(lingering dead, bound spirits, demons). Minimum quality:
Tier + 2.
*Drawbacks:* Unreliable (fortune roll each use — on 1-3 the
fork picks up overwhelming noise and the user takes 2 stress),
Conspicuous (the ringing tone is audible to spirits, who know
they've been detected).

## Crafting

During downtime, a PC can Tinker with special materials and
tools to produce **alchemicals**, **build or modify items**,
create **spark-craft gadgets**, or enchant **arcane implements
or weapons**.

### Inventing

To invent a new formula or design, **Study** it as a
**long-term project** (usually 8-segment clock).

The player and GM answer four questions:

1. **GM asks**: "What type of creation is it and what does it
   do?" *Player answers.* Types: mundane, alchemical, arcane,
   or spark-craft. PCs with Alchemist, Artificer, or Strange
   Methods get bonuses for their type.
2. **Player asks**: "What's the minimum quality level of this
   item?" *GM answers*, using magnitude of effects as
   guideline.
3. **GM asks**: "What rare, strange, or adverse aspect has kept
   this in obscurity?" *Player answers.*
4. **Player asks**: "What drawbacks does this item have, if
   any?" *GM answers* (choosing from drawbacks list or none).

A PC with Alchemist, Artificer, or Strange Methods invents and
learns one special formula/design when they first take the
ability (no project needed for the first one).

Once invented, only you can craft it unless someone else learns
your design as a long-term project.

Common alchemicals and ordinary items don't require special
formulas -- anyone may attempt to craft them from commonly
available instructions.

### Crafting Roll

Spend one **downtime activity** and make a **Tinker** roll.
Base quality equals your crew's Tier:

| Result | Quality Level |
|--------|:------------:|
| Critical | Tier + 2 |
| 6 | Tier + 1 |
| 4-5 | Tier |
| 1-3 | Tier - 1 |

**Modifiers**:
- +1 quality per coin spent (can exceed Tier + 2)
- +1 quality for Workshop crew upgrade (effective Tier is +1)

The GM sets a **minimum quality** that must be met (using
magnitude as a guideline). An item may be crafted at higher
quality than minimum if desired.

### Modifying an Item

Adding a feature or function to an existing item is simpler --
no invention required. Make a crafting roll:

| Modification | Minimum Quality |
|-------------|:--------------:|
| Simple, useful | Tier + 1 |
| Significant | Tier + 2 |
| Arcane, spark-craft, or alchemical | Tier + 3 |

Examples:
- Simple: A rifle that breaks down for concealment
- Significant: Strengthened barrel for longer range
- Arcane: A dagger that can harm a demon; an electrified hull

### Drawbacks

A creation or modification may have one or more drawbacks
(chosen by the GM):

- **Complex**: Must be created in multiple stages. One downtime
  activity and crafting roll per stage.
- **Conspicuous**: Take +1 heat if used on an operation.
- **Consumable**: Limited uses (all alchemicals must have this,
  usually one use).
- **Rare**: Requires a rare item or material to craft.
- **Unreliable**: When used, make a fortune roll (item quality)
  to see how well it performs.
- **Volatile**: Produces a dangerous side-effect for the user
  (specified by GM). The side-effect is a consequence and may
  be resisted.

### Crafting and the Workshop

The **Workshop** crew upgrade provides tools for tinkering and
alchemy, plus a small library. With a Workshop:
- You may accomplish long-term projects without leaving lair
- Your effective Tier is +1 for crafting rolls
