# Forged in the Dark -- Magnitude Scale

*GM activities: Run, Prep*

The magnitude scale helps the GM judge supernatural entities,
arcane forces, quality levels, and various effects consistently.
It measures area, scale, duration, range, force, and quality.

Use magnitude as a dice pool for fortune rolls to determine
the effect of an entity or power when the outcome is uncertain.
Magnitude levels can be combined to describe complex effects
(e.g., force 6 + range 5 for a hurricane across a district).

The table is a guideline, not a rigid formula. Use it to inform
judgment calls about supernatural effects, ritual stress costs,
crafting quality requirements, and asset quality levels.

---

## Area / Scale

| Mag | Area | Scale (people) |
|:---:|------|----------------|
| 0 | A closet | 1-2 people |
| 1 | A small room | A small gang (3-6) |
| 2 | A large room / several rooms | A medium gang (12) |
| 3 | A small building | A large gang (20) |
| 4 | A large building | A huge gang (40) |
| 5 | A city block | A massive gang (80) |
| 6 | (larger) | A colossal gang (160) |

## Duration / Range

| Mag | Duration | Range |
|:---:|----------|-------|
| 0 | A few moments | Within reach |
| 1 | A few minutes | A dozen paces |
| 2 | An hour | A stone's throw |
| 3 | A few hours | Down the road |
| 4 | A day | Several blocks away |
| 5 | Several days | Across the district |
| 6 | A week | Across the city |

## Tier & Quality

| Mag | Quality | Description |
|:---:|---------|-------------|
| 0 | Poor | A rusty knife, worn & tattered clothing, rickety shack |
| 1 | Adequate | A fighting blade, ordinary clothing, shared apartment, cheap food/drugs |
| 2 | Good | A pistol, respectable clothing, private rented room, typical ghost |
| 3 | Excellent | A coach, boat, military rifle, fashionable clothing, small home |
| 4 | Superior | A luxury vehicle, townhouse, typical demon or powerful ghost |
| 5 | Impeccable | A large townhouse, small ship, custom clothing, lightning barrier |
| 6 | Legendary | A mansion, large ship, rare arcane artifacts, powerful demon |

## Force

| Mag | Force | Examples |
|:---:|-------|---------|
| 0 | Weak | A firm shove, a candle flame, breeze, tiny spark |
| 1 | Moderate | A solid punch, steady wind, torch flame, electrical shock |
| 2 | Strong | A powerful blow, howling wind, burning brand |
| 3 | Serious | A crushing blow, staggering wind, grenade, searing fire, electrical surge |

## Using Magnitude in Play

### Ritual Stress Costs

The stress cost for performing a ritual is based on the
magnitude of the forces brought to bear. The GM uses the
tables above as a guideline:

- A ritual affecting a small room for a few hours might be
  magnitude 1-2 (1-2 stress)
- A ritual affecting a city block for several days might be
  magnitude 5 (5 stress)
- A ritual unleashing devastating force across a district
  might be magnitude 11 (force 6 + range 5)

Longer casting time reduces stress cost (use duration row).

### Crafting Quality Requirements

The GM sets minimum quality for crafted items based on the
magnitude of their effects:

- A simple useful item: quality 1-2
- A significant combat item: quality 3-4
- An arcane or extraordinary device: quality 4-6

### Fortune Rolls for Supernatural Effects

When a supernatural entity or power acts with uncertain results,
use its magnitude as a fortune roll dice pool:

| Result | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Critical | Extreme effect |
| 6 | Full effect |
| 4-5 | Partial effect |
| 1-3 | Little effect |

### Assessing NPC and Faction Power

The scale column aligns with the gang scale by Tier table:

| Tier | Gang Scale | Magnitude |
|------|------------|:---------:|
| 0 | 1-2 people | 0 |
| I | 3-6 people | 1 |
| II | 12 people | 2 |
| III | 20 people | 3 |
| IV | 40 people | 4 |
| V | 80 people | 5 |

This helps the GM quickly assess scale advantages and
disadvantages in conflicts between groups of different sizes.
