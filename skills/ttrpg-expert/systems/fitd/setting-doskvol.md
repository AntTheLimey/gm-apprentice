# Forged in the Dark -- The City of Doskvol

*GM activities: Prep, Build*

## Overview

Doskvol (also called Duskwall) is a haunted industrial city
in the Shattered Isles. The sun was destroyed long ago, and
the world exists in perpetual darkness lit only by electroplasmic
streetlamps and the occasional glow of leviathan blood. The
city is trapped behind massive lightning barriers -- crackling
walls of energy that hold back the death-lands and the hungry
ghosts that swarm the desolate wastes beyond.

## The World

- The sun was shattered. The world is dark.
- The gates of death were broken. When people die, their spirits
  linger as ghosts unless destroyed by the Spirit Wardens.
- Leviathan blood (electroplasm) is the primary energy source.
  Leviathan hunters sail the void sea on massive ships to
  harvest it.
- The Imperium rules from Iruvia, but the Emperor's authority
  is distant. Local power belongs to noble houses, criminal
  factions, the military, and the Church of the Ecstasy of the
  Flesh.

## The City's Character

Doskvol is a city of:
- **Perpetual gloom**: The sky is dark. Electroplasmic lamps
  cast pale light on rain-slicked streets.
- **Industrial grime**: Smoke and soot from factories and
  refineries. The canals run with oily water.
- **Haunted streets**: Ghosts are a constant threat. The Spirit
  Wardens patrol with their death-bells and spirit masks.
- **Criminal enterprise**: Every district has its gangs, rackets,
  and schemes. The underworld is layered and competitive.
- **Social stratification**: Noble houses in their mansions,
  merchants in their shops, and the masses in crowded tenements.
- **Weird occultism**: Forgotten gods, demon cults, arcane
  artifacts, and spirit wells beneath the streets.

## Districts

### Barrowcleft
A steep hill neighborhood. Home to many academics and students
who attend Doskvol Academy. Relatively quiet, with modest
homes and scholarly pursuits. The Dimmer Sisters maintain a
mysterious manor here.

### Brightstone
The wealthy district. Grand manors, well-lit streets, manicured
parks. Home to noble families and powerful merchants. The
Bluecoats patrol here frequently and keep the peace for the
rich.

### Charhollow
A poor, densely packed residential district. The people here
are hardworking laborers, dockworkers, and factory hands.
Gangs compete for control of the streets. The air smells of
coal smoke and cheap food.

### Coalridge
Industrial heartland. Factories, refineries, and workshops
dominate the landscape. The air is thick with smoke. Workers
toil in dangerous conditions. The Billhooks hold considerable
influence here.

### Crow's Foot
A relatively poor district, home to several criminal gangs
who vie for control. The Crows, the Lampblacks, and the Red
Sashes all operate here. A good starting district for a new
crew -- lots of opportunity and conflict.

### The Docks
The commercial and shipping hub of the city. Warehouses, piers,
and taverns line the waterfront. Sailors, smugglers, and
merchants mix here. The Dockers keep order among the
longshoremen.

### Dunslough
A blighted, flooded district. Much of it is partially
submerged. The poorest of the poor live here amid ruins and
refuse. Spirit activity is high. Few patrols venture in.

### Nightmarket
The commercial heart of the city. Shops, stalls, and market
squares bustle at all hours. You can buy almost anything here
-- legal or otherwise. The Hive and other merchant factions
compete for commercial dominance.

### Silkshore
The "entertainment" district. Vice dens, taverns, theaters,
and brothels. Home to artists, performers, and pleasure-seekers.
The Fog Hounds and other gangs manage the illicit trade here.

### Six Towers
A residential district for the middle class. Modest homes and
small businesses. Named for the old watchtowers that dot the
area. The Spirit Wardens maintain a notable presence.

### Whitecrown
The seat of government. The Lord Governor's manor, the City
Council chambers, the main Bluecoat garrison, and Ironhook
Prison are all located here. Heavily guarded and patrolled.

## Notable Landmarks

- **Ironhook Prison**: The city's massive prison complex in
  Whitecrown. Brutal and overcrowded. PCs who are incarcerated
  serve time here.
- **Doskvol Academy**: A prestigious institution of higher
  learning in Barrowcleft. Research into natural philosophy,
  history, and (quietly) the arcane.
- **The Lightning Barrier**: The massive electroplasmic wall
  that surrounds the city. Maintained by the Sparkwrights.
  Without it, the ghosts and horrors of the death-lands would
  swarm in.
- **Bellweather Crematorium**: Where the Spirit Wardens destroy
  the ghosts of the dead. The death-bells toll when a spirit
  is captured. Citizens are cremated to prevent ghostly return.
- **The Void Sea**: The dark, haunted ocean beyond the city.
  Leviathan hunter ships venture out to harvest the blood that
  powers the city.
- **The Lost District**: A walled-off section of the city
  overrun by ghosts long ago. No one enters. Something
  terrible is said to lurk within.

## Atmosphere and Tone

Doskvol evokes:
- Victorian London with its fog, gas lamps, and class divisions
- Industrial-era grime, smoke, and factory life
- Gothic horror with ghosts, demons, and occult secrets
- Noir crime fiction with its web of alliances and betrayals
- A sense of claustrophobia -- the city is a cage, surrounded
  by death

**Key sensory details for play:**
- Rain and mist are constant; the air is damp
- Electroplasmic lamps cast a cold, bluish-white light
- The death-bells toll when the Spirit Wardens are active
- Canal boats and gondolas are common transport
- Coal smoke, machine oil, and the tang of electroplasm
- The hum and crackle of the lightning barrier at the city's
  edge
