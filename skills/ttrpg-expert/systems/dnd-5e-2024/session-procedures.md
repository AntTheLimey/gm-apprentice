# D&D 5e 2024 -- Session Procedures

How to run a D&D 5e session: encounter building, exploration, social encounters, and session flow.

## Combat Encounter Design

### XP Budget per Character

Multiply by number of characters for total encounter budget. Spend creature XP from the budget without going over.

### Encounter Difficulty Descriptions

- **Low:** Characters emerge victorious with no casualties; may need healing resources. A single monster whose CR equals the party level is roughly low difficulty for 4 characters.
- **Moderate:** Could go badly without healing/resources; weaker characters might go down; slim chance of death.
- **High:** Could be lethal; requires smart tactics and quick thinking.

**Troubleshooting:** If an encounter has more than two creatures per character, include fragile creatures that can be defeated quickly -- especially at levels 1-2. Adjust on the fly: creatures flee (easier) or reinforcements arrive (harder).

### Lazy DM Deadly Benchmark (Sly Flourish)

Quick estimate without XP budgets:
- **Levels 1-4:** Deadly when total monster CR exceeds one quarter of total character levels.
- **Levels 5+:** Deadly when total monster CR exceeds one half of total character levels.

### Action-Oriented Monsters (Colville/MCDM)

Boss monsters with scripted villain actions that create escalating pressure across combat rounds. Villain actions trigger at end of a round, not on the monster's turn. See https://mcdm.gg/ for the full framework.

## Exploration Procedures

### Dungeon Exploration Checklist

1. Establish marching order and light sources
2. Per room: describe entrances, features, ambient details
3. Perception checks only when something is hidden
4. Track time in 10-minute exploration turns
5. Use passive Perception for traps

### Extended Travel

After 8 hours per day, each additional hour requires CON save or gain 1 Exhaustion level (DC 10 + 1 per hour past 8).

## Social Encounter Structure

### NPC Attitude System

| Attitude | Effect on Influence Checks |
|----------|---------------------------|
| Hostile | Disadvantage |
| Indifferent | No modifier (default) |
| Friendly | Advantage |

GM determines if creature is **Willing** (complies without check), **Unwilling** (refuses regardless), or **Hesitant** (requires ability check modified by attitude).

### Influence Checks

| Ability Check | Interaction |
|---------------|------------|
| CHA (Deception) | Deceiving a monster that understands you |
| CHA (Intimidation) | Intimidating a monster |
| CHA (Performance) | Amusing a monster |
| CHA (Persuasion) | Persuading a monster that understands you |
| WIS (Animal Handling) | Gently coaxing a Beast or Monstrosity |

Default DC: 15 or the monster's INT score, whichever is higher. On failure, wait 24 hours before same approach.

### Skill Challenge Framework (4e legacy)

1. Set the goal
2. Complexity: (2 x party size) successes before (party size) failures
3. DC by tier: 10 easy, 15 moderate, 20 hard
4. No skill used more than twice per challenge
5. Narrate each success and failure

## Typical D&D 5e Session Flow

1. **Recap** (5 min): Player-given summary
2. **Strong start** (10-15 min): Immediate action or tension
3. **Discovery/exploration** (45-60 min): Dungeons, wilderness, investigation
4. **Challenge** (45-60 min): Combat, social, or environmental
5. **Revelation** (10-15 min): Secret found, story advances
6. **Cliffhanger** (5 min): End at maximum tension
