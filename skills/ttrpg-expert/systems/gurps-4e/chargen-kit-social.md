character. Read mechanics.md and character-generation.md

Archetypes covered: diplomat, con artist, noble/socialite,
politician, face/fixer, courtier, information broker.

## Build Framework

Point allocation guidance for social archetypes. Scale
proportionally for other point totals.

| Category | 150 pts | 175 pts | 250 pts |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|
| Attributes | 60-80 | 70-90 | 90-120 |
| Secondary mods | 0-5 | 0-5 | 5-10 |
| Advantages | 40-60 | 50-70 | 70-100 |
| Disadvantages | -40 to -50 | -50 to -70 | -50 to -75 |
| Quirks | -5 | -5 | -5 |
| Skills | 45-70 | 55-85 | 80-120 |

**Attribute priorities:** IQ 13+ is the foundation for social
characters — all social and knowledge skills run off IQ. Raise
IQ before DX or ST. HT 10-11 is adequate. DX 10-11 is fine;
social characters rarely need high physical capability. ST 9-10
is acceptable.

**Secondary characteristics:** Will may be worth buying up
(Intimidation is Will-based; resisting coercion matters). Per
helps Detect Lies and Body Language.

**Near-mandatory advantages:** Charisma [5/level] stacks with
Smooth Operator talent and adds to reaction rolls and Leadership.
Voice [10] is extremely efficient at +2 reactions. Status and
Wealth are situational but often campaign-required. Smooth
Operator talent [15/level] is the best single purchase for a
dedicated face character.

**Skills budget is large.** Social characters invest in a wide
breadth of skills rather than depth in combat. Budget 50-70% of
skill points toward social and knowledge skills, leaving ~10-20
points for basic self-defence and covert options.

## Social Mechanics Summary

Condensed from social-rules.md. For full procedure see that file.

### Reaction Rolls

When PCs meet an NPC whose attitude is not predetermined, the GM
rolls 3d6 and consults the Reaction Table.

| Roll | Reaction | Behaviour |
|------|----------|-----------|
| 0 or less | Disastrous | Attacks or acts with extreme hostility |
| 1-3 | Very Bad | Hostile; attacks if possible |
| 4-6 | Bad | Uncooperative; insults or threatens |
| 7-9 | Poor | Reluctantly cooperates only if benefit is clear |
| 10-12 | Neutral | Indifferent; cooperates if it costs nothing |
| 13-15 | Good | Friendly; willing to help; gives benefit of doubt |
| 16-18 | Very Good | Enthusiastic; actively helpful |
| 19+ | Excellent | Exceptional help; strong loyalty |

**Key reaction modifiers for social characters:**

| Source | Modifier |
|--------|----------|
| Charisma | +1/level |
| Voice | +2 |
| Status (positive) | +1/level |
| Reputation (positive) | +1 to +4 |
| Appearance (Attractive) | +1 |
| Appearance (Handsome/Beautiful) | +4 |
| Appearance (Very Handsome/Beautiful) | +6 |
| Fashion Sense | +1 |
| Social Stigma | -1 to -4 |
| Odious Personal Habit | -1 to -3 |

### Influence Rolls

When a PC actively tries to change an NPC's mind, use an
Influence roll (a Quick Contest). Choose the appropriate skill:

| Skill | vs. NPC's | Use when... |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Diplomacy | IQ or Will | Negotiating through reason and tact |
| Fast-Talk | Will | Deceiving or rushing to agreement |
| Intimidation | Will | Threatening or coercing |
| Sex Appeal | Will | Using attractiveness to persuade |
| Savoir-Faire | — | Fitting into a specific social context |
| Streetwise | Will | Dealing with criminals and underworld |
| Leadership | Will | Inspiring or commanding groups |
| Merchant | IQ or Will | Haggling and deal evaluation |

**Influence Procedure:**

1. Player describes approach (what they say/do).
2. GM determines appropriate skill.
3. Roll Quick Contest: PC's skill vs NPC's Will (or NPC's same
   skill for Diplomacy and Fast-Talk contests).
4. PC wins: NPC is influenced as desired.
   NPC wins: Attempt fails; reaction may worsen.
   Critical failure: NPC reacts very badly (–2 or worse to
   future interactions).

**Key notes:**
- Diplomacy never worsens on a normal failure; only on critical
  failure. Best choice when the stakes are high.
- Fast-Talk works quickly but the NPC realises the deception
  after a number of minutes equal to the margin of success.
- Intimidation compels immediate compliance but creates
  lasting resentment; the NPC will act against you when safe.
- Sex Appeal requires appropriate Appearance; GM may rule it
  ineffective if the NPC is not attracted to the character.

### Social "Combat" Loop

Social encounters follow the same contest structure as physical
combat but using influence skills. A structured negotiation
can involve multiple Quick Contests. Track mood shifts; a
failed intimidation can shift a Good reaction to Bad.

## Languages

Costs from B24. Native language is free. Buy components
separately: Spoken and Written.

| Comprehension Level | Cost (Spoken) | Cost (Written) |
|---------------------|---------------|----------------|
| None | 0 | 0 |
| Broken | 1 | 1 |
| Accented | 2 | 2 |
| Native | 3 | 3 |

**Typical purchases:**
- Spoken + Written at Accented: 4 pts total per language.
- Spoken + Written at Native: 6 pts total per language (or 3 pts
  each component).
- Spoken only (Accented): 2 pts. Spoken only (Native): 3 pts.

**Language Talent [10]:** Each language costs 1 pt less per
level purchased (i.e., each component costs 1 less).

**Cultural Familiarity [1/2]:** Even with a language, an
unfamiliar culture imposes –1 to social skill rolls. Buy
Cultural Familiarity [1] to remove this for one culture.
Alien culture: [2].

**Note for social characters:** A diplomat or face operating
internationally should budget 10–20 pts for 2–4 languages at
Accented level, plus matching Cultural Familiarity purchases.
