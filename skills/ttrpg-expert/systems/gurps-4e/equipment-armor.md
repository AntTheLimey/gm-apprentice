## How Armor Works

Armor provides **Damage Resistance (DR)**. When an attack hits,
subtract DR from the damage rolled. Remaining damage is then
multiplied by the wounding modifier for the damage type.

**Split DR**: Some armor has split DR, written as **DR X/Y***.
Use the higher DR against piercing (pi) and cutting (cut)
attacks; use the lower DR against all other damage types
(crushing, burning, etc.). This reflects armor designed to
stop penetrating attacks but offering less protection against
blunt force.

**Flexible armor** (marked with * after DR) can be worn under
clothing. However, flexible armor does not protect well against
crushing attacks -- the blow still transfers force through the
material.

**Layering**: Armor can be layered. Add the DR values, but only
the outermost rigid layer counts its full DR; inner flexible
layers add normally.

## Shields

Shields provide a **Defense Bonus (DB)** that adds to all
active defenses (Block, Dodge, Parry) against attacks from the

x2 HP. At TL7+, plastic shields have x0.5 weight. Shield
composition never affects DB. A spiked shield adds $20 and 5 lbs.

## Encumbrance

Basic Lift (BL) = ST x ST / 5 (round to nearest whole number).
BL determines how much weight you can carry before penalties.

| Level | Weight | Move | Dodge |
|-------|--------|------|-------|
| None (0) | up to BL | Full Move | Full Dodge |
| Light (1) | up to 2 x BL | Move - 1 | Dodge - 1 |
| Medium (2) | up to 3 x BL | Move - 2 | Dodge - 2 |
| Heavy (3) | up to 6 x BL | Move - 3 | Dodge - 3 |
| X-Heavy (4) | up to 10 x BL | Move - 4 | Dodge - 4 |

You cannot carry more than 10 x BL. Move is calculated as
Basic Speed (no fractions) minus encumbrance penalty.

### Armor Weight Guidelines

A character with ST 10 (BL 20) wearing a mail hauberk (25 lbs)
is already at Medium encumbrance (Move -2, Dodge -2). Adding
plate legs (20 lbs) pushes to Heavy encumbrance (Move -3,
Dodge -3) at 45 total lbs.

Full plate armor (corselet + plate arms + plate legs + greathelm
+ gauntlets + sollerets) weighs roughly 80-100 lbs, requiring
ST 14+ (BL 39) to avoid X-Heavy encumbrance.
