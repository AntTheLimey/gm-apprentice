character. Read mechanics.md and character-generation.md
first, then this file.

Covers archetypes: explorer, wilderness guide, tracker, survivalist,
park ranger, big game hunter, frontier scout.

## Build Framework

Point allocation guidance for outdoor archetypes. Scale
proportionally for other point totals.

| Category | 150 pts | 175 pts | 250 pts |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|
| Attributes | 80-100 | 90-110 | 120-150 |
| Secondary mods | 0-10 | 0-10 | 5-15 |
| Advantages | 25-40 | 30-50 | 50-80 |
| Disadvantages | -40 to -50 | -45 to -60 | -50 to -75 |
| Quirks | -5 | -5 | -5 |
| Skills | 35-60 | 50-75 | 70-110 |

**Attribute priorities:** Balanced spread is key. HT 12+ for
endurance, Hiking/Swimming, FP pool, and wilderness HT rolls.
DX 12-13 for physical skills and ranged combat. Per 13+ for
Tracking, Observation, and Survival. IQ 11-12 for Navigation
and knowledge skills. ST 10-11 is usually sufficient.

**Near-mandatory advantages:** Fit or Very Fit [5/15] — HT rolls
and FP recovery are constant concerns. Outdoorsman talent [10/level]
covers Camouflage, Fishing, Naturalist, Navigation, Survival, and
Tracking — the most efficient spend in this build. Absolute Direction
[5] saves points otherwise wasted on Navigation skill.

**Scouts need breadth over depth.** Aim for many skills at 12-14
rather than one at 18. Survival (primary terrain) at 14+ is the
anchor; secondary terrain and Navigation carry you everywhere else.

## Encumbrance

Basic Lift (BL) = ST x ST / 5 (round to nearest whole number).

| Level | Max Weight | Move Penalty | Dodge Penalty |
|-------|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| None (0) | up to BL | 0 | 0 |
| Light (1) | up to 2 x BL | -1 | -1 |
| Medium (2) | up to 3 x BL | -2 | -2 |
| Heavy (3) | up to 6 x BL | -3 | -3 |
| X-Heavy (4) | up to 10 x BL | -4 | -4 |

Cannot carry more than 10 x BL. Move = Basic Speed (drop fractions) minus encumbrance penalty.

### Common BL Values

| ST | BL | Light (1) max | Medium (2) max | Heavy (3) max |
|----|-----|---------------|----------------|---------------|
| 9 | 16 | 32 | 48 | 96 |
| 10 | 20 | 40 | 60 | 120 |
| 11 | 24 | 48 | 72 | 144 |
| 12 | 29 | 58 | 87 | 174 |
| 13 | 34 | 68 | 102 | 204 |

**Encumbrance is critical for outdoorsmen** — they carry everything
on their backs for days or weeks. Hiking skill rolls apply once per
hour of travel; at Medium or above encumbrance, HT rolls are required
more frequently. FP loss from heavy encumbrance is the main attrition
hazard. Very Fit doubles FP recovery during rest periods.

**Example:** ST 11 (BL 24) with full wilderness kit (~42 lbs) +
bolt-action rifle (8.9) + ammo (~3) + knife (1) = ~55 lbs = Medium
encumbrance (Move -2, Dodge -2). Upgrade to ST 12 (BL 29) or reduce
kit weight to maintain Light.

### Hiking Roll Summary

One roll per hour of travel. On failure, the party uses more time
and the character loses FP. Encumbrance level directly modifies
the effective skill.

| Encumbrance | Hiking Modifier | Daily March |
|-------------|----------------|-------------|
| None | +0 | 24 miles |
| Light | -1 | ~20 miles |
| Medium | -2 | ~16 miles |
| Heavy | -4 | ~8 miles |

See B351 for full Hiking rules.
