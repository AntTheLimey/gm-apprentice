## Build Framework

Point allocation guidance for thief/spy archetypes. Scale
proportionally for other point totals.

| Category | 150 pts | 175 pts | 250 pts |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|
| Attributes | 80-100 | 90-115 | 120-160 |
| Secondary mods | 0-10 | 0-10 | 5-15 |
| Advantages | 20-35 | 25-45 | 40-70 |
| Disadvantages | -40 to -50 | -50 to -70 | -50 to -75 |
| Quirks | -5 | -5 | -5 |
| Skills | 40-60 | 50-75 | 70-110 |

**Attribute priorities:** The thief/spy archetype splits cleanly
into two paths — choose one as the primary emphasis.

- **Physical path** (burglar, assassin, courier): DX 13+ is the
  foundation — it raises Stealth, Lockpicking, Climbing, and
  melee attack/defense. HT 11-12 for endurance. ST 10 is fine;
  this archetype avoids fights. Per 13+ for trap detection,
  spotting surveillance, and Shadowing.
- **Social path** (con artist, spy, intelligence agent): IQ 13+
  is primary — it raises Fast-Talk, Acting, Disguise, Forgery,
  and most operational skills. Per 12+ for Detect Lies and
  reading people. DX 11-12 to avoid embarrassment.

Most builds benefit from Per 12+ regardless of path. HT 11 is
worth the investment for Escape and resisting poison.

**Near-mandatory:** Physical infiltrators should take Flexibility
[5] or Double-Jointed [15] for Escape/Climbing bonuses. Social
infiltrators should take Smooth Operator [15/level] — it covers
Acting, Carousing, Diplomacy, Fast-Talk, Intimidation, Leadership,
Savoir-Faire, and Sex Appeal. Luck [15] is efficient for either
path. Zeroed [10] is worth considering for any spy-type build.

## Combat Skills (Light)

Thief/spy combat doctrine: avoid fights entirely. When unavoidable,
the goal is to escape or incapacitate quickly, not to win. Invest
lightly — enough to not be helpless.

| Skill | Diff | Page | Defaults | Notes |
|-------|------|------|----------|-------|
| Guns (Pistol) | DX/E | B198 | DX-4 | TL. Primary spy firearm; concealable, suppress-able |
| Knife | DX/E | B208 | DX-4 | Backup melee; concealable, always on hand |
| Brawling | DX/E | B182 | — | Basic unarmed; +1/die above 2; Parry Skill/2+3 |
| Garrote | DX/E | B202 | — | Requires grapple first; silent incapacitation |
| Fast-Draw (Pistol) | DX/E | B194 | — | Draw as free action; essential for ambush scenarios |
| Stealth | DX/A | B222 | DX-5, IQ-5 | Fighting is a failure state — Stealth is combat avoidance |
| Jumping | DX/E | B203 | — | Escape over fences, gaps; no roll needed under normal conditions |
| Running | HT/A | B218 | HT-5 | Sustained running when sprint fails |
| Acrobatics | DX/H | B174 | DX-6 | +2 Dodge when successful; rooftop chases |

**Combat notes:** Do not invest heavily in combat at the cost of
operational skills. A thief caught is a thief who failed. One
level of Brawling and Guns (Pistol) at DX+1 gives adequate
self-defense. Knife is cheaper to maintain than most melee
weapons. Garrote requires grapple at Brawling/Wrestling default
and allows silent incapacitation (see B232). Fast-Draw (Pistol)
is high-value for ambush scenarios.

Fast-Draw.
