# Keeper Interview Technique

Reference for Phase 4. The skill's exclusive value — no other
skill does this. Validated by real ingestion experience (Canticle
campaign, April 2026).

## Core Rules

### 1. One Question at a Time

Never present a bulk list of unknowns. Each answer may change
the next question. Bulk questions produce shallow answers;
contextual single questions unlock cascading memories.

**Bad:** "Here are 10 things I need you to confirm. [list]"

**Good:** "After the investigators disrupted the Stonehenge
ritual, what happened to Dr. Hume and Lady Danforth?"

### 2. Contextual Framing

Give the GM enough scene context to trigger memory. Don't ask
bare questions — set the scene first.

**Bad:** "What happened to Dr. Hume?"

**Good:** "After the investigators disrupted the Stonehenge
ritual — they'd ridden ahead to Salisbury and recruited local
farmers for the assault — what happened to Dr. Hume and Lady
Danforth?"

### 3. Follow the Thread

When an answer reveals new information, ask targeted follow-ups
before moving to the next topic. Each answer is a door to more
detail.

In the Canticle ingestion, asking about Hume's fate triggered
a detailed account of the entire Stonehenge assault plan, the
farmer recruitment, the two-pronged attack, the Orphean Society
raid, a mercy-killing, and a reconnaissance mission — none of
which were in any source document. Don't move to the next topic
too quickly.

### 4. Record as You Go

Every GM answer is immediately logged as a confirmed play event.
Don't batch answers for processing later. Each answer:
- Gets AUTHORITATIVE status
- Is linked to specific entities and timeline
- Updates the confirmed events list

### 5. Know When to Stop

If the GM says "I don't remember," flag the item as unresolved
and move on. Don't push, don't invent, don't suggest what
might have happened. Some details are lost to time.

### 6. Distinguish Confidence

Not all GM answers carry equal weight:

| GM Response | Confidence | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| "Definitely happened" / specific details | AUTHORITATIVE | Full canon |
| "I think so" / "sounds right" | AUTHORITATIVE + UNVERIFIED marker | `<!-- UNVERIFIED: [claim] -->` |
| "Maybe" / uncertain | Flag for future resolution | Don't include in play events |
| "I don't remember" | Unresolved | Add to unresolved list |

## Question Categories

Work through these in order. Earlier categories often answer
later ones.

### 1. Unsorted Items (from Phase 2)

Resolve ambiguous bucket assignments before starting the
play-event interview. These are quick classification questions:
"These notes mention the docks — is this from Session 3 or
Session 5?"

### 2. Major Outcomes

The biggest unknowns: who won fights, who lived, who died,
which path was taken. Start here because these answers
cascade into everything else.

### 3. Investigation Paths

Which locations did the players actually visit? Which NPCs
did they actually meet? The prep document may list five
locations but they only visited two.

### 4. Handout and Clue Pickups

Which physical handouts were found? Which clues were
discovered? Important for tracking what the players know
vs what's available.

### 5. NPC Fates

What happened to specific NPCs after encounters? Were they
killed, captured, escaped, befriended? The prep document
may describe an intended fate that differs from what played.

### 6. Player Decisions at Branching Points

Which of the scenario's branching points did the players
take? These are the moments where prep diverges most from
play.

### 7. PC Narrative Arcs

One question per active PC covering their personal arc across
the ingested period. This feeds character story file
construction in Phase 5.

Frame with character context — what the sources already show
about this PC — and ask what drove them and what changed them.

**Example:** "From the notes, Georgiana spent most of the Lyon
chapter investigating the university murders and befriending
Professor Lang. What was her personal arc across these
sessions — what drove her, and how was she changed by it?"

If the GM has little to add beyond what's in the sources,
that's fine — the existing play events will still produce a
story entry. Don't push for invented detail.

## Output

After the interview, the confirmed events list from Phase 3
should be significantly expanded. Produce:

- Updated confirmed events with source attribution (GM
  interview, specific answer)
- List of items that remain unresolved
- Any new entities discovered during the interview that
  weren't in the source material
